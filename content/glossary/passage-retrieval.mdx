**Passage retrieval pulls the paragraph, not the page.** Instead of ranking whole
documents, modern systems index and return individual passages — the specific chunk
that best answers a query — which the engine then quotes or grounds its answer in.
The unit of competition in AI search is the passage, not the article.

This single fact reshapes how you write for AEO. It means every paragraph has to
stand on its own as a complete answer, because it may be retrieved with none of its
surrounding context attached. A passage that depends on the sentence before it, or
trails off without making its point, fails on retrieval even if the full page is
excellent. Writing self-contained, answer-first passages is the
[extractability](/pillars/extractability) pillar, and it's a direct response to how
passage retrieval works.

**Example.** Search inside a long guide and the engine surfaces one paragraph — say,
the exact step that answers your question — and cites that page. The other 1,900
words didn't lose; they simply weren't the passage that got retrieved.
