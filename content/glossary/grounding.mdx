**Grounding is what keeps an AI answer tethered to real sources instead of
invention.** A grounded response is built from documents the system actually
retrieved, so its claims can be traced back to and cited from those sources;
an ungrounded response is the model guessing from memory, which is where
hallucinations come from.

For AEO, grounding is the mechanism that turns your content into a citation. When
an engine grounds an answer in your page, it both uses your words and names you as
the source. To be reliably grounded, your claims need to be clearly stated,
self-contained, and backed by evidence — the [credibility](/pillars/credibility)
and [extractability](/pillars/extractability) work — so the engine treats your
passage as the safe thing to quote and attribute.

**Example.** Ask Google AI Overviews a factual question and it shows a short answer
with source links beneath it. Those links are the grounding — the pages the answer
was built from. Being one of them is the goal of every AEO tactic.
