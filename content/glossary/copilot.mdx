**Microsoft Copilot is Microsoft's AI assistant, built on the Bing index.** It
answers conversationally and cites web sources, and because it draws on Bing,
your presence in Copilot answers depends on being indexed by
[Bingbot](/glossary/bingbot) — a different crawler from the OpenAI and Perplexity
bots.

Once you're in Bing's index, Copilot citations follow the familiar pattern: the
clearest, most [extractable](/pillars/extractability) answer wins retrieval, and
[authority](/pillars/authority) signals help decide which source gets named. Because
many sites focus only on Google, Bing-and-Copilot visibility is often an
underexploited opportunity — confirming you're crawlable by Bingbot is a quick,
high-value check.

**Example.** A brand that's well-indexed in Bing can appear in Copilot's answers
across Windows, Edge, and Microsoft 365 even if competitors who ignore Bing don't —
reach that's easy to miss if you only watch Google.
