**Disambiguation is sorting out which "Apple," or which "you," is meant.** Many names
are shared — companies, people, products — so engines must decide which
[entity](/glossary/entity) a mention refers to using surrounding context. Get it wrong
and your mentions get attributed to someone else, scattering the trust you've earned.

Helping engines disambiguate is part of the [authority](/pillars/authority) pillar.
Consistent identifying details, a distinctive description, and [sameAs](/glossary/sameas)
links to authoritative profiles all give engines the context to map a mention to the
correct node in the [knowledge graph](/glossary/knowledge-graph). It matters most when
your brand or personal name collides with a more famous one — clarity is how you avoid
being merged into, or overshadowed by, the wrong entity.

**Example.** If your consultancy is named "Summit" — a name dozens of businesses share
— pairing every mention with consistent location, industry, and profile links helps an
engine tell your Summit apart from the rest and credit your reputation to you.
