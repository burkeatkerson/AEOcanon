**CCBot is the crawler that feeds Common Crawl.** It gathers public web pages into
[Common Crawl](/glossary/common-crawl), the large open dataset that many AI models
have trained on. Because so many downstream models derive from it, CCBot's reach is
broad — allowing it means your content can flow into numerous training sets at once.

It respects [robots.txt](/glossary/robots-txt) via the `CCBot` user-agent, so the
[access](/pillars/access) decision is yours. Like other training crawlers, blocking
CCBot is a [training](/glossary/training-data)-rights choice that won't change
whether a search-grounded engine cites you today. Note that historical crawls
already collected can't be retroactively removed by blocking it now.

**Example.** A site that wants to limit its presence in open training corpora adds a
`User-agent: CCBot` / `Disallow: /` rule — affecting future Common Crawl snapshots,
though not copies already distributed.
