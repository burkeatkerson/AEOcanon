**Embeddings turn text into coordinates that encode meaning.** Each passage is
converted into a long list of numbers (a vector) positioned so that pieces of text
with similar meaning sit close together — regardless of whether they use the same
words. This is what lets an answer engine match a conversational question to a
relevant passage on your site.

Embeddings are why keyword stuffing is obsolete and why writing for *meaning*
matters. Retrieval no longer looks for an exact phrase; it looks for the passage
whose embedding is nearest to the question's embedding. So a paragraph that clearly
and completely expresses one idea — the [extractability](/pillars/extractability)
discipline — embeds cleanly and gets matched, while a vague or padded one lands in
fuzzy, low-relevance space and gets skipped.

**Example.** A page that says "how to lower your monthly energy bill" can be
retrieved for the query "ways to spend less on electricity" because their
embeddings are close in meaning, even with almost no shared words. That semantic
match is embeddings at work.
