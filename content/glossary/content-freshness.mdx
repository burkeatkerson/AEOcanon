**Content freshness is how current and maintained your pages are.** It's signaled by
genuine, substantive updates and a visible, recent date — and engines pay attention,
because retrieval lets them prefer up-to-date sources and they tend to cycle older
content out of answers over time.

It's the [freshness](/pillars/freshness) pillar, and it directly counters the model's
[knowledge cutoff](/glossary/knowledge-cutoff): fresh, retrievable pages supply the
current facts a model's frozen memory can't. The key word is *substantive* — quietly
changing a date without improving the content is the kind of trick engines increasingly
ignore. Real freshness means revisiting pages as facts change, updating numbers and
claims, and showing the update honestly.

**Example.** A "best laptops 2026" guide that's genuinely refreshed when new models ship
stays citable, while a 2023 version decays out of answers — the same query, won by
whoever kept their page actually current.
