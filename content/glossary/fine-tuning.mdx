**Fine-tuning specializes a general model on focused data.** Starting from a
pre-trained [large language model](/glossary/large-language-model), you continue
training it on a smaller, targeted dataset so it adopts a particular style, domain,
or task. The base knowledge stays; the behavior is nudged.

For AEO, fine-tuning is worth understanding mainly to separate it from retrieval.
Being cited in an answer engine comes from [grounding](/glossary/grounding) in
retrieved sources, not from being in a model's fine-tuning set — so AEO targets
retrieval, not training. Where your original work does influence models is through
the broader [training data](/glossary/training-data) pipeline over time, which is one
more reason that genuinely [original](/pillars/originality) content, found nowhere
else, is valuable.

**Example.** A company might fine-tune a model on its support transcripts to build a
help bot. That's a product decision; it has little to do with whether a public answer
engine cites the company's website, which is governed by retrieval.
