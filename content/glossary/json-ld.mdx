**JSON-LD is the cleanest way to add structured data.** It places your
[schema markup](/glossary/schema-markup) in a self-contained `<script type="application/ld+json">`
block, separate from your visible HTML — so you describe your content to machines
without altering the page's appearance or tangling markup into the layout. It's
Google's recommended format and the industry default.

For AEO, the practical advantages are maintainability and accuracy: because the
structured data lives in one discrete block, it's easy to generate, validate, and keep
in sync with the page. That makes it straightforward to express the entities and
relationships an engine needs to read you correctly — the
[extractability](/pillars/extractability) of your *metadata*. Tools like Google's Rich
Results Test let you confirm it parses.

**Example.** A recipe page includes a JSON-LD block listing its ingredients, cook
time, and rating. Visitors see a normal page; machines read the structured block and
know precisely what each value means.
