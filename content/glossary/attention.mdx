**Attention is how a model decides what to focus on.** Within a
[transformer](/glossary/transformer), the attention mechanism scores how relevant
every word is to every other, so the model can emphasize the parts that carry meaning
for the task — connecting a pronoun to its subject, a claim to its evidence, an answer
to its question.

The practical AEO takeaway is that attention isn't evenly spread. Clear, prominent,
well-structured statements draw more of it than buried or tangential text, which
reinforces [position bias](/glossary/position-bias) and the case for answer-first,
[extractable](/pillars/extractability) writing. Make the sentence you want quoted
prominent and unambiguous, and you make it the part the model attends to and reuses.

**Example.** In a paragraph that opens with a crisp answer and then elaborates, the
model's attention lands on that opening claim — exactly the sentence you'd want an
engine to lift and cite.
