**sameAs connects the dots between your profiles.** It's a
[schema.org](/glossary/schema-markup) property that points from your page to your
other authoritative presences — LinkedIn, Wikipedia, Wikidata, Crunchbase, official
social accounts — explicitly asserting that they all describe the same
[entity](/glossary/entity).

For AEO it's a direct aid to entity recognition and the
[authority](/pillars/authority) pillar. By stating that "this brand here" is "this
brand on Wikidata" and "this profile on LinkedIn," sameAs helps engines unify your
scattered presence into a single, trusted node — supporting how you appear in the
[knowledge graph](/glossary/knowledge-graph). It's most powerful when it points to
already-authoritative, corroborating sources rather than thin or self-controlled
pages.

**Example.** A founder's bio uses sameAs to link their Wikidata entry, university
faculty page, and verified social profiles. An engine reads those links and confidently
treats all of them — and the work attributed across them — as one recognized person.
