**Organization schema makes your business a clear entity to machines.** It's the
[schema.org](/glossary/schema-markup) type for a company, stating the official name,
logo, URL, and — through [sameAs](/glossary/sameas) — links to your authoritative
profiles, so engines can consolidate everything about you into one recognized
[entity](/glossary/entity).

Its role in the [authority](/pillars/authority) pillar is consistency. When your
organization details are declared the same way everywhere, engines connect your
mentions confidently and are likelier to surface accurate facts about you. It works
hand in hand with consistent off-site information; the schema asserts your canonical
identity, and corroboration around the web confirms it. As ever, the details must be
accurate and consistent to help rather than confuse.

**Example.** A company adds Organization schema with its exact legal name, logo, and
sameAs links to its LinkedIn and Crunchbase profiles. An engine can then reliably tie
together references to the business and answer "who is [company]" correctly.
