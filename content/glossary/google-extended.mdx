**Google-Extended is a training opt-out, not a search setting.** It's a token you
place in [robots.txt](/glossary/robots-txt) to tell Google not to use your content
for training its generative models (Gemini, Vertex AI). Critically, it is not a
separate crawler and has no effect on how Googlebot indexes you for Search — or on
whether you appear in AI Overviews.

This distinction trips people up constantly. Blocking Google-Extended does not
remove you from AI Overviews, because those are served through Googlebot, and Google
offers no dedicated opt-out from AI Overviews short of blocking Googlebot entirely —
which would also delete you from Search. So Google-Extended is purely a
content-rights lever about model training; it is not an AEO visibility control. Your
actual AI Overview visibility is governed by ordinary crawlability — the
[access](/pillars/access) pillar — and the quality of your answer.

**Example.** A site adds `User-agent: Google-Extended` / `Disallow: /` to keep its
content out of Gemini's training set, and its Google Search rankings and AI Overview
appearances are completely unchanged — exactly as intended.
