**Share of voice is how big a slice of the AI conversation is yours.** Across a
fixed set of questions your customers ask, it's the percentage of answers where
your brand shows up at all — named, mentioned, or cited — usually measured against
your competitors so you can see who dominates a topic.

It's the headline AEO metric because, in a zero-click world, presence in the answer
matters more than rank. Share of voice tracks the trend you actually care about:
are engines naming you more or less often over time, and where are competitors
winning answers you're absent from? Measuring it requires a stable [prompt
set](/glossary/prompt-set) and repeated sampling — the [adaptability](/pillars/adaptability)
discipline of instrumenting and watching the trend rather than any single reading.

**Example.** If you track 25 questions across ChatGPT and Perplexity and your brand
appears in 8 of the 25 answers, your share of voice is roughly 32% on that set.
Watching that number move month over month tells you whether your AEO work is
compounding. For the stricter, cite-only version of this metric, see [citation
share](/glossary/citation-share).
