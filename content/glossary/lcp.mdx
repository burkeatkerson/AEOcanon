**LCP measures when the main content appears.** As a [Core Web
Vitals](/glossary/core-web-vitals) metric, it clocks the moment the largest visible
element — usually a hero image or headline block — finishes rendering, which is a good
proxy for when the page feels usefully loaded to a visitor.

Where [First Contentful Paint](/glossary/fcp) marks the first pixel of content, LCP marks
the main content, so it's a stricter speed signal. For the [access](/pillars/access)
pillar, a fast LCP means your primary content is available quickly to users and well-placed
for crawling. The usual improvements — serving content as HTML rather than assembling it in
the browser, optimizing images, and reducing render-blocking work — also tend to make your
content more reliably readable by AI.

**Example.** If a page's headline and lead image render in under a couple of seconds, it has
a good LCP; if the main content only appears after a long JavaScript load, LCP suffers — and
so does the page's overall readiness for both users and crawlers.
