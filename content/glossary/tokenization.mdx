**Tokenization is the step that turns your text into model-readable pieces.** Before
a model can do anything with a passage, it breaks the text into
[tokens](/glossary/token) — splitting words into common sub-pieces so it can handle
any input, including names and rare terms it never saw whole during training.

For AEO it's mostly background plumbing, but it has one practical implication: clear,
conventional language tokenizes predictably and is represented cleanly, whereas
odd formatting, run-together strings, or gimmicky spellings can fragment into messy
tokens that are harder for the model to interpret and quote. Plain, well-formed
prose — part of the [extractability](/pillars/extractability) pillar — is the safest
input. Tokenization is also the precursor to creating [embeddings](/glossary/embeddings).

**Example.** A normal word like "optimization" tokenizes neatly, while a stylized
string like "Op-Ti-Miz-Ation!!!" fragments into many awkward tokens — needless
friction for a model trying to read and reuse your point.
