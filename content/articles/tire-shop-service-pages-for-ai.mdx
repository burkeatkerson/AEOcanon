**Write tire shop service pages AI will cite by giving each service its own page that leads with the
answer to the cost, scope, and service-area questions, in plain language a driver and an engine can
lift.** One self-contained, crawlable page per service beats a single bloated services page every time.

<Callout variant="key" title="Quick answer">

    — and <strong>lead with the answer</strong> to what it costs, what's included, how long it takes, and
    whether you serve their area. Make each page <strong>self-contained and crawlable</strong>. One
    focused page per service beats one bloated services page.
  </p>

## Why one page per service?

Because a citation is awarded to the page that best answers one specific question — and a catch-all
services page answers none of them well. When you give new tires, flat repair, rotation, alignment, and
wheels each [their own page](/learn/should-content-sections-stand-alone), each can go deep on its own
cost, scope, and details — and each becomes citable for its own query. A single page trying to cover
everything is shallow on all of them, so the engine cites a competitor with a dedicated, focused page.

## What should each page lead with?

The answer the driver came for, before anything about you. Compare these two openings:

<BeforeAfter
  label="A tire service page's opening"
  before="At Summit Tire we treat every customer like family and stand behind our work with decades of combined experience and a commitment to honest service..."
  after="A set of 4 installed tires at Summit typically runs $480–$900 depending on size and brand, including mount, balance, and a road-force check — usually done in about an hour. We carry [brands] and take walk-ins."
  beforeLabel="Weak (buries the answer)"
  afterLabel="Strong (answer-first, citable)"
  note="The engine can lift the second opening directly into 'how much for 4 tires near me' — the first gives it nothing to quote."
/>

Then add the detail, the brands, and the proof below. This is [answer-first
writing](/learn/answer-first-sentence) applied to the trade.

## What makes a service page extractable?

Plain language and a clean structure. Write the way a driver asks — "a set of 4 tires installed
typically runs …" — not in jargon, and use [question-shaped headings](/learn/question-shaped-headings)
the engine can match to a query. Keep each answer in a self-contained passage so it can be [lifted without
the surrounding page](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage), reinforce it with [TireShop
schema](/learn/tire-shop-schema-markup), and make sure the page is fast and crawlable. Answer-first,
focused, and proven — that's the page an engine cites and a driver visits.
