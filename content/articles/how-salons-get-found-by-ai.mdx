## What does an AI engine actually do to find you?

It retrieves, ranks, and names. When someone asks for a salon, the engine pulls the places it can read,
judges which best fit the request and look well-reviewed, and [names just a
few](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Each step is a filter: if the crawler can't read your
menu and pricing, you're out before ranking; if your hours are wrong, you're dropped from near-me; if
your reviews are thin, a competitor gets named. Being found means clearing every filter — and for
salons, the readable menu and visible pricing are often what's missing.

## Why can't clients find my salon?

Almost always one specific broken gate — and for salons it's often the menu and pricing. If they're
trapped in a [booking widget or image the crawler can't read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site),
the engine doesn't know what you offer or what it costs, so it can't recommend you for "affordable salon
near me" or "balayage specialist nearby." Other common breaks: inconsistent hours, or thin reviews. The
fix is diagnostic — find the *highest* broken gate and repair it first. For many salons, getting the
menu and pricing into plain text is the highest-leverage move.

## Does ranking in Google mean AI can find me?

Not on its own. Ranking and a map listing help, but AI citation also needs your [services and pricing in
readable text](/learn/salon-service-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews. A salon can show on a map yet be
absent from "best salon near me for color" answers if the engine can't read the menu or the reviews are
thin. Clear all three gates and you get [found in both](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).
