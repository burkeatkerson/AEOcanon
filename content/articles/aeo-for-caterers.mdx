## Why does AEO matter so much for caterers?

Because catering decisions are high-value, planned, and made on the first good answer. When someone asks
"who can cater my wedding," they act on what the assistant names — and the [AI answer names only two or
three businesses](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations), not a page of listings. Pew Research [found
people clicked a link just 8% of the time when an AI summary appeared, versus 15%
without](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer *is* the decision. Being the cited caterer is the new equivalent of being the name every
planner passes along — and it sends an inquiry that books directly, not a lead resold to four competitors.

## How do caterers get recommended by AI?

By clearing the three gates a client — and an engine — judge you on, in order.

<Steps
  items={[
    { title: "Be readable (Access)", body: "Your packages, per-head pricing, cuisines, and service area as real text AI crawlers can fetch — not trapped in a PDF, an image, or behind a 'request a quote' wall. Many catering sites hide everything until you inquire." },
    { title: "Answer the real question (Alignment + Extractability)", body: "Plain-text answers to 'how much per person', 'what's included', 'do you do weddings', 'can you accommodate allergies and vegan', 'how far ahead to book', and 'where do you serve'." },
    { title: "Be trusted (Authority + Credibility)", body: "Consistent name, address, and phone everywhere; a complete Google Business Profile; and genuine, recent reviews that mention events and dishes. This is what decides who gets named." },
  ]}

Most caterers fail gate one without knowing it — pricing and packages are a PDF or hidden behind a quote
form, invisible to the engine. Fixing that, plus accurate listings and reviews, is the highest-leverage
work, and it's the same foundation that [ranks you in Google](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together) too.

## Isn't this just SEO?

It's mostly the local SEO you should already be doing, pointed at a new surface. The fast, crawlable,
well-reviewed caterer with readable packages that ranks is the same one that gets
[cited](/learn/is-aeo-replacing-seo). AEO adds putting your pricing and answers in [plain
text](/learn/answer-first-sentence), [structured data](/learn/caterer-schema-markup) for cuisine and
service area, and the [review consistency](/learn/caterer-reviews-ai-recommendations) answer engines
lean on. You don't choose between them — you run one program that wins both.

## Where do independent caterers have the advantage?

In specificity and authenticity. AI names only a few businesses and [citations spread
thin](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands), so an independent with readable packages, clear pricing, and
genuine reviews that name events can win the recommendation over a chain or a faceless lead-site listing.
That's the [Originality](/pillars/originality) and [local authority](/learn/local-aeo-for-caterers) edge
a platform can't copy — and every direct inquiry skips the lead fee.

## What should a caterer do first?

Start at the binding constraint. [Make your packages and pricing readable](/learn/caterer-menu-pages-for-ai)
as real text, then fix your [service area and listings](/learn/local-aeo-for-caterers) so 'catering near
me' places you correctly, then build [genuine reviews](/learn/caterer-reviews-ai-recommendations). Work
down the rest of this library from there — [winning weddings](/learn/wedding-catering-ai-search),
[corporate accounts](/learn/corporate-catering-aeo),
[Google Business Profile](/learn/caterer-google-business-profile-ai),

[questions clients actually ask](/learn/catering-questions-customers-ask-ai).
