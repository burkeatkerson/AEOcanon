## Why is recognition the key to local AEO?

Recognition is the key because AI can only recommend a local business it can tell
apart from every other. It builds that picture by merging [mentions](/glossary/branded-mention) of you from
across the web into a single business — your site, your profiles, directories, and
reviews. If those agree, you become one clear, trusted local [entity](/glossary/entity); if they
conflict, you fragment and fade. So local AEO starts with being unmistakable. (The
deeper idea is [entity recognition](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities); locally, it
mostly comes down to consistency.)

## Step 1 — Make your details identical everywhere

Make your business name, address, and phone number identical on every site they
appear — your website, Google Business Profile, Bing Places, Yelp, industry
directories, and social profiles. Even small differences (a suite number here, an
old phone there, "Co." vs "Company") make you harder to recognize as one business.
This is [NAP consistency](/learn/nap-consistency), and for a local business it's the
single highest-leverage move.

## Step 2 — Complete your Google Business Profile

Fully complete and verify your [Google Business Profile](https://www.google.com/business/) — category, hours, service
area, description, photos, and services. A complete, consistent profile strengthens
how search and AI systems recognize you as a real local entity and feeds the local
information they draw on. An empty or out-of-date profile does the opposite.

## Step 3 — Earn genuine reviews

Earn a steady stream of honest reviews on Google and the platforms that matter in
your industry. Reviews do double duty: they're a trust signal AI can see, and they
supply the real language customers use about your service. Ask happy customers
directly, make it easy with a link, and aim for a consistent trickle rather than a
one-time push. Never buy or fake reviews — it's against platform rules and engines
increasingly detect manipulation.

## Step 4 — Answer local questions on your site

Write clear pages that answer the local questions people actually ask AI — "best
[service] in [town]", "[service] near me", "how much does [service] cost in
[area]". Lead with the answer, name your area plainly, and be specific. This pairs
your local recognition with content AI can quote, the same [answer-first
approach](/learn/answer-first-sentence) that works everywhere, pointed at local
intent.
