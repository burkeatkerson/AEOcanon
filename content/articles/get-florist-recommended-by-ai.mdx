## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a shop the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine can
read your arrangements and place you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as where to order
— and that leans harder on whether you deliver reliably and buyers love you. The engine recommends the
shop it can [confidently confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, reliable, and well-reviewed,
because when it recommends you it's putting its credibility behind the experience. Your job is to make
that confirmation easy — readable arrangements, accurate delivery, and genuine reviews.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). Wrong hours, an unreadable site, or a thin review profile introduces
doubt, and doubt sends the buyer elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one florist over another?

Because it picks the one it's most confident will deliver. Faced with several options, the engine names
the shop whose signals best match the request — right area, delivers in time — and whose reviews most
clearly say buyers love it and orders arrive on time. The competitor with an image-only site, wrong
hours, or thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it loses. Winning is about being the [easiest to verify
as the reliable, loved local choice](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — which is
exactly what this library builds, piece by piece.
