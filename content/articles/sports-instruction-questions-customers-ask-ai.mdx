**Students and parents ask AI lesson questions in four buckets — cost ('how much are golf lessons'), fit
('beginner golf coach near me'), lesson type ('private vs group lessons'), and logistics ('do you teach
juniors', 'where do you teach').** Mapping each to readable content that answers it is the core of a
coaching AEO content plan.

## How do I find the exact questions?

Listen where students already ask. Note what people ask at the first lesson and on inquiry calls, read
your reviews and the questions on your Google profile, scan local sport forums and parent groups, and
[prompt the assistants directly](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask) on your sport and area to see
the follow-ups they surface. Capture the *natural wording* — "is there a golf coach near me who's good
with total beginners" beats "novice-oriented golf instruction" — because engines match the student's
phrasing. Then prioritize by what genuinely fits your coaching.

## Should I publish pricing if other coaches hide it?

Yes — and it's an edge, not a risk. Students and parents constantly ask AI "how much are golf lessons
near me," and a clear starting rate or package range in readable text lets the engine recommend you for
those high-intent cost searches — while competitors who bury pricing in a widget get skipped.
Transparency also builds trust with a price-conscious student before they ever book. It's pure
[Alignment](/pillars/alignment): the real question, answered where the engine and the student can read
it. Map every bucket to readable content and you've built the plan that gets a coach cited.
