**A painting company needs a website rebuild for AEO when the site is a slow photo gallery with little
readable text, hard for AI crawlers to read, or without per-service answer-first pages and schema —
because no amount of content fixes a foundation engines can't parse.** The rebuild is the access layer
everything else depends on.

<Callout variant="key" title="Quick answer">
  <p>
    You need a rebuild when the site is a <strong>slow photo gallery, uncrawlable, or
    structureless</strong> — image-only, little text, no per-service pages, no pricing, missing schema.
    Engines can't cite what they can't <strong>read and parse</strong>, so content layered on a broken
    foundation is wasted. <strong>Fix the access layer first</strong>.
  </p>
</Callout>

## Why is the site the binding constraint?

Because [access is the first gate](/pillars/access), and a gate you fail ends the contest before content
matters. If an AI crawler fetches your page and sees a wall of before-and-after photos with little text
— or the page renders only in the browser — or it's too slow, you're invisible no matter how good your
work or your reviews are. Many painting sites are beautiful portfolios that [hand a bot almost
nothing](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site). That's not a content problem you can write your way out
of; it's a foundation problem.

## How do I tell if my site is hurting me?

Run two quick tests, and look for the structural gaps.

<Steps
  items={[
    { title: "The JavaScript-off test", body: "Load a key page with JavaScript disabled (or view source). If the content vanishes or it's all images, AI crawlers likely see an empty page." },
    { title: "The speed test", body: "Check your load time. Image-heavy painting portfolios are often slow, and slow pages get crawled and trusted less." },
    { title: "The structure test", body: "Do you have a dedicated page per service with text, or just a gallery? No per-service text means nothing focused to cite." },
    { title: "The schema test", body: "Is there accurate HousePainter/LocalBusiness structured data, or none? Missing or wrong schema leaves the engine guessing." },
  ]}
/>

If a page is empty without scripts, all images, slow, has no [dedicated service
pages](/learn/painter-service-pages-for-ai), or lacks [clean schema](/learn/painter-schema-markup), the
site is working against you. A fast, server-rendered foundation with real text — not just photos — is
what makes everything else possible.

## Can't I just add content instead?

Only if the foundation is already sound. Adding [answer-first
pages](/learn/answer-first-sentence) to a fast, crawlable site works beautifully — that's the whole
program. But adding content to a slow, image-heavy, client-rendered site is building on sand: the engine
still can't read or trust it, so the new pages never get cited. The honest sequence is foundation first,
content second. Get the access layer right —
[server-rendered](/learn/server-side-vs-client-side-rendering), fast, structured, with real text — and
the content you publish on top finally has a chance to be found.
