## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a local entity the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine
can retrieve you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as the answer — and that's a higher bar of
trust. The engine recommends the mechanic it can [confidently confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is
real, fair, and well-regarded, because when it recommends you it's putting its own credibility behind the
choice. Your job is to make that confirmation effortless and consistent — which, for a business with no
storefront, means making your service area and reputation unmistakable.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). One inconsistency (an old phone number, a vague service area) introduces
doubt, and doubt routes the recommendation elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one mobile mechanic over another?

Because it picks the one it's most sure about. Faced with several options, the engine names the mechanic
whose signals most confidently match the request — right service, right area, available now, genuinely
reviewed. The competitor with vague coverage or thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so they lose even if the
work is just as good. Winning is less about being the best mechanic in town and more about being the
[easiest to verify as the best](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — which is exactly what
this library builds, piece by piece.
