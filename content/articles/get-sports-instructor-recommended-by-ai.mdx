## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be an instructor the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine
can read your lessons and place you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as the coach to
learn from — and that leans harder on whether students love you and improve. The engine recommends the
coach it can [confidently confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, skilled, and
well-reviewed, because when it recommends you it's putting its credibility behind the experience. Your
job is to make that confirmation easy — readable lessons, accurate hours, and genuine, loving reviews.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). Wrong hours, hidden lessons, or a thin review profile introduces
doubt, and doubt sends the student elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one coach over another?

Because it picks the one it's most confident students will love and improve with. Faced with several
options, the engine names the coach whose signals best match the request — right sport, right level,
right area — and whose reviews most clearly say people love it and got results. The competitor with
widget-hidden lessons, wrong hours, or thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it loses. Winning is about
being the [easiest to verify as the loved local choice](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend)
— which is exactly what this library builds, piece by piece.
