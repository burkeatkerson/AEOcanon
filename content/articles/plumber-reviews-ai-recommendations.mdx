## Why do reviews decide who AI recommends?

Because they're independent evidence of quality, and an engine recommending a plumber
wants to be safe. When AI is asked to recommend, it
[synthesizes sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from Google and
third-party review platforms rather than taking your word for it — so the volume,
recency, and quality of your reviews directly shape whether you're named. For a trade
where a bad call means water damage, that trust signal carries real weight. It's the
[Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made concrete.

## How many reviews does a plumber need?

There's no magic number — [consistency and recency beat a
total](/learn/how-many-mentions-to-get-cited). A steady stream of genuine recent
reviews signals an active, trusted business far better than a pile of three-year-old
ones, so aim for an ongoing flow rather than a one-time push. A handful of fresh,
specific reviews each month does more than a single burst that then goes stale.

## Do specific reviews help more than generic praise?

Yes — specificity corroborates. A review that names what you did — "fixed our burst
pipe at midnight," "replaced our water heater same day" — gives engines concrete detail
tying you to those services and to [fast emergency
response](/learn/emergency-plumber-ai-search), reinforcing exactly the questions
customers ask. Generic five-star praise is fine, but specific reviews map to real
queries and pull more weight. Encourage customers to mention the job and the timing.

## Do fake reviews work?

No — they hurt. Fake or incentivized reviews risk detection, violate platform rules,
and create inconsistent patterns that [engines and platforms increasingly
catch](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) — adding no genuine trust and
potentially damaging your standing. The durable path is earning real reviews: ask
satisfied customers at the right moment, make it easy, respond to show you're active,
and keep the flow steady. Slower, but it's what actually moves recommendations — and
managing it is part of the [done-for-you](/pricing) trust work.
