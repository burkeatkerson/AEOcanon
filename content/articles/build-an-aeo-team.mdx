**An AEO team isn't a new department — it's a cross-functional operating model
spanning content, technical, off-site authority, and analytics, with one owner
accountable for citation share.** You can run it with a single coordinator or a
full pod; what matters is clear ownership of the metric and a steady measure-and-fix
cadence.

## Who owns AEO?

One person should own AEO — typically a head of SEO, content, or growth — and be
accountable for **[citation share](/glossary/citation-share)** as the headline metric. Because the work spans
teams, the failure mode is diffusion: everyone contributes a little, no one owns the
outcome, and it quietly stalls. A single accountable owner who coordinates the
contributing functions and reports the metric is the difference between a program
and a good intention.

## What capabilities does the model need?

The model needs four capabilities, which map cleanly onto the AEO Canon's layers —
not necessarily four headcount:

    { title: "Off-site authority", body: "Digital PR, brand mentions, and community presence — the authority pillar. Earns the trust engines weight most." },

  ]}

These correspond to the pillars: [access](/pillars/access),
[extractability](/pillars/extractability), [credibility](/pillars/credibility),
[authority](/pillars/authority), and [adaptability](/pillars/adaptability). The
technical capability owes a lot to standard SEO hygiene — controlling crawler access
with [robots.txt](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/robots/intro)
is part of what the access pillar requires. Staffing
AEO is really staffing the Canon.

The through-line: you rarely need *new* people to begin, because AEO reuses existing
SEO, content, and PR capabilities. You need an owner, a cadence, and the
measurement to direct effort.

## What's the operating cadence?

The operating cadence is a continuous loop, not a project plan: **measure → prioritize
the biggest citation gaps → ship fixes → re-measure.** In practice that's a weekly or
biweekly look at citation-share trends per engine, a monthly planning cycle that
turns the worst gaps into content and authority work, and a quarterly strategy and
budget review. Because [citations are volatile](/glossary/citation-volatility) and engines change monthly, the loop
runs indefinitely — which is the [adaptability](/pillars/adaptability) pillar made
operational. Measure [per engine](/learn/per-engine-vs-blended-measurement), since a
blended number hides where the team should act.

## What do the first 90 days look like?

The first 90 days should move from baseline to repeatable loop, not to a big
reorg. A workable ramp:

By day 90 you want three things in place: a baseline you can trend against, a few
proof-point wins, and a standing cadence — that's a functioning operating model, not
a finished project. Everything after is the loop, run continuously.
