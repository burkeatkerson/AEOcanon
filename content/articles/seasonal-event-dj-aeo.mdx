## Why does seasonality matter for DJs?

Because event demand comes in waves, and clients plan them far ahead. Wedding season, prom and homecoming,
holiday office parties, New Year's, and festival season all bring searches weeks or months before the date —
"wedding DJ for next June," "prom DJ near me," "holiday party DJ." The DJ whose answer is
already published, crawled, and trusted [gets cited the moment planning starts](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo)
— while competitors are still updating their PDF. It's the [Freshness pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned
into a booking calendar.

## How do I prepare for each wave?

Build the content while it's calm, so it's ready when clients start booking.

## Should I make a new page each year?

No — update one durable page per recurring occasion. A persistent 'prom and homecoming DJ' or
'holiday party DJ' page [accumulates authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh the
dates, packages, and pricing each year. That beats spinning up a throwaway page annually that starts from
zero and splits your signals. Keep one page per occasion, refresh it ahead of the wave, and link it to your
[wedding](/learn/wedding-dj-ai-search) and [corporate and private event](/learn/corporate-and-private-events-dj-aeo)
pages — one durable page, refreshed and ready, beats scrambling when the wave arrives.
