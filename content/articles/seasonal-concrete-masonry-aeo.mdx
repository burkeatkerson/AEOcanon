## Why does seasonality matter for concrete contractors?

Because demand is weather-driven and the research happens before the pour. Concrete needs the right
temperatures to cure, so pours cluster from spring through fall — but homeowners plan a spring
driveway or a summer patio over the winter, asking the assistant *"when should I schedule"* and *"can
you pour concrete in cold weather"* months ahead. The contractor whose answer is already published,
crawled, and trusted [gets cited the moment planning
starts](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo) — well before bids. It's the [Freshness
pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned into a planning calendar, and a way to fill the pipeline ahead of
the spring rush.

Build the content while it's quiet, so it's ready when homeowners start researching.

## Should I make a new page each year?

No — update one durable page per seasonal topic. A persistent URL [accumulates
authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh it each year with current cost ranges and
details. That beats spinning up a throwaway page annually that starts from zero and splits your
signals. Keep one 'driveway cost' page, one 'best time of year to pour concrete' page, one 'planning a
spring patio' page — and refresh each ahead of its planning wave. One durable page, refreshed and
ready, beats scrambling when the calls come all at once in spring.
