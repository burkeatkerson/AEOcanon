**No, not for the fundamentals — answer-first content, clean structure, and
crawlability are doable in-house.** An agency earns its fee on technical depth, scaled
authority and PR, and capacity you don't have, so hire one to extend your team, not to
do the basics you can handle yourself.

## What does an agency actually add?

Specialized expertise and capacity. A good AEO agency handles the high-effort work —
complex [technical fixes](/learn/make-spa-citable), large-scale content production,
[authority and digital-PR campaigns](/learn/build-ai-authority-from-scratch),
measurement infrastructure, and strategy — that goes beyond what an in-house team can
do alone. What it shouldn't be is someone running generic tactics you could
[do yourself](/learn/can-i-do-aeo-myself); the fundamentals deliver most of the value
without one.

## When is it worth hiring?

When the work exceeds your time or expertise and the payoff justifies the cost.
Competitive markets, large sites, deep technical problems, or ambitious
[authority](/pillars/authority) goals are where an agency adds the most — and where
[building or extending a team](/learn/build-an-aeo-team) makes sense. For a small site
still doing the fundamentals, the spend usually isn't justified yet; size it against
the [business case](/learn/business-case-for-aeo).

## How do I choose one?

Vet for genuine AEO understanding, not repackaged SEO. Ask how they measure [citation
share per engine](/learn/per-engine-vs-blended-measurement), how they [earn
mentions](/learn/get-brand-mentions-without-links), and for real results — and walk
away from anyone promising guaranteed rankings or treating AEO as
[keyword stuffing](/learn/keyword-stuffing-ai) with a new name. The right agency thinks
in the citation cascade and the [Canon](/learn/aeo-canon); the wrong one sells you
yesterday's playbook.
