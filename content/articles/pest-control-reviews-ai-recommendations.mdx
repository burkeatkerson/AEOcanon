## Why do reviews carry so much weight for pest control?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge trust — and people are letting you
treat their home, often on an ongoing basis. An assistant deciding whom to recommend
[synthesizes sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and
third-party reviews to gauge whether you're effective, safe, and reliable. For a trade where
results need to last and treatments touch a family's home, that corroborated reputation is
decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the
[off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than
backlinks do.

## How many reviews do I need?

Fewer than you think, fresher than you have. There's no magic count — consistency and recency
outweigh a raw total. A steady stream of genuine recent reviews signals an active, trusted
business far better than a big pile from three years ago. The goal is an ongoing flow: build a
simple habit of asking after every treatment and every plan visit, so new reviews keep
arriving and the engine keeps seeing a business that's [currently
trusted](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations), not formerly busy.

## Do the words in a review matter?

Yes — specific reviews do more work than generic praise.

Reviews that name what you did — cleared bed bugs for good, removed a nest same day, kept mice
out on a plan — give engines specific detail tying you to those exact services and to lasting,
safe results. They reinforce the [questions customers
ask](/learn/pest-control-questions-customers-ask-ai), so a handful of detailed reviews can
outperform a wall of generic ones. Ask customers what to mention, and the reviews start doing
your AEO for you.
