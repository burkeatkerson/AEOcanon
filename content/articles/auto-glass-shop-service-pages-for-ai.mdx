answer to the cost, scope, and timing questions, in plain language a driver and an engine can lift.** One
self-contained, crawlable page per service beats a single bloated services page every time.

## Why one page per service?

Because a citation is awarded to the page that best answers one specific question — and a catch-all
services page answers none of them well. When you give windshield replacement, chip repair, ADAS
calibration, mobile service, door and back glass, and insurance claims each [their own
page](/learn/should-content-sections-stand-alone), each can go deep on its own cost, scope, and details —
and each becomes citable for its own query. A single page trying to cover everything is shallow on all of
them, so the engine cites a competitor with a dedicated, focused page.

## What should each page lead with?

The answer the driver came for, before anything about you. Compare these two openings:

Then add the detail, the insurers and brands you work with, and the proof below. This is [answer-first
writing](/learn/answer-first-sentence) applied to the trade.

## What makes a service page extractable?

Plain language and a clean structure. Write the way a driver asks — "a windshield replacement typically
runs …" — not in jargon, and use [question-shaped headings](/learn/question-shaped-headings) the engine
can match to a query. Keep each answer in a self-contained passage so it can be [lifted without the
surrounding page](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage), reinforce it with [AutoGlass
schema](/learn/auto-glass-schema-markup), and make sure the page is fast and crawlable. Answer-first,
focused, and proven — that's the page an engine cites and a driver books.
