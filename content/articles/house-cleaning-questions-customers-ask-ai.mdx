## How do I find the exact questions?

Listen where homeowners already ask. Note what prospects ask when they call or book, read your reviews
and the questions on your Google profile, scan local and home forums, and [prompt the assistants
directly](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask) on cleaning and your area to see the follow-ups
they surface. Capture the *natural wording* — "how much to clean a 3-bedroom house" beats "per-square-
foot residential cleaning rates" — because engines match the homeowner's phrasing. Then prioritize by
what you offer.

## Should I answer trust questions like 'are you insured'?

Yes — they're critical and easy to win. Because a homeowner is letting someone into their home, they ask
AI whether you're insured, bonded, and background-checked before they book. Stating these plainly in
readable text makes you the cited, trusted service — and reassures the homeowner at the exact moment
they're deciding. Trust content wins the wary homeowner; service-type content steers them to recurring.
Both build the credibility engines [reward](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore) — the opposite of
a thin booking widget. Map every bucket to a page and you've built the content plan that gets a cleaner
cited.
