## What's different about a zero budget?

What's different is the constraint — money — but it turns out to bind far less than
people assume. AEO's biggest levers aren't paid: a clean, [crawlable](/glossary/crawlability) site,
an answer-first rewrite, a genuinely original insight, a consistent identity, and an
earned mention all cost time and honesty rather than dollars. Paid tools enter the
picture when you need to *measure* [citation share](/glossary/citation-share) at scale
or *run a content program* fast — useful, but not what earns the citation in the first
place. So on zero budget
you don't do a watered-down AEO; you do the same high-impact work and simply skip the
instrumentation. It's the same logic as [the cheapest high-impact
moves](/learn/cheapest-high-impact-aeo-moves), taken all the way to free.

## Where this fits in the Canon

Zero-budget AEO is the full [AEO Canon](/learn/aeo-canon) executed for free — every
pillar in its no-cost form, led by [extractability](/pillars/extractability)
(answer-first writing) and [originality](/pillars/originality) (your first-hand
insight), the two highest free levers, with effort substituting for spend. It's the limit case of
[the cheapest high-impact moves](/learn/cheapest-high-impact-aeo-moves) and overlaps
heavily with [AEO without a marketing team](/learn/aeo-without-a-marketing-team) and
[AEO for small business](/learn/aeo-for-small-business). When you're ready to invest,
[how to budget for AEO](/learn/how-to-budget-for-aeo) shows where the first dollars go.
