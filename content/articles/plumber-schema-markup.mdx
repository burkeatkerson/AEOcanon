## What schema should a plumber use?

The Plumber type — a specific subtype of LocalBusiness — with the full set of local
details: accurate name, address, phone, geo and service area, hours, and your list of
services. Add [FAQ schema](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations) on pages that answer
common questions. Together these help engines parse and confirm who you are, where you
work, and what you do, reinforcing the [local trust
signals](/learn/local-aeo-for-plumbers) that decide who gets recommended. It's part of
the [Access and Extractability](/pillars/extractability) foundation, machine-readable.

## Does schema actually get me cited?

It helps, but it's a reinforcement, not a magic switch. Schema [clarifies content
engines can already read](/learn/schema-vs-clean-html) — it can't rescue a slow site, a
[buried answer](/learn/answer-first-sentence), or thin authority. Put it on accurate,
[answer-first pages](/learn/plumbing-service-pages-for-ai) and it strengthens an already
good signal; bolt it onto a broken foundation and it does little. Markup labels the
substance; it never substitutes for it, which is why
[clean HTML and a real answer come first](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations).

## LocalBusiness or Plumber type?

Plumber, because specificity removes ambiguity. Plumber is a subtype of LocalBusiness,
so using it (with all the LocalBusiness properties) tells engines precisely what kind of
business you are rather than leaving them to infer it. Either works, but the more
specific type gives the cleaner signal — and clean signals are the whole point of
[entity recognition](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) for a local trade.

## Should my schema match my Google Business Profile?

Exactly — consistency is the point. Your schema, [Google Business
Profile](/learn/plumber-google-business-profile-ai), and on-site details should all
state the same name, address, phone, hours, and service area. Matching them
[removes doubt and reinforces trust](/learn/nap-consistency); conflicts between your
schema and your listings create the ambiguity that costs citations. Getting schema and
listings consistent across a whole site is fiddly, exacting work — and part of the
[done-for-you rebuild](/pricing), which ships correct schema and a cleaned-up profile
together.
