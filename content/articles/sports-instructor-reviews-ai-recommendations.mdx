## Why do reviews carry so much weight for coaches?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge which instructors students actually love and
improve with — and choosing a coach is a trust-and-results decision. An assistant deciding what to
recommend [synthesizes sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and
review-platform reviews to gauge whether you're a great coach to learn from. For an instructor, that
corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible,
and the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than
backlinks do.

## Do reviews that mention the coach and results matter more?

Yes — specific reviews are gold, because they speak the language of what students want.

A review naming the coach, a lesson, the approach, or a result gives the engine detail to tie you to
"beginner-friendly golf coach near me" or "best swing instructor nearby" — the exact [fit and result
queries](/learn/sports-instruction-questions-customers-ask-ai) students and parents ask. Invite happy
students to mention what improved, and the reviews start matching you to the searches that fill your
calendar.

## How many reviews — and how fresh?

Fresh beats big. There's no magic count — recency and a steady flow outweigh a stale total. A coach with
current reviews that mention real student progress signals an active, effective instructor far better
than one coasting on old ratings. Build a simple habit: invite happy students and parents to review at
milestones — a fixed swing, a made team, a finished package — and [respond to
them](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations) — engagement signals an active coach who cares, which
is exactly the instructor an engine wants to recommend.
