## Why does Google Business Profile matter for AI search?

Because it's a structured, trusted confirmation of where you serve, what you shoot, and how clients rate you —
exactly what an engine needs to recommend a photographer. When the assistant decides whom to name for a near-me
or session-type query, it leans on the profile to verify your location, service area, session types, and
reputation [before recommending you](/learn/does-google-business-profile-help-ai-search). A complete, accurate
profile makes you an easy recommendation; a thin profile or an unclear service area leaves the engine unsure —
or sends you an inquiry outside the area you actually cover.

The reviews on your profile do double duty — they're a top input to [whether AI recommends

## Does GBP replace a good website?

No — it's half the picture. The profile is a powerful placement and service-area signal, but engines also
retrieve and cite your own [readable packages](/learn/photographer-service-pages-for-ai) and pages for pricing,
inclusions, and detailed questions — "wedding photography package price," "does it include a second shooter."
You need both: a complete, accurate profile so the engine can place you and confirm you cover the area, and
readable packages and pages for the substance that wins the high-intent query. Treat them as one system, kept
[consistent everywhere](/learn/nap-consistency).
