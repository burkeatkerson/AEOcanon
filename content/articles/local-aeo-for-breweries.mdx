## What makes a brewery 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about where you are, what you pour, and whether you're open. An answer engine recommends a
brewery for "near me" only when it can [place you with certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend)
— right area, right styles, open now. That certainty comes from signals that all agree: matching address
and hours, a complete profile, a readable tap list, and reviews. When the signals agree, you're an easy
recommendation; when your hours conflict or your tap list is unreadable, the engine sends the visitor
elsewhere.

This is the [near-me ranking](/learn/how-to-rank-in-ai-for-near-me-searches) discipline applied to the
taproom — and accurate hours are where most breweries quietly lose.

## Why do accurate hours matter so much?

Because 'open now' and 'taproom open tonight' are among the most common queries, and the engine answers
them straight from your hours. If your hours are wrong or inconsistent across listings, the engine either
drops you from 'open now' answers or — worse — sends a thirsty visitor to a closed door, costing you the
round and a review. Keep hours accurate and identical everywhere, update them for holidays and special
events, and you remove the single most common reason a brewery gets skipped on a near-me query. It's the
same [NAP consistency](/learn/nap-consistency) discipline, with the clock attached.
