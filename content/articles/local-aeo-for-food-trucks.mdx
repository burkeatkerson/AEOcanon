## What makes a food truck 'local' to an engine when it keeps moving?

Confidence about where you are *right now*, what you serve, and that the spot is current. An answer engine
recommends a truck for "near me" only when it can [place you with certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend)
— right area, right cuisine, parked there today. For a fixed restaurant that certainty is easy; for a
truck it's the whole game, because your location resets daily. The certainty comes from signals that all
agree and stay current: a live schedule, a defined service area, a readable menu, and reviews. When
today's spot is clear and fresh, you're an easy recommendation; when it's stale or buried in an old post,
the engine sends the customer elsewhere.

This is the [near-me ranking](/learn/how-to-rank-in-ai-for-near-me-searches) discipline applied to a
moving business — and keeping today's location current is where most trucks quietly lose.

## Why does keeping my location current matter so much?

Because your whole value is being somewhere specific right now, and the engine answers 'near me today'
straight from your location signals. If today's spot is stale or only lives in an old social post, the
engine either drops you from the answer or — worse — sends a hungry customer to where you were yesterday,
costing you the crowd and a review. Keep today's location and your schedule current and readable
everywhere, and you remove the single most common reason a truck gets skipped on a near-me query. It's the
same [NAP consistency](/learn/nap-consistency) discipline, with a live clock and a moving pin attached.
