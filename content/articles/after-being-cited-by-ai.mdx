## What does being cited actually send you?

Being cited sends two distinct kinds of value. The visible part is a **small number
of high-intent clicks** — people who read your cited answer and clicked through to
learn or act, already pre-qualified by the AI. The larger, invisible part is
**[zero-click brand exposure](/glossary/zero-click-search)** — everyone who read your answer, saw your brand named,
and never clicked, because AI answers resolve most questions on the spot. So the
honest picture is a trickle of excellent visitors riding on top of broad,
unmeasured exposure — the dynamic explained in [does AI search send real
traffic?](/learn/does-ai-search-send-traffic) and [how AI search differs from
traditional search](/learn/ai-search-vs-traditional-search).

## Why isn't it a traffic flood?

It isn't a flood because AI answers are designed to satisfy the question without a
click. Where a #1 organic ranking once captured a large share of searchers, an AI
answer hands them the information directly, so far fewer click through — and the
ones who do are the minority with a reason to go deeper. This is why measuring
post-citation success by pageviews disappoints: the volume is intentionally lower.
The value moved from quantity to quality, and from clicks to presence.

## Why is being cited still worth it?

It's worth it because the few clicks convert far better and the exposure builds your
brand. [Ahrefs found AI-referred visitors converted about 23x better than
organic](/learn/does-ai-search-send-traffic), with a tiny fraction of visitors
driving a large share of signups — they arrive having already been convinced by the
answer. Layer on the brand value of being repeatedly named as the source, and a
small, high-intent stream plus broad exposure can outproduce a larger pile of
ordinary traffic. The full economics are in the [business case for
AEO](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) and [the ROI of AEO](/learn/roi-of-aeo).
