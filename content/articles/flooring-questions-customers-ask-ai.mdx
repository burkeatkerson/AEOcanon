## How do I find the exact questions?

Listen where homeowners already ask. Mine your consultations and measures for the questions people
actually voice, check Reddit threads, home-improvement forums, and the People Also Ask box, and
[prompt the assistants directly](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask) on common materials and
rooms to see the follow-ups they surface. Capture the *natural wording* — "best flooring for a kitchen
with dogs" beats "optimal floor covering for high-traffic households" — because engines match the
homeowner's phrasing, not your jargon. Then prioritize by intent and value.

## Should I answer material and process questions?

Yes — they're how you earn the trust that wins the install. Answering "LVP vs tile for a kitchen" or
"do you remove old flooring" honestly makes you the source a homeowner remembers when they're ready to
buy. Material content captures the homeowner still deciding what to put down; trust content wins the
wary buyer who's heard horror stories about hack jobs and gaps. Both build the credibility and
visibility engines [reward](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore) — the opposite of a thin gallery
page. Map every bucket to a page and you've built the content plan that gets a flooring contractor
cited.
