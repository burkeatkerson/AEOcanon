## Why do reviews carry so much weight for hobby shops?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge which stores people trust — and a hobby shop's
whole edge is expertise and community. An assistant deciding what to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform reviews to
gauge whether your staff knows their stuff and your scene is welcoming. For a hobby shop, that
corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible,
and the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than
backlinks do.

## Do reviews that mention staff and events matter more?

Yes — because expertise and community are exactly what a local store offers that Amazon can't.

A review naming knowledgeable staff and a great game night reassures the engine you offer the expertise
and community a [local scene](/learn/events-and-community-hobby-shop-aeo) needs — exactly what a hobbyist
searching for their store wants to hear. Invite happy customers and league regulars to mention what they
bought, who helped them, and which event they love, and the reviews start matching you to the searches
that bring visits.

## How many reviews — and how fresh?

Fresh beats big. There's no magic count — recency and a steady flow outweigh a stale total. A store with
current reviews that mention selection, staff, and events signals an active, trusted business far better
than one coasting on old ratings. Build a simple habit: invite happy customers and league regulars to
review, and [respond to them](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations) — engagement signals an active
store that cares, which is exactly what an engine wants to recommend.
