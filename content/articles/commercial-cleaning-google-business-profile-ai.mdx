## Why does Google Business Profile matter for AI search?

Because it's a structured, trusted confirmation of who and where you are — exactly what an engine needs
to recommend a company that crews will let into a building after hours. When the assistant decides whom
to name for a near-me or 'commercial cleaning company for [building type]' query, it leans on the
profile to verify your identity, service area, services, and reputation [before recommending
you](/learn/does-google-business-profile-help-ai-search). A complete, accurate profile makes you an easy
recommendation; a thin or stale one leaves the engine unsure and routes the inquiry to a directory or a
lead broker that resells it to three of your competitors.

## Does GBP replace a good website?

No — it's half the picture. The profile is a powerful placement and trust signal, but engines also
retrieve and cite your [actual service pages](/learn/commercial-cleaning-service-pages-for-ai) for the
detailed questions a profile can't answer — what office cleaning costs per square foot, how often a
medical office should be cleaned, whether you handle post-construction cleanup. You need both: a
complete profile so the engine can confidently place and trust you, and answer-first pages for the
substance that wins the detailed query. Treat them as one system, kept [consistent
everywhere](/learn/nap-consistency), and reinforce it with [ProfessionalService
schema](/learn/commercial-cleaning-schema-markup).
