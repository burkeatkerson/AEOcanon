## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each job produces a review and a referral — and the best ones turn into
recurring commercial accounts; reviews and [answer-first
content](/learn/locksmith-service-pages-for-ai) earn citations and rankings; those bring direct
inquiries; and the money saved on purchased leads funds more content and more capacity. The directory
model is the opposite: a faucet you pay to keep running, that stops the moment you stop — and that
lumps you in with the scam listings. AEO builds the [compounding
asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead, on a foundation of genuine trust.

## How do commercial accounts and car keys accelerate it?

They add recurring revenue and high-margin growth. Commercial and property-manager
[accounts](/learn/commercial-locksmith-aeo) turn one-off jobs into ongoing relationships — rekeys
after tenant turnover, maintenance, access-control work — that stabilize your revenue and generate a
steady stream of reviews. Meanwhile the [car-key and fob business](/learn/automotive-locksmith-aeo) is
high-margin work in growing demand, where you undercut the dealer. Own both and the flywheel spins
faster: recurring B2B revenue plus profitable automotive jobs, all feeding the reputation signals AI
uses to recommend you.

## Can I really lower my cost per job?

Yes — by replacing recurring fees with assets that keep paying. A purchased lead costs every time and
is [resold to your competitors](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands); an earned citation, ranking,
commercial account, or referral costs mostly upfront and pays for years. As your owned pipeline and
recurring base grow, your cost per job falls and your margins improve, because you're no longer renting
every customer. And because AI names only a few sources, a real, licensed, genuinely reviewed local
locksmith can win recommendations the scam-listing operators [can't
replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
