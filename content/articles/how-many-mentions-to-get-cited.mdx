**There's no magic number — what matters is consistent, credible corroboration across
reputable sources in your topic, not a mention count.** Engines build trust from a
pattern of how the web describes you, so a handful of authoritative, on-topic
mentions can outweigh hundreds of low-quality ones.

## Why isn't there a number?

Because trust is a pattern, not a tally. Engines decide who to cite from how
consistently and credibly the web describes you — [mentions and
corroboration](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that agree about who you are and what
you're known for. A threshold expressed as "you need N mentions" misses the point:
ten authoritative, on-topic mentions that reinforce each other build more
[authority](/pillars/authority) than a thousand scattered, low-quality ones — and [Ahrefs found web mentions correlate with AI visibility far more strongly than backlinks](https://ahrefs.com/blog/ai-overview-brand-correlation/).

## So is it quality or quantity?

Quality and consistency, with quantity a distant third. One mention from a respected,
relevant source that corroborates your claims is worth many generic name-drops,
because the engine is looking for *agreement among trustworthy sources* about your
expertise. A coherent picture from a few strong sources beats a noisy one from many
weak ones — which is also why [a handful of mentions can sometimes be
enough](/learn/get-brand-mentions-without-links).

## How do I know if I have enough?

Test the engines and read the pattern. Run your priority questions across ChatGPT,
Perplexity, and Google AI Mode: if they cite competitors but not you, your
corroboration is thin or inconsistent regardless of raw numbers, and the fix is more
credible, on-topic coverage — not just more of anything. When you start getting
[named for your topics](/learn/track-competitor-ai-citations), you've crossed the
threshold the engines needed.
