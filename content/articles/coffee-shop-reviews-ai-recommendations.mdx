## Why do reviews carry so much weight for coffee shops?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge which cafés customers actually love — and
coffee is driven by reputation and habit. An assistant deciding what to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform reviews to
gauge whether you're a great choice. For a café, that corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the
[Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the [off-site
mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks do.

## Do reviews that name the coffee or vibe matter more?

Yes — specific reviews are gold, because they speak the language of discovery.

A review naming the coffee, the wifi, or the atmosphere gives the engine detail to tie you to "best
oat milk latte near me" or "café to work with wifi nearby" — the exact [discovery and niche
queries](/learn/cafe-questions-customers-ask-ai) customers ask. Invite happy regulars to mention what
they loved, and the reviews start matching you to the searches that fill seats.

## How many reviews — and how fresh?

Fresh beats big. There's no magic count — recency and a steady flow outweigh a stale total. A café with
current reviews that mention the coffee signals an active, loved place far better than one coasting on
old ratings. Build a simple habit: invite happy regulars to review, and [respond to
them](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations) — engagement signals an active café that cares,
which is exactly the place an engine wants to recommend.
