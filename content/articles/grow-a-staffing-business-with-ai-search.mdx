## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each successful placement produces reviews and referrals from both the client
and the candidate — and the client comes back with more reqs; reviews and [answer-first
content](/learn/staffing-agency-service-pages-for-ai) earn citations and rankings; those bring direct
inquiries from employers and candidates; and the money saved on job-board spend funds more content and
a sharper [niche](/learn/niche-staffing-aeo). The rent model is the opposite: a faucet you pay to keep
running, selling the same lead to three firms, that stops the moment you stop. AEO builds the
[compounding asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead — clients and candidates who found you and
stay.

## Why is recurring client revenue central to growth?

Because a satisfied client company brings reqs again and again, and filling the top of the funnel only
pays off if clients stay. A client who places with you for years is worth many times a single
placement — and happy clients and placed candidates are exactly who write the [reviews that mention
results](/learn/staffing-agency-reviews-ai-recommendations) and refer others, feeding the AI
recommendations that bring the next clients and candidates. So growth isn't just AEO at the top; it's
AEO plus retention plus a strong [candidate supply](/learn/attracting-candidates-staffing-aeo)
together, each compounding the others.

## How do I lower my cost per placement?

By replacing recurring fees with assets that keep paying. A job-board click and a purchased lead cost
every time and are [resold to competitors](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands); an earned citation, a
repeat client, or a candidate referral costs mostly upfront and pays for years. As your cited,
niche-focused, and retained base grows, your cost per placement falls and your revenue stabilizes. And
because AI names only a few firms, a focused, specialized, genuinely reviewed agency can win
recommendations a generic job board [can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
