## What makes a pool company 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about who you are and where you work. An answer engine recommends a local company only when
it can [place you with certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — right business,
right area. That certainty comes from signals that all agree: matching name-address-phone, a complete
profile, a defined service area, and pages that name real places. When the signals agree, you're an
easy recommendation; when they conflict, the engine favors a clearer competitor.

## How do I win 'near me' in AI answers?

Build location certainty deliberately.

## Why does NAP consistency matter so much?

Because a mismatch is a doubt, and engines route around doubt. If your phone number differs between your
site and your Google profile, or an old address still shows in a directory, the engine can't be sure
which company you are or where you operate — so it favors a competitor it can [place with
confidence](/learn/nap-consistency). For seasonal pool businesses that change numbers or run multiple
crews, this is a common and quiet leak. Clean it up and you remove the single most fixable reason an
engine skips you on a near-me query.
