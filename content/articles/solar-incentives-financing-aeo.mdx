## Why do incentive and financing searches matter so much?

Because the incentive math is what makes solar pencil out, and the answer is the new front door. A
homeowner weighing solar researches the federal tax credit, local rebates, net metering, and how to
finance the system before they commit — and the [AI answer names only a few
sources](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Pew Research [found people click a link just 8% of
the time when an AI summary
appears](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the installer cited on these decision-driving questions shapes the shortlist before a single call.
Own the money questions and you own the moment the homeowner makes up their mind.

## Which incentive and financing questions should I own?

The ones that decide whether the homeowner moves forward — each on its own durable page.

## How do I keep incentive content accurate as programs change?

Keep one durable page per topic and refresh it on a schedule. A persistent URL [accumulates
authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you keep it current, rather than baking in figures that
go stale. Note that incentives change and that numbers are current as of a date, so a skeptical buyer
trusts the page and an engine sees a [fresh, maintained answer](/pillars/freshness). That beats a vague
"incentives may apply" dodge or an outdated number — and because stale or wrong incentive claims erode
the trust this trade runs on, an accurate, refreshed page is both the honest choice and the one that
keeps earning citations. Tie each page to your area — 'solar rebates in [city]' — so it wins the local,
timely query too.
