## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a taproom the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine can
read your tap list and place you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as where to grab a
beer — and that leans harder on whether drinkers love you. The engine recommends the brewery it can
[confidently confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, open, and well-reviewed, because when
it recommends you it's putting its credibility behind the experience. Your job is to make that
confirmation easy — a readable tap list, accurate hours, and genuine, loving reviews.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). Wrong hours or a thin review profile introduces doubt, and doubt
sends the drinker elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one brewery over another?

Because it picks the one it's most confident drinkers will love. Faced with several options, the engine
names the brewery whose signals best match the request — right styles, right area, open now, dog-friendly
or with food if asked — and whose reviews most clearly say people love it. The competitor with an
unreadable tap list, wrong hours, or thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it loses. Winning is about
being the [easiest to verify as the loved local choice](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend)
— which is exactly what this library builds, piece by piece.
