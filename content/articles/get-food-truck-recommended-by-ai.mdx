## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a truck the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine can
read your menu and place you today; being *recommended* means it actively names you as where to go — and
that leans harder on whether people love you. The engine recommends the truck it can [confidently
confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, here right now, and well-reviewed, because when it
recommends you it's putting its credibility behind the experience. Your job is to make that confirmation
easy — a readable menu, a current location, and genuine, loving reviews.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). A stale location or a thin review profile introduces doubt, and doubt
sends the customer to another truck.

## Why does AI pick one food truck over another?

Because it picks the one it's most confident people will love — and can place right now. Faced with
several options, the engine names the truck whose signals best match the request — right cuisine, right
area, here today — and whose reviews most clearly say people love it. The competitor with a menu trapped
on social, a stale location, or thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it loses. Winning is about being the
[easiest to verify as the loved local choice](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — which
is exactly what this library builds, piece by piece.
