**You already use AI every day to run your auto glass shop — but that's a
completely different game from being the shop AI *recommends* when a driver with
a cracked windshield asks their phone who to call.** You use it to quote jobs and
answer insurance questions fast; meanwhile drivers have started asking AI *who
can replace my windshield today* — and it names one or two shops. If yours isn't
one of them, AI is handing that job to a competitor.

## How are auto glass shops using AI today?

More than you'd think, and it helps. Owners use booking tools like Square or
Calendly so a customer can lock in a mobile install without a phone call. They
use ChatGPT to draft quotes, explain the difference between OEM and aftermarket
glass, and walk a nervous customer through an insurance claim in plain language.
Many use AI to reply to reviews, summarize ADAS recalibration requirements for a
specific make and model, and write social posts showing a clean chip repair or a
full windshield swap. It's genuinely useful work. But all of it is about your
*operations* — how fast and smooth you run — and it makes you more efficient,
nothing more.

## But is AI recommending your shop?

Here's the catch: none of that operational AI makes an assistant *name you* when
a driver asks who to hire. Being recommended is about your *visibility*, and it
runs on a separate track entirely. When a customer asks an engine for auto glass,
it retrieves and quotes the sources it can find and trust *about you on the open
web* — your website, your reviews, mentions of you elsewhere. Your private
ChatGPT habit is invisible to that. You can run the whole front desk on AI and
still never surface when a prospect asks AI who to call.

## How do customers use AI to find an auto glass shop?

They ask it like a friend who's been through it. Instead of scrolling listings,
more drivers now type "windshield replacement near me," "best auto glass shop in
[town]," or "who does mobile windshield replacement and recalibration" — and act
on the short list the assistant returns. Because the AI answers in place and
names only a couple of options, this is a **winner-take-most** moment. The shops
it cites get the call; everyone else is invisible. For a driver who just wants
the crack gone before it spreads, that one-or-two-name answer *is* the search.

## How do you know if AI is sending your customers to a competitor?

Ask the engines yourself. Open ChatGPT, [Google AI Mode](/glossary/google-ai-mode),
and [Perplexity](/glossary/perplexity), and run the real questions drivers ask —
"best auto glass shop in [your city]," "who can replace my windshield today,"
"mobile windshield replacement near me." Note who gets named. If competitors show
up and you don't — or the AI misses that you do mobile service or ADAS
recalibration — you've found the gap. It costs nothing to check, and it's the
fastest way to see what your customers see. Related reading —
[you use AI, but is AI recommending you](/learn/use-ai-but-is-ai-recommending-you).

## What should an auto glass shop do about it?

You optimize to be the answer — that's
[Answer Engine Optimization](/learn/what-is-aeo). Practically, make your most
important page lead with a complete, self-contained answer to your core question —
what you replace and repair, whether you offer mobile service and recalibration,
and the area you cover — on a page an AI crawler can actually read. Then earn the
reviews and off-site mentions engines trust. Keep using AI to run the shop; just
don't mistake it for being found by one. Start with the
