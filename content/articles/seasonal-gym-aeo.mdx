resolutions in January, summer-body season in spring, back-to-school in fall — before each surge, on
durable pages you update yearly.** Be the cited answer when motivation spikes, not scrambling after the
rush starts.

## Why does seasonality matter for gyms?

Because motivation — and sign-ups — surge in predictable waves, and the cited gym catches them. January
brings the resolution wave, the biggest sign-up window of the year; spring brings summer-body
searches; fall brings back-to-school routines. The gym whose answer is already published, crawled, and
trusted [gets cited the moment the surge hits](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo) — while
competitors are still updating their site. It's the [Freshness pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned into
a motivation calendar, and the difference between a packed January and a quiet one.

## How do I prepare for each surge?

Build the content while it's calm, so it's ready when motivation spikes.

## Should I make a new 'New Year' page each year?

No — update one durable evergreen page. A persistent 'beginner-friendly gym' or 'start your fitness
journey' page [accumulates authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh it each year,
which beats spinning up a throwaway 'New Year 2026' page that starts from zero and splits your signals.
Keep one strong page that answers the beginner and membership questions, sharpen it before each surge,
and link it to your [pricing and class pages](/learn/gym-service-pages-for-ai). One durable page,
refreshed and ready, beats scrambling when the resolution wave hits.
