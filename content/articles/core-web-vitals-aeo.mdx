**Core Web Vitals thresholds are fixed numbers, and you pass only when 75% of real visits hit "good" on all three.** They do not directly rank you in AI answers — but a slow, unstable page quietly costs you the crawls and the visits that citations depend on.

## What are the exact thresholds?

Each metric has three bands. [Largest Contentful Paint](/glossary/lcp) is good under 2.5s and poor past 4.0s; Interaction to Next Paint is good under 200ms and poor past 500ms; [Cumulative Layout Shift](/glossary/cls) is good under 0.1 and poor past 0.25. Google defines these on [web.dev](https://web.dev/articles/defining-core-web-vitals-thresholds) and in [Search Central](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/core-web-vitals).

## What does it take to "pass"?

You pass when 75% of real-user visits land in the good band, measured at the 75th percentile, across all three metrics at once. One weak metric fails the whole page — so a fast-loading page with jumpy layout still fails.

## Why do Core Web Vitals matter for AEO if they don't rank citations?

Because they protect the conditions for citation. AI engines cite content, not speed, but slow servers waste [crawl efficiency](/learn/page-speed-ai-citations) and unstable pages lose the human visitors who read, link, and mention you. Speed is a supporting signal, not a direct one.
