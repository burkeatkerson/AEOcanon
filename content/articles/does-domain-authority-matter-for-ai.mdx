## Do engines actually read DA?

No. [Domain Authority](/glossary/brand-authority) (Moz) and Domain Rating (Ahrefs) are
proprietary estimates built by SEO vendors to predict ranking strength — they're not
inputs an answer engine consumes. Engines assess trust from how the web genuinely
describes and references you, so treating a third-party score as the target optimizes
for the wrong thing. It's a useful diagnostic, not a signal.

## Then why do high-DA sites get cited more?

Because the score usually reflects the real thing. A high DA typically means a site
has earned lots of credible [mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks), links, and
recognition — which *is* what engines reward. So the correlation is real, but it runs
through the underlying [authority](/pillars/authority), not the number. Build the
genuine trust and both the score and the citations tend to rise together.

## What should I optimize instead?

The real signals. Consistent [brand mentions](/learn/get-brand-mentions-without-links)
and corroboration across reputable sources, a [recognized
entity](/learn/what-is-entity-aeo) with clean [structured
data](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo), and demonstrable topical expertise are what
engines use to decide who to trust. Those move citation directly, in a way a vendor
score never does — so measure with DA if you like, but optimize for the substance
beneath it.
