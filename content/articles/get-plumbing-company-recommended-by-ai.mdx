## Why does AI recommend one plumber over another?

Because it can confirm that one fits the request with confidence. An engine
recommending a plumber is effectively vouching for them, so it favors the business
whose signals are cleanest — right service, right area, clearly trustworthy. That
confidence comes from [consistent listings](/learn/local-aeo-for-plumbers), [strong
reviews](/learn/plumber-reviews-ai-recommendations), a [complete
profile](/learn/plumber-google-business-profile-ai), and pages that directly answer the
question — consistent with [survey data showing nearly half of consumers now use AI to find local businesses](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey/). The plumber with the most corroborated signals wins; the one the engine can't
verify gets skipped, even if they're better in reality.

## Is being recommended different from being found?

Subtly, yes — and the difference is trust. Being [found](/learn/how-plumbers-get-found-by-ai)
means the engine can retrieve you; being recommended means it actively names you as the
answer. Both need the same foundation, but recommendation leans harder on the
[Authority](/pillars/authority) and [Credibility](/pillars/credibility) signals —
reviews, reputation, consistency — because the engine is putting its own credibility
behind naming you. Discovery gets you eligible; trust gets you chosen.

## What's the foundation to put in place?

The full local trust stack. A [crawlable, fast site](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site);
[answer-first service pages](/learn/plumbing-service-pages-for-ai) for each service;
[identical name, address, and phone](/learn/nap-consistency) everywhere; a complete
Google Business Profile; and a steady stream of [genuine
reviews](/learn/plumber-reviews-ai-recommendations). Each removes a reason for the
engine to hesitate. Together they make you the obvious, safe recommendation — the whole
arc of [AEO for plumbers](/learn/aeo-for-plumbers).

## How long does it take?

It compounds. The on-page fixes — crawlability, answer-first pages — can show up in
weeks because you control them; the review and reputation signals that most drive
recommendations [build over months](/learn/how-long-to-build-aeo-authority) because
they depend on customers and other sources. Start with the fast technical and content
work and let the trust signals compound behind it. If you'd rather not run that program
