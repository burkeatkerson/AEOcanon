## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a real local entity the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means
the engine can retrieve you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as the answer — and
that's a higher bar of trust, especially in a trade where engines are learning to distrust the scam
listings. The engine recommends the locksmith it can [confidently
confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is genuine, capable, and well-regarded, because when it
recommends you it's putting its own credibility behind the choice. Your job is to make that
confirmation effortless — and to be unmistakably real.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). One inconsistency (an old phone number, a mismatched address)
introduces doubt — and doubt is exactly what gets the scam listings filtered out.

## Why does AI pick one locksmith over another?

Because it picks the one it's most sure is genuine. Faced with several options, the engine names the
locksmith whose signals most confidently match the request and prove legitimacy — right service, right
area, clearly licensed, genuinely reviewed. The faceless listing with a virtual address and thin
reviews is the riskier vouch, so it loses. Winning is about being the [easiest to verify as the real,
trusted choice](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — which is exactly what this
library builds, piece by piece.
