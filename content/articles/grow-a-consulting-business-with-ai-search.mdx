## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each well-served client produces reviews, named results, and referrals — and
often stays on retainer; published expertise and [results](/learn/thought-leadership-aeo-for-consultants)
earn citations and rankings; those bring inbound inquiries; and the time saved on cold outreach funds
more content and a sharper [niche](/learn/niche-consulting-aeo). Cold outreach is the opposite: a
treadmill you run every month, starting from zero each time, that stops the moment you stop. AEO builds
the [compounding asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead — qualified clients who found you and
stay.

## Why is an owned pipeline central to growth?

Because cold outreach and a thin referral list reset to zero every month, while filling the top of the
funnel with inbound leads only pays off if clients stay. A retainer client who stays for many months is
worth multiples of a one-off project — and happy, long-tenured clients are exactly who write the
[reviews that mention results](/learn/consultant-reviews-ai-recommendations) and refer peers, feeding the
AI recommendations that bring the next clients. So growth isn't just AEO at the top; it's AEO plus
retention together, each compounding the other.

## How do I lower my cost per client?

By replacing the outreach treadmill with assets that keep paying. A cold campaign costs your time every
month and converts thinly; an earned citation, a long retainer, or a referral costs mostly upfront and
pays for months or years. As your cited, niche-focused, and retained base grows, your cost per client
falls and your revenue stabilizes. And because AI names only a few experts, a focused, proven, genuinely
reviewed consultant can win recommendations a generic directory [can't
replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
