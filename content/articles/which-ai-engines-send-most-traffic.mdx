## Which AI engine sends the most referral traffic?

ChatGPT sends the most, and it is not close. [SearchEngineLand's referral session
data found ChatGPT drives roughly 87–92% of all AI referral
traffic](https://searchengineland.com/chatgpt-ai-referral-traffic-sessions-data-481630),
leaving every other engine to split what remains. For most sites, this means the
single highest-leverage move is being citable in ChatGPT — see
[how to get cited by ChatGPT](/learn/how-to-get-cited-by-chatgpt).

## Where does Perplexity fit?

Perplexity sends far less volume than its reputation suggests — roughly 2.8% of AI
referral traffic in the same data. It cites many sources per answer, so it offers a
lot of citation slots, but each one drives comparatively few clicks. Its traffic is
high-intent and worth capturing; just don't mistake citation frequency for click
volume. More on earning those citations: [get mentioned by
Perplexity](/learn/get-mentioned-by-perplexity).

## Is Claude worth tracking yet?

Yes — Claude is the fastest riser. [Trakkr tracked Claude referral sessions growing
roughly 64x, from about 133 in November 2024 to about 8,528 by May
2026](https://trakkr.ai/ai-search-traffic), and Claude overtook Perplexity in
referral volume around March 2026. It is still small next to ChatGPT, but the
trajectory means it belongs on your dashboard now, not later. Gemini and Copilot
follow a similar pattern: small today, growing steadily.

## What should you do with this?

Prioritize by where your audience actually is, measured per engine rather than as a
blended "AI traffic" number. ChatGPT carries the volume, so it is the safe first
bet, but a research-heavy B2B audience may skew toward Perplexity or Claude. Set up
[AI-citation tracking](/learn/how-to-track-ai-citations) so you see your own split,
then read [why AI gives different answers](/learn/why-ai-gives-different-answers)
to understand why winning one engine doesn't win them all.
