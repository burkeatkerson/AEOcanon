me', 'brewery open now', 'dog-friendly brewery with food nearby', 'things to do tonight' — with accurate
hours, a readable tap list, and genuine reviews that name beers.** The visitor acts on the first good
answer, so the cited brewery wins the visit.

## Why is 'near me' the highest-value brewery search?

Because it's the moment of decision, and it converts immediately. When someone asks "best brewery near
me" or "brewery open now," they're choosing where to drink in the next hour — and the [AI answer names
only a few places](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Pew Research [found people click a link just
8% of the time when an AI summary
appears](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer *is* the visit. For a brewery, owning that moment is the difference between a packed
taproom and an empty one.

## What do visitors ask AI when choosing a brewery?

In-the-moment, specific questions — and you should own the ones that fit your taproom.

## Why is 'open now' the make-or-break signal?

Because a huge share of where-to-drink searches are in-the-moment, and the engine answers 'open now'
straight from your hours. Accurate, consistent hours mean you get recommended when a thirsty visitor is
deciding; wrong or inconsistent hours mean you're dropped from the answer — or you send someone to a
closed door and earn a bad review. Keeping hours current everywhere, including holidays and food-truck
nights, is the highest-leverage move for winning the in-the-moment visit, and it's the same [local
certainty](/learn/local-aeo-for-breweries) that wins every near-me query.
