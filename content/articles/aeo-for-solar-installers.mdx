## Why does AEO matter so much for solar?

Because a solar system is a big, researched, twenty-year decision, and the answer is the new front
door. A homeowner weighing solar asks an assistant long before they call anyone — and the [AI answer
names only two or three sources](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations), not a page of links. Pew
Research [found people clicked a link just 8% of the time when an AI summary appeared, versus 15%
without](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer shapes the shortlist before you ever hear from them. Being the cited installer is the
new equivalent of being the trusted solar company a neighbor recommends.

## How do solar installers get recommended by AI?

By clearing the three gates a homeowner — and an engine — judge you on, in order.

Most installers have a slow, animation-heavy site and a reputation that's inconsistent across
listings — so they fail gates one and three without realizing the contest happened. Fixing those is
the highest-leverage work, and it's the same foundation that [ranks you in
Google](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together) too.

## Isn't this just SEO?

It's mostly the SEO you should already be doing, pointed at a new surface. Roughly
[70 to 80 percent overlaps](/learn/is-aeo-replacing-seo): the crawlable, fast, locally-trusted site
that ranks is the same one that gets cited. AEO adds answering questions
[directly and answer-first](/learn/answer-first-sentence), building [service pages for
extraction](/learn/solar-installer-service-pages-for-ai), and tightening the [reviews and
listings](/learn/solar-installer-reviews-ai-recommendations) that answer engines lean on. You don't
choose between them — you run one program that wins both.

## Where do local installers have the advantage?

In specificity, honesty, and proof. AI names only a few sources and citations spread thin, so a
trusted local installer who answers "is solar worth it in [city]" and "what's the payback here"
better than a national platform can take the recommendation. The platform is generic and faceless;
you're specific, local, certified, and real, with real installs to show — and you can answer the
skeptical buyer's cost and payback questions honestly. That's the [Originality](/pillars/originality)
and [local authority](/learn/local-aeo-for-solar-installers) edge a big aggregator can't easily copy.
