## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a dojo the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine can
read your programs and place you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as where to train — and
that leans harder on whether families love you. The engine recommends the school it can [confidently
confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, well-run, and well-reviewed, because when it
recommends you it's putting its credibility behind the experience. Your job is to make that confirmation
easy — readable programs, accurate hours, and genuine, loving reviews.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). Wrong hours, hidden programs, or a thin review profile introduces
doubt, and doubt sends the parent elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one martial arts school over another?

Because it picks the one it's most confident families will love. Faced with several options, the engine
names the dojo whose signals best match the request — right disciplines, right area, right for a child or
an adult — and whose reviews most clearly say people love it. The competitor with widget-hidden programs,
wrong hours, or thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it loses. Winning is about being the [easiest to
verify as the loved local choice](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — which is exactly
what this library builds, piece by piece.
