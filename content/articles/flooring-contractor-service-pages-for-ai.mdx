**Write flooring service pages AI will cite by giving each material its own page that leads with the
answer to the cost, timeline, and process questions, in plain language a homeowner and an engine can
lift.** One self-contained, crawlable page per material beats a single bloated services page every
time.

<Callout variant="key" title="Quick answer">
  <p>
    Give <strong>each material its own page</strong> — hardwood, LVP/LVT, tile, laminate, carpet,
    refinishing — and <strong>lead with the answer</strong> to what it costs per square foot, how long
    the install takes, whether you remove old flooring, and whether you serve their area. Make each
    page <strong>self-contained and crawlable</strong>. One focused page per material beats one
    bloated services page every time.
  </p>
</Callout>

## Why one page per material?

Because a citation is awarded to the page that best answers one specific question — and a catch-all
services page answers none of them well. When you give hardwood install, refinishing, luxury vinyl,
tile, laminate, and carpet each [their own
page](/learn/should-content-sections-stand-alone), each can go deep on its own cost, durability, and
install process — and each becomes citable for its own query. A single page trying to cover everything
is shallow on all of them, so the engine cites a competitor with a dedicated, focused page instead.

## What should each page lead with?

The answer the homeowner came for, before anything about you.

This is [answer-first writing](/learn/answer-first-sentence) applied to the trade: the quotable answer
up top, the depth below, the proof at the bottom.

## What makes a service page extractable?

Plain language and a clean structure. Write the way a homeowner asks — "installed hardwood typically
costs $8 to $15 per square foot…" — not in jargon, and use [question-shaped
headings](/learn/question-shaped-headings) the engine can match to a query. Keep each answer in a
self-contained passage so it can be [lifted without the surrounding
page](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage), reinforce it with [GeneralContractor
schema](/learn/flooring-contractor-schema-markup), and make sure the page is fast and crawlable —
flooring gallery sites are often too image-heavy for bots to read. Answer-first, focused, and
proven — that's the page an engine cites and a homeowner calls.
