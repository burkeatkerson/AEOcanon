**Author authority is making the people behind your content recognized,
credentialed [entities](/glossary/entity) that engines trust.** It operationalizes E-E-A-T — real named
authors with verifiable expertise, [Person schema](/glossary/person-schema), and a consistent cross-web
identity — so the experience and expertise behind a claim are attributable to
someone an engine can identify.

## What is author authority?

Author authority is the practice of making the people behind your content into
recognized, credentialed [entities](/learn/what-is-entity-aeo) — so a claim isn't
made by an anonymous page but by an identifiable person who would credibly know.
It's the application of [E-E-A-T](/glossary/eeat) at the person level. [Google's Search Quality Rater
Guidelines center E-E-A-T — Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and
Trust](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/creating-helpful-content)
— as the lens for assessing quality, and author authority is how you give an engine
a real person to attach that assessment to.

## Does the author actually affect AI visibility?

Author signals don't mechanically raise citation counts the way a great passage
does — but they feed the trust engines weigh when choosing whom to surface. A
generative engine is deciding whose words are safe to repeat; content from a
credible, identifiable expert is a lower-risk choice than content from an anonymous
source. This is the human face of the [credibility](/pillars/credibility) pillar: an
evidenced claim is stronger still when it's attributable to someone with relevant
[experience and expertise](/learn/what-is-geo). Treat author authority as a trust
input, not a trick.

## What is E-E-A-T, exactly?

E-E-A-T is Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trust — the framework in
Google's quality guidelines for judging content. Author authority operationalizes
each: **Experience** (demonstrable first-hand involvement), **Expertise** (relevant
knowledge and credentials), **Authoritativeness** (a recognized reputation in the
field), and **Trust** (signals that make the author and content reliable and
identifiable). It's not a score you set — it's a quality bar your author entities
help you meet.

## Where this fits in the Canon

Author authority extends [entity AEO](/learn/what-is-entity-aeo) to people and is
the human expression of [credibility](/pillars/credibility) and
[authority](/pillars/authority) — attributable expertise an engine can trust. Build
the person entity with the same tools as the brand: [Person schema and
sameAs](/learn/sameas-strategy), [Wikidata](/learn/how-to-get-into-wikidata) for
notable authors, and the [entity-building program](/learn/build-your-entity) overall.
