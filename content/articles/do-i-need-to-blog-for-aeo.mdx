can be a blog, a help center, product pages, or a Q&A library.** What matters is
publishing answer-first, evidenced content for the questions your audience asks, in
whatever format fits, not maintaining a blog for its own sake.

## Does the format matter?

Less than the substance. A blog is one good vehicle for [answering
questions](/learn/what-questions-should-my-content-answer) and publishing regularly,
but help centers, documentation, comparison pages, and [Q&A
libraries](/learn/turn-page-into-qa-library) can be just as citable. Engines cite
[answer-first passages](/learn/answer-first-sentence) wherever they live, so choose the
format that best fits the questions you're answering and that you can keep current —
not the one convention says you "should" have.

## What does AEO content actually need?

Substance over type. The requirement is [answer-first](/learn/answer-first-sentence),
[evidenced](/learn/statistics-quotes-citations-aeo), [original
content](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore) that addresses the specific
questions people ask engines, on crawlable pages. Whether that sits on a blog or
elsewhere is secondary — directly answering real questions with credible detail is
what earns the citation, which is the whole point of the
[Extractability](/pillars/extractability) and [Alignment](/pillars/alignment) pillars.

## Can I do AEO without publishing new content?

To a point. Optimizing existing pages to be answer-first and crawlable can earn
citations with no new content at all — often the
[fastest wins](/learn/cheapest-high-impact-aeo-moves). But answering more [real
questions](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask) generally expands your citation
surface, so some ongoing publishing — in whatever format fits — usually helps once your
existing pages are optimized. Fix what you have first, then expand.
