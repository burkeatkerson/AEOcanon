**Clients ask AI catering questions in four buckets — pricing ('how much per person'), inclusions ('does
it include staff and rentals'), dietary ('vegan and gluten-free catering'), and logistics ('how far ahead
to book', 'do you deliver').** Mapping each to readable content that answers it is the core of a catering
AEO content plan.

## How do I find the exact questions?

Listen where clients already ask. Note what they ask on inquiry calls and at tastings, read your reviews
and the questions on your Google profile, scan wedding and corporate-event forums, and [prompt the
assistants directly](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask) on your cuisines and service area to see
the follow-ups they surface. Capture the *natural wording* — "how much does wedding catering cost per head
near [city]" beats "catering price inquiry" — because engines match the client's phrasing. Then prioritize
by what genuinely fits your catering business.

## Should I answer pricing and inclusions questions?

Yes — they're the highest-intent citations most caterers leave on the table. Clients constantly ask about
per-head price, minimums, what's included, and dietary options, and answering them in plain text — not
buried in a PDF or a quote form — makes you the caterer the engine confidently recommends. It also
pre-qualifies the inquiry and saves your team from fielding the same questions all day. These answers are
pure [Alignment](/pillars/alignment): the real question, answered where the engine can read it. Map every
bucket to readable content and you've built the plan that gets a caterer cited.
