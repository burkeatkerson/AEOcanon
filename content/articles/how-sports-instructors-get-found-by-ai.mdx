## What does an AI engine actually do to find you?

It retrieves, ranks, and names. When someone asks for a coach, the engine pulls the instructors it can
read, judges which best fit the request and look well-reviewed, and [names just a
few](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Each step is a filter: if the crawler can't read your
lessons and pricing, you're out before ranking; if your hours are wrong, you're dropped from near-me;
if your reviews are thin, a competitor gets named. Being found means clearing every filter — and for
coaches, the readable lesson menu and visible pricing are often what's missing.

## Why can't students find my coaching business?

Almost always one specific broken gate — and for coaches it's often the lesson menu and pricing. If
they're trapped in a [booking widget or image the crawler can't
read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), the engine doesn't know what you teach or what it costs,
so it can't recommend you for "affordable golf lessons near me" or "tennis coach for beginners nearby."
Other common breaks: inconsistent hours, or thin reviews. The fix is diagnostic — find the *highest*
broken gate and repair it first. For many coaches, getting lessons and pricing into plain text is the
highest-leverage move.

## Does ranking in Google mean AI can find me?

Not on its own. Ranking and a map listing help, but AI citation also needs your [lessons and pricing in
readable text](/learn/sports-instructor-service-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews. A coach can show on a
map yet be absent from "best golf instructor near me" answers if the engine can't read the lesson menu
or the reviews are thin. Clear all three gates and you get [found in
both](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).
