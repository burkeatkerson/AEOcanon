**Clients ask AI maid-service questions in four buckets — cost ('how much is a maid service, flat or
hourly'), trust ('are you insured, bonded, and background-checked'), service type ('recurring vs deep
clean'), and specific need ('move-out cleaning').** Mapping each to readable content is the core of a
maid-service AEO content plan.

## How do I find the exact questions?

Listen where clients already ask. Note what prospects ask when they call or book, read your reviews and
the questions on your Google profile, scan local and home forums, and [prompt the assistants
directly](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask) on maid services and your area to see the follow-ups
they surface. Capture the *natural wording* — "how much to clean a 3-bedroom house" beats "per-square-foot
residential cleaning rates" — because engines match the client's phrasing. Then prioritize by what you
offer.

## Should I answer trust questions like 'are you insured and bonded'?

Yes — they're critical and easy to win. Because a client is letting someone into their home, they ask AI
whether you're insured, bonded, and background-checked before they book. Stating these plainly in readable
text makes you the cited, trusted service — and reassures the client at the exact moment they're deciding.
Trust content wins the wary client; service-type content steers them to recurring. Both build the
credibility engines [reward](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore) — the opposite of a thin booking
widget. Map every bucket to a page and you've built the content plan that gets a maid service cited.
