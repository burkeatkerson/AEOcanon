## What makes a florist 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about where you are and where you deliver. An answer engine recommends a florist for "near
me" or a delivery query only when it can [place you with certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend)
— right area, delivers to the right town, by the cutoff. That certainty comes from signals that all
agree: matching name-address-phone, a complete profile, a stated delivery area and cutoff, and reviews.
When the signals agree, you're an easy recommendation; when your delivery area is vague or buried in an
image, the engine favors a shop that made it clear.

This is the [near-me ranking](/learn/how-to-rank-in-ai-for-near-me-searches) discipline applied to
flowers — and accurate delivery info is where most shops quietly lose.

## Why does the delivery area matter so much?

Because most flower searches are really about getting an arrangement somewhere by a deadline — "florist
that delivers to [town]," "same-day delivery near me." If your delivery area and cutoff aren't clear,
accurate, and in [readable text](/learn/florist-product-pages-for-ai), the engine can't confirm you can
fulfill the order, so it recommends a shop that made it obvious. State exactly where and by when you
deliver, keep it consistent with your [listings](/learn/nap-consistency), and you remove the most
common reason a florist gets skipped on a delivery query.
