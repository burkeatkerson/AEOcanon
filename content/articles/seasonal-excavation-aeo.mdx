questions — dig-season cost and timing in spring, planning and permit content in winter — before each
planning wave, on durable pages you update yearly.** Be the cited answer when customers and builders
start planning, not scrambling after the thaw.

## Why does seasonality matter for excavators?

Because demand is weather-dependent and the research happens before the ground is even workable.
Excavation runs a busy spring-to-fall dig season and a quiet, frozen off-season — but customers and
builders plan, price, and schedule their site work over the winter, well before a machine can break
ground. The excavator whose answer is already published, crawled, and trusted [gets cited the moment
planning starts](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo) — months before the thaw and the bids. It's
the [Freshness pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned into a planning calendar, and a way to fill the
pipeline ahead of the rush.

## How do I prepare for each planning wave?

Build the content while the ground is frozen, so it's ready when customers start researching.

## Should I make a new page each year?

No — update one durable page per seasonal topic. A persistent URL [accumulates
authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh it each year with current cost ranges and
details. That beats spinning up a throwaway page annually that starts from zero and splits your
signals. Keep one 'site prep cost' page, one 'do I need a permit to grade' page, one 'when to schedule
a foundation dig' page — and refresh each ahead of its planning wave. One durable page, refreshed and
ready, beats scrambling when the calls come all at once at the thaw.
