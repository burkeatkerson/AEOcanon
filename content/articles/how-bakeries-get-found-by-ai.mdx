## What does an AI engine actually do to find you?

It retrieves, ranks, and names. When someone asks where to buy a cake, the engine pulls the bakeries
it can read, judges which best fit the request and look well-reviewed, and [names just a
few](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Each step is a filter: if the crawler can't read what
you make, you're out before ranking; if your hours are wrong, you're dropped from 'open now'; if your
reviews are thin, a competitor gets named. Being found means clearing every filter — and for bakeries,
the readable product list is the one most often missed.

## Why can't customers find my bakery?

Almost always one specific broken gate — and for bakeries it's usually the products. If your cakes and
breads live in an [Instagram feed or a PDF the crawler can't read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site),
the engine doesn't know what you make, so it can't recommend you for "custom birthday cake near me" or
"gluten-free bakery nearby." Other common breaks: inconsistent hours that make 'open now' wrong, or
thin reviews. The fix is diagnostic — find the *highest* broken gate and repair it first. For most
bakeries, putting the products in plain text on your own site is the single highest-leverage move.

## Does ranking in Google mean AI can find me?

Not on its own. Ranking and a Google listing help, but AI citation also needs your [products and
custom-order answers in readable text](/learn/bakery-menu-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews. A bakery
can show up on a map yet be absent from "best custom cakes near me" answers if the engine can't read
the products or the reviews are thin. Clear all three gates and you get [found in
both](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).
