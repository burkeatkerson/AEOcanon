## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each great visit earns a review and a follower; a [readable menu and
schedule](/learn/food-truck-menu-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews earn citations for 'best [cuisine] near
me'; those bring a crowd that found you on purpose; and the loyal following that tracks you down means a
line wherever you park. The social-only, hope-for-a-crowd model is the opposite: a faucet you keep
running with posts and boosts, that stops the moment a slow location empties the street. AEO builds the
[compounding asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead — loyal customers and event bookings you control.

## What's the fastest place to start?

Fix the [readable menu and schedule](/learn/food-truck-menu-pages-for-ai) first — it's the highest-leverage
gap — then keep your location current so you win [near-me and find-a-truck-now](/learn/near-me-food-truck-ai-search),
and build a steady flow of [genuine reviews](/learn/food-truck-reviews-ai-recommendations). Those win
citations within weeks. Then compound it: turn every happy customer into a review and a follower, and add
[catering and event pages](/learn/food-truck-catering-and-events-aeo) for high-margin, plannable bookings.
Small habits, compounding into a base of loyal, direct demand.

## How does this lower my cost per customer?

By replacing luck and paid social with assets that keep paying. A boosted post or a slow location costs
money or returns nothing; an earned citation, a loyal follower, or a booked event costs mostly upfront and
brings people for years. As your owned demand and event bookings grow, your cost per customer falls and
your margins improve — and because AI names only a few places, a real, well-reviewed truck can win
recommendations a faceless feed [can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
