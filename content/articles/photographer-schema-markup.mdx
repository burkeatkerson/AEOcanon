## What does photographer schema actually do?

It makes your photography details unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness and ProfessionalService
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business) label your name,
address, price range, package link, and service area so engines parse them cleanly rather than guessing —
reinforcing the [consistent identity](/learn/nap-consistency) and coverage local recommendations depend on.
There's no specific Photographer type in schema.org, so LocalBusiness (or the more specific
ProfessionalService) is the right base. It's the [structured data for AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo)
pattern applied to photography: clarity for the parser, on top of packages and a service area that are already
clear and readable for the client.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your photography business — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your details machine-readable, which *supports*
recognition, but the citation still depends on [readable
packages](/learn/photographer-service-pages-for-ai) the crawler can actually parse, a [clear service
area](/learn/local-aeo-for-photographers), and [genuine
reviews](/learn/photographer-reviews-ai-recommendations). Schema can't rescue an image-only gallery the engine
can't read or a service area buried in a contact form — and faking reviews or details in markup is a misuse
engines can [detect](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations). Accurate schema on top of real, readable packages
is the combination that works.
