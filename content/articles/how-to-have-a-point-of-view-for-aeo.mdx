**Have a point of view for AEO by taking a clear, defensible stance and backing it
with evidence and reasoning, rather than hedging into safe generalities.** A genuine
POV is hard to replicate and gives engines a distinctive source — but it earns
citations only when it's supported, not when it's just contrarian.

## Why does a point of view help citation?

Because it's distinctive and synthesizes rather than summarizes. A genuine POV
interprets the evidence and takes a position — something competitors merely
[repeating consensus](/learn/will-ai-cite-generic-content) don't offer — which makes
you a unique source, the kind of [original insight and analysis Google's quality
guidance rewards](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/creating-helpful-content). That's the [Originality pillar](/pillars/originality) at work:
distinctiveness earns the citation, and a reasoned stance is one of the clearest ways
to be distinct without needing proprietary data.

## Isn't a strong opinion risky?

Only an unsupported one. A clear, [evidenced
stance](/learn/does-citing-sources-help-get-cited) differentiates you and can become
the cited answer; a hot take with no backing is risky because it
[contradicts the corroboration engines rely on](/learn/does-contradicting-consensus-hurt-aeo).
The dividing line is support — the value is in the reasoning and evidence behind the
view, not the boldness of it. Have the position, then prove it.

## How is this different from being contrarian?

A point of view is a reasoned position; being contrarian is disagreeing for effect.
Engines reward the former when it's supported and discount the latter when it isn't —
so aim for a stance you can defend with [data and
logic](/learn/how-to-add-unique-data-to-content), not a provocation you can't back up.
The goal is to interpret the topic better than anyone, which is
[differentiation](/learn/how-to-differentiate-content-for-aeo), not contrarianism for
its own sake.
