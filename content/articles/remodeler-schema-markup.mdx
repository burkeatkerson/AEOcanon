rescues a slow site or a buried answer.

## What does remodeler schema actually do?

It makes your business data unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business), and
the GeneralContractor subtype, labels your name, address, phone, hours, area served, services, and
reviews so engines parse them cleanly rather than guessing from prose — reinforcing the [consistent
identity](/learn/nap-consistency) local recognition depends on. It's the [structured data for
AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern applied to the trade: clarity for the parser, on top of
content that's already clear for the homeowner.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your remodeling business — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your data machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [consistent
identity](/learn/local-aeo-for-remodelers), [genuine
reviews](/learn/remodeler-reviews-ai-recommendations), verifiable credentials, and [pages
that answer project questions](/learn/remodeler-service-pages-for-ai). And don't fake it:
marking up reviews or credentials that don't match your visible page is a misuse engines can detect —
[especially damaging](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations) for a high-trust, high-ticket trade.
Accurate schema on top of real proof is the combination that works.
