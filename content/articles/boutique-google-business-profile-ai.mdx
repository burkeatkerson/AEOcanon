## Why does Google Business Profile matter for AI search?

Because it's a structured, trusted confirmation of where you are, when you're open, and what kind of
shop you are — exactly what an engine needs to recommend a boutique. When the assistant decides whom to
name for a near-me or style query, it leans on the profile to verify your location, hours, category, and
reputation [before recommending you](/learn/does-google-business-profile-help-ai-search). A complete,
accurate profile makes you an easy recommendation; wrong hours or a thin profile leaves the engine
unsure — or sends a shopper to a store that's closed.

The reviews on your profile do double duty — they're a top input to [whether AI recommends
you](/learn/boutique-reviews-ai-recommendations) at all.

## Does GBP replace a good website?

No — it's half the picture. The profile is a powerful placement and near-me signal, but engines also
retrieve and cite your own [readable product and style pages](/learn/boutique-product-pages-for-ai) for
detailed questions — "plus-size dresses near me," "sustainable denim shop." You need both: a complete,
accurate profile so the engine can place you and confirm your niche, and readable products for the
substance that wins the style query. Treat them as one system, kept [consistent
everywhere](/learn/nap-consistency).
