## What does jeweler schema actually do?

It makes your store's details unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business), and the
JewelryStore subtype, labels your name, address, hours, services, and reviews so engines parse them
cleanly rather than guessing — reinforcing the [consistent identity](/learn/nap-consistency) local
recommendations depend on. Add Product schema to your collection pages and the engine reads each ring's
price range, metal, and brand. It's the [structured data for AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern
applied to the trade: clarity for the parser, on top of collections and services that are already clear
and readable for the buyer.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your store — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your details machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [readable collections and
services](/learn/jeweler-product-pages-for-ai) the crawler can parse, [stated
pricing](/learn/local-aeo-for-jewelers), and [genuine
reviews](/learn/jeweler-reviews-ai-recommendations). Schema can't rescue an image-only catalog or missing
prices — and faking reviews or details in markup is a misuse engines can
[detect](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations). Accurate schema on top of a readable, real site is the
combination that works.
