## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a local entity the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the
engine can retrieve you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as the answer — and that's a
higher bar of trust. The engine recommends the installer it can [confidently
confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, capable, and well-regarded, because when it
recommends you it's putting its own credibility behind the choice. For a twenty-year, five-figure
decision in a market full of fly-by-night door-knockers, that bar is high — so make confirmation
effortless and consistent.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). One inconsistency (an old phone number, a mismatched address)
introduces doubt, and doubt routes the recommendation elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one installer over another?

Because it picks the one it's most sure about. Faced with several options, the engine names the
installer whose signals most confidently match the request — right service, right area, clearly
certified, genuinely reviewed, with real installs to show. The competitor with conflicting listings or
thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it loses even if the work is just as good. Winning is less about
being the best installer in town and more about being the [easiest to verify as the
best](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — which is exactly what this library builds,
piece by piece.
