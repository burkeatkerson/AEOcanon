**Proximity matters in AI local search, but it's one factor among several — not the
deciding one.** Engines weigh relevance, distance, and prominence together, so a
slightly farther business with a clearer identity, stronger reviews, and better answers
can out-recommend a closer but ambiguous one. Distance is a tiebreaker, not a trump
card.

## How much does distance actually weigh?

Enough to matter, not enough to decide alone. Google frames [local ranking as relevance,
distance, and prominence](https://support.google.com/business/answer/7091), and AI local
recommendations lean on the same balance — so the engine asks not just "who's closest"
but "who can I [place and trust](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) for
this question." A nearby business that's ambiguous or thinly represented loses to a
slightly farther one that's clear, well-reviewed, and relevant.

## When does proximity matter most?

When the need is urgent and on-the-ground. For a tow truck, an emergency plumber, or
anything "right now," distance carries more weight because the customer has to act fast
and local. For considered choices — a specialist, a contractor for a big project —
reputation and fit dominate, and a farther business can easily win.

## Can I beat a closer competitor?

Yes — because proximity is only one signal. Strengthen the things you control:
[consistent identity](/learn/nap-consistency), [genuine
reviews](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations), [consistent
citations](/learn/local-citations-and-ai-search), and [local content that answers the
question](/learn/local-service-area-pages-for-ai). Do that, and distance becomes a
smaller part of the decision — the local business that does the work can out-recommend a
closer one that doesn't, which is the whole promise of [local
AEO](/learn/local-aeo).
