## What does it take to become an AEO specialist?

It takes understanding how AI answer engines retrieve and cite, then applying the
[eight-pillar Canon](/learn/aeo-canon) to real pages until it's second nature. Because
AEO reuses most of strong SEO and adds a new layer — passage-level extraction,
conversational queries, per-engine measurement — most people reach working competence in
a few months. You don't need to be a developer: AEO spans technical, content, and
authority work, so writers and marketers have a clear path too.

## Which skills matter most?

The eight pillars are the skill map: access and extractability (technical and writing),
authority and credibility (off-site and evidence), originality and freshness (content),
and adaptability (measurement). The 2026 view of these is in [the AEO skills that
matter](/learn/aeo-skills-2026). Lean into the cluster that fits your background, but be
literate across all eight — specialists who can connect content, technical, and
measurement are the most valuable.

## How do you prove you can do it?

With results, not just a certificate. Build a [portfolio](/learn/build-an-aeo-portfolio)
of measured wins — citation share gained, before/after answers, tools or schema you
shipped — and pair it with a [credential](/learn/aeo-canon-certification) to signal
structured knowledge. For where these skills lead, see [AEO career
paths](/learn/aeo-career-paths) and the honest take on [the job market and
pay](/learn/aeo-job-market-salaries).

## Where this fits in the Canon

Becoming a specialist is the [AEO Canon](/learn/aeo-canon) turned into a learning path:
