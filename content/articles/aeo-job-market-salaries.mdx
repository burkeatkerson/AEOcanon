## What does an AEO specialist get paid?

Honestly: there's no reliable universal answer yet, and you should distrust anyone who
gives one. AEO is an emerging discipline without the published salary surveys that
established roles have. In practice the work sits inside SEO, content, and AI-marketing
jobs, so compensation tracks *those* roles — which vary enormously by region, seniority,
company, and the results you can show. The useful framing isn't "what's the AEO salary"
but "AEO is a premium skill on roles that already have ranges, and provable wins move you
within them."

## Is there real demand for AEO skills?

Yes — and it's growing as [AI answers absorb search
traffic](/learn/does-ai-search-send-traffic). But today that demand mostly appears as AEO
being *added* to SEO, content, and marketing job descriptions rather than as a flood of
dedicated "AEO specialist" postings. The skill is wanted; the standalone title is still
forming. That gap is the opportunity.

## Should you wait for it to become an established career?

No. The advantage goes to people who build the skill while the field is still
uncontested — the same logic by which [a focused site can win citations big brands
haven't claimed](/learn/small-business-ai-search). Learn AEO now, prove it with results,
and you're positioned both for the adjacent roles hiring today and the dedicated roles
forming over time. Start with [how to become an AEO
specialist](/learn/become-an-aeo-specialist) and the [career
paths](/learn/aeo-career-paths).

## How do you raise your earning potential?

Demonstrate outcomes. Because pay tracks results rather than the label, the highest-
leverage move is a [portfolio](/learn/build-an-aeo-portfolio) of measured citation
wins — share-of-voice gained, before/after answers, tools or schema you shipped — plus a
[specialization](/courses) and a [credential](/learn/aeo-canon-certification) to signal
structured knowledge. In an emerging field, provable results are the whole game.
