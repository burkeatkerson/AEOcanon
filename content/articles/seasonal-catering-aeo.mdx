## Why does seasonality matter for caterers?

Because catering demand comes in waves, and clients plan them far ahead. Wedding season, holiday office
parties, graduation, summer events, and New Year's all bring searches weeks or months before the date —
"wedding caterer for next June," "holiday office party catering near me." The caterer whose answer is
already published, crawled, and trusted [gets cited the moment planning starts](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo)
— while competitors are still updating their PDF. It's the [Freshness pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned
into a booking calendar.

## How do I prepare for each wave?

Build the content while it's calm, so it's ready when clients start booking.

## Should I make a new page each year?

No — update one durable page per recurring occasion. A persistent 'holiday office party catering' or
'wedding season catering' page [accumulates authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh the
dates, menus, and pricing each year. That beats spinning up a throwaway page annually that starts from zero
and splits your signals. Keep one page per occasion, refresh it ahead of the wave, and link it to your
[wedding](/learn/wedding-catering-ai-search) and [corporate catering](/learn/corporate-catering-aeo)
pages — one durable page, refreshed and ready, beats scrambling when the wave arrives.
