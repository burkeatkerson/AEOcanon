because engines synthesize sentiment from Google and third-party platforms to judge who's

work and area make you the safe recommendation; thin or fake ones lose it.

## Why do reviews decide who AI recommends?

Because they're independent proof of quality, and an engine recommending a roofer for a
five-figure job wants to be safe. When AI recommends, it [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from Google and third-party
platforms rather than taking your word for it — so the volume, recency, and quality of
your reviews directly shape whether you're named. For roofing, where a bad call means
leaks and expensive damage, that trust signal carries even more weight than in a lower-
stakes trade. It's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made local and concrete.

## How do roofers earn more reviews?

Turn every completed job into one — it's the highest-return habit in the trade.

Reviews that name the job and the place corroborate the exact [questions homeowners
ask](/learn/roofing-questions-customers-ask-ai), so they do double duty as trust and as
content.

## Do fake reviews ever work?

No — they hurt, and for a trust-driven trade they hurt more. Fake or incentivized reviews
risk detection, violate platform rules, and create inconsistent patterns [engines and
platforms increasingly catch](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) — adding no
genuine trust and risking real damage if exposed. The durable path is earning real
reviews steadily, which is slower but is what actually moves recommendations and holds up
under scrutiny — and it compounds into the [owned reputation](/learn/how-roofers-get-leads-without-buying-them)
that replaces bought leads.
