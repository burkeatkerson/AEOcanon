## Why do reviews carry so much weight for detailers?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge quality — and an owner is trusting you with a
car they care about. An assistant deciding whom to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform reviews to
gauge whether you do flawless, careful work. For a quality-driven service, that corroborated reputation
is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the [off-site
mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks do.

## How many reviews do I need?

Fewer than you think, fresher than you have. There's no magic count — consistency and recency outweigh a
raw total. A steady stream of genuine recent reviews signals an active, trusted shop far better than a
big pile from three years ago. The goal is an ongoing flow: ask happy clients after each detail, so new
reviews keep arriving and the engine keeps seeing a shop that's [currently
trusted](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations), not formerly busy.

## Do the words in a review matter?

Yes — specific reviews do more work than generic praise.

Reviews that name what you did — flawless ceramic, removed every pet hair, corrected the paint — give
engines specific detail tying you to those services and to quality work. They reinforce the [questions
owners ask](/learn/auto-detailing-questions-customers-ask-ai), so a handful of detailed reviews can
outperform a wall of generic ones. Ask clients what to mention, and the reviews start doing your AEO for
you.
