## What makes a dog trainer 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about where you are and what you offer. An answer engine recommends a trainer for "near me"
only when it can [place you with certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — right
area, right kind of training. That certainty comes from signals that all agree: matching
name-address-phone, accurate hours, a complete profile, readable programs and pricing, and reviews.
When the signals agree, you're an easy recommendation; when they conflict or your programs are hidden
in a widget, the engine favors a clearer competitor.

## Why does NAP consistency matter so much?

Because a mismatch is a doubt, and engines route around doubt. If your phone number differs between
your site and your Google profile, or an old address still shows in a listing, the engine can't be sure
which business you are or where you operate — so it favors a competitor it can [place with
confidence](/learn/nap-consistency). For trainers who run from home, work mobile, or have moved, this
is a common and quiet leak. Clean it up and you remove the single most fixable reason an engine skips
you on a near-me query.
