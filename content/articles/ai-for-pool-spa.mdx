**Using AI to run your pool and spa service and being recommended by AI to
customers are two different games — and you've probably won the first while
quietly losing the second.** You use software to optimize routes and balance
water; meanwhile homeowners have started asking AI *who to hire* for weekly
service or a green-pool rescue — and it names one or two companies. If yours
isn't one of them, AI is booking those accounts with a competitor.

## How are pool and spa businesses using AI today?

More than owners let on. Route and service platforms like Skimmer and Jobber
plan the week's stops, log chemistry readings, and auto-text customers when the
tech has been out. Water-balancing calculators and pool-math apps turn a test
strip into an exact dose of acid, chlorine, and salt. ChatGPT drafts the "why
your pool went green" explainer, the winterizing reminder, and the equipment-
upgrade proposal. AI answering services catch the new-account calls that come in
while you're elbow-deep in a filter. All of it makes you leaner — and none of it
makes you the company AI recommends.

## But is AI recommending your pool service?

That's the question that actually fills your route, and it has nothing to do with
your software. When a homeowner opens an assistant and asks "who should I hire to
service my pool," the engine doesn't see how tight your routing is. It retrieves
and quotes what it can *read and trust about you on the open web* — your service
pages, your Google reviews, your listings. You can run a fully automated, chemistry-
dialed operation and still never surface in that answer, because the tools you use
on your route are invisible to the system doing the recommending.

## How do customers use AI to find a pool pro?

They ask it like they'd ask a neighbor at the pool store. Instead of scrolling
listings, more people now type "best pool service near me," "who can fix a green
pool fast," or "weekly pool cleaning [town] cost" — and act on the short list the
assistant gives back. Because the AI answers in place and names only a couple of
companies, this is a **winner-take-most** moment: the services it cites win the
recurring account, and everyone else is invisible. And a pool account isn't a
one-time job — it's monthly revenue for years, which makes each named slot worth
far more than a single call.

## How do you know if AI is sending your customers to a competitor?

Ask the engines yourself. Open ChatGPT, [Google AI Mode](/glossary/google-ai-mode),
and [Perplexity](/glossary/perplexity) and run the real questions your customers
ask: "pool service in [your city]," "best pool cleaning company near me,"
"green pool cleanup [your area]." Note who gets named. If competitors show up and
you don't — or the AI lists the wrong service area, stale pricing, or a number you
no longer use — you've found the gap. That mismatch is exactly what
[AEO for pool services](/learn/aeo-for-pool-services) is built to fix.
