## Why does seasonality matter for dog pros?

Because demand — for both training and boarding — surges in predictable waves, and the cited business
catches them. Spring brings a wave of new puppies and the owners searching for their first training
class; the winter holidays and summer bring travel, and a rush to book boarding weeks ahead. The
business whose answer is already published, crawled, and trusted [gets cited the moment the surge
hits](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo) — while competitors are still updating their site. It's
the [Freshness pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned into a demand calendar, and the difference between a
full boarding calendar over the holidays and a quiet one.

## How do I prepare for each surge?

Build the content while it's calm, so it's ready when demand spikes.

## Should I make a new holiday page each year?

No — update one durable evergreen page. A persistent 'holiday dog boarding' or 'puppy training' page
[accumulates authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh it each year, which beats
spinning up a throwaway 'Christmas 2026' page that starts from zero and splits your signals. Keep one
strong page per theme that answers the seasonal questions, sharpen it before each surge, and link it to
your [boarding](/learn/dog-boarding-and-daycare-aeo) and [program and pricing
pages](/learn/dog-training-service-pages-for-ai). One durable page, refreshed and ready, beats
scrambling when the holiday booking wave hits.
