**Students ask AI studio questions in four buckets — cost ('how much is a yoga membership'), fit
('beginner yoga classes near me'), styles ('hot yoga vs vinyasa', 'reformer Pilates'), and logistics
('do you offer prenatal', 'what should I bring').** Mapping each to readable content that answers it is
the core of a studio AEO content plan.

## How do I find the exact questions?

Listen where students already ask. Note what people ask at the front desk and on intro visits, read
your reviews and the questions on your Google profile, scan local wellness forums and groups, and
[prompt the assistants directly](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask) on your styles and area to
see the follow-ups they surface. Capture the *natural wording* — "is there a yoga studio near me that's
good for total beginners" beats "novice-oriented yoga facility" — because engines match the student's
phrasing. Then prioritize by what genuinely fits your studio.

## Should I publish pricing if competitors hide it?

Yes — and it's an edge, not a risk. Students constantly ask AI "how much is a yoga membership near me,"
and a clear drop-in, class-pack, or membership range in readable text lets the engine recommend you for
those high-intent cost searches — while competitors who bury pricing in a widget get skipped.
Transparency also builds trust with a price-conscious student before they ever visit. It's pure
[Alignment](/pillars/alignment): the real question, answered where the engine and the student can read
it. Map every bucket to readable content and you've built the plan that gets a studio cited.
