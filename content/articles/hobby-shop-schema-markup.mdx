**Hobby shops should use the Store (a LocalBusiness subtype) schema with accurate name, address, phone,
and hours, plus Product schema for what they carry and FAQ schema on answer pages — it helps engines
parse where you are, when you're open, and what you stock.** Schema clarifies readable content for AI;
it never rescues an image-only site or wrong hours.

## What does hobby shop schema actually do?

It makes your store's details unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business), and the
Store subtype, labels your name, address, hours, and reviews so engines parse them cleanly rather than
guessing — reinforcing the [consistent identity](/learn/nap-consistency) local recommendations depend
on, while Product schema labels the categories and brands you carry. It's the [structured data for
AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern applied to the trade: clarity for the parser, on top of
brands and events that are already clear and readable for the customer.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your store — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your details machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [readable brands and
categories](/learn/hobby-shop-product-pages-for-ai) the crawler can parse, [accurate
hours](/learn/local-aeo-for-hobby-shops), and [genuine
reviews](/learn/hobby-shop-reviews-ai-recommendations). Schema can't rescue an image-only site or wrong
hours — and faking reviews or details in markup is a misuse engines can
[detect](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations). Accurate schema on top of a readable, real site is the
combination that works.
