## How is commercial plumbing AEO different?

It's a B2B research problem, not an emergency one. Where residential is urgent and
fast, commercial plumbing involves facility managers, property managers, and general
contractors who [research thoroughly before contacting
anyone](/learn/does-aeo-work-for-b2b) — working through code, capacity, and contract
questions, often via AI. So the goal shifts from winning the 2am call to being the
cited expert that shapes the consideration set well before a conversation. The
[Alignment pillar](/pillars/alignment) here means answering evaluation questions, not
emergency ones.

## What do commercial buyers ask AI?

Detailed, technical, evaluation-focused questions. Backflow and code requirements,
system sizing for a building type, the scope of a preventive-maintenance contract,
emergency response times and SLAs, and experience with their specific facility type.
These are substantive questions that reward [evidenced, expert
content](/learn/how-to-show-expertise-to-ai) — exactly the kind of depth a generic
directory can't provide and a real commercial plumber can. Map them the same way you'd
map [residential questions](/learn/plumbing-questions-customers-ask-ai), tuned to the
buyer.

## Does it need different content?

Yes — more depth and evidence. Commercial decisions carry higher stakes and longer
research, so content should [demonstrate expertise](/learn/how-to-show-expertise-to-ai):
code knowledge, project examples, certifications, and clear service scope, in thorough,
[answer-first pages](/learn/plumbing-service-pages-for-ai). The fundamentals are
identical to residential — crawlable, answer-first, trusted — but commercial rewards
[credibility](/pillars/credibility) and depth more heavily, because the buyer is
evaluating, not reacting.

## Is it worth it?

Often very much so. Commercial contracts carry high value and long, increasingly
AI-mediated research cycles, so [being the cited expert](/learn/does-aeo-work-for-b2b)
when a facility manager researches shapes the shortlist early — and even a few captured
contracts can make the whole program pay off. Building and maintaining that depth of
commercial content is a sustained effort, and one the [done-for-you
program](/pricing) can run alongside your residential pages.
