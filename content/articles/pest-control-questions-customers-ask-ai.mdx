**Customers ask AI pest control questions in four buckets — identification ('are these
termites or ants'), urgent ('how do I get rid of bed bugs'), cost ('how much is termite
treatment'), and prevention ('is a quarterly plan worth it').** Mapping each question to the
page that should own it is the core of a pest control AEO content plan.

## How do I find the exact questions?

Listen where customers already ask. Mine your phone log and service calls for the questions
people actually voice, check Reddit threads, community forums, and the People Also Ask box,
and [prompt the assistants directly](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask) on your
services to see the follow-ups they surface. Capture the *natural wording* — "are these
termites or flying ants" beats "subterranean termite identification" — because engines match
the customer's phrasing, not your jargon. Then prioritize by intent and value.

## Should I answer identification and prevention questions?

Yes — they're how you earn the trust that books the job *and* the plan. Answering "are these
termites or ants" or "is a quarterly plan worth it" helpfully makes you the source a customer
remembers when the problem is bigger than a DIY fix. Identification content captures the
alarmed discovery moment; prevention content sells the recurring plan that turns one
treatment into [predictable revenue](/learn/grow-a-pest-control-business-with-ai-search). Both
build the credibility and visibility engines [reward](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore)
— the opposite of a thin directory page. Map every bucket to a page and you've built the
content plan that gets a pest control company cited.
