## Why does seasonality matter for food trucks?

Because food-truck demand spikes in weather-driven waves, and people plan the big ones ahead. Festival and
event season, wedding and graduation catering, summer markets and patio season, and holiday parties all
bring searches weeks or months before the date — "food truck for a summer wedding," "food trucks at
[festival]." The truck whose answer is already published, crawled, and trusted [gets cited the moment
planning starts](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo) — while competitors are still scrambling once
the season hits. It's the [Freshness pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned into a season calendar.

## How do I prepare for each wave?

Build the content while it's calm, so it's ready when organizers and crowds start planning.

## Should I make a new page each year?

No — update one durable page per recurring season or occasion. A persistent 'wedding catering' or 'festival
and event bookings' page [accumulates authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh the dates,
menu, and availability each year. That beats spinning up a throwaway page annually that starts from zero and
splits your signals. Keep one page per occasion, refresh it ahead of the wave, and link it to your
[catering and events page](/learn/food-truck-catering-and-events-aeo) — one durable page, refreshed and
ready, beats scrambling when the season arrives.
