## Why do reviews carry so much weight for furniture stores?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge which stores people trust — and a furniture
purchase is a high-ticket, careful decision. An assistant deciding what to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform reviews to
gauge whether you sell quality, price fairly, and deliver well. For a furniture store, that corroborated
reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the
[off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks
do.

## Do reviews that mention quality and delivery matter more?

Yes — because a sofa or bed is expensive, and it has to arrive in good shape.

A review naming solid quality, fair pricing, and reliable, on-time delivery reassures the engine you
fulfill high-ticket orders dependably — exactly what a careful buyer searching for a [furniture store
near me](/learn/near-me-furniture-ai-search) needs to hear. Invite happy buyers to mention what they
bought, the quality, and that it arrived and was assembled on time, and the reviews start matching you to
the searches that bring sales.

## How many reviews — and how fresh?

Fresh beats big. There's no magic count — recency and a steady flow outweigh a stale total. A store with
current reviews that mention quality and delivery signals an active, trusted business far better than one
coasting on old ratings. Build a simple habit: invite happy buyers to review after delivery, and
[respond to them](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations) — engagement signals an active store that
cares, which is exactly what an engine wants to recommend.
