## Why is high-intent the most valuable concrete search?

Because it's the moment money is about to move, and the assistant frames the whole decision. A
homeowner ready to pour researches cost per square foot, timeline, and the concrete-vs-alternatives
question before they contact anyone — and the [AI answer names only a few
sources](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Pew Research [found people click a link just 8% of
the time when an AI summary
appears](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer shapes the shortlist before you ever hear from them. The contractor cited at this stage
is the one the homeowner trusts enough to call first.

The practical, commitment-stage ones — and you should own every one.

## Why does answering cost honestly matter so much?

Because cost is the first thing a homeowner researches and the question most concrete sites dodge. A
clear, honest cost per square foot — with the factors that move it — earns trust and citations
precisely when intent is highest. Dodging it ("every job is unique, call us") sends the homeowner to a
competitor or a lead-gen platform that actually answered. Being the contractor who explains the money
honestly is the [Credibility](/pillars/credibility) and [Originality](/pillars/originality) edge that
gets you cited and called — map every one of these questions to a page in your
[questions library](/learn/concrete-masonry-questions-customers-ask-ai).
