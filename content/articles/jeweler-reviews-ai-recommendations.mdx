## Why do reviews carry so much weight for jewelers?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge which stores people trust — and jewelry is one
of the highest-stakes, most emotional purchases someone makes. An assistant deciding what to recommend
[synthesizes sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform
reviews to gauge whether you craft beautiful rings, deliver custom work as promised, and repair heirlooms
safely. For a jeweler, that corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility
pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that
correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks do.

## Do reviews that mention custom work and repairs matter more?

Yes — because so much of a jewelry decision is about trusting the store with something precious.

A review naming a beautiful custom ring and a reliable repair reassures the engine you handle high-value
work dependably — exactly what a buyer researching an [engagement ring](/learn/engagement-ring-ai-search)
needs to hear. Invite happy buyers to mention what you made or fixed, and the reviews start matching you to
the searches that bring high-value sales.

## How many reviews — and how fresh?

Fresh beats big. There's no magic count — recency and a steady flow outweigh a stale total. A store with
current reviews that mention rings, custom work, and repairs signals an active, trusted business far
better than one coasting on old ratings. Build a simple habit: invite happy buyers to review after a ring
purchase or repair pickup, and [respond to them](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations) — engagement
signals an active store that cares, which is exactly what an engine wants to recommend.
