## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each well-served client produces reviews, a case study, and referrals — and
stays for years; reviews and [answer-first content](/learn/marketing-agency-service-pages-for-ai) earn
citations and rankings; those bring direct inquiries; and the money saved on Clutch and UpCity leads
funds more content and a sharper [specialization](/learn/niche-marketing-agency-aeo). The marketplace
model is the opposite: a faucet you pay to keep running, selling the same lead to three agencies, that
stops the moment you stop. AEO builds the [compounding asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead —
recurring clients who found you and stay.

## Why is recurring revenue central to growth?

Because an agency client *is* monthly recurring retainer revenue, and filling the top of the funnel
only pays off if clients stay. A retainer client who stays for years is worth many times a one-off
project — and long-tenured, happy clients are exactly who write the [reviews that mention
results](/learn/marketing-agency-reviews-ai-recommendations) and become the [case
studies](/learn/agency-case-studies-and-results-aeo) that feed the AI recommendations bringing the next
clients. So growth isn't just AEO at the top; it's AEO plus retention together, each compounding the
other.

## How do I lower my cost per client?

By replacing recurring fees with assets that keep paying. A purchased marketplace lead costs every time
and is [resold to competitors](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands); an earned citation, a long-tenured
retainer, or a referral costs mostly upfront and pays for years. As your cited, specialized, and
retained base grows, your cost per client falls and your revenue stabilizes. And because AI names only a
few firms, a specialized, genuinely reviewed agency with published results can win recommendations a
generic directory [can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
