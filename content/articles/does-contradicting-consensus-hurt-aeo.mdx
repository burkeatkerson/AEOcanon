**It can — because engines lean on corroboration, a claim that contradicts the

but an unsupported one usually gets passed over for the agreed answer.

## Why does contradicting consensus carry risk?

Because corroboration is the engine's main proxy for accuracy. Since engines [don't
rigorously fact-check](/learn/do-ai-engines-fact-check-sources) and instead trust
claims that [agree across sources](/learn/how-many-mentions-to-get-cited), a lone
source breaking from the consensus looks riskier to cite. It's not that the consensus
is always right — it's that agreement is how an engine manages the
[credibility](/pillars/credibility) it stakes on every citation, so a contradiction
without backing is a hard sell.

## Can I still get cited for a contrarian view?

Yes — if you earn it with evidence. [Original, well-evidenced
positions](/learn/does-original-research-get-cited-more) that genuinely advance a topic
can be cited and even differentiate you, because [originality is
rewarded](/pillars/originality). The deciding factor is verifiable support — data,
primary sources, clear reasoning — that gives the engine grounds to trust a claim
that departs from the norm. Strong evidence converts risk into distinctiveness.

## How do I challenge consensus safely?

Lead with proof, not assertion. Present [original data or primary
sources](/learn/does-citing-sources-help-get-cited), acknowledge the prevailing view
so you're clearly engaging with it, and show exactly why your position differs. The
more verifiable your support, the more an engine can trust a claim it can't
corroborate elsewhere — turning a risky contradiction into a credible, citable
original contribution rather than an unsupported outlier.
