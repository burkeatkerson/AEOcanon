**Multi-location businesses win AEO by treating each location as its own distinct local
entity — a dedicated page, its own Google Business Profile, consistent local citations,
and local reviews — all tied to one trusted brand.** The mistake is one generic page
for all locations, which leaves every individual market ambiguous.

## Why treat each location separately?

Because each location is a separate local recognition problem. An engine recommending a
business in one city needs to [place that specific location
confidently](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — which requires its own
address, phone, profile, and local content. A single shared page can't do that for ten
markets at once; it leaves each one ambiguous. The brand carries
[authority](/pillars/authority) across all locations, but recognition has to be earned
[location by location](/learn/local-aeo).

## How do I structure it?

A consistent local presence per location, under a coherent brand.

## What's the biggest mistake?

One generic page and inconsistent details. A single page for all locations leaves every
market ambiguous, and mismatched [name, address, or phone](/learn/nap-consistency) across
profiles and directories fragments each location's entity — the same
[consistency](/learn/local-citations-and-ai-search) problem multiplied by your location
count. The fix is a dedicated, consistent, genuinely-local presence per location. (For
the broader strategy, the [local-and-multi-location AEO
course](/courses/local-and-multi-location-aeo) sequences this end to end.)
