**Boutiques get found by AI search when their products and sizing are readable as real text, they
answer the questions shoppers ask, and they're backed by accurate listings and genuine reviews.** The
AI names only a few shops, so the boutique that clears all three is the one recommended.

## What does an AI engine actually do to find you?

It retrieves, ranks, and names. When someone wants clothing, the engine pulls the shops it can read,
judges which best fit the request and look well-reviewed, and [names just a
few](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Each step is a filter: if the crawler can't read your
products, you're out before ranking; if your location or hours are wrong, you're dropped from near-me
queries; if your reviews are thin, a competitor gets named. Being found means clearing every filter —
and for boutiques, the readable products and sizing are often what's missing.

## What are the three gates?

The same three an engine — and a shopper — apply, in order.

## Why can't shoppers find my boutique?

Almost always one specific broken gate — and for boutiques it's usually the image-only site. If your
products and sizing live only in [lookbook photos the crawler can't read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site),
the engine doesn't know what you sell or who you fit, so it can't recommend you for "plus-size boutique
near me" or "where to buy sustainable dresses in [town]." Other common breaks: inconsistent hours, or
thin reviews. The fix is diagnostic — find the *highest* broken gate and repair it first. For most
boutiques, putting products and sizing in real text is the highest-leverage move.

## Does ranking in Google mean AI can find me?

Not on its own. Ranking and a map listing help, but AI citation also needs your [products and sizing in
readable text](/learn/boutique-product-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews. A shop can show on a map yet
be absent from "best plus-size boutique near me" answers if the engine can't read the site or the
reviews are thin. Clear all three gates and you get [found in
both](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).
