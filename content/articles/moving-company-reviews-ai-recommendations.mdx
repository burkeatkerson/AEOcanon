## Why do reviews carry so much weight for moving companies?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge trust — and customers fear damaged belongings,
surprise charges, and no-show crews. An assistant deciding whom to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform reviews to
gauge whether you're careful, fair, and reliable. For a high-trust, high-value decision, that corroborated
reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the
[off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks
do.

## How many reviews do I need?

Fewer than you think, fresher than you have. There's no magic count — consistency and recency outweigh a
raw total. A steady stream of genuine recent reviews signals an active, trusted mover far better than a
big pile from three years ago. The goal is an ongoing flow: ask happy customers after each move, so new
reviews keep arriving and the engine keeps seeing a company that's [currently
trusted](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations), not formerly busy.

## Do the words in a review matter?

Yes — specific reviews do more work than generic praise.

Reviews that name what you did — moved a home with no damage, quote matched the bill, showed up on time —
give engines specific detail tying you to careful, honest work. They reinforce the [questions customers
ask](/learn/moving-questions-customers-ask-ai), so a handful of detailed reviews can outperform a wall of
generic ones. Ask customers what to mention, and the reviews start doing your AEO for you.
