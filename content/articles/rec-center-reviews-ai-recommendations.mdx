## Why do reviews carry so much weight for climbing gyms?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge which places people actually love — and
choosing where to climb or take the kids is a trust-and-community decision. An assistant deciding what
to recommend [synthesizes sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and
review-platform reviews to gauge whether you're a great place to climb. For a gym, that corroborated
reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the
[off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks
do.

## Do reviews that mention the walls and programs matter more?

Yes — specific reviews are gold, because they speak the language of what visitors want.

A review naming the walls, a class, the community, or a kids program gives the engine detail to tie you
to "beginner-friendly climbing gym near me" or "kids climbing nearby" — the exact [fit and activity
queries](/learn/rec-center-questions-customers-ask-ai) visitors ask. Invite happy members to mention
what they loved, and the reviews start matching you to the searches that fill passes, classes, and
memberships.

## How many reviews — and how fresh?

Fresh beats big. There's no magic count — recency and a steady flow outweigh a stale total. A gym with
current reviews that mention real experiences signals an active, loved community far better than one
coasting on old ratings. Build a simple habit: invite happy visitors and members to review after a
great session or a party, and [respond to them](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations) —
engagement signals an active gym that cares, which is exactly the place an engine wants to recommend.
