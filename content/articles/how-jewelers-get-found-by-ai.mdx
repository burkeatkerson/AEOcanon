**Jewelers get found by AI search when their collections and services are readable as real text, they
answer the questions buyers ask, and they're backed by accurate listings and genuine reviews.** The AI
names only a few stores, so the jeweler that clears all three is the one recommended.

## What does an AI engine actually do to find you?

It retrieves, ranks, and names. When someone needs a ring or a repair, the engine pulls the stores it can
read, judges which best fit the request and look well-reviewed, and [names just a
few](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Each step is a filter: if the crawler can't read your
collections, you're out before ranking; if your custom and repair services aren't described, you're
dropped from those queries; if your reviews are thin, a competitor gets named. Being found means clearing
every filter — and for jewelers, the readable collections and service info are often what's missing.

## What are the three gates?

The same three an engine — and a buyer — apply, in order.

## Why can't buyers find my jewelry store?

Almost always one specific broken gate — and for jewelers it's usually the image-only site. If your
collections and pricing live only in [photos the crawler can't read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site),
the engine doesn't know what you offer, so it can't recommend you for "custom engagement rings near me" or
"jewelry repair near me." Other common breaks, your custom and repair services aren't described in text,
or your reviews are thin. The fix is diagnostic — find the *highest* broken gate and repair it first. For
most jewelers, putting collections, pricing, and services in real text is the highest-leverage move.

## Does ranking in Google mean AI can find me?

Not on its own. Ranking and a map listing help, but AI citation also needs your [collections and services
in readable text](/learn/jeweler-product-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews. A store can show on a map yet
be absent from "best custom jeweler near me" answers if the engine can't read the site or the reviews are
thin. Clear all three gates and you get [found in both](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).
