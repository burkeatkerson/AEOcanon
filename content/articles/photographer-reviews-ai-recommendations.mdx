## Why do reviews carry so much weight for photographers?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge which businesses clients actually trust with a
high-stakes, unrepeatable day — and photography is a trust purchase. A couple hiring a wedding photographer is
betting one of the biggest days of their life on you, with no do-overs, so an assistant deciding whom to
recommend [synthesizes sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and
review-platform reviews to gauge whether you deliver. For a photographer, that corroborated reputation is
decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the [off-site
mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks do.

## Do reviews that name the wedding matter more?

Yes — event-level reviews are gold, because they speak the language of the photography decision.

A review naming the wedding, the venue, the session type, or the style gives the engine detail to tie you to
"wedding photographer for [venue]" or "documentary photographer who's great with families" — the exact
[questions clients ask](/learn/photography-questions-customers-ask-ai). Invite happy clients to mention the
wedding or session and what they loved, and the reviews start matching you to the searches that book shoots.

## How many reviews — and how fresh?

Fresh beats big. There's no magic count — recency and a steady flow outweigh a stale total. A photographer with
current reviews that name recent weddings and sessions signals an active, trusted business far better than one
coasting on old ratings. Build a simple habit: invite happy clients to review after each shoot, and [respond to
them](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations) — engagement signals an active photographer who cares,
which is exactly the business an engine wants to recommend.
