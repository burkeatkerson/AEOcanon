## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each great cake earns a review and a regular; a [readable product
list](/learn/bakery-menu-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews earn citations for 'custom cakes near me';
those bring direct orders; and the high-margin custom and wedding work funds a better product and a
better team — which create more great cakes. Chasing walk-ins or buying ads is the opposite: a faucet
that stops the moment you stop. AEO builds the [compounding asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo)
instead — loyal customers and high-margin orders that book direct.

## What's the fastest place to start?

Fix the [readable product list](/learn/bakery-menu-pages-for-ai) and a clear
[custom-order page](/learn/custom-cakes-and-orders-aeo) first — they're the highest-leverage gaps —
then make hours and listings accurate so you win [near-me and 'open
now'](/learn/near-me-bakery-ai-search), and build a steady flow of [genuine
reviews](/learn/bakery-reviews-ai-recommendations). Those win citations within weeks. Then compound it:
turn every happy customer into a review and a regular, and add [custom-cake and seasonal
pages](/learn/seasonal-bakery-aeo) for high-value, plannable bookings. Small habits, compounding into a
base of loyal, direct demand.

## How does this lower my cost per order?

By replacing ad spend and walk-in churn with assets that keep paying. Paid discovery costs every single
time; an earned citation, a loyal regular, or a wedding referral costs mostly upfront and brings orders
for years. As your owned, direct demand grows — especially the high-margin custom and wedding cakes —
your cost per order falls and your margins improve, and because AI names only a few places, a real,
well-reviewed local bakery can win recommendations a faceless chain listing
[can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
