## What are homeowners actually asking?

The expensive, anxious questions — the ones with a five-figure decision behind them.
These cluster into a few high-value buckets, and each is a moment where being the cited
answer wins the relationship.

## How do I find my list?

Mine where the questions already live. Your estimate calls and inquiries are the richest
source — real homeowners, real anxieties — followed by reviews (the words they use),
'near me' and 'roof repair cost' autocomplete and People Also Ask, and [prompting the
assistants](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask) on roofing topics in your area to
see the follow-ups they surface. The questions homeowners already ask you are the ones
they now ask AI.

## Should I really answer pricing?

Yes — honestly, with ranges. Homeowners ask "what does a new roof cost" first, and a
page that gives a transparent answer (typical ranges, what drives the price, how to get
an exact quote) earns trust and gets cited, while "call for pricing" gets skipped by both
engines and anxious homeowners. You don't need to commit to a fixed number to be useful
and [citable](/learn/statistics-quotes-citations-aeo) — you need to stop hiding the
answer.
