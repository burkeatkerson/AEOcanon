## Why do reviews carry so much weight for tire shops?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge trust — and drivers fear being overcharged or
upsold. An assistant deciding whom to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform reviews to
gauge whether you're fair, fast, and honest. For a trade built on trust, that corroborated reputation is
decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the [off-site
mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks do.

## How many reviews do I need?

Fewer than you think, fresher than you have. There's no magic count — consistency and recency outweigh a
raw total. A steady stream of genuine recent reviews signals an active, trusted shop far better than a big
pile from three years ago. The goal is an ongoing flow: ask happy customers after each visit, so new
reviews keep arriving and the engine keeps seeing a shop that's [currently
trusted](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations), not formerly busy.

## Do the words in a review matter?

Yes — specific reviews do more work than generic praise.

Reviews that name what you did — fixed a flat fast, fair price, no upsell — give engines specific detail
tying you to fast, honest service. They reinforce the [questions
drivers ask](/learn/tire-shop-questions-customers-ask-ai), so a handful of detailed reviews can outperform
a wall of generic ones. Ask customers what to mention, and the reviews start doing your AEO for you.
