## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a shop the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine can
read your products and place you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as where to buy the
cake — and that leans harder on whether customers love you. The engine recommends the bakery it can
[confidently confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, open, and well-reviewed, because when
it recommends you it's putting its credibility behind the cake. Your job is to make that confirmation
easy — a readable product list, clear custom-order pages, accurate hours, and genuine, loving reviews.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). Wrong hours or a thin review profile introduces doubt, and doubt
sends the customer elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one bakery over another?

Because it picks the one it's most confident customers will love. Faced with several options, the engine
names the bakery whose signals best match the request — right specialty, right area, open now — and
whose reviews most clearly say people love it. The competitor with an unreadable product list, wrong
hours, or thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it loses. Winning is about being the [easiest to verify
as the loved local choice](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — which is exactly what
this library builds, piece by piece.
