## Why is the product list your most important page?

Because what you bake is what customers search for — and a citation goes to the page the engine can
read. When someone asks "gluten-free birthday cake near me" or "where can I get fresh sourdough," the
engine matches the query against product lists it can [actually parse](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site).
If your products are a PDF, an image, or an Instagram feed, the engine doesn't know what you make, so
you're invisible for every product-level query — the most valuable discovery searches there are. A
readable product list turns your whole case into citable answers.

## What makes a product page citable?

Readable text, organized the way customers think.

## Why describe products, not just list them?

Because description is the language of discovery. Customers ask AI for "the best gluten-free chocolate
cake near me" or "a vegan birthday cake," and a bare list of names gives the engine little to match. A
sentence describing each signature item — its style, flavor, what makes it special — gives the engine
the [extractable detail](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage) that ties you to those exact searches,
and mirrors the [reviews that name your cakes](/learn/bakery-reviews-ai-recommendations). A readable,
described product list — with a clear [custom-order page](/learn/custom-cakes-and-orders-aeo) — is the
foundation every other bakery AEO move builds on.
