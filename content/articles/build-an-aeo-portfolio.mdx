## What should an AEO portfolio include?

It should include proof, not promises — each case study showing what you did and the
measured result. The strongest items:

## How do you show results without client data?

Use what you control. Your own site, or a volunteer/portfolio project, makes a perfectly
good live case study: capture the AI answers before your work, make the changes, and
[track citation share](/learn/share-of-voice-ai-search) on a fixed prompt set so the
shift is measurable. Building and publishing a [tool](/tools) or an original study is
doubly valuable — it demonstrates skill *and* becomes a citable asset that earns you
mentions.

## Why does a portfolio beat a certificate alone?

Because a certificate signals knowledge while a portfolio proves *output*. In an emerging
field where [pay and hiring track demonstrable results](/learn/aeo-job-market-salaries),
the work you can show is the deciding factor. The two are complementary — use the
[credential](/learn/aeo-canon-certification) to get in the door and the portfolio to win
the role — but if you build only one, build the portfolio.

## How do you prove the win was yours?

Attribute honestly — it reads as more credible, not less. Show the before/after, the
exact changes you made, and the timeframe, and acknowledge the other factors at play
(freshness, competitor moves, engine volatility) rather than claiming sole credit. That
integrity is the same standard [engines reward in
content](/learn/statistics-quotes-citations-aeo): show your work.
