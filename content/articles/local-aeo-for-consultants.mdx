## What makes a consultant 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about who you are, where you're based, and who you serve. An answer engine recommends an
expert for "near me" or "consultant for [industry]" only when it can [place you with
certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — right area, right kind of client. That
certainty comes from signals that all agree: matching name and contact details, a complete profile, a
stated service area (local and remote), named industries, and reviews. When the signals agree, you're an
easy recommendation; when they conflict, the engine favors a clearer competitor.

## How do I win 'near me' even if I work remotely?

Build location and service certainty deliberately.

This is the [near-me ranking](/learn/how-to-rank-in-ai-for-near-me-searches) discipline applied to the
profession — and it's also where [service-area and industry pages](/learn/local-service-area-pages-for-ai)
earn their keep.

## Why does listing consistency matter so much?

Because a mismatch is a doubt, and engines route around doubt. If your firm name differs between your
site and your profiles, or an old number still shows in a directory, the engine can't be sure which
expert you are or where you operate — so it favors a competitor it can [place with
confidence](/learn/nap-consistency). For consultants who've rebranded, gone solo, or shifted to remote
work, this is a common and quiet leak. Clean it up and you remove the single most fixable reason an
engine skips you on a near-me or niche query.
