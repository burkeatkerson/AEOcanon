**The AEO Canon is an eight-pillar framework for making your content the source
AI answer engines read, trust, and quote.** It organizes everything that
governs AI citation into three layers — Foundation, Reputation, and Momentum —
so you can diagnose exactly why an engine recommends a competitor instead of
you, and fix it in the right order.

## Why AEO is its own discipline

For three decades, the defining question of the web was _can users find you in
search results?_ The answer was a click. That question has been joined by a new
one: _does the AI cite you when it answers?_

When an answer engine responds, it does not hand the user a menu of ten links.
It returns a single synthesized answer, assembled from the handful of sources it
trusts most. The cited source receives the visibility, the credibility, and the
click. Every uncited source receives nothing.

<Callout variant="definition" title="Answer Engine Optimization (AEO)">
  <p>
    The practice of structuring content so AI answer engines — ChatGPT,
    Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Gemini, and Claude — can extract, trust,
    and cite it as a direct answer.
  </p>
</Callout>

This is not a reason to abandon SEO. AEO _extends_ it. The same clean semantics,
authority, and quality that help a search engine rank you also help a model
quote you. But there is a genuine, measurable divergence — retrieval at the
**passage** level, **mentions** over links, **conversational** queries,
aggressive **freshness** weighting, and **per-engine** divergence (engines
overlap on only about 11% of their citations). The Canon is the map of that new
territory.

## The eight pillars

### 1. Access — can a crawler read your page at all?

If a machine cannot read your page, it cannot quote you. AI crawlers fetch raw
HTML and execute essentially no JavaScript, so client-rendered content is
invisible to them. Access is a binary gate: you pass or you fail.
[Read the Access pillar →](/learn/aeo-pillar-access)

### 2. Alignment — are you answering the real question?

You can be the best answer to a question nobody is asking AI. Search queries
were shorthand; AI queries are full conversations. Alignment means writing for
the actual question, and using that question as your heading.
[Read the Alignment pillar →](/learn/aeo-pillar-alignment)

### 3. Extractability — is your answer easy to lift?

Answer engines cite passages, not pages. Extractability is writing the sentence
you want quoted and putting it first — the inverted pyramid, applied to AI.
[Read the Extractability pillar →](/learn/aeo-pillar-extractability)

### 4. Authority — does the web vouch for you?

AI trusts what the web already trusts, and it weighs **mentions** more heavily
than backlinks. Authority is earned off your own site, in the places engines
already read.
[Read the Authority pillar →](/learn/aeo-pillar-authority)

### 5. Credibility — do you show your work?

Statistics, named quotations, and cited sources measurably raise AI visibility.
Credibility is the difference between a claim and a source.
[Read the Credibility pillar →](/learn/aeo-pillar-credibility)

### 6. Originality — are you the primary source?

Generic content is now infinite; original data exists in exactly one place, so
the engine cites that place. Originality is the one advantage that cannot be
copied.
[Read the Originality pillar →](/learn/aeo-pillar-originality)

### 7. Freshness — is your content current?

Answer engines prefer the recent and read undated pages as expired. Freshness is
substantive updating, shown with a visible date.
[Read the Freshness pillar →](/learn/aeo-pillar-freshness)

### 8. Adaptability — can your system bend as engines change?

The engines change monthly; a tactic that worked last quarter may not work now.
Adaptability is measuring share of voice per engine and treating every tactic as
a hypothesis.
[Read the Adaptability pillar →](/learn/aeo-pillar-adaptability)

## How to use the Canon: a cascade, not a checklist

The most common mistake is to treat the pillars as a list to tick off in any
order. They are sequential. A brilliantly written, perfectly credible page sits
behind a blocked crawler and earns nothing. A fast, readable page that answers
the wrong question earns nothing either.

Walk down the pillars in order. The **first pillar where you break is the place
to work.** Fix it, then move to the next. This is what makes the Canon a
diagnostic and not just a philosophy.
