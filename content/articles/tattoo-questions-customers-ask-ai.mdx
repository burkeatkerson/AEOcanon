**Clients ask AI tattoo questions in four buckets — cost ('how much does a tattoo cost', 'hourly rate'),
process ('how do deposits and booking work', 'do you take walk-ins'), style and fit ('fine-line artist
near me'), and aftercare ('how do I care for a new tattoo').** Mapping each to readable content that
answers it is the core of a tattoo AEO content plan.

## How do I find the exact questions?

Listen where clients already ask. Note what people ask at consultations and the front desk, read your
reviews and the questions on your Google profile, scan local tattoo and city forums and groups, and
[prompt the assistants directly](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask) on your styles and area to
see the follow-ups they surface. Capture the *natural wording* — "who does fine-line near me that's good
with first tattoos" beats "delicate linework practitioner" — because engines match the client's phrasing.
Then prioritize by what genuinely fits your studio and artists.

## Should I publish pricing if competitors hide it?

Yes — and it's an edge, not a risk. Clients constantly ask AI "how much does a tattoo cost near me," and
a clear hourly rate, minimum, or range in readable text lets the engine recommend you for those
high-intent cost searches — while shops that bury pricing get skipped. Transparency also reassures
someone nervous about a permanent, often pricey decision before they ever visit. It's pure
[Alignment](/pillars/alignment): the real question, answered where the engine and the client can read it.
Map every bucket to readable content and you've built the plan that gets a studio cited.
