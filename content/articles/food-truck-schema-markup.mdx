## What does food truck schema actually do?

It makes your mobile-food details unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness and FoodEstablishment
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business) label your
name, contact, service area, hours, cuisine, price range, and menu so engines parse them cleanly rather
than guessing — reinforcing the [consistent identity](/learn/nap-consistency) and service-area certainty
local recommendations depend on. It's the [structured data for AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo)
pattern applied to a moving business: clarity for the parser, on top of a menu and schedule that are
already clear and readable for the customer.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your truck — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your details machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on a [readable menu and live
schedule](/learn/food-truck-menu-pages-for-ai) the crawler can actually parse, a [current
location](/learn/local-aeo-for-food-trucks), and [genuine
reviews](/learn/food-truck-reviews-ai-recommendations). Schema can't rescue a menu trapped as an image or
a location that's stale — and faking reviews or details in markup is a misuse engines can
[detect](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations). Accurate schema on top of a real, readable menu and a
fresh schedule is the combination that works.
