**Your site usually isn't cited by AI because of one broken gate — AI crawlers
can't read it, your answer is buried, or the web doesn't vouch for you — not
because of vague "low quality."** AEO failures are specific and locatable, and the
fix is to diagnose top-down and repair the highest break first.

## What's the most common reason AI doesn't cite a site?

The most common reason is that AI crawlers literally can't read the page. Most AI
crawlers fetch raw HTML and don't run JavaScript —
[Vercel found GPTBot executed zero JavaScript across more than 500 million
requests](https://vercel.com/blog/the-rise-of-the-ai-crawler). If your content is
assembled [client-side](/glossary/client-side-rendering), the crawler sees an empty shell, and nothing downstream can
save you. Serving [server-rendered](/glossary/ssr) or static HTML, and allowing AI crawlers in
[robots.txt](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/robots/intro), is the precondition for everything else — the
[Access pillar](/pillars/access).

## Why do I rank in Google but get ignored by AI?

If you rank but AI ignores you, your foundation is fine and the missing piece is
the extraction-and-trust layer. Google can read and rank a page whose best answer
is buried; an answer engine can't lift it. Likewise, links may earn you rankings
while thin off-site *mentions* leave you under-trusted for citation. The fix is
answer-first rewriting plus genuine [brand mentions](/glossary/branded-mention) — not more of what already got
you ranked.

## What should I fix first?

Fix the highest broken gate, then re-check — don't spread effort across all of
them. If access is broken, no amount of great content reaches an engine; if the
answer is buried, no amount of authority gets it quoted. One decisive fix at the
binding constraint beats ten diffuse tweaks. Start from
[what is AEO](/learn/what-is-aeo) for the full model.
