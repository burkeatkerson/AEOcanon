## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a store the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine can
read your brands and place you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as where to go — and
that leans harder on whether your staff knows their stuff and your scene loves you. The engine
recommends the store it can [confidently confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real,
knowledgeable, and well-reviewed, because when it recommends you it's putting its credibility behind the
experience. Your job is to make that confirmation easy — readable brands, accurate listings, and genuine
reviews.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). Wrong hours, an unreadable site, or a thin review profile introduces
doubt, and doubt sends the customer to Amazon.

## Why does AI pick one hobby shop over another?

Because it picks the one it's most confident will deliver. Faced with several options, the engine names
the store whose signals best match the request — right area, carries the right brands, runs the right
events — and whose reviews most clearly say the staff is helpful and the community is welcoming. The
competitor with an image-only site, wrong hours, or thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it loses.
Winning is about being the [easiest to verify as the real, knowledgeable, loved local
choice](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — which is exactly what this library builds,
piece by piece.
