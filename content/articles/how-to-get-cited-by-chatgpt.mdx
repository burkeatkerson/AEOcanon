**To get cited by ChatGPT, write the answer to a specific question in a
self-contained opening sentence, back it with inline evidence, make the page
crawlable, and earn off-site authority — because ChatGPT quotes passages, not
pages.** Being cited is the product of surviving its retrieve-rerank-cite
pipeline, and you optimize for each stage.

    passage that best, most credibly answers the question.

## What does ChatGPT actually cite?

ChatGPT cites the specific passage it used to answer — not your whole page and not
your domain. When it searches the web, it retrieves candidate passages, reranks
them for the exact query, and attributes the sources of the few it draws on. That
means a single excellent paragraph can earn a citation even on an otherwise
ordinary page, and a great page can be skipped if its best answer is buried. We
break this down in
[how AI engines choose what to cite](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations).

## Where on the page should the answer go?

Put the answer first — in the opening sentence under a question-shaped heading.
[Profound's analysis found 44% of ChatGPT citations come from the first third of
a page](/pillars/extractability), so a buried answer rarely gets lifted.
Lead with a complete, quotable statement, then support it. This is the discipline
of [extractability](/pillars/extractability): write the sentence you want
ChatGPT to quote, and put it at the top.

## How do I make the answer credible enough to quote?

Back every important claim with a specific statistic, a named source, or a direct
quotation, in the same passage. A grounded model prefers low-risk material it can
repeat safely, so an evidenced sentence is easier to cite than an unsupported one.
The [Princeton GEO study (arXiv 2311.09735)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.09735)
confirmed this — adding citations, quotations, and statistics were the
top-performing tactics for raising a source's visibility in AI answers. This is
[credibility](/pillars/credibility) — showing your work — and it's why
concrete, sourced answers outperform confident-but-vague ones.

## Why won't ChatGPT cite me even though I rank?

If you rank in Google but ChatGPT ignores you, the usual cause is that your best
answer isn't extractable, isn't reachable, or your source isn't trusted off-site.
ChatGPT can't cite a page it can't crawl (serve real HTML, allow its crawler), it
won't lift an answer that's buried, and it favors sources the wider web vouches
for. Walk the [AEO Canon diagnostic](/learn/aeo-canon-diagnostic) to find your
first broken gate.

## What should I do first?

Start by rewriting your most important page answer-first: open each section with a
complete answer, add one inline stat or source, and confirm ChatGPT's crawler can
read the rendered HTML. Then build genuine off-site mentions so your source is
trusted on competitive questions. That sequence — extractable, evidenced,
reachable, trusted — is the core of [what is AEO](/learn/what-is-aeo).
