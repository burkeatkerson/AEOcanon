## Why does Google Business Profile matter for AI search?

Because it's a structured, trusted confirmation of where you are, what you do, and which areas you serve —
exactly what an engine needs to recommend a moving company. When the assistant decides whom to name for a
near-me or route query, it leans on the profile to verify your location, service area, services, and
reputation [before recommending you](/learn/does-google-business-profile-help-ai-search). A complete,
accurate profile makes you an easy recommendation; a vague service area or thin profile leaves the engine
unsure — and it names a competitor it can confidently place.

The reviews on your profile do double duty — they're a top input to [whether AI recommends
you](/learn/moving-company-reviews-ai-recommendations) at all.

## Does GBP replace a good website?

No — it's half the picture. The profile is a powerful placement and service-area signal, but engines also
retrieve and cite your own [service pages](/learn/moving-company-service-pages-for-ai) for the detailed
questions a profile can't answer — what a 2-bedroom move costs, your long-distance process, how binding
estimates work. You need both: a complete, accurate profile so the engine can place you and confirm your
service area, and answer-first pages for the substance that wins the detailed query. Treat them as one
system, kept [consistent everywhere](/learn/nap-consistency).
