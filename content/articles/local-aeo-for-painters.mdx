## What makes a painter 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about who you are and where you work. An answer engine recommends a local contractor only
when it can [place you with certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — right
business, right area. That certainty comes from signals that all agree: matching name-address-phone, a
complete profile, a defined service area, and pages that name real places. When the signals agree,
you're an easy recommendation; when they conflict, the engine favors a clearer competitor.

## How do I win 'near me' in AI answers?

Build location certainty deliberately.

This is the [near-me ranking](/learn/how-to-rank-in-ai-for-near-me-searches) discipline applied to the
trade — and it's also where [service-area pages](/learn/local-service-area-pages-for-ai) earn their keep
for painters covering a wide metro.

## Why does NAP consistency matter so much?

Because a mismatch is a doubt, and engines route around doubt. If your phone number differs between your
site and your Google profile, or an old address still shows in a directory, the engine can't be sure
which contractor you are or where you operate — so it favors a competitor it can [place with
confidence](/learn/nap-consistency). For painters who operate from home, change numbers, or rebrand,
this is a common and quiet leak. Clean it up and you remove the single most fixable reason an engine
skips you on a near-me query.
