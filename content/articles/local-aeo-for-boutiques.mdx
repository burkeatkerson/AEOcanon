accurate hours, and a stated niche and price range.** Engines recommend the shop they can confidently
place and confirm fits the request.

## What makes a boutique 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about where you are and what you carry. An answer engine recommends a boutique for "near me"
or a style query only when it can [place you with certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend)
— right area, carries the right styles, in the shopper's size and budget. That certainty comes from
signals that all agree: matching name-address-phone, a complete profile, a stated niche, readable price
ranges, and reviews. When the signals agree, you're an easy recommendation; when your styles are vague
or buried in a lookbook image, the engine favors a shop that made it clear.

This is the [near-me ranking](/learn/how-to-rank-in-ai-for-near-me-searches) discipline applied to
clothing — and a clear, readable niche is where most shops quietly lose.

## Why does my niche matter so much?

Because most boutique searches are really about a specific style or fit — "plus-size boutique near me,"
"vintage clothing shop nearby," "sustainable fashion in [town]." If your niche and styles aren't clear,
accurate, and in [readable text](/learn/boutique-product-pages-for-ai), the engine can't confirm you
carry what the shopper wants, so it recommends a shop that made it obvious. State exactly what you
specialize in and stock, keep it consistent with your [listings](/learn/nap-consistency), and you
remove the most common reason a boutique gets skipped on a style query.
