## What's different about a personal brand?

What's different is that the brand *is a person*, so the [entity](/glossary/entity) AI
has to recognize and trust is you — not a company. That makes two things central: a
clear, consistent *identity* (so AI can connect your scattered mentions into one
person) and genuine *originality and expertise* (so you're worth citing rather than
interchangeable). It's the [author-authority](/learn/author-authority) and
[entity](/learn/build-your-entity) work pointed at an individual: make yourself
machine-knowable (with [Person schema](https://schema.org/Person) and
[sameAs](https://schema.org/sameAs) links across your profiles), and be the
credentialed person with something only you could say.

## Where this fits in the Canon

A personal brand is the [AEO Canon](/learn/aeo-canon) applied to a person —
[originality](/pillars/originality) (your POV), [authority](/pillars/authority) and
[credibility](/pillars/credibility) (recognized expertise). It builds directly on
[author authority](/learn/author-authority) and [building your
entity](/learn/build-your-entity); for a one-person operation, pair it with [AEO
without a marketing team](/learn/aeo-without-a-marketing-team).
