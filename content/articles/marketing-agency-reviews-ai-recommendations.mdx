## Why do reviews carry so much weight for agencies?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge trust — and a buyer is betting their
marketing budget on you. An assistant deciding whom to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google, Clutch, and review-platform
reviews to gauge whether you're effective, responsive, and trustworthy. For a business built on
delivering measurable results, that corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility
pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks)
that correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks do.

## How many reviews do I need?

Fewer than you think, fresher than you have. There's no magic count — consistency and recency outweigh a
raw total. A steady stream of genuine recent reviews signals an active, trusted agency far better than a
big pile from three years ago. The goal is an ongoing flow: ask happy clients at natural milestones — a
campaign win, a quarterly review, a renewal — so new reviews keep arriving and the engine keeps seeing
an agency that's [currently trusted](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations), not formerly busy.

## Do the words in a review matter?

Yes — specific reviews do more work than generic praise.

Reviews that name what you did — doubled the leads, cut the acquisition cost, ranked the key terms —
give engines specific detail tying you to those outcomes. They reinforce the [questions buyers
ask](/learn/marketing-agency-questions-customers-ask-ai) and echo your published [case
studies](/learn/agency-case-studies-and-results-aeo), so a handful of detailed reviews can outperform a
wall of generic ones. Ask clients what to mention, and the reviews start doing your AEO for you.
