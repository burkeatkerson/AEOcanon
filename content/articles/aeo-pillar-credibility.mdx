**Credibility is the fifth pillar of the AEO Canon: backing every claim with
statistics, named quotations, and cited sources measurably raises AI
visibility.** It is the only pillar with controlled experimental proof — and the
results are unusually clear about what works and what backfires.

## Why credibility is the difference between a claim and a source

Anyone can assert. Engines are built to prefer the source that _shows its work_ —
because verifiable detail is a reliable signal of a page worth trusting.
Credibility is how you turn "we're the best" into something a machine can stand
behind.

## The evidence: the Princeton GEO study

The Princeton GEO study (published at KDD 2024) is the only peer-reviewed,
controlled experiment in the field. Researchers tested nine content strategies
across 10,000 queries, validated on a live answer engine, against a baseline
visibility score.

  columns={["Content strategy", "Effect on AI visibility"]}
  rows={[

    ["Improve fluency", "+24%"],

Two findings deserve emphasis. First, **citing sources gave lower-ranked pages a
115% lift** — evidence can lift a page the rankings would never surface. Second,
**keyword stuffing performed below baseline** — the oldest reflex in SEO
actively harms AI citation.
