**Yes — each section should make sense lifted out of the page, because that's exactly
what an answer engine does when it quotes you.** Self-contained passages that don't
rely on "as mentioned above" or undefined pronouns are far more citable, since the
engine rarely shows the surrounding context.

## Why does standalone structure matter?

Because the engine shows the fragment, not the page. When you're cited, the answer
engine typically surfaces a single [lifted
passage](/learn/passage-length-for-ai-citation) with none of the surrounding
paragraphs, so a passage whose meaning depends on three earlier sections either can't
be used or gets quoted incompletely. Writing each section to stand on its own is the
practical core of [extractability](/pillars/extractability) — it's what makes your
content liftable in the first place.

## What does a self-contained passage look like?

It names its own subject and delivers the full answer. Instead of "It also helps with
this," write "Server-side rendering also helps AI crawlers read your page" — the
passage states the thing and answers the question without borrowing from its
neighbors. Lead each one with [the answer](/learn/answer-first-sentence) and assume
the reader arrived with no prior context, because as far as the engine is concerned,
they often did — and [human readers scan rather than read in
order](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/how-users-read-on-the-web/) too, so
self-contained passages serve both.

## Doesn't that mean repeating myself?

Only enough to stand alone. Re-state the subject and any key term a passage relies on,
but don't duplicate entire explanations across the page — that tips into
[thin, repetitive content](/learn/should-i-use-faq-sections-for-aeo). The target is
*independent understandability*, which usually costs a short clause or two of context,
not a full rehash. A passage should be complete, not redundant.
