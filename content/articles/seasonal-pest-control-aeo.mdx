**Seasonal AEO for pest control means publishing and refreshing the answers to seasonal pest
questions — termites and ants in spring, mosquitoes and wasps in summer, rodents in fall —
before demand spikes, on durable pages you update each year.** Be the cited answer when each
season turns, not scrambling after it.

## Why is seasonality pest control's biggest AEO lever?

Because demand arrives in predictable waves, pest by pest, and the cited company catches the
whole wave. Late winter and spring bring termite swarms and ant questions; summer brings
mosquitoes, wasps, and hornets; fall brings rodents seeking warmth; winter brings spiders and
overwintering pests. The company whose answer is already published, crawled, and trusted [gets
cited the moment each spike hits](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo) — while
competitors are still writing. It's the [Freshness pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned into a
pest calendar.

## How do I prepare for each pest's season?

Build the content while it's calm, so it's ready when the surge hits.

## Should I make a new page each year?

No — update one durable page per seasonal pest. A persistent URL [accumulates
authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh it each year with current details
and pricing ranges. That beats spinning up a throwaway page annually that starts from zero and
splits your signals. Keep one 'termite season' page, one 'mosquito control' page, one 'keep
rodents out' page — and refresh each ahead of its season. One durable page, refreshed and
ready, beats scrambling after the swarm every time.
