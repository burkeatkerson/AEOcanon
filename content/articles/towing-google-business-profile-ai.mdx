## Why does Google Business Profile matter for AI search?

Because it's a structured, trusted confirmation of where you are, what you do, and whether you're available
— exactly what an engine needs to recommend a tow company to a stranded driver. When the assistant decides
whom to name for a near-me or 'available now' query, it leans on the profile to verify your location,
24-hour hours, services, and reputation [before recommending you](/learn/does-google-business-profile-help-ai-search).
A complete, accurate profile makes you an easy recommendation; wrong hours or a thin profile leaves the
engine unsure — or sends an anxious driver to a company that can't come, while you lose a full-price call.

## Does GBP replace a good website?

No — it's half the picture. The profile is a powerful placement and 'available now' signal, but engines
also retrieve and cite your own [service pages](/learn/towing-service-pages-for-ai) for the detailed
questions a profile can't answer — what a tow costs, how fast you arrive, whether you do heavy-duty or
winch-outs. You need both: a complete, accurate profile so the engine can place you and confirm you're
available, and answer-first pages for the substance that wins the call. Treat them as one system, kept
[consistent everywhere](/learn/nap-consistency).
