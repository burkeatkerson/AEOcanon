## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each job produces a review and a referral — and ideally a recurring schedule;
reviews and [answer-first content](/learn/window-gutter-service-pages-for-ai) earn citations and
rankings; those bring direct bookings; and the money saved on purchased leads funds more crews. The
lead-gen model is the opposite: a faucet you pay to keep running, selling the same lead to three crews,
that stops the moment you stop. AEO builds the [compounding asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead
— a recurring route you own.

## Why is route density central to growth?

Because a tight route of nearby recurring jobs is far more profitable than scattered one-time jobs — less
drive time, predictable revenue, and efficient crews. AEO that wins [near-me recurring
work](/learn/local-aeo-for-window-gutter) builds that density, and density plus retention is what makes a
window and gutter business durable and valuable. Every one-time job is a chance to add a recurring
schedule in an area you already serve — tightening the route and compounding the revenue.

## How do I lower my cost per job?

By replacing recurring fees with assets that keep paying. A purchased lead costs every time and is
[resold to competitors](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands); an earned citation, a recurring customer, or
a referral costs mostly upfront and pays season after season. As your cited, recurring, dense route
grows, your cost per job falls and your revenue stabilizes. And because AI names only a few companies, a
real, well-reviewed local crew can win recommendations a generic directory
[can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
