## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a place the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine can
read your offerings and place you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as where to climb or
play — and that leans harder on whether members love you. The engine recommends the gym it can
[confidently confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, well-run, and well-reviewed, because
when it recommends you it's putting its credibility behind the experience. Your job is to make that
confirmation easy — readable offerings, accurate hours, and genuine, loving reviews.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). Wrong hours, hidden offerings, or a thin review profile introduces
doubt, and doubt sends the visitor elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one climbing gym over another?

Because it picks the one it's most confident members will love. Faced with several options, the engine
names the gym whose signals best match the request — right kind of place, right area — and whose
reviews most clearly say people love it. The competitor with widget-hidden offerings, wrong hours, or
thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it loses. Winning is about being the [easiest to verify as the
loved local choice](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — which is exactly what this
library builds, piece by piece.
