## Why is 'near me' the highest-value restaurant search?

Because it's the moment of decision, and it converts immediately. When someone asks "best tacos near
me" or "good restaurant open now," they're choosing where to eat in the next hour — and the [AI answer
names only a few places](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Pew Research [found people click a
link just 8% of the time when an AI summary
appears](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer *is* the table. For a restaurant, owning that moment is the difference between a full
dining room and an empty one.

## What do diners ask AI when choosing where to eat?

In-the-moment, specific questions — and you should own the ones that fit your restaurant.

and you become the obvious answer to the queries your ideal diner asks.

## Why is 'open now' the make-or-break signal?

Because a huge share of dining searches are in-the-moment, and the engine answers 'open now' straight
from your hours. Accurate, consistent hours mean you get recommended when a hungry diner is deciding;
wrong or inconsistent hours mean you're dropped from the answer — or you send someone to a closed door
and earn a bad review. Keeping hours current everywhere, including holidays, is the highest-leverage
move for winning the in-the-moment table, and it's the same [local
certainty](/learn/local-aeo-for-restaurants) that wins every near-me query.
