## Why do reviews carry so much weight for auto glass shops?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge trust — and a windshield is a safety part
drivers don't want done badly. An assistant deciding whom to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform reviews to
gauge whether you're careful, fair, and reliable. For a trade where a poor install leaks or compromises
ADAS, that corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made
visible, and the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more
than backlinks do.

## How many reviews do I need?

Fewer than you think, fresher than you have. There's no magic count — consistency and recency outweigh a
raw total. A steady stream of genuine recent reviews signals an active, trusted shop far better than a big
pile from three years ago. The goal is an ongoing flow: ask happy customers after each install, so new
reviews keep arriving and the engine keeps seeing a shop that's [currently
trusted](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations), not formerly busy.

## Do the words in a review matter?

Yes — specific reviews do more work than generic praise.

Reviews that name what you did — same-day ADAS recalibration, an insurance claim handled, a mobile install
done on time — give engines specific detail tying you to careful, convenient work. They reinforce the
[questions drivers ask](/learn/auto-glass-questions-customers-ask-ai), so a handful of detailed reviews can
outperform a wall of generic ones. Ask customers what to mention, and the reviews start doing your AEO for
you.
