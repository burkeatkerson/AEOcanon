## Why does the profile carry so much weight?

Because it's structured, trusted, and exactly what an engine needs to place a local
business. A Google Business Profile states your identity, service area, hours, services,
and reviews in a form engines can read directly — so it's a fast, high-confidence way
to confirm you're a real plumber operating where the searcher is. For
[near-me and "open now" questions](/learn/emergency-plumber-ai-search), that confirmation
is often what decides who gets named. It's the [Authority](/pillars/authority) and
[local placement](/learn/local-aeo-for-plumbers) signal in one.

## What should a plumber's profile include?

Everything an engine would want to confirm. Accurate name, address, and phone that
[match your site exactly](/learn/nap-consistency); correct hours including any
emergency availability; a defined service area; your full service list; photos; and a
steady flow of [genuine reviews](/learn/plumber-reviews-ai-recommendations).
Completeness and accuracy matter more than anything clever — gaps and conflicts create
the doubt that drops you from the answer.

## Does the profile replace a good website?

No — it's half the picture. The profile wins you *placement and trust*, but engines
still retrieve and cite your [actual pages](/learn/plumbing-service-pages-for-ai) for
detailed questions about cost, process, and specific services the profile can't hold.
A complete profile with a thin website leaves the substantive questions to a
competitor; a strong website with a neglected profile struggles to get placed. You
need both, working together — which is the whole point of running
[AEO and SEO as one program](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).

## How does it connect to citations?

By removing doubt. A profile whose details match your site and other listings
corroborates your identity and location, so an engine can confidently name you for
local plumbing questions. Inconsistency does the opposite — it makes you the riskier
choice. Keeping the profile complete, accurate, and consistent is foundational local
AEO, and it's part of the [done-for-you rebuild](/pricing) that includes a Google
Business Profile cleanup alongside the site.
