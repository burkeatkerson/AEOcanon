one focused topic — establish a clean entity identity, earn genuine mentions where
your audience and the engines already look, and publish original, evidenced
answers.** Start narrow, be consistently described, and let topical depth compound.

## Where do I actually start?

With your entity. Before mentions can build trust, engines need to [recognize who you
are](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) — so set a consistent name and description
across your site and profiles, add [Organization or Person
schema](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo), and connect your official pages with a
[sameAs trail](/learn/sameas-strategy). A clear [entity](/learn/what-is-entity-aeo) is
the anchor every later mention attaches to; without it, corroboration has nothing to
accumulate against.

## How do I earn the mentions?

Go where trust is already concentrated. Earn [genuine
mentions](/learn/get-brand-mentions-without-links) from credible, on-topic sources —
expert commentary, useful contributions in [communities engines
cite](/learn/reddit-ai-citations), and coverage that corroborates what you claim.
Because [mentions matter more than backlinks](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) for AI
visibility, the goal is to be *described* consistently by reputable sources, linked
or not. Pair that with original, evidenced content so people have a reason to cite
you.

## Why narrow, and how long?

Narrow because [authority is topical](/pillars/authority), and patient because it
depends on others. Being consistently described as expert in one specific area earns
trust faster than spreading thin, so win citation in a focused niche before expanding
to adjacent topics. Expect it to take months — on-page work lands in weeks, but the
[off-site corroboration engines trust](/learn/how-long-does-aeo-take) builds slowly
because other people have to do the corroborating.
