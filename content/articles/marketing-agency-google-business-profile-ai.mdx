## Why does Google Business Profile matter for AI search?

Because it's a structured, trusted confirmation of who and where you are — exactly what an engine needs
to recommend a firm. When the assistant decides whom to name for a near-me or 'agency for [industry]'
query, it leans on the profile to verify your identity, service area, services, and reputation [before
recommending you](/learn/does-google-business-profile-help-ai-search). A complete, accurate profile
makes you an easy recommendation; a thin or stale one leaves the engine unsure and routes the inquiry
elsewhere.

## What should my profile include?

Everything an engine checks before vouching for you.

The reviews on your profile do double duty — they're a top input to [whether AI recommends
you](/learn/marketing-agency-reviews-ai-recommendations) at all.

## Does GBP replace a good website?

No — it's half the picture. The profile is a powerful placement and trust signal, but engines also
retrieve and cite your [actual service pages](/learn/marketing-agency-service-pages-for-ai) and [case
studies](/learn/agency-case-studies-and-results-aeo) for the detailed questions a profile can't answer —
what a retainer costs, whether you serve SaaS or e-commerce, what results you've driven. You need both:
a complete profile so the engine can confidently place and trust you, and answer-first pages for the
substance that wins the detailed query. Treat them as one system, kept [consistent
everywhere](/learn/nap-consistency).
