## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each detail produces a review and a referral — and ideally a maintenance
membership; reviews and [answer-first content](/learn/auto-detailing-service-pages-for-ai) earn citations
and rankings; those bring direct bookings; and the money saved on purchased leads funds more capacity.
The lead-gen model is the opposite: a faucet you pay to keep running, selling the same lead to three
shops, that stops the moment you stop. AEO builds the [compounding asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo)
instead — full-margin, repeat demand you own.

## How do I lower my cost per booking?

By replacing recurring fees with assets that keep paying. A purchased lead costs every time and is
[resold to competitors](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands); an earned citation, a member, or a referral
costs mostly upfront and pays for years. As your cited, membership-backed base grows, your cost per
booking falls and your margins improve — and because AI names only a few shops, a real, well-reviewed
local detailer can win recommendations a generic directory
[can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
