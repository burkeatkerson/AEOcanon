## What makes a concrete contractor 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about who you are and where you work. An answer engine recommends a local contractor only
when it can [place you with certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — right
business, right area. That certainty comes from signals that all agree: matching name-address-phone,
a complete profile, a defined service area, and pages that name real places. When the signals agree,
you're an easy, safe recommendation; when they conflict, the engine hedges toward someone clearer.

## How do I win 'near me' in AI answers?

Build location certainty deliberately.

This is the [near-me ranking](/learn/how-to-rank-in-ai-for-near-me-searches) discipline applied to
the trade — and it's also where [service-area pages](/learn/local-service-area-pages-for-ai) earn
their keep.

## Why does NAP consistency matter so much?

Because a mismatch is a doubt, and engines route around doubt. If your phone number differs between
your site and your Google profile, or an old address still shows in a directory, the engine can't be
sure which business you are or where you operate — so it favors a competitor it can [place with
confidence](/learn/nap-consistency). For concrete contractors who work across a wide metro from one
yard, or have rebranded, this is a common and quiet leak. Clean it up and you remove the single most
fixable reason an engine skips you on a near-me query.
