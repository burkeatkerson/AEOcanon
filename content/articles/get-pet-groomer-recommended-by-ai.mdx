## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a place the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine can
read your services and place you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as where to take a
pet — and that leans harder on whether pet parents love you. The engine recommends the groomer it can
[confidently confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, skilled, and well-reviewed, because
when it recommends you it's putting its credibility behind the care. Your job is to make that
confirmation easy — readable services, accurate hours, and genuine, loving reviews.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). Wrong hours, hidden services, or a thin review profile introduces
doubt, and doubt sends the pet parent elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one groomer over another?

Because it picks the one it's most confident pet parents will love. Faced with several options, the
engine names the groomer whose signals best match the request — right services, right area — and whose
reviews most clearly say people trust them with their dogs. The competitor with widget-hidden services,
wrong hours, or thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it loses. Winning is about being the [easiest to
verify as the loved local choice](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — which is exactly
what this library builds, piece by piece.
