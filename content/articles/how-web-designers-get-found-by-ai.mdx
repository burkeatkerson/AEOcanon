**Web designers get found by AI search when their site is crawlable, answers the real questions owners
ask, and is backed by a real portfolio and genuine reviews.** The AI names only a few studios, so the
designer who clears all three is the one recommended instead of a marketplace bid.

## What does an AI engine actually do to find you?

It retrieves, ranks, and names. When an owner asks who should build their site, the engine pulls the
studios it can read, judges which best answer the question and look trustworthy, and [names just a
few](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Each step is a filter: if a crawler can't fetch your
page, you're out before ranking; if your page doesn't answer the cost or timeline question, you're out
at ranking; if your trust signals are thin, a competitor gets named. Being found means clearing every
filter.

## What are the three gates?

The same three an engine — and an owner — apply, in order.

## Why can't clients find my web design business?

Almost always one specific broken gate — not bad luck. Your portfolio renders only in the browser so
[crawlers see an empty shell](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), your pages dodge the cost and
timeline questions, or your reviews and listings are inconsistent. The fix is diagnostic: find the
*highest* broken gate and repair it first, because a great reputation can't rescue a site bots can't
read, and a fast site can't rescue a page that never answers the question. This is the same
[why-isn't-my-business-showing-up](/learn/why-isnt-my-local-business-showing-up-in-ai) logic, applied to
a studio.

## Does ranking in Google mean AI can find me?

Not on its own. Ranking proves Google can read and rank you — a good sign for the first gate — but AI
citation also needs an [extractable, answer-first page](/learn/answer-first-sentence) and trusted
signals like a real portfolio and reviews. A studio can rank yet be absent from AI answers if the answer
is buried or trust is thin. The overlap is real but not total: clear all three gates and you get [found
in both](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).
