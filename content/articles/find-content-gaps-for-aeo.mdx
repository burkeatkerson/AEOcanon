the questions that matter — run your priority prompts across engines, note where
competitors are named and you aren't, and cross-check coverage against the real
questions your audience asks.** The gaps are your highest-leverage backlog.

## What counts as a gap in AEO?

A question you should own but don't. Specifically, it's a query that triggers AI
answers in your space where [competitors get cited and you
don't](/learn/track-competitor-ai-citations) — a gap between demand (the engine is
answering the question) and your presence (you're not the source). Unlike a keyword
gap, it's measured in *citations*, not rankings, so you find it by testing real
prompts rather than comparing keyword lists.

## How do I find them?

Test the questions and watch who wins. Run your priority questions — drawn from your
[prompt set and the real questions people
ask](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask) — across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and
Google AI Mode, and log which ones name competitors but not you. Cross-check those
against your existing coverage. Where a question matters, a rival is cited, and you're
absent or thin, you've found a gap. This is [alignment](/pillars/alignment) measured
against the live answer surface.

## How do I prioritize what to close?

By value and winnability. A high-intent question near a buying decision, currently
held by a beatable competitor, is worth far more than a low-intent question dominated
by an entrenched authority. Rank your gaps accordingly and close the high-leverage
ones with a better, more [answer-first](/learn/answer-first-sentence), evidenced
treatment. Feed the prioritized list into your [Q&A production
pipeline](/learn/scale-a-qa-library) so the gaps become published answers.
