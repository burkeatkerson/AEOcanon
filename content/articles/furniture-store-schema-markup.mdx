## What does furniture-store schema actually do?

It makes your store's details unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business), and the
FurnitureStore subtype, labels your name, address, hours, area served, and reviews so engines parse them
cleanly rather than guessing — while Product schema labels each item's material, dimensions, and price.
It reinforces the [consistent identity](/learn/nap-consistency) and delivery area local recommendations
depend on. It's the [structured data for AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern applied to the
trade: clarity for the parser, on top of products and delivery info that are already clear and readable
for the shopper.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your store and products — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your details machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [readable products and delivery
info](/learn/furniture-product-pages-for-ai) the crawler can parse, [accurate hours and delivery
info](/learn/local-aeo-for-furniture-stores), and [genuine
reviews](/learn/furniture-store-reviews-ai-recommendations). Schema can't rescue an image-only catalog
or wrong hours — and faking reviews or prices in markup is a misuse engines can
[detect](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations). Accurate schema on top of a readable, real site is the
combination that works.
