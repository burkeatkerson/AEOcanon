detailer who clears all three is recommended instead of a resold lead.

## What does an AI engine actually do to find you?

It retrieves, ranks, and names. When an owner asks for detailing, the engine pulls the shops it can
read, judges which best answer the question and look trustworthy, and [names just a
few](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Each step is a filter: if a crawler can't fetch your page,
you're out before ranking; if your page doesn't answer the cost or package question, you're out at
ranking; if your reviews are thin, a competitor gets named. Being found means clearing every filter.

## What are the three gates?

The same three an engine — and an owner — apply, in order.

## Why can't customers find my detailing business?

Almost always one specific broken gate — and for detailers it's often the missing real website. A great
Instagram is brand-building, but [AI engines mostly read crawlable web
pages](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), not your feed — so without a real site that states your
packages and pricing, the engine has little to cite. Other common breaks: inconsistent listings, or
thin reviews. The fix is diagnostic — find the *highest* broken gate and repair it first. For most
detailers, building a fast, readable site is the highest-leverage move.

## Does being big on Instagram help me get found by AI?

It helps your brand and your reviews, but it doesn't replace a crawlable site. Engines read the open
web to answer "best car detailing near me," and a feed of photos gives them little structured to lift.
Pair your social proof with a real site that [answers the
questions](/learn/auto-detailing-service-pages-for-ai) in readable text, and you turn followers into
citations. Ranking and a Google listing help, but AI citation also needs that extractable,
[answer-first page](/learn/answer-first-sentence) and genuine reviews — clear all three gates and you're
[found in both](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).
