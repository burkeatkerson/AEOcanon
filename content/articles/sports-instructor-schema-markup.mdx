## What does coaching schema actually do?

It makes your instruction details unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business), and the
SportsActivityLocation subtype, labels your name, address, hours, lessons, and reviews so engines parse
them cleanly rather than guessing — reinforcing the [consistent identity](/learn/nap-consistency) local
recommendations depend on. It's the [structured data for AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern
applied to coaching: clarity for the parser, on top of lessons and pricing that are already clear and
readable for the student.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your coaching business — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your details machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [readable lessons and
pricing](/learn/sports-instructor-service-pages-for-ai) the crawler can parse, [accurate
hours](/learn/local-aeo-for-sports-instructors), and [genuine
reviews](/learn/sports-instructor-reviews-ai-recommendations). Schema can't rescue a lesson menu trapped
in a booking widget or hours that are wrong — and faking reviews or details in markup is a misuse
engines can [detect](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations). Accurate schema on top of readable lessons
and pricing is the combination that works.
