**Print and sign shops get found by AI search when their site is crawlable, answers the real questions
buyers ask, and is backed by consistent listings and genuine reviews.** The AI names only a few shops,
so the one that clears all three gates is recommended instead of the job going to an online printer.

## What does an AI engine actually do to find you?

It retrieves, ranks, and names. When a buyer asks where to get cards, a banner, or a sign, the engine
pulls the shops it can read, judges which best answer the question and look trustworthy, and [names
just a few](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Each step is a filter: if a crawler can't fetch
your page — or your page is all images with no text — you're out before ranking; if your page doesn't
answer the pricing or turnaround question, you're out at ranking; if your trust signals are thin, a
competitor gets named. Being found means clearing every filter.

## What are the three gates?

The same three an engine — and a buyer — apply, in order.

## Why can't customers find my print shop?

Almost always one specific broken gate — not bad luck. Your site is an image-heavy gallery so [crawlers
see little text](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), your pages dodge the pricing and turnaround
questions, or your reviews and listings are inconsistent. The fix is diagnostic: find the *highest*
broken gate and repair it first, because great reviews can't rescue a site bots can't read, and a fast
site can't rescue a page that never answers the question. This is the same
[why-isn't-my-business-showing-up](/learn/why-isnt-my-local-business-showing-up-in-ai) logic, applied
to the trade.

## Does ranking in Google mean AI can find me?

Not on its own. Ranking proves Google can read and rank you — a good sign for the first gate — but AI
citation also needs an [extractable, answer-first page](/learn/answer-first-sentence) and trusted
signals like reviews and consistent listings. A shop can rank yet be absent from AI answers if the
answer is buried in images or trust is thin. The overlap is real but not total: clear all three gates
and you get [found in both](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).
