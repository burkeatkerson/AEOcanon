## Why do reviews carry so much weight for staffing agencies?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge trust — and handing over a req or a career is
a trust decision. An assistant deciding whom to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform reviews to
gauge whether you fill roles fast, place people well, and treat both sides fairly. For a business built
entirely on trust, that corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility
pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks)
that correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks do.

## Why collect reviews from candidates, not just employers?

Because staffing is two-sided, and a one-sided review profile tells half the story.

A placed candidate saying you found them a better role and communicated honestly is exactly what an
employer checks before sending a req, and what a job seeker checks before [working with
you](/learn/attracting-candidates-staffing-aeo). Most agencies never ask candidates — so two-sided
reviews are an easy, uncontested edge.

## Do the words in a review matter?

Yes — specific reviews do more work than generic praise. Reviews that name what you did — filled a
warehouse role in four days, placed someone into a higher-paying job, found a hard-to-fill medical
position — give engines concrete detail tying you to those outcomes. They reinforce the [questions
employers ask](/learn/staffing-questions-customers-ask-ai), so a handful of detailed reviews can
outperform a wall of generic ones. Ask both clients and candidates what to mention, and the reviews
start doing your AEO for you.
