**Yes — duplicate and near-duplicate content hurts AEO by diluting your citation
surface and giving engines interchangeable pages with no clear best version to
cite.** Whether it's copied from elsewhere or repeated across your own site,
duplication adds pages without adding citable value, and originality is the cure.

## How does duplication hurt citation?

By giving the engine no clear choice. When several pages — yours or others' — say the
same thing, the engine has interchangeable options and no reason to prefer one, so any
potential citation gets split or skipped. Duplication is the opposite of the
[Originality pillar](/pillars/originality): it adds pages without adding anything
[worth citing](/learn/will-ai-cite-generic-content), expanding your footprint while
shrinking your impact.

## Is internal duplication a problem?

Yes — it's self-inflicted cannibalization. Near-identical pages on your own site
compete with each other, leaving the engine several similar options and no basis to
pick one, which scatters value that should be concentrated. The fix is to
[consolidate overlapping pages](/learn/how-many-questions-per-page) into one strong,
comprehensive answer — the same discipline that keeps a [scaled Q&A
library](/learn/scale-a-qa-library) from going thin. One authoritative page beats five
overlapping ones.

## How do I fix it?

Consolidate, then differentiate. Merge overlapping internal pages into a single
comprehensive answer, give each remaining page a distinct angle and [original
substance](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore), and replace any copied material
with your own [data](/learn/does-original-research-get-cited-more),
[experience](/learn/does-first-hand-experience-help-aeo), and point of view. The target
is one strong, original version per question — distinct enough that no other page,
including your own, is a substitute for it.
