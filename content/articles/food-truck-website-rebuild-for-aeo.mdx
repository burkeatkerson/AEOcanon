depends on.

## Why is a social-only presence the binding constraint?

Because [access is the first gate](/pillars/access), and for a truck a social-only feed is where you most
often fail it. Your menu and location are what people search for — "best birria near me," "food truck open
now" — but if they live only as [images in a feed the crawler can't read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site),
the engine doesn't know what you serve or where you are, so it can't recommend you. Add a slow,
browser-only build or a schedule trapped in old posts, and even your basics are invisible. That's not a
content problem you can post your way around; it's a foundation problem.

## How do I tell if my site is hurting me?

Run two quick tests, and look for the structural gaps.

    { title: "The schedule test", body: "Is today's location and your weekly schedule readable text on a page, or only in scrolling social posts? Engines need it as text to answer 'where are you today'." },
    { title: "The speed test", body: "Check your load time. Truck sites built on heavy image galleries and embeds are often slow, and slow pages get crawled and trusted less." },

If you live only on social, your menu is an image, or your schedule is buried in posts, the setup is
working against you. A fast site with a [readable menu and live schedule](/learn/food-truck-menu-pages-for-ai)
and [clean schema](/learn/food-truck-schema-markup) is what makes everything else possible.

## Can't I just keep posting on Instagram?

Keep posting for your followers — but it can't be your only home. Because your menu and location are
exactly what people search for, a social-only presence makes you invisible for dish-level and 'near me
today' queries no matter how often you post. Putting your menu and a live schedule on a readable site is
the single highest-leverage move most trucks can make. Get the access layer right —
[server-rendered](/learn/server-side-vs-client-side-rendering), fast, with a readable menu and current
location — and the rest of your food-truck AEO finally has something to build on.
