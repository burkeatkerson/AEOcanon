**Write handyman service pages AI will cite by giving each service its own page that leads with the
answer to the price, scope, and area questions, in plain language a homeowner and an engine can lift.**
One self-contained, crawlable page per service beats a single bloated services list every time.

<Callout variant="key" title="Quick answer">
  <p>
    Give <strong>each service its own page</strong> — drywall repair, TV mounting, faucet swap, door and
    lock repair, furniture assembly, gutter cleaning — and <strong>lead with the answer</strong> to what
    it costs or your hourly rate, what's included, whether you do small jobs, and whether you serve their
    area. Make each page <strong>self-contained and crawlable</strong>. One focused page per service beats
    one bloated list every time.
  </p>
</Callout>

## Why one page per service?

Because a citation is awarded to the page that best answers one specific question — and a catch-all
services list answers none of them well. When you give drywall repair, TV mounting, faucet replacement,
door and lock repair, and gutter cleaning each [their own
page](/learn/should-content-sections-stand-alone), each can go deep on its own price, scope, and how the
job runs — and each becomes citable for its own query. A single page trying to list everything is shallow
on all of them, so the engine cites a competitor with a dedicated, focused page instead.

## What should each page lead with?

The answer the homeowner came for, before anything about you.

This is [answer-first writing](/learn/answer-first-sentence) applied to the trade: the quotable answer up
top, the depth below, the proof at the bottom.

## What makes a service page extractable?

Plain language and a clean structure. Write the way a homeowner asks — "most TV mounts run $X–$Y depending
on the wall…" — not in jargon, and use [question-shaped headings](/learn/question-shaped-headings) the
engine can match to a query. Keep each answer in a self-contained passage so it can be [lifted without the
surrounding page](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage), reinforce it with [HomeAndConstructionBusiness
schema](/learn/handyman-schema-markup), and make sure the page is fast and crawlable — handyman one-pagers
are often too thin for bots to read. Answer-first, focused, and proven — that's the page an engine cites
and a homeowner calls.
