**You don't need a public storefront for local AEO, but you do need a verifiable
location and a clearly defined service area.** Service-area businesses can hide their
address on Google Business Profile while still defining where they work, and the rest of
local AEO applies exactly the same.

## What does local AI actually require?

That engines can verify you and place your service area — not that you have a walk-in
location. A home-based or mobile business is fully eligible for local recommendations as
long as it's a verifiable real business with a clearly defined area. Google supports this
directly: [service-area businesses](https://support.google.com/business/answer/9157481)
can hide the street address and set the regions they serve. The requirement is
clarity about *where you work*, which is the same [recognition](/learn/local-aeo)
problem a storefront faces.

## Does the lack of a storefront hurt me?

Not if you're set up correctly. A verified service-area profile, [consistent
details](/learn/nap-consistency), [genuine
reviews](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations), and clear [service-area
content](/learn/local-service-area-pages-for-ai) let engines place and trust a business
with no public address. What hurts is being unverifiable or [ambiguous about where you
work](/learn/why-isnt-my-local-business-showing-up-in-ai) — and that's fixable. The
storefront was never the requirement; verifiable clarity about your area is.
