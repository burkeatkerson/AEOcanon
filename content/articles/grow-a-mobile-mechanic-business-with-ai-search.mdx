## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each repair produces a review, a referral, and a customer who calls you again for
the next problem; reviews and [answer-first content](/learn/mobile-mechanic-service-pages-for-ai) earn
citations and rankings; those bring direct calls; and the money saved on lead-app fees funds more capacity.
The lead-app model is the opposite: a faucet you pay to keep running, selling the same job to three shops,
that stops the moment you stop. AEO builds the [compounding asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead —
direct demand you own.

## How do I lower my cost per job?

By replacing recurring fees with assets that keep paying. A lead-app job costs every time and is [resold to
competitors](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands); an earned citation, a repeat customer, or a referral costs
mostly upfront and pays for years. As your cited, repeat, fleet-backed base grows, your cost per job falls
and your margins improve — and because AI names only a few mechanics, a real, well-reviewed local operator
can win recommendations a faceless app [can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
