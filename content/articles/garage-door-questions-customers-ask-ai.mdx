## How do I find the exact questions?

Listen where homeowners already ask. Mine your phone log and service calls for the questions people
actually voice, check Reddit threads, home-improvement forums, and the People Also Ask box, and
[prompt the assistants directly](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask) on common problems to
see the follow-ups they surface. Capture the *natural wording* — "why won't my garage door close
all the way" beats "photo-eye sensor misalignment" — because engines match the homeowner's
phrasing, not your jargon. Then prioritize by intent and value.

## Should I answer problem and safety questions?

Yes — they're how you earn the trust that books the job. Answering "why won't my door open" or "is
a broken spring dangerous" helpfully makes you the source a homeowner remembers when the fix is
plainly a pro's job. Problem content captures the worried failure moment; safety content earns
trust by steering homeowners away from dangerous DIY (a torsion spring under tension can cause
serious injury). Both build the credibility and visibility engines
[reward](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore) — the opposite of a thin directory page. Map
every bucket to a page and you've built the content plan that gets a company cited.
