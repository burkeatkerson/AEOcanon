## What makes a cleaning company 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about who you are, where you're based, and which buildings you serve. An answer engine
recommends a company for "near me" only when it can [place you with
certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — right area, right kind of facility.
That certainty comes from signals that all agree: matching name-address-phone, a complete profile, a
stated service area, and reviews. When the signals agree, you're an easy recommendation; when they
conflict, the engine favors a clearer competitor.

## Why does NAP consistency matter so much?

Because a mismatch is a doubt, and engines route around doubt. If your phone number differs between
your site and your Google profile, or an old address still shows in a directory, the engine can't be
sure which company you are or where you operate — so it favors a competitor it can [place with
confidence](/learn/nap-consistency). For companies that have moved, expanded their coverage, or
rebranded, this is a common and quiet leak. Clean it up and you remove the single most fixable reason
an engine skips you on a near-me query.
