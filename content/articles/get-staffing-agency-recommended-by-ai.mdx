## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be an entity the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine
can retrieve you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as the answer — and that's a higher
bar of trust. The engine recommends the agency it can [confidently
confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, specialized, and well-regarded, because when it
recommends you it's putting its own credibility behind the choice. Your job is to make that
confirmation effortless and consistent — for both the employer asking and the candidate asking.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). One inconsistency (an old phone number, a mismatched branch address)
introduces doubt, and doubt routes the recommendation elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one agency over another?

Because it picks the one it's most sure about. Faced with several options, the engine names the agency
whose signals most confidently match the request — right roles, right market, clearly specialized,
genuinely reviewed. The competitor with conflicting listings or thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so
it loses even if the placements are just as good. Winning is less about being the best agency in town
and more about being the [easiest to verify as the best](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend)
— which is exactly what this library builds, piece by piece.
