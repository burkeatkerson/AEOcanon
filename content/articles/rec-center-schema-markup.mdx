subtypes) schema with accurate name, address, phone, hours, and offerings, plus FAQ schema on answer
pages — it helps engines parse what you offer and confirm where and when you're open.** Schema
clarifies readable content for AI; it never rescues a booking widget or wrong hours.

## What does this schema actually do?

It makes your activity details unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business), and the
SportsActivityLocation or ExerciseGym subtype, labels your name, address, hours, offerings, and reviews
so engines parse them cleanly rather than guessing — reinforcing the [consistent
identity](/learn/nap-consistency) local recommendations depend on. It's the [structured data for
AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern applied to climbing and recreation: clarity for the
parser, on top of passes and programs that are already clear and readable for the visitor.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your gym — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your details machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [readable passes and
pricing](/learn/rec-center-service-pages-for-ai) the crawler can parse, [accurate
hours](/learn/local-aeo-for-rec-centers), and [genuine
reviews](/learn/rec-center-reviews-ai-recommendations). Schema can't rescue a schedule trapped in a
booking widget or hours that are wrong — and faking reviews or details in markup is a misuse engines can
[detect](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations). Accurate schema on top of readable passes and programs
is the combination that works.
