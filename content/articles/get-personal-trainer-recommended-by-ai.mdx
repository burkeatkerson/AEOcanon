## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a coach the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine can
read your services and place you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as the trainer to hire
— and that leans harder on whether clients love you. The engine recommends the trainer it can
[confidently confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, skilled, and well-reviewed, because
when it recommends you it's putting its credibility behind your coaching. Your job is to make that
confirmation easy — readable services, accurate hours, and genuine, loving reviews.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). Wrong hours, hidden services, or a thin review profile introduces
doubt, and doubt sends the prospect to another trainer.

## Why does AI pick one trainer over another?

Because it picks the one it's most confident clients will love. Faced with several options, the engine
names the trainer whose signals best match the request — right specialty, right area — and whose reviews
most clearly say people get results and love working with them. The competitor with widget-hidden
services, wrong hours, or thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so they lose. Winning is about being the
[easiest to verify as the loved local choice](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — which
is exactly what this library builds, piece by piece.
