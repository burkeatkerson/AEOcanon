## Why does an About page matter for trust?

Because engines favor sources whose identity is clear. A transparent About page
signals a real, accountable organization rather than an anonymous site, and that
accountability makes your content safer to trust and cite. Hidden ownership and vague
"we're passionate about X" filler send the opposite signal. It's a small but genuine
[credibility](/pillars/credibility) input — and an easy one to get right.

## What should it include?

Substance and structure. State who you are, what you do, your credentials and track
record, real contact and organizational details, and links to your profiles and key
people. Then reinforce it with [Organization
schema](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) and a [consistent identity across the
web](/learn/sameas-strategy) so engines can connect the page to a known entity. The
goal is transparency a machine can read, not a marketing narrative.

## How does it help entity recognition?

By consolidating your identity in one readable place. A well-structured About page
gathers your name, description, people, and links where an engine can find them, which
helps it [recognize and disambiguate your entity](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities).
Combined with schema and consistent off-site signals, it strengthens how engines
understand who you are — supporting the [entity-based
AEO](/learn/what-is-entity-aeo) that authority and credibility both depend on.
