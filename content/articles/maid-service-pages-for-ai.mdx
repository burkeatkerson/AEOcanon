**Make maid service and pricing pages AI will cite by giving each service its own page that leads with
the answer to the cost, what's-included, and trust questions, in plain readable text a client and an
engine can lift.** One self-contained, crawlable page per service beats a single bloated services page
every time.

## Why one page per service?

Because a citation is awarded to the page that best answers one specific question — and a catch-all
services page or booking widget answers none of them well. When you give recurring, deep clean,
move-in/out, and one-time each [their own page](/learn/should-content-sections-stand-alone), each can go
deep on its own cost, scope, and details — and each becomes citable for its own query. A single page
trying to cover everything is shallow on all of them, so the engine cites a competitor with a dedicated,
focused page.

## What should each page lead with?

The answer the client came for, before anything about you.

This is [answer-first writing](/learn/answer-first-sentence) applied to the trade: the quotable answer up
top, the depth below, the trust and proof prominent — because someone is letting you into their home.

## What makes a maid-service page extractable?

Plain language and a clean structure. Write the way a client asks — "a recurring cleaning typically runs …"
— not in jargon, and use [question-shaped headings](/learn/question-shaped-headings) the engine can match
to a query. Keep each answer in a self-contained passage so it can be [lifted without the surrounding
page](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage), reinforce it with [LocalBusiness
schema](/learn/maid-service-schema-markup), and make sure the page is fast and crawlable — not just a
booking widget. Answer-first, focused, and proven — that's the page an engine cites and a client books.
