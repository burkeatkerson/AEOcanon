## Why do reviews carry so much weight for flooring contractors?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge trust — and new flooring is a big
commitment a homeowner lives on for years. An assistant deciding whom to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and third-party reviews to
gauge whether you're reliable, tidy, communicative, and deliver clean work on time and budget. For a
trade where a homeowner is spending $3k–$25k on a surface they'll walk on for a decade, that
corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made
visible, and the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility
more than backlinks do.

## How many reviews do I need?

Fewer than you think, fresher than you have. There's no magic count — consistency and recency outweigh
a raw total. A steady stream of genuine recent reviews signals an active, trusted installer far better
than a big pile from three years ago. The goal is an ongoing flow: build a simple habit of asking at
install completion, so new reviews keep arriving and the engine keeps seeing an installer that's
[currently trusted](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations), not formerly busy.

## Do the words in a review matter?

Yes — specific reviews do more work than generic praise.

Reviews that name what you did — installed LVP throughout on budget, refinished oak floors with great
communication, handled the subfloor and left no mess — give engines specific detail tying you to those
exact installs and to reliable delivery. They reinforce the [questions homeowners
ask](/learn/flooring-questions-customers-ask-ai), so a handful of detailed reviews can outperform a
wall of generic ones. Ask clients what to mention, and the reviews start doing your AEO for you.
