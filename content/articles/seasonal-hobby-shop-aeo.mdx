## Why is seasonality a big AEO lever for a hobby shop?

Because hobby demand spikes hard on gift season and new releases, and the cited store catches the surge.
The winter holidays are the biggest gift season of the year — parents and partners searching "best gifts
for a board gamer" or "model kit for a 10-year-old" — with big set launches, back-to-school, and
summer-break hobby surges close behind, each bringing a wave of "where to buy [item] near me" searches.
The store whose answer is already published, crawled, and trusted [gets cited the moment the spike
hits](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo) — while competitors scramble. It's the [Freshness
pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned into a hobby calendar, and the difference between a record season and
a missed one.

## How do I prepare for each peak?

Build the content while it's calm, so it's ready when shoppers surge.

## Should I make a new page each year?

No — update one durable page per peak. A persistent 'holiday gifts for gamers' or 'hobby gift ideas' page
[accumulates authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh the picks, prices, and stock each
year, which beats spinning up a throwaway page annually that starts from zero and splits your signals.
Keep one strong page per peak, sharpen it before each season, and link it to your [brand and category
pages](/learn/hobby-shop-product-pages-for-ai) and your [events](/learn/events-and-community-hobby-shop-aeo).
One durable page, refreshed and ready, beats scrambling when the rush hits.
