## Why are your lessons the most important pages?

Because what you teach is what students search for — and a citation goes to the page the engine can
read. When someone asks "beginner golf lessons near me" or "tennis coach for kids nearby," the engine
matches the query against lessons it can [actually parse](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site). If
your lesson menu and rates live only inside a booking widget, the engine doesn't know what you offer, so
you're invisible for those program-level queries — the most valuable, highest-intent searches there are.
Readable lessons turn your whole coaching business into citable answers.

## What makes a lesson page citable?

Readable text, organized the way students and parents think.

## Why describe lessons, not just list them?

Because description is the language of fit. Students ask AI for "a beginner-friendly golf coach" or "a
good instructor to fix my swing," and a bare lesson list gives the engine little to match. A sentence
describing each lesson — who it's for, what to expect, the method — gives the engine the [extractable
detail](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage) that ties you to those specific, high-intent searches,
and mirrors the [reviews](/learn/sports-instructor-reviews-ai-recommendations) students write about your
coaching. A readable, described lesson menu is the foundation every other coaching AEO move builds on.
