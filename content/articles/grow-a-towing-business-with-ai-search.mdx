## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each rescue you win directly produces a review, a referral, and a driver who
remembers who came for them; reviews and [answer-first content](/learn/towing-service-pages-for-ai) earn
citations that win the next emergency call at full price; and the margin you keep — instead of handing it
to a club — funds more trucks and drivers. The motor-club model is the opposite: a faucet that dispatches
you scraps and owns the customer, that stops the moment they route elsewhere. AEO builds the [compounding
asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead — demand you own.

## How do I lower my cost per call?

By replacing low-margin dispatch with assets that keep paying. A dispatched job pays a contracted fraction
every time and is [routed to whoever's cheapest](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands); an earned citation, a
repeat caller, or a referral costs mostly upfront and pays full price for years. As your cited,
direct-call, account-backed base grows, your cost per call falls and your margins improve — and because AI
names only a few companies in an emergency, a real, well-reviewed local operator can win recommendations a
faceless app [can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
