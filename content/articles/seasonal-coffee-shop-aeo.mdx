## Why does seasonality matter for coffee shops?

Because coffee demand swings with the season, and customers chase the seasonal drink. Summer brings
"iced coffee near me" and "best cold brew nearby" the moment it warms up; fall and the holidays bring
"pumpkin spice latte near me" and "peppermint mocha," plus gift cards and bean boxes. The café whose
answer is already published, crawled, and trusted [gets cited the moment the craving
starts](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo) — while competitors are still updating their PDF.
It's the [Freshness pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned into a seasonal calendar.

## How do I prepare for each season?

Build the content while it's calm, so it's ready when the craving hits.

## Should I make a new page each year?

No — update one durable page per recurring season. A persistent 'summer iced drinks' or 'fall and
holiday lattes' page [accumulates authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh the drinks,
dates, and details each year. That beats spinning up a throwaway page annually that starts from zero and
splits your signals. Keep one page per season, refresh it ahead of the wave, and link it to your
[catering and wholesale page](/learn/coffee-catering-and-events-aeo) — one durable page, refreshed and
ready, beats scrambling when the rush arrives.
