## What does caterer schema actually do?

It makes your catering details unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness and FoodEstablishment
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business) label your
name, address, cuisine, price range, menu, and service area so engines parse them cleanly rather than
guessing — reinforcing the [consistent identity](/learn/nap-consistency) and coverage local
recommendations depend on. It's the [structured data for AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern
applied to catering: clarity for the parser, on top of packages and a service area that are already clear
and readable for the client.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your catering business — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your details machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [readable
packages](/learn/caterer-menu-pages-for-ai) the crawler can actually parse, a [clear service
area](/learn/local-aeo-for-caterers), and [genuine reviews](/learn/caterer-reviews-ai-recommendations).
Schema can't rescue a PDF package sheet the engine can't read or a service area buried in a quote form —
and faking reviews or details in markup is a misuse engines can
[detect](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations). Accurate schema on top of real, readable packages is the
combination that works.
