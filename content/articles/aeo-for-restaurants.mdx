**AEO for restaurants means becoming the place AI assistants name when someone asks where to eat — by
making your menu and hours readable, answering the real dietary-and-reservation questions, and earning
genuine reviews.** The reward is a directly-booked table instead of a commissioned cover from an app.

<Callout variant="key" title="Quick answer">
  <p>
    Win three things, in order: <strong>be readable</strong> (menu, hours, and location as real text
    AI can fetch — not a PDF), <strong>answer the real question</strong> (cuisine, dietary options,
    reservations, "open now"), and <strong>be trusted</strong> (a complete Google Business Profile and
    genuine, recent reviews). The AI names a <strong>few</strong> places — do all three and you're one
    of them, booked directly.
  </p>
</Callout>

## Why does AEO matter so much for restaurants?

Because dining decisions are fast, local, and made on the first good answer. When someone asks "where
should we eat," they act on what the assistant names — and the [AI answer names only two or three
places](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations), not a page of listings. Pew Research [found people
clicked a link just 8% of the time when an AI summary appeared, versus 15%
without](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer *is* the decision. Being the cited restaurant is the new equivalent of being the spot
everyone recommends — and it sends a diner who books directly, not a commissioned cover.

## How do restaurants get recommended by AI?

By clearing the three gates a diner — and an engine — judge you on, in order.

    { title: "Answer the real question (Alignment + Extractability)", body: "Plain-text answers to 'what's on the menu', 'do you take reservations', 'are you open now', 'do you have vegan or gluten-free options', and 'where are you'." },

Most restaurants fail gate one without knowing it — the menu is a PDF or a photo, invisible to the
engine. Fixing that, plus accurate listings and reviews, is the highest-leverage work, and it's the
same foundation that [ranks you in Google](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together) too.

## Isn't this just SEO?

It's mostly the local SEO you should already be doing, pointed at a new surface. The fast, crawlable,
well-reviewed restaurant with a readable menu that ranks is the same one that gets
[cited](/learn/is-aeo-replacing-seo). AEO adds putting your menu and answers in [plain
text](/learn/answer-first-sentence), [structured data](/learn/restaurant-schema-markup) for menu and
cuisine, and the [review consistency](/learn/restaurant-reviews-ai-recommendations) answer engines
lean on. You don't choose between them — you run one program that wins both.

## Where do independent restaurants have the advantage?

In specificity and authenticity. AI names only a few places and [citations spread
thin](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands), so an independent with a readable menu, accurate listings,
and genuine reviews that name dishes can win the recommendation over a chain or a faceless aggregator
listing. That's the [Originality](/pillars/originality) and [local
authority](/learn/local-aeo-for-restaurants) edge a platform can't copy — and every directly-booked
table skips the commission.

## What should a restaurant do first?

Start at the binding constraint. [Make your menu readable](/learn/restaurant-menu-pages-for-ai) as
real text, then fix your [hours and listings](/learn/local-aeo-for-restaurants) so 'open now' is
accurate, then build [genuine reviews](/learn/restaurant-reviews-ai-recommendations). Work down the
rest of this library from there — [winning 'near me'](/learn/near-me-restaurant-ai-search),
[Google Business Profile](/learn/restaurant-google-business-profile-ai),
[schema](/learn/restaurant-schema-markup), and the
[questions diners actually ask](/learn/restaurant-questions-customers-ask-ai).
