## How is corporate-yoga AEO different?

The intent is a researched B2B decision, not an individual sign-up. An HR manager or office manager
setting up a wellness benefit researches carefully — and asks the assistant detailed questions long
before they call. They're researching group rates, billing, and whether you can bring yoga on-site or
run it virtually for their team. Because [AEO works for B2B](/learn/does-aeo-work-for-b2b) exactly here,
the studio whose pages answer those questions clearly gets [cited during the
research](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations) — and wins a recurring contract most studios never
even surface for. And a corporate contract is a block of recurring members at once, worth far more than
a single drop-in.

## What questions do companies ask?

B2B, logistics-focused, and specific — each worth a clear answer on a dedicated page.

## How do I win corporate-yoga citations?

Give corporate and workplace wellness its own answer-first page — not a buried line on your homepage.
Publish what you offer (on-site, virtual, group rates), how pricing and billing work, program options,
and your experience in readable text, reinforced by your
[reviews](/learn/yoga-studio-reviews-ai-recommendations) and a clear inquiry path. Most studios either
omit this entirely or hide it, so a clear, dedicated page is an easy way to get cited for valuable,
recurring B2B contracts — a block of members at once that compounds your
[growth](/learn/grow-a-yoga-studio-with-ai-search).
