## Why do reviews carry so much weight for towing?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge trust — and a stranded driver fears being
overcharged or left waiting. An assistant deciding whom to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform reviews to
gauge whether you're fast, fair, and reliable. For a trade where the customer is anxious and vulnerable,
that corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made
visible, and the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility far
more than backlinks do. Win those and you win the full-price call instead of the dispatched scrap.

## How many reviews do I need?

Fewer than you think, fresher than you have. There's no magic count — consistency and recency outweigh a
raw total. A steady stream of genuine recent reviews signals an active company that actually shows up, far
better than a big pile from three years ago. The goal is an ongoing flow: ask grateful drivers right after
the rescue, while the relief is fresh, so new reviews keep arriving and the engine keeps seeing a company
that's [currently trusted](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations), not formerly busy.

## Do the words in a review matter?

Yes — specific reviews do more work than generic praise.

Reviews that name what you did — arrived fast, fair price, no gouging — give engines specific detail tying
you to reliable roadside help. They reinforce the [questions drivers
ask](/learn/towing-questions-customers-ask-ai), so a handful of detailed reviews can outperform a wall of
generic ones. Ask drivers what to mention, and the reviews start doing your AEO for you.
