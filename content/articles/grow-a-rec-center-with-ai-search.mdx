## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each great visit earns a review and a referral; [readable
offerings](/learn/rec-center-service-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews earn citations for 'climbing gym
near me'; those bring day-pass visitors; visitors convert to members; and members who stay produce more
reviews and referrals — while the ad spend you save funds better walls, programs, and staff. The
paid-ad model is the opposite: a faucet you pay to keep running, that stops the moment you stop. AEO
builds the [compounding asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead — visitors and members who found
you and stay.

## Why is retention central to gym growth?

Because a gym's value *is* recurring revenue, and filling the top of the funnel only pays off if
visitors convert and members stay. A member who climbs for years is worth many times one day pass — and
long-tenured, happy members are exactly who write the [reviews that mention the walls and
programs](/learn/rec-center-reviews-ai-recommendations) and refer friends, feeding the AI
recommendations that bring the next visitors. So growth isn't just AEO at the top; it's AEO plus
conversion and retention together, each compounding the other. Win visitors with citations, convert and
keep them with a great experience, and the flywheel accelerates.

## How does this lower my cost per member?

By replacing recurring ad spend with assets that keep paying. A paid ad costs every time and stops when
you stop; an earned citation, a loyal long-term member, a referral, or a [booked
party](/learn/group-events-parties-rec-center-aeo) costs mostly upfront and pays for years. As your
cited, referred, and retained base grows, your cost per member falls and your revenue stabilizes — and
because AI names only a few places, a real, well-reviewed local gym can win recommendations a faceless
chain listing [can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
