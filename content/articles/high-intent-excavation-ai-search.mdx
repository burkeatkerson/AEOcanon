## Why is high-intent the most valuable excavation search?

Because it's the moment money is about to move, and the assistant frames the whole decision. A customer
ready to dig — or a builder lining up a foundation crew — researches cost, permits, and access before
they contact anyone, and the [AI answer names only a few
sources](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Pew Research [found people click a link just 8% of
the time when an AI summary
appears](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer shapes the shortlist before you ever hear from them. The excavator cited at this stage is
the one they trust enough to call first.

## What questions do ready-to-dig customers ask?

The practical, commitment-stage ones — and you should own every one.

[Alignment](/pillars/alignment) of answering the real question over marketing copy.

## Why does answering cost honestly matter so much?

Because cost is the first thing a customer researches and the question most excavation sites dodge. A
clear, honest cost range — with the factors that move it, like soil, slope, rock, and haul-off — earns
trust and citations precisely when intent is highest. Dodging it ("every site is unique, call us")
sends the customer to a competitor or a lead-gen platform that actually answered. Being the excavator
who explains the money honestly is the [Credibility](/pillars/credibility) and
[Originality](/pillars/originality) edge that gets you cited and called — map every one of these
questions to a page in your [questions library](/learn/excavation-questions-customers-ask-ai).
