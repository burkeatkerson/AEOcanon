**To show up in Google AI Overviews, make your page crawlable and authoritative in
Google's index and write answer-first passages Google can lift — because AI
Overviews are generated from that index and quote the passages that best answer
the query.** There's no separate submission; you earn it through strong relevance
plus extractability.

## How do AI Overviews choose what to include?

AI Overviews choose passages the way the broader answer-engine pipeline does:
Google retrieves relevant results from its index, reranks them for the specific
query, and synthesizes an answer that cites the sources of the passages it used.
Because the retrieval base is Google's own index, the crawlability, speed, and
authority you build for traditional search feed directly into AI Overview
eligibility — but the citation goes to the best *passage*, not just the
top-ranked page.

## Does being cited matter if AI Overviews reduce clicks?

Yes — being the cited source is where the surviving clicks go.
[Seer Interactive found that pages cited inside an AI Overview earned a 35% higher
organic clickthrough rate](https://www.seerinteractive.com/insights/how-ai-overviews-are-impacting-ctr-5-initial-takeaways)
than uncited pages, even as overall clicks fell on AI-Overview queries. The click
didn't disappear for everyone; it concentrated on the named sources. So inclusion
in the Overview is the goal, not something to avoid.

## What makes my page eligible?

Your page becomes eligible when Google can read it, trust it, and lift a clean
answer from it. That means three things: technical [access](/learn/aeo-pillar-access)
(server-rendered HTML, allowed crawlers, fast load); genuine authority and
relevance for the query; and [extractable](/learn/aeo-pillar-extractability)
passages that answer the question in the opening sentence. Miss any one and you
fall out of contention before synthesis.

## Why am I ranking but not appearing in the Overview?

If you rank yet never appear in the Overview, your best answer probably isn't the
most liftable or most credible one for that query. Google retrieved you, but the
reranker preferred a passage that answered more completely or carried better
evidence. Rewrite the section answer-first, add a specific stat or source, and
make sure the answer stands alone — then re-check. The
[AEO Canon diagnostic](/learn/aeo-canon-diagnostic) helps locate the gap.

## What should I do first?

Start with the page already ranking for your target query: rewrite its key
section answer-first, add inline evidence, and confirm it's fully crawlable and
fast. You're not chasing a new ranking — you're making an already-relevant page
the easiest, most credible passage for Google to quote. That's the essence of
[what is AEO](/learn/what-is-aeo).
