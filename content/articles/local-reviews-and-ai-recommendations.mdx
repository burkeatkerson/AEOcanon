## Why do reviews carry so much weight locally?

Because they're independent evidence of quality, and an engine recommending a local
business wants to be safe. When AI is asked to recommend, it [synthesizes sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations)
from Google and third-party platforms rather than taking your word for it — so the
volume, recency, and quality of your reviews directly shape whether you're named. For a
local service where a bad call costs the customer real money, that trust signal carries
outsized weight. It's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made local and
concrete.

## What kind of reviews help most?

Recent, plentiful, and specific. A steady stream of genuine recent reviews signals an
active business far better than a stale pile, and reviews that *name the service and the
place* corroborate the exact [local questions customers
ask](/learn/how-to-do-local-keyword-research-for-ai).

## Do fake reviews ever work?

No — they hurt. Fake or incentivized reviews risk detection, violate platform rules, and
create inconsistent patterns that platforms and engines increasingly catch — adding no
genuine trust and risking your standing. The durable path is earning real reviews
steadily, which is slower but is what actually moves recommendations and holds up under
scrutiny. It's the same lesson as [manipulated authority
signals](/learn/do-press-releases-help-aeo) everywhere in AEO: genuine beats gamed.
