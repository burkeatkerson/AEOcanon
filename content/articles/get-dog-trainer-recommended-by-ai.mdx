## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a place the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine can
read your programs and place you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as who to hire — and
that leans harder on whether clients love you. The engine recommends the business it can [confidently
confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, skilled, and well-reviewed, because when it
recommends you it's putting its credibility behind the experience. Your job is to make that
confirmation easy — readable programs, accurate hours, and genuine, loving reviews.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). Wrong hours, hidden programs, or a thin review profile introduces
doubt, and doubt sends the owner elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one dog trainer over another?

Because it picks the one it's most confident clients will love. Faced with several options, the engine
names the trainer whose signals best match the request — right kind of training, right area — and whose
reviews most clearly say people love it. The competitor with widget-hidden programs, wrong hours, or
thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it loses. Winning is about being the [easiest to verify as the
loved local choice](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — which is exactly what this
library builds, piece by piece.
