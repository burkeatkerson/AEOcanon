## Why does seasonality matter for photographers?

Because photography demand comes in waves, and clients plan them far ahead. Wedding season, fall family
mini-sessions, holiday card photos, graduation and senior portraits, and spring newborn sessions all bring
searches weeks or months before the date — "wedding photographer for next June," "fall mini-sessions near me,"
"holiday card photographer." The photographer whose answer is already published, crawled, and trusted [gets
cited the moment planning starts](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo) — while competitors are still
updating a gallery with no dates. It's the [Freshness pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned into a booking
calendar.

## How do I prepare for each wave?

Build the content while it's calm, so it's ready when clients start booking.

## Should I make a new page each year?

No — update one durable page per recurring occasion. A persistent 'fall mini-sessions' or 'wedding season
photography' page [accumulates authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh the dates, pricing,
and availability each year. That beats spinning up a throwaway page annually that starts from zero and splits
your signals. Keep one page per occasion, refresh it ahead of the wave, and link it to your
[wedding](/learn/wedding-photography-ai-search) and [portrait and
commercial](/learn/portrait-and-commercial-photography-aeo) pages — one durable page, refreshed and ready,
beats scrambling when the wave arrives.
