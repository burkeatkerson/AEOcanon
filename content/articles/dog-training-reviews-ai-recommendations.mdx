## Why do reviews carry so much weight for dog trainers?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge which businesses clients actually trust — and
handing over your dog is a high-trust decision. An assistant deciding what to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform reviews to
gauge whether you're a great, safe place to train or board. For a dog business, that corroborated
reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the
[off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks
do.

## Do reviews that mention trainers and results matter more?

Yes — specific reviews are gold, because they speak the language of what owners want.

A review naming a trainer, a method, a result, or how the dog was cared for gives the engine detail to
tie you to "positive-reinforcement trainer near me" or "safe dog boarding nearby" — the exact [method
and result queries](/learn/dog-training-questions-customers-ask-ai) owners ask. Invite happy clients to
mention what changed and how their dog was treated, and the reviews start matching you to the searches
that fill programs and the boarding calendar.

## How many reviews — and how fresh?

Fresh beats big. There's no magic count — recency and a steady flow outweigh a stale total. A business
with current reviews that mention real results and care signals an active, trusted operation far better
than one coasting on old ratings. Build a simple habit: invite happy clients to review at milestones
(graduation day, after a boarding stay), and [respond to
them](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations) — engagement signals an active business that cares,
which is exactly the place an engine wants to recommend.
