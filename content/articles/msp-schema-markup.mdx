## What does MSP schema actually do?

It makes your firm's details unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business), and the
ProfessionalService subtype, labels your name, address, phone, hours, area served, services, and
reviews so engines parse them cleanly rather than guessing — reinforcing the [consistent
identity](/learn/nap-consistency) local recognition depends on. It's the [structured data for
AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern applied to the industry: clarity for the parser, on top of
content that's already clear for the buyer.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your firm — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your details machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [consistent
identity](/learn/local-aeo-for-msps), [genuine reviews](/learn/msp-reviews-ai-recommendations), stated
certifications, and [pages that answer buyer questions](/learn/msp-service-pages-for-ai). And don't
fake it: marking up reviews or certifications that don't match your visible page is a misuse engines
can detect — [especially damaging](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations) for a trust industry handling
security. Accurate schema on top of real proof is the combination that works.
