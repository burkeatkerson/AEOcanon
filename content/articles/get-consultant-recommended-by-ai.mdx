## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be an expert the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine can
retrieve you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as the answer — and that's a higher bar of
trust. The engine recommends the consultant it can [confidently
confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, qualified, and demonstrably skilled, because when
it recommends you it's putting its own credibility behind the choice. Your job is to make that
confirmation effortless and consistent.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). One inconsistency (an old number, a mismatched firm name) introduces
doubt, and doubt routes the recommendation elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one consultant over another?

Because it picks the one it's most sure about. Faced with several options, the engine names the
consultant whose signals most confidently match the request — right specialization, right area or
remote, clearly qualified, genuinely proven. The competitor with conflicting listings or unpublished
expertise is the riskier vouch, so they lose even if the work is just as good. Winning is less about
being the best consultant in town and more about being the [easiest to verify as the
best](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — which is exactly what this library builds,
piece by piece.
