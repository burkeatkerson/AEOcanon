## Why is the site the binding constraint?

Because [access is the first gate](/pillars/access), and many detailers fail it by having no real site
at all. A great Instagram is brand-building, but if an AI crawler has nothing crawlable to fetch — or
finds a thin, slow one-pager — you're invisible no matter how good your work or your reviews are.
Engines answer "best car detailing near me" from the [open web they can
read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), not your feed. That's not a content problem you can post
your way out of; it's a foundation problem.

## How do I tell if my site is hurting me?

Run two quick tests, and look for the structural gaps.

If you have no real site, a slow one-pager, no [package pages](/learn/auto-detailing-service-pages-for-ai),
or no [schema](/learn/auto-detailing-schema-markup), the site is working against you. A fast,
server-rendered foundation with real content is what makes everything else possible.

## Can't I just use my Instagram instead?

No — social is excellent for brand and reviews, but it doesn't replace a crawlable site. Engines read
the open web to answer detailing queries, and a feed of photos gives them little structured to lift.
Build a real, fast site that [answers the questions](/learn/auto-detailing-service-pages-for-ai) in
readable text — [server-rendered](/learn/server-side-vs-client-side-rendering), fast, structured — and
use social to feed it. The content you publish on that foundation finally has a chance to be found.
