**A maid service needs a website rebuild for AEO when the site is thin or built only in a booking widget,
hard for AI crawlers to read, or without per-service answer-first pages and schema — because no amount of
content fixes a foundation engines can't parse.** The rebuild is the access layer everything else depends
on.

  <p>
    You need a rebuild when the site is <strong>thin, widget-only, slow, or structureless</strong> —
    rendered only in the browser, no per-service pages, no pricing, missing schema. Engines can't cite
    what they can't <strong>read and parse</strong>, so content layered on a broken foundation is
    wasted. <strong>Fix the access layer first</strong>.
  </p>
</Callout>

## Why is the site the binding constraint?

Because [access is the first gate](/pillars/access), and a gate you fail ends the contest before content
matters. If an AI crawler fetches your page and sees a thin brochure or just a booking widget — or the
content renders only in the browser — you're invisible no matter how good your service or your reviews
are. Many maid-service sites are little more than a [widget the bot can't
read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site). That's not a content problem you can write your way out of;
it's a foundation problem.

## How do I tell if my site is hurting me?

Run two quick tests, and look for the structural gaps.

<Steps
  items={[
    { title: "The JavaScript-off test", body: "Load a key page with JavaScript disabled. If the content vanishes or it's just a widget, AI crawlers likely see an empty page." },
    { title: "The speed test", body: "Check your load time. Slow pages get crawled less and trusted less; speed is part of whether you're readable at all." },
    { title: "The structure test", body: "Do you have a dedicated page per service with pricing and trust signals, or just a booking form? No per-service pages means nothing focused to cite." },
    { title: "The schema test", body: "Is there accurate LocalBusiness structured data, or none? Missing or wrong schema leaves the engine guessing." },
  ]}
/>

If a page is empty without scripts, just a widget, slow, has no [dedicated service and pricing
pages](/learn/maid-service-pages-for-ai), or lacks [clean schema](/learn/maid-service-schema-markup), the
site is working against you. A fast, server-rendered foundation with real content is what makes everything
else possible.

## Can't I just keep my booking widget?

Keep the widget for booking — but you also need your services, pricing, and trust signals in readable HTML
text on the page. Because the widget is usually invisible to crawlers, relying on it alone hides exactly
the offerings clients search for. Add readable text alongside it, get the access layer right —
[server-rendered](/learn/server-side-vs-client-side-rendering), fast, structured — and the content you
publish on top finally has a chance to be found.
