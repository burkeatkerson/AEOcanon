**Customers ask AI bakery questions in four buckets — custom orders ('who makes custom wedding cakes'),
logistics ('open now', 'do you deliver', 'how far ahead to order'), dietary ('gluten-free bakery near
me'), and pricing ('how much is a custom cake').** Mapping each to readable content that answers it is
the core of a bakery AEO content plan.

## How do I find the exact questions?

Listen where customers already ask. Note what people ask at the counter and on the phone, read your
reviews and the questions on your Google profile, scan local wedding and parenting forums, and [prompt
the assistants directly](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask) on custom cakes and your specialty
in your area to see the follow-ups they surface. Capture the *natural wording* — "where can I get a
gluten-free birthday cake near [neighborhood]" beats "allergen-conscious confectionery" — because
engines match the customer's phrasing. Then prioritize by what genuinely fits your bakery.

## Should I answer lead-time and pricing questions?

Yes — they're the easiest citations most bakeries leave on the table. Customers constantly ask how far
ahead to order a custom cake, whether you deliver or ship, and roughly what it costs, and answering them
in plain text — not buried in a contact form or a PDF — makes you the bakery the engine confidently
recommends. It also saves your staff from fielding the same questions all day. These answers are pure
[Alignment](/pillars/alignment): the real question, answered where the engine can read it. Map every
bucket to readable content and you've built the plan that gets a bakery cited.
