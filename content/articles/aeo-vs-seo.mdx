**AEO and SEO are not rivals: they share most of one foundation, then diverge on
the win condition — SEO competes for ranked links, AEO competes to be the source
cited inside an AI answer.** For nearly every business the answer isn't to choose,
it's to layer AEO on top of solid SEO and optimize one body of content for both
surfaces.

## What's the core difference between AEO and SEO?

The core difference is the win condition. [SEO (search engine
optimization)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Search_engine_optimization) works to
rank your page in a list of results, where the prize is a click. AEO (answer engine
optimization) works to get your content cited inside an AI-generated answer, where
the prize is being the source the engine quotes — whether or not anyone clicks. SEO
optimizes a whole page to compete for a position; AEO optimizes a self-contained
passage to compete for a citation.

That single shift in the win condition is why AEO isn't a rebrand of SEO. For the
full breakdown of what's genuinely new, see [is AEO just SEO with a new
name?](/learn/is-aeo-just-seo) — the short version is that a real
extraction-and-trust layer sits on top of the shared foundation.

## Do AEO and SEO share the same foundation?

Yes — roughly 70–80% of the work overlaps, because the signals that earn rankings
are the same ones that earn citations. A crawlable site with clean [semantic HTML](/glossary/semantic-html), fast
[Core Web Vitals](https://web.dev/articles/vitals), and genuine authority is what both
a search index and an [answer engine](/glossary/answer-engine) reward. This is why skipping SEO to "do AEO" backfires: an engine can't cite a page
it can't crawl, can't read without JavaScript, or doesn't trust. The technical
[access](/pillars/access) and off-site [authority](/pillars/authority) pillars of
the AEO Canon are, in large part, SEO fundamentals carried forward.

## Where does AEO go beyond SEO?

AEO goes beyond SEO in the extraction-and-trust layer: writing answer-first,
self-contained passages an engine can lift ([extractability](/pillars/extractability)),
backing claims with inline evidence ([credibility](/pillars/credibility)), earning
[brand mentions](/glossary/branded-mention) that engines weigh above backlinks,
matching conversational queries, and measuring [citation
share](/glossary/citation-share) per engine rather than a single ranking. That layer is
small as a share of total effort but decisive — it's where teams with great SEO
still lose the citation to a competitor who structured the answer better.

## So which should you invest in?

Invest in both, in sequence: secure the SEO foundation, then add the AEO layer on
top — because they're the same content optimized for two surfaces, not two separate
budgets. The practical rule is "write once, optimize for both": one crawlable,
authoritative, well-structured page that ranks in the results list *and* gets
quoted in the answer. As AI answers capture a growing share of queries, the AEO
layer only becomes more valuable — but it never stops depending on the foundation
beneath it.
