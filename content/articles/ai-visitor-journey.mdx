**The AI visitor journey starts inside the answer — the person reads a synthesized
response, sees you cited, and clicks expecting continuity.** Most never click
(zero-click), and those who do arrive mid-journey, pre-qualified — so continuity from
the answer and a clear next step are what convert these high-value visitors.

## Where does the journey actually begin?

The journey begins inside the AI answer, not on your homepage. By the time someone
considers clicking, the awareness and consideration steps have already happened in
the response: they asked a question, read a synthesized answer, and saw your brand
[cited](/glossary/citation) as a credible source. So your page is a *continuation* of a journey already in
progress, not a cold first touch. This is the structural shift from [traditional
search](/learn/ai-search-vs-traditional-search) — the engine did the front of the
funnel, and your site picks it up later.

## What does the full path look like?

The full path runs from query to conversion, with most people exiting before they
ever reach you:

## Why do most visitors never reach step 4?

Most never reach your site because AI answers are largely [zero-click](/glossary/zero-click-search) — the answer
resolves the question, so there's no need to visit. [Pew Research found users clicked
a link far less often when an AI summary appeared](/learn/does-ai-search-send-traffic).
That's not a failure; it's the model. It means a large share of your citation value
is the zero-click brand exposure at step 3, and the visitors who do reach step 4 are
a self-selected, high-intent minority — which is exactly why they're worth optimizing
for.

## How do you optimize the journey?

Optimize by designing step 4 as a continuation, not a beginning. Confirm the claim
or recommendation that brought the visitor, add the depth, proof, or specifics the
answer couldn't fit, and make the next action unmistakable. Don't re-introduce the
topic or route a warm, qualified visitor through a generic funnel — that makes them
restart a journey they're already deep into. The page-level specifics are in
[converting AI search visitors](/learn/convert-ai-search-visitors) and [landing pages
for AI traffic](/learn/landing-pages-for-ai-traffic).
