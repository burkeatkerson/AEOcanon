## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each completed job produces reviews and referrals; reviews and
[answer-first content](/learn/electrician-service-pages-for-ai) earn citations and
rankings; those bring direct inquiries; and the money saved on purchased leads funds more
content and more crews — which produce more jobs. The directory model is the opposite: a
faucet you pay to keep running, that stops the moment you stop. AEO builds the [compounding
asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead.

## What's the fastest way to start?

Fix the highest-leverage gaps, then compound. Get a [crawlable, fast
site](/learn/electrician-website-rebuild-for-aeo), [answer-first
pages](/learn/electrician-service-pages-for-ai) for your top services and
[emergency intent](/learn/emergency-electrician-ai-search), and consistent [reviews and
listings](/learn/local-aeo-for-electricians) — those produce direct inquiries within weeks
to months. Then make every job feed the flywheel: a photo, a [review
ask](/learn/electrician-reviews-ai-recommendations), a referral request, and a published
answer to a question customers ask. Small habits, compounding.

## Can I really lower my cost per job?

Yes — by replacing recurring fees with assets that keep paying. A purchased lead costs
every time and is [resold to your competitors](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands); an
earned citation, ranking, or referral costs mostly upfront and sends jobs for years. As
your owned pipeline grows, your cost per job falls and your margins improve, because you're
no longer renting every customer. And because AI names only a few sources, a focused,
licensed, genuinely reviewed local electrician can win recommendations a generic national
directory [can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore) — lower cost,
better margins, and demand you own.
