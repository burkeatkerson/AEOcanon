## What do these tools actually do?

They automate the manual loop. An AEO tracking tool runs your [prompt
set](/learn/how-to-track-ai-citations) across engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and
Google AI on a schedule, then logs whether you're cited, your [share of
voice](/learn/share-of-voice-ai-search), and which competitors appear — so you can
monitor far more questions and engines than you could by hand. The method is the same
one you'd do manually; the tool removes the labor and adds historical trends.

## How do I choose one?

By coverage and granularity, not brand. Look at which engines it covers, whether it
[measures citation per engine rather than blending](/learn/per-engine-vs-blended-measurement),
how it handles [competitor tracking](/learn/track-competitor-ai-citations), and how it
shows share of voice over time. Per-engine measurement matters most, because a blended
number hides the fact that you might dominate one engine and be invisible on another —
exactly the detail the [Adaptability pillar](/pillars/adaptability) needs.

## Do I even need one?

Not at first. Start manually with a spreadsheet and your own prompt runs to learn the
patterns; a tool becomes worth it once you have more prompts, engines, and competitors
than you can check by hand, or you need reporting without the manual effort. Many SEO
platforms have also added AI-visibility modules — judge those on the same coverage and
per-engine criteria, since the right choice is about capability, not category.
