**AEO for web designers means becoming the studio AI assistants name when a business owner needs a new
website — by being crawlable, answering the real cost-and-timeline questions, and earning trust through
a real portfolio and reviews.** The reward is a project plus a recurring care plan, not a bid lost on a
marketplace.

<Callout variant="key" title="Quick answer">
  <p>
    Win three things, in order: <strong>be readable</strong> (a fast, crawlable, server-rendered site
    AI bots can fetch), <strong>answer the real question</strong> (cost, timeline, "custom vs template",
    "do you do SEO and AEO"), and <strong>be trusted</strong> (a real portfolio, consistent listings,
    genuine reviews). The AI names a <strong>few</strong> studios — do all three and you're one of them,
    not a marketplace bid.
  </p>
</Callout>

## Why does AEO matter so much for web designers?

Because hiring someone to rebuild your website is a credibility decision, made on the first good answer.
When an owner decides their site needs work, they ask an assistant and act on what it names — and the
[AI answer names only two or three studios](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations), not a page of
profiles. Pew Research [found people clicked a link just 8% of the time when an AI summary appeared,
versus 15%
without](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer shapes the shortlist. Being the cited studio is the new equivalent of being the shop a
fellow owner refers — and each client is a project plus an ongoing care plan.

## How do web designers get recommended by AI?

By clearing the three gates an owner — and an engine — judge you on, in order.

Most studios have a beautiful but uncrawlable portfolio and inconsistent listings — so they fail gates
one and three without realizing the contest happened. Fixing those is the highest-leverage work, and
it's the same foundation that [ranks you in Google](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together) too.

## Isn't this just SEO?

It's mostly the SEO you should already be doing, pointed at a new surface. The fast, crawlable,
well-reviewed site that ranks is the same one that gets [cited](/learn/is-aeo-replacing-seo). AEO adds
answering owner questions [directly and answer-first](/learn/answer-first-sentence), building [service
pages for extraction](/learn/web-design-service-pages-for-ai), and tightening the [reviews and
portfolio proof](/learn/web-design-reviews-ai-recommendations) answer engines lean on. You don't choose
between them — you run one program that wins both, and it doubles as a [demo you can
sell](/learn/selling-aeo-and-seo-web-design-aeo).

## Where do small studios have the advantage?

In specificity and proof. AI names only a few studios and [citations spread
thin](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands), so a solo designer or small shop that answers "how much does
an ecommerce site cost" or "custom vs Shopify template" better than a marketplace can win the
recommendation. The marketplace is generic; you're specific, proven, and real. That's the
[Originality](/pillars/originality) and [local authority](/learn/local-aeo-for-web-designers) edge a
faceless aggregator can't copy — and a [high-value niche](/learn/ecommerce-web-design-aeo) sharpens it
further.
