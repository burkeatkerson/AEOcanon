## Why do reviews carry so much weight for landscapers?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge reliability — and a homeowner is trusting
you on their property, often every week. An assistant deciding whom to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform reviews to
gauge whether you're reliable, do quality work, and communicate well. For a recurring service, that
corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible,
and the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than
backlinks do.

## How many reviews do I need?

Fewer than you think, fresher than you have. There's no magic count — consistency and recency outweigh
a raw total. A steady stream of genuine recent reviews signals an active, trusted company far better
than a big pile from three years ago. The goal is an ongoing flow: ask happy clients after a project or
at the start of the season, so new reviews keep arriving and the engine keeps seeing a company that's
[currently trusted](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations), not formerly busy.

## Do the words in a review matter?

Yes — specific reviews do more work than generic praise.

Reviews that name what you did — transformed a yard, never miss a mowing, built a clean patio — give
engines specific detail tying you to those services and to reliable work. They reinforce the [questions
homeowners ask](/learn/landscaping-questions-customers-ask-ai), so a handful of detailed reviews can
outperform a wall of generic ones. Ask clients what to mention, and the reviews start doing your AEO
for you.
