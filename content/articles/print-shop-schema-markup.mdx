## What does print shop schema actually do?

It makes your shop's details unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business) labels
your name, address, phone, hours, area served, services, and reviews so engines parse them cleanly
rather than guessing — reinforcing the [consistent identity](/learn/nap-consistency) local recognition
depends on. It's the [structured data for AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern applied to the
trade: clarity for the parser, on top of content that's already clear for a buyer asking about cards,
banners, or signs.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your shop — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your details machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [consistent
listings](/learn/local-aeo-for-print-shops), [genuine
reviews](/learn/print-shop-reviews-ai-recommendations), and [product pages that answer buyer
questions](/learn/print-shop-service-pages-for-ai). And don't fake it: marking up reviews or services
you don't actually offer is a misuse engines can detect — and a fast way to lose trust. Accurate schema
on top of real, [answer-first pages](/learn/answer-first-sentence) is the combination that earns the
[citation](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations).
