## Why does Google Business Profile matter for AI search?

Because it's a structured, trusted confirmation of who and where you are — exactly what an engine needs
to recommend an expert. When the assistant decides whom to name for a near-me or 'consultant for
[industry]' query, it leans on the profile to verify your identity, service area, offerings, and
reputation [before recommending you](/learn/does-google-business-profile-help-ai-search). A complete,
accurate profile makes you an easy recommendation; a thin or stale one leaves the engine unsure and
routes the inquiry elsewhere.

## What should my profile include?

Everything an engine checks before vouching for you.

The reviews on your profile do double duty — they're a top input to [whether AI recommends
you](/learn/consultant-reviews-ai-recommendations) at all.

## Does GBP replace a good website?

No — it's half the picture. The profile is a powerful placement and trust signal, but engines also
retrieve and cite your [actual offering pages](/learn/consultant-service-pages-for-ai) and [published
frameworks](/learn/thought-leadership-aeo-for-consultants) for the detailed questions a profile can't
answer — what an engagement costs, whether you serve a manufacturer or a SaaS company, your point of
view. You need both: a complete profile so the engine can confidently place and trust you, and
answer-first pages plus published expertise for the substance that wins the detailed query. Treat them
as one system, kept [consistent everywhere](/learn/nap-consistency).
