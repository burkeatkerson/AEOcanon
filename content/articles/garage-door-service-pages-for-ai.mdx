## Why one page per service?

Because a citation is awarded to the page that best answers one specific question — and a catch-all
services page answers none of them well. When you give spring repair, opener repair, new door
installation, and off-track service each [their own
page](/learn/should-content-sections-stand-alone), each can go deep on its own cost, timing, and
details — and each becomes citable for its own query. A single page trying to cover everything is
shallow on all of them, so the engine cites a competitor with a dedicated, focused page instead.

## What should each page lead with?

The answer the homeowner came for, before anything about you.

This is [answer-first writing](/learn/answer-first-sentence) applied to the trade: the quotable
answer up top, the depth below, the proof at the bottom.

## What makes a service page extractable?

Plain language and a clean structure. Write the way a homeowner asks — "a broken spring repair
typically costs…" — not in jargon, and use [question-shaped
headings](/learn/question-shaped-headings) the engine can match to a query. Keep each answer in a
self-contained passage so it can be [lifted without the surrounding
page](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage), reinforce it with [LocalBusiness
schema](/learn/garage-door-schema-markup), and make sure the page is fast and crawlable.
Answer-first, focused, and proven — that's the page an engine cites and a homeowner calls.
