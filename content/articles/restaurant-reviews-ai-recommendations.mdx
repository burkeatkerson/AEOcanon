## Why do reviews carry so much weight for restaurants?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge which places diners actually love — and
dining is driven by reputation. An assistant deciding what to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform reviews to
gauge whether you're a great choice. For a restaurant, that corroborated reputation is decisive — it's
the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the [off-site
mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks do.

## Do reviews that mention dishes matter more?

Yes — dish-level reviews are gold, because they speak the language of discovery.

A review naming a dish, the service, or the vibe gives the engine detail to tie you to "best carbonara
near me" or "romantic dinner nearby" — the exact [discovery and occasion
queries](/learn/restaurant-questions-customers-ask-ai) diners ask. Invite happy guests to mention what
they loved, and the reviews start matching you to the searches that fill tables.

## How many reviews — and how fresh?

Fresh beats big. There's no magic count — recency and a steady flow outweigh a stale total. A restaurant
with current reviews that mention dishes signals an active, loved place far better than one coasting on
old ratings. Build a simple habit: invite happy diners to review, and [respond to
them](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations) — engagement signals an active restaurant that cares,
which is exactly the place an engine wants to recommend.
