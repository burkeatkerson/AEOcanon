## Why target both local and online?

Because the same boutique can be the answer to two different questions — and most shops only answer one.
A local shopper asks "boutique near me for [style]"; an online shopper asks "where can I buy [item]
online" or "ships to me." Both are [answered by AI naming a few shops](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations),
and a boutique with readable products, clear shipping, and consistent listings can win both. Skipping
the online intent hands the ship-to-me sale to a marketplace; skipping the local intent hands the visit
to a competitor. Omnichannel AEO captures the whole demand.

## How does AI recommend products to online shoppers?

By matching the request against products it can read — from your pages and your feed.

## What wins the online, ship-to-me shopper?

Clarity and reassurance. The online shopper can't try it on, so the engine recommends the boutique whose
[readable products](/learn/boutique-product-pages-for-ai) make fit and style obvious, whose shipping and
returns are clear, and whose [reviews](/learn/boutique-reviews-ai-recommendations) say the fit was true
and returns were easy. Get those right and the same content that wins
[near-me](/learn/near-me-boutique-ai-search) also wins ship-to-me — one program, two revenue streams,
both [owned by you](/learn/grow-a-boutique-with-ai-search) instead of a marketplace.
