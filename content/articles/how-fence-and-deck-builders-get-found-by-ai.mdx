**Fence and deck builders get found by AI search when their site is crawlable, answers the real
questions homeowners ask, and is backed by consistent local trust signals like reviews, license, and
project photos.** The AI retrieves and ranks the few sources that clear all three — so the builder who
does is the one named in the answer.

## What does an AI engine actually do to find you?

It retrieves, ranks, and names. When someone asks for a deck builder or about a fencing project, the
engine pulls the sources it can read, judges which best answer the question and look trustworthy, and
[names just a few](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations) in the answer. Each step is a filter: if a
crawler can't fetch your page, you're out before ranking; if your page doesn't answer the question,
you're out at ranking; if your trust signals are thin, a competitor gets named instead. Being found
means clearing every filter.

## What are the three gates?

The same three an engine — and a homeowner — apply, in order.

## Why can't customers find my fence and deck business?

Almost always one specific broken gate — not bad luck. Your site is a photo-heavy portfolio that
[crawlers see as empty](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), or it's slow; your pages bury the
answer under a gallery of past projects; or your reviews and listings are thin and inconsistent. The
fix is diagnostic: find the *highest* broken gate and repair it first, because a great reputation
can't rescue a site bots can't read, and a fast site can't rescue a page that never answers what a
composite deck costs. This is the same
[why-isn't-my-business-showing-up](/learn/why-isnt-my-local-business-showing-up-in-ai) logic, applied
to the trade.

## Does ranking in Google mean AI can find me?

Not on its own. Ranking proves Google can read and rank you — a good sign for the first gate — but AI
citation also needs an [extractable, answer-first page](/learn/answer-first-sentence) and trusted
local signals. A builder can rank on page one yet be absent from AI answers if the answer is buried
below a slideshow of past decks or off-site trust is thin. The overlap is real but not total: clear
all three gates and you get [found in both](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).
