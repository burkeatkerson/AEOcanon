## Why do reviews carry so much weight for electricians?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge trust — and electrical work is
all about trust. An assistant deciding whom to recommend [synthesizes sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations)
from your Google and third-party reviews to gauge whether you're reliable, safe, and good
at the work. For a trade where the customer is letting you into their panel, that
corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility)
made visible, and the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate
with AI visibility more than backlinks do.

## How many reviews do I need?

Fewer than you think, fresher than you have. There's no magic count — consistency and
recency outweigh a raw total. A steady stream of genuine recent reviews signals an active,
trusted business far better than a big pile from three years ago. The goal is an ongoing
flow: build a simple habit of asking after every completed job, so new reviews keep
arriving and the engine keeps seeing a business that's [currently
trusted](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations), not formerly busy.

## Do the words in a review matter?

Yes — specific reviews do more work than generic praise.

Reviews that name what you did — panel upgrade, EV charger, fixed a tripping breaker —
give engines specific detail tying you to those exact services and to safe, professional
work. They reinforce the [questions customers ask](/learn/electrical-questions-customers-ask-ai),
so a handful of detailed reviews can outperform a wall of generic ones. Ask customers what
to mention, and the reviews start doing your AEO for you.
