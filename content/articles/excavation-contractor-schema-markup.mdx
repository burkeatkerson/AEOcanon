## What does excavation schema actually do?

It makes your business data unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business), and the
GeneralContractor or HomeAndConstructionBusiness subtype, labels your name, address, phone, hours, area
served, services, and reviews so engines parse them cleanly rather than guessing from prose —
reinforcing the [consistent identity](/learn/nap-consistency) local recognition depends on. It's the
[structured data for AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern applied to the trade: clarity for the
parser, on top of content that's already clear for the customer.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your excavation business — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your data machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [consistent
identity](/learn/local-aeo-for-excavation-contractors), [genuine
reviews](/learn/excavation-contractor-reviews-ai-recommendations), verifiable license and bonding, and
[pages that answer the cost and permit questions](/learn/excavation-contractor-service-pages-for-ai).
And don't fake it: marking up reviews or credentials that don't match your visible page is a misuse
engines can detect — [especially damaging](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations) for a high-trust,
high-ticket trade where a bad dig is costly. Accurate schema on top of real proof is the combination
that works.
