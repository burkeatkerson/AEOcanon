## Do the fundamentals transfer across engines?

Yes — that's the key point. Crawlable pages, [answer-first
content](/learn/answer-first-sentence), [authority](/pillars/authority), and
[freshness](/pillars/freshness) earn citations on ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI, and
the rest, because all of them reward the same underlying qualities. So you build one
strong foundation rather than maintaining separate programs — the
[engine-specific guides](/learn/how-to-get-cited-by-chatgpt) are refinements on that
common base, not replacements for it.

## Where do engines actually differ?

In emphasis, mainly. Engines draw on [different
sources](/learn/chatgpt-vs-perplexity-vs-google-ai-overviews), weight signals
differently, and some lean more on [live retrieval than
others](/learn/base-vs-search-augmented-models) — so citation results vary between
them. But those are differences in how the *same* fundamentals are weighted, not
entirely different rulebooks. That's why one foundation serves all engines, while the
variation is real enough to be worth measuring.

## How do I handle the differences without duplicating work?

Measure per engine, then refine selectively. Build the shared foundation once, [track
citation separately for each engine](/learn/per-engine-vs-blended-measurement), and
make targeted adjustments only where the data shows a gap — emphasizing a particular
source or freshness for the engine that needs it. Treating engine-specific work as
refinement on top of the common foundation, rather than a parallel effort, is how the
[Adaptability pillar](/pillars/adaptability) captures the differences without
multiplying the work.
