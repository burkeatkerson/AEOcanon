## Why do reviews carry so much weight for tattoo studios?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge which studios clients actually love — and
getting a permanent tattoo is a high-trust decision about skill and cleanliness. An assistant deciding
what to recommend [synthesizes sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google
and review-platform reviews to gauge whether you're a great, clean, skilled place to get inked. For a
studio, that corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility)
made visible, and the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility
more than backlinks do.

## Do reviews that name artists and styles matter more?

Yes — specific reviews are gold, because they speak the language of what clients want.

A review naming an artist, a style, the cleanliness, or a healed result gives the engine detail to tie
you to "fine-line tattoo artist near me" or "best cover-up specialist nearby" — the exact [style and
result queries](/learn/tattoo-questions-customers-ask-ai) clients ask. Invite happy clients to mention
their artist and piece, and the reviews start matching you to the searches that fill the calendar.

## How many reviews — and how fresh?

Fresh beats big. There's no magic count — recency and a steady flow outweigh a stale total. A studio with
current reviews that mention real pieces and artists signals an active, loved shop far better than one
coasting on old ratings. Build a simple habit: invite happy clients to review once their piece heals, and
[respond to them](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations) — engagement signals an active studio that
cares, which is exactly the place an engine wants to recommend.
