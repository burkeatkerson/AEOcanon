## How is brewery events and bookings AEO different?

The intent is planning, not a walk-in. An event planner, office manager, or host booking a birthday
researches carefully — and asks the assistant detailed logistics questions long before they call.
They're researching capacity, pricing, tours, and whether you can handle their group and food needs.
Because [AEO works for these higher-consideration decisions](/learn/does-aeo-work-for-b2b), the brewery
whose pages answer those questions clearly gets [cited during the
research](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations) — and wins a high-value booking most breweries never
even surface for.

## What questions do planners ask?

Logistics-focused and specific — each worth a clear answer on a dedicated page.

## How do I win event and booking citations?

Give private events, buyouts, tours, and corporate bookings their own answer-first pages — not a buried
line on your homepage. Publish capacity, pricing structure, package and tour options, food arrangements,
and lead time in readable text, reinforced by your [reviews](/learn/brewery-reviews-ai-recommendations)
and a clear inquiry path. Most breweries either omit this entirely or trap it in a PDF, so a clear,
dedicated page is an easy way to get cited for valuable corporate, group, and fundraiser bookings —
repeat, high-margin revenue that compounds your [growth](/learn/grow-a-brewery-with-ai-search).
