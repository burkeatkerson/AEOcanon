## Why is the emergency search the whole game for towing?

Because it's the most urgent search there is, and it converts on the spot. When a driver is stranded on
the shoulder, they aren't comparison-shopping — they ask an assistant, get an answer, and call the first
credible name. There's no page two, and the [AI answer names only one or two
companies](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Pew Research [found people click a link just 8% of the
time when an AI summary
appears](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer *is* the call. For a tow company, owning that moment is the entire business — and a direct
call pays full price.

## Why does 24-hour availability decide the call?

Because 'tow truck near me now' is a placement-and-availability question, and the engine answers it from
your stated hours. If your availability conflicts between your site and your Google profile, the engine
can't confidently say you can come *now* — so it names a competitor it's sure about. State your 24-hour
availability and dispatch radius accurately and identically everywhere, and you remove the single most
common reason a tow company loses the emergency call. This is the same [local
certainty](/learn/local-aeo-for-towing) that wins every near-me query, sharpened to the minute.
