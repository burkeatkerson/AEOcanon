## Why is the tap list the most important page?

Because what you pour is what drinkers search for — and a citation goes to the page the engine can read.
When someone asks "best hazy IPA near me" or "where can I get a good sour," the engine matches the query
against tap lists it can [actually parse](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site). If your tap list is a
chalkboard photo or an embed, the engine doesn't know what you pour, so you're invisible for every
style-level query — the most valuable discovery searches there are. A readable tap list turns your whole
lineup into citable answers.

## What makes a tap list page citable?

Readable text, organized the way drinkers think.

## Why describe beers, not just list them?

Because description is the language of discovery. Drinkers ask AI for "the best hazy IPA near me" or "a
good barrel-aged stout," and a bare list of names gives the engine little to match. A sentence describing
each flagship beer — its style, ABV, flavor, what makes it stand out — gives the engine the [extractable
detail](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage) that ties you to those exact searches, and mirrors the
[beer-level reviews](/learn/brewery-reviews-ai-recommendations) that drinkers write. A readable,
described tap list is the foundation every other brewery AEO move builds on.
