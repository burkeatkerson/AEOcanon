## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each gentle, great groom earns a review and a referral; [readable
services](/learn/pet-grooming-service-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews earn citations for 'best dog
groomer near me'; those bring new clients; and clients who rebook produce more reviews and referrals —
while the ad spend you save funds better tools, training, and time per pet. The paid-ad and
marketplace-app model is the opposite: a faucet you pay to keep running, that stops the moment you stop
(and the app owns the client). AEO builds the [compounding asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead
— recurring clients who found you and come back.

## Why is rebooking central to grooming growth?

Because a grooming business's value *is* recurring revenue, and filling the top of the funnel only pays
off if clients come back. A dog groomed every 6 weeks for years is worth many times a one-time wash —
and loyal, happy clients are exactly who write the [reviews that mention gentle handling and
breeds](/learn/pet-grooming-reviews-ai-recommendations) and refer friends, feeding the AI
recommendations that bring the next clients. So growth isn't just AEO at the top; it's AEO plus
rebooking together, each compounding the other. Win clients with citations, keep them with gentle, great
grooming, and the flywheel accelerates.

## How does this lower my cost per client?

By replacing recurring ad spend and app commissions with assets that keep paying. A paid ad costs every
time and stops when you stop; an app takes a cut of every booking forever. An earned citation, a loyal
rebooking client, a referral, or a [premium mobile or de-shed
package](/learn/mobile-pet-grooming-aeo) costs mostly upfront and pays for years. As your cited,
referred, and rebooking base grows, your cost per client falls and your revenue stabilizes — and
because AI names only a few places, a real, well-reviewed local groomer can win recommendations a
faceless chain listing [can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
