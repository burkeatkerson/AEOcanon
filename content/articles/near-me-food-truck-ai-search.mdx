## Why is 'near me' the highest-value food truck search?

Because it's the moment of decision, and it converts immediately. When someone asks "food trucks near me
right now" or "best tacos nearby," they're choosing where to eat in the next few minutes — and the [AI
answer names only a few places](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Pew Research [found people click
a link just 8% of the time when an AI summary
appears](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer *is* the crowd. For a truck, owning that moment is the difference between a line at the
window and an empty street.

In-the-moment, specific questions — and you should own the ones that fit your truck.

## Why is a current location the make-or-break signal?

Because a truck's whole value is being somewhere specific right now, and the engine answers
find-a-truck-now and 'open now' straight from your location signals. A current, readable spot means you get
recommended when a hungry customer is deciding; a stale one means you're dropped from the answer — or you
send someone to yesterday's location and earn a bad review. Keeping today's spot and your schedule current
and readable everywhere is the highest-leverage move for winning the in-the-moment crowd, and it's the same
[local certainty](/learn/local-aeo-for-food-trucks) that wins every near-me query.
