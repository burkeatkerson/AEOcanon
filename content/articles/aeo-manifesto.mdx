**We believe the only durable strategy in Answer Engine Optimization is to
genuinely deserve the citation.** [Answer engines](/glossary/answer-engine) are built to surface the most
readable, relevant, trustworthy, and current source — so the winning move is not
to trick the machine but to become that source. This is the conviction behind
[the AEO Canon](/learn/aeo-canon): every pillar is also, underneath, just a
principle of honest and useful communication.

## What changed, and why it matters

For thirty years, the web asked one question: can people find you? The answer was
a list of ten blue links, and the goal was to be filed correctly in the
directory. That question has been joined by a new one, and it changes the work
entirely: **when the machine speaks, does it say your name?**

When an answer engine responds, it does not hand the user a menu of links to
evaluate. It returns a single synthesized answer, assembled from the handful of
sources it trusts most. The [cited source](/glossary/citation) receives the visibility, the
credibility, and the click. Every uncited source receives nothing. In that world,
the only visibility worth having is being the source the answer was built from —
which is why we care so much about deserving it. For the mechanics of how that
selection works, see [how AI engines choose what to cite](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations).

This is a higher bar than the old game, and we think that is good. Ranking could
be gamed at the margins — a little keyword density here, a few purchased links
there — because the engine was matching strings and counting votes. An answer
engine is doing something closer to judgment: deciding whose words are safe to
repeat under its own name. You cannot bluff your way into that. The only reliable
path to being quoted is to be genuinely worth quoting — clear, correct,
well-evidenced, and original. The discipline rewards substance because substance
is what it is built to detect.

## What do we believe about AEO?

We believe AEO is not a set of tricks for manipulating machine behavior — it is
the practice of being genuinely excellent at communicating, and building the
conditions under which any well-designed engine recognizes that excellence. These
are our eight beliefs, one for each pillar of the Canon.

## Why does deserving the citation matter?

It matters because every other approach is borrowed time. The history of search
optimization is a graveyard of tricks that worked until the engine got smarter —
keyword stuffing, link schemes, doorway pages. The research on AEO already shows
the same pattern: the [Princeton GEO study (arXiv 2311.09735)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.09735)
found that keyword stuffing produced no visibility gain, while citations,
quotations, and statistics did. The tactics that survive are the ones aligned with
what the engine is genuinely trying to do.

This is the quiet good news of the whole discipline. Because answer engines are
built to find the best source, the work of AEO converges with the work of being
worth finding. Even the technical pillars reward honesty over trickery:
[Vercel's analysis of 500 million crawler requests](https://vercel.com/blog/the-rise-of-the-ai-crawler)
shows engines simply read the plain HTML you serve — there is no clever rendering
to exploit, only the question of whether you made your content readable. You do not have to choose between optimizing for machines and
serving people — done right, they are the same act. The
[Ahrefs study of 75,000 brands](https://ahrefs.com/blog/ai-overview-brand-correlation/)
makes the point concretely: the strongest driver of AI visibility was being
genuinely talked about across the web, not any on-page manipulation.

## What do we stand for?

We stand for content that deserves to be cited — content that answers the real
question, backs every claim with evidence, says something nobody else has said,
and is structured to be lifted and quoted in the first sentence. We stand against
hollow optimization: against gaming a signal rather than earning a reputation,
against padding rather than answering, against chasing the engine instead of
serving the reader the engine serves.

We hold these beliefs not because they are clever, but because they are right —
and because, conveniently, what is right is also what works. The cynical reading
of AEO is that it is a new arena for manipulation; the honest reading, and the one
the evidence supports, is that it finally aligns incentives — rewarding the people
who do the real work of being clear, correct, and original. That is the wager of
the AEO Canon: that the most thorough, honest, original, and useful answer is the
one the engine pulls. To put the beliefs into practice, work
[the AEO Canon](/learn/aeo-canon) pillar by pillar, run the
[Canon diagnostic](/learn/aeo-canon-diagnostic) to find where you fall short, and
start from [what is AEO](/learn/what-is-aeo) if the discipline is new to you.
