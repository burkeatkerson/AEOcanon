location and remote-support signals clear — consistent name, address, and phone, a complete Google
Business Profile, and pages that state who and where you serve.** Engines recommend the MSP they can
confidently place.

## What makes an MSP 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about who you are, where you're based, and who you serve. An answer engine recommends a
firm for "near me" only when it can [place you with certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend)
— right area, right kind of client. That certainty comes from signals that all agree: matching
name-address-phone, a complete profile, a stated service area (local on-site and remote), and reviews.
When the signals agree, you're an easy recommendation; when they conflict, the engine favors a clearer
competitor.

Build location and service certainty deliberately.

This is the [near-me ranking](/learn/how-to-rank-in-ai-for-near-me-searches) discipline applied to the
industry — and it's also where [service-area pages](/learn/local-service-area-pages-for-ai) earn their
keep.

## Why does NAP consistency matter so much?

Because a mismatch is a doubt, and engines route around doubt. If your phone number differs between
your site and your Google profile, or an old address still shows in a directory, the engine can't be
sure which firm you are or where you operate — so it favors a competitor it can [place with
confidence](/learn/nap-consistency). For MSPs that have moved, merged, or rebranded, this is a common
and quiet leak. Clean it up and you remove the single most fixable reason an engine skips you on a
near-me query.
