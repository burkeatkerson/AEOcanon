## How is commercial garage door AEO different?

The intent is research and uptime, not rescue. Where a homeowner with a stuck door acts on the first
answer, a facility manager evaluates carefully — and asks the assistant detailed questions long
before contacting anyone. They're researching door types, dock equipment, and whether you can keep
their operation running. Because [AEO works for B2B](/learn/does-aeo-work-for-b2b) exactly here, the
company whose pages answer those questions expertly gets [cited during the
research](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations) — and lands on the shortlist before a single call.
And commercial accounts are recurring relationships, so each one won is worth far more than a single
repair.

## What questions do commercial buyers ask?

Operational, evaluation-focused, and substantive — each worth a thorough page.

## What content wins commercial citations?

Depth backed by proof. Commercial decisions carry uptime stakes and longer research cycles, so your
pages should demonstrate genuine expertise — product knowledge, maintenance program design,
certifications, and clear response commitments — in thorough,
[answer-first](/learn/answer-first-sentence) pages. The fundamentals are the same as residential: be
readable, answer the real question, be trusted. Commercial just rewards [depth and
credibility](/pillars/credibility) more heavily, because the buyer is betting their operation's
uptime on getting the choice right. Be the page that proves you're the safe expert, and you shape
the shortlist.
