## How is catering and events AEO different?

The intent is planning, not a walk-up. A couple booking a wedding, an office manager planning a corporate
lunch, or a host throwing a party researches carefully — and asks the assistant detailed logistics questions
long before they call. They're researching whether you can handle their guest count, their budget, and
their dietary needs. Because [AEO works for these higher-consideration
decisions](/learn/does-aeo-work-for-b2b), the truck whose pages answer those questions clearly gets [cited
during the research](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations) — and wins the high-value booking most trucks
never even surface for.

## What questions do organizers ask?

Logistics-focused and specific — each worth a clear answer on a dedicated page.

## How do I win catering and event citations?

Give catering and private events their own answer-first page — not a buried highlight on your social feed.
Publish capacity, pricing structure, sample catering menus, dietary accommodations, travel area, and lead
time in readable text, reinforced by your [reviews](/learn/food-truck-reviews-ai-recommendations) and a
clear inquiry path. Most trucks either omit this entirely or trap it in social, so a clear, dedicated page
is an easy way to get cited for valuable wedding, corporate, and party bookings — repeat, high-margin
revenue that compounds your [growth](/learn/grow-a-food-truck-business-with-ai-search).
