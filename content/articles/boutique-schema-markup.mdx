## What does boutique schema actually do?

It makes your shop's details unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business), and the
ClothingStore subtype, labels your name, address, hours, and category so engines parse them cleanly
rather than guessing — and Product schema labels each item's brand, sizes, and price — reinforcing the
[consistent identity](/learn/nap-consistency) local recommendations depend on. It's the [structured
data for AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern applied to retail: clarity for the parser, on top
of products and policies that are already clear and readable for the shopper.

The full, accurate picture of your shop — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your details machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [readable products and
sizing](/learn/boutique-product-pages-for-ai) the crawler can parse, [accurate hours and
policies](/learn/local-aeo-for-boutiques), and [genuine
reviews](/learn/boutique-reviews-ai-recommendations). Schema can't rescue a lookbook-only site or wrong
hours — and faking reviews or details in markup is a misuse engines can
[detect](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations). Accurate schema on top of a readable, real site is the
combination that works.
