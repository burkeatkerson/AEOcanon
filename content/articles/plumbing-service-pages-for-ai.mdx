**Write plumbing service pages AI will cite by giving each service its own page that
leads with the answer to the cost, timing, and service-area questions, in plain
language a customer and an engine can lift.** One self-contained, crawlable page per
service beats a single bloated services page every time.

## Why one page per service?

Because engines cite the page that answers a *specific* question best, and a bloated
"all our services" page answers none of them in depth. A dedicated page for water
heater repair can fully address the cost factors, timing, and process for *that*
service — so it gets cited for "how much to replace a water heater," while a generic
services page loses to a competitor's focused one. This is the
[Extractability pillar](/pillars/extractability) and the
[one-question-per-page principle](/learn/how-many-questions-per-page) applied to a
trade.

## What should each page lead with?

The answer the customer came for. Open with a clear, direct statement of what the
service costs (or what drives the price), how fast you can do it, and the areas you
serve — *before* company history or marketing copy. That [answer-first
opening](/learn/answer-first-sentence) is what both a hurried customer and an engine
lift — [Nielsen Norman Group found people scan rather than read web pages word-for-word](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/how-users-read-on-the-web/). Bury the cost under three paragraphs of "why choose us" and you've hidden the one
thing the question was about.

## How do I structure the rest of the page?

In self-contained sections, each answering one real question. After the answer-first
lead, cover the cost factors, the process, what's included, the timing, and the service
area — each [standing on its own](/learn/should-content-sections-stand-alone) so an
engine can lift any one. Add proof: real photos, reviews, and specifics only you'd
know. Map the sections to the actual
[questions customers ask](/learn/plumbing-questions-customers-ask-ai), and reinforce the
page with [correct schema](/learn/plumber-schema-markup).

## How long should a service page be?

Complete, not long. Cover cost, timing, process, what's included, and area thoroughly —
depth that answers genuine questions helps citation — but don't pad for word count,
which [does nothing](/learn/long-form-vs-short-form-content). The test is whether a
customer's real questions about that service are all answered on the page. Hit that and
stop. Building these pages for every service across a site is exactly the kind of work
the [done-for-you rebuild](/pricing) handles in one pass.
