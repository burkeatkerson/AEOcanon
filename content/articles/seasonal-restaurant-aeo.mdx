## Why does seasonality matter for restaurants?

Because dining demand spikes around occasions, and diners plan them ahead. Valentine's Day, Mother's
Day, holiday parties, patio season, restaurant week, and New Year's Eve all bring searches weeks before
the date — "Valentine's dinner reservations near me," "restaurants for a holiday party." The restaurant
whose answer is already published, crawled, and trusted [gets cited the moment planning
starts](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo) — while competitors are still updating their PDF.
It's the [Freshness pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned into an occasion calendar.

## How do I prepare for each occasion?

Build the content while it's calm, so it's ready when diners start booking.

## Should I make a new page each year?

No — update one durable page per recurring occasion. A persistent 'Valentine's Day dinner' or 'private
holiday parties' page [accumulates authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh the date,
menu, and details each year. That beats spinning up a throwaway page annually that starts from zero and
splits your signals. Keep one page per occasion, refresh it ahead of the date, and link it to your
[catering and events page](/learn/catering-and-events-restaurant-aeo) — one durable page, refreshed and
ready, beats scrambling when the rush arrives.
