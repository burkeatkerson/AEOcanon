## Why do reviews carry so much weight for florists?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge which shops people trust — and a flower order
is an emotional, often time-sensitive purchase. An assistant deciding what to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform reviews to
gauge whether you deliver beautiful arrangements, on time. For a florist, that corroborated reputation
is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the [off-site
mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks do.

## Do reviews that mention delivery matter more?

Yes — because so much of flower-buying is about getting an order there on time.

A review naming a beautiful arrangement and reliable, on-time delivery reassures the engine you fulfill
orders dependably — exactly what an urgent buyer searching [same-day](/learn/same-day-flower-delivery-ai-search)
needs to hear. Invite happy buyers to mention what they ordered and that it arrived on time, and the
reviews start matching you to the searches that bring orders.

## How many reviews — and how fresh?

Fresh beats big. There's no magic count — recency and a steady flow outweigh a stale total. A shop with
current reviews that mention arrangements and delivery signals an active, trusted business far better
than one coasting on old ratings. Build a simple habit: invite happy buyers to review, and [respond to
them](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations) — engagement signals an active shop that cares,
which is exactly what an engine wants to recommend.
