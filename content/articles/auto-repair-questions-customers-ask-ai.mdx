('how much for brakes'), maintenance ('when do I need a timing belt'), and trust ('is this repair
quote fair').** Mapping each question to the page that should own it is the core of an auto repair
AEO content plan.

## How do I find the exact questions?

Listen where drivers already ask. Mine your phone log and service calls for the questions people
actually voice, check Reddit threads, car forums, and the People Also Ask box, and [prompt the
assistants directly](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask) on common repairs to see the
follow-ups they surface. Capture the *natural wording* — "why is my car shaking when I brake"
beats "brake rotor runout symptoms" — because engines match the driver's phrasing, not your
jargon. Then prioritize by intent and value.

## Should I answer symptom and trust questions?

Yes — they're how you earn the trust that books the repair *and* the next one. Answering "why is
my car shaking" or "is this quote fair" helpfully makes you the source a driver remembers when
they need a shop. Symptom content captures the worried discovery moment; trust content wins the
wary driver who's been burned before. Both build the credibility and visibility engines
[reward](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore) — the opposite of a thin directory page. Map
every bucket to a page and you've built the content plan that gets a shop cited.
