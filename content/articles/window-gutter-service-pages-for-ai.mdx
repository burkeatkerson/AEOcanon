**Write window and gutter service pages AI will cite by giving each service its own page that leads with
the answer to the cost, scope, and service-area questions, in plain language a homeowner and an engine
can lift.** One self-contained, crawlable page per service beats a single bloated services page every
time.

    pressure washing — and <strong>lead with the answer</strong> to what it costs, what's included, and
    whether you serve their area. Make each page <strong>self-contained and crawlable</strong>, not a
    photo gallery. One focused page per service beats one bloated services page.
  </p>
</Callout>

## Why one page per service?

Because a citation is awarded to the page that best answers one specific question — and a catch-all
services page or photo gallery answers none of them well. When you give window cleaning, gutter
cleaning, gutter guards, and pressure washing each [their own
page](/learn/should-content-sections-stand-alone), each can go deep on its own cost, scope, and details
— and each becomes citable for its own query. A single page trying to cover everything is shallow on all
of them, so the engine cites a competitor with a dedicated, focused page.

## What should each page lead with?

The answer the homeowner came for, before anything about you.

This is [answer-first writing](/learn/answer-first-sentence) applied to the trade: the quotable answer up
top, the depth below, the proof at the bottom — described, not just shown.

## What makes a service page extractable?

Plain language and a clean structure. Write the way a homeowner asks — "gutter cleaning on a two-story
home typically runs …" — not in jargon, and use [question-shaped
headings](/learn/question-shaped-headings) the engine can match to a query. Keep each answer in a
self-contained passage so it can be [lifted without the surrounding
page](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage), reinforce it with [LocalBusiness
schema](/learn/window-gutter-schema-markup), and make sure the page is fast and crawlable — not a slow
gallery. Answer-first, focused, and proven — that's the page an engine cites and a homeowner books.
