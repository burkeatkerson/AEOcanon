## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a store the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine can
read your products and place you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as where to buy — and
that leans harder on whether you deliver reliably and buyers love the experience. The engine recommends
the store it can [confidently confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, reliable, and
well-reviewed, because when it recommends a high-ticket purchase it's putting its credibility behind the
experience. Your job is to make that confirmation easy — readable products, accurate delivery and
pricing, and genuine reviews.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). Wrong hours, an unreadable catalog, or a thin review profile
introduces doubt, and doubt sends a high-ticket buyer elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one furniture store over another?

Because it picks the one it's most confident will deliver. Faced with several options, the engine names
the store whose signals best match the request — right area, carries the style, delivers and assembles
reliably — and whose reviews most clearly say buyers love the quality and the orders arrive on time. The
competitor with an image-only catalog, wrong hours, or thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it loses.
Winning is about being the [easiest to verify as the reliable, loved local
choice](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — which is exactly what this library builds,
piece by piece.
