## Why is 'near me' the highest-value salon search?

Because it's the moment someone decides to book, and it converts into recurring revenue. When a client
asks "best salon near me" or "balayage specialist nearby," they're ready to commit — and the [AI answer
names only a few places](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Pew Research [found people click a link
just 8% of the time when an AI summary
appears](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer *is* the shortlist. For a salon, owning that moment is the difference between a full
calendar and an empty chair — and every client won rebooks again and again.

Ready-to-book, specific questions — and you should own the ones that fit your salon.

## Why does matching your specialty matter so much?

Because clients search by exactly what they want, and a generic salon matches nothing in particular. The
person searching "balayage specialist near me" or "barber that does proper fades" wants a place that
clearly fits — and if your site states your specialty in plain text, the engine can match you to that
specific, high-intent query. A vague "we do all hair for everyone" page is invisible to these searches.
Be specific about what you're great at, back it with [reviews that prove
it](/learn/salon-reviews-ai-recommendations), and you win the clients most likely to rebook — the
[Originality](/pillars/originality) and [Alignment](/pillars/alignment) edge that fills the chair.
