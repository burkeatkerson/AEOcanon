**A marketing agency needs a website rebuild for AEO when the site is a heavy client-rendered showcase,
slow, or built without per-service answer-first pages and schema — because no amount of content fixes a
foundation engines can't parse.** For a marketing firm, an unreadable site is also a credibility
problem.

## Why is the site the binding constraint?

Because [access is the first gate](/pillars/access), and a gate you fail ends the contest before
content matters. If an AI crawler fetches your page and sees an empty shell — because the showcase
renders only in the browser — or the page is too slow, you're invisible no matter how good your work or
your reviews are. Many agency sites are gorgeous, animation-heavy builds that [give a bot almost
nothing](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site). That's not a content problem you can write your way
out of; it's a foundation problem — and an uncomfortable one for a firm that sells marketing.

## How do I tell if my site is hurting me?

Run two quick tests, and look for the structural gaps.

<Steps
  items={[
    { title: "The JavaScript-off test", body: "Load a key page with JavaScript disabled. If the content vanishes, AI crawlers likely see the same empty page — a fatal access problem common to showcase sites." },
    { title: "The speed test", body: "Check your load time. Slow, heavy pages get crawled less and trusted less; speed is part of whether you're readable at all." },
    { title: "The structure test", body: "Do you have a dedicated page per service with scope and results, or one thin 'what we do' page? No per-service pages means nothing focused to cite." },
    { title: "The schema test", body: "Is there accurate ProfessionalService/LocalBusiness structured data, or none? Missing or wrong schema leaves the engine guessing." },
  ]}
/>

If a page is empty without scripts, thin, slow, has no [dedicated service
pages](/learn/marketing-agency-service-pages-for-ai), or lacks [clean
schema](/learn/marketing-agency-schema-markup), the site is working against you. A fast, server-rendered
foundation with real text content is what makes everything else possible.

## Can't I just add content instead?

Only if the foundation is already sound. Adding [answer-first
pages](/learn/answer-first-sentence) to a fast, crawlable site works beautifully — that's the whole
program. But adding content to a heavy, slow, or client-rendered showcase is building on sand: the
engine still can't read or trust it, so the new pages never get cited. The honest sequence is foundation
first, content second. Get the access layer right —
[server-rendered](/learn/server-side-vs-client-side-rendering), fast, structured — and the content you
publish on top finally has a chance to be found.
