## Why are readable products the most important pages?

Because what you carry is what shoppers search for — and a citation goes to the page the engine can
read. When someone asks "plus-size dresses near me" or "where to buy sustainable denim," the engine
matches the query against products and styles it can [actually
parse](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site). If your site is all lookbook photos with no
descriptions, the engine doesn't know what you sell or who you fit, so you're invisible for those style
and sizing queries — the most valuable, highest-intent searches. Readable products turn your whole shop
into citable answers.

## What makes a product page citable?

Readable text, organized the way shoppers think.

## Why describe products, not just show them?

Because description is the language of discovery. Shoppers ask AI for "a wedding-guest dress under $150"
or "ethical everyday basics in my size," and a wall of lookbook photos gives the engine nothing to
match. A sentence describing each piece — its style, fit, fabric, the occasion it suits, the sizes it
comes in — gives the engine the [extractable detail](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage) that ties
you to those searches, and mirrors the [reviews](/learn/boutique-reviews-ai-recommendations) shoppers
write about fit and style. Readable, described products and sizing are the foundation every other
boutique AEO move builds on.
