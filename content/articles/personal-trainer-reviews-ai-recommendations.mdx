## Why do reviews carry so much weight for personal trainers?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge which trainers clients actually get results
with — and hiring a coach is a trust-and-results decision. An assistant deciding whom to recommend
[synthesizes sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform
reviews to gauge whether you're a coach who delivers. For a trainer, that corroborated reputation is
decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the [off-site
mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks do.

## Do reviews that mention results and specialty matter more?

Yes — specific reviews are gold, because they speak the language of what clients want.

A review naming a result, a specialty, or a transformation gives the engine detail to tie you to
"weight-loss trainer near me" or "postpartum personal trainer nearby" — the exact [fit and result
queries](/learn/personal-trainer-questions-customers-ask-ai) clients ask. Invite happy clients to
mention what they achieved, and the reviews start matching you to the searches that fill your calendar.

## How many reviews — and how fresh?

Fresh beats big. There's no magic count — recency and a steady flow outweigh a stale total. A trainer
with current reviews that mention real results signals an active, in-demand coach far better than one
coasting on old ratings. Build a simple habit: invite happy clients to review at milestones (first goal
hit, package renewal), and [respond to them](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations) — engagement
signals an active coach who cares, which is exactly whom an engine wants to recommend.
