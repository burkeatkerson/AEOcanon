**AEO should have one accountable owner for citation share — usually whoever
coordinates SEO and content — with input from PR, dev, and analytics, because the
signals span teams.** The key isn't a new department but a single owner aligning the
existing functions toward being cited.

## Should AEO be its own department?

Usually not, at least at first. Because AEO [reuses most of your SEO and content
foundation](/learn/aeo-seo-integration-guide) and depends on PR, dev, and analytics, it
works best as a cross-functional responsibility with one owner rather than a separate
silo competing for budget. As [it sits across the
stack](/learn/aeo-in-the-marketing-stack) rather than beside it, a dedicated team can
come later as investment grows — but you start by pointing existing teams at a new
outcome.

## Why does it need a single owner?

Because the signals are spread across teams, and without one accountable person they
fall through the cracks. Pages come from SEO and dev, mentions from [PR and
social](/learn/get-brand-mentions-without-links) — which [correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks do](https://ahrefs.com/blog/ai-overview-brand-correlation/) — content from editorial, and
[measurement from analytics](/learn/what-aeo-metrics-to-track). One owner for citation
share makes sure those align toward the same goal rather than each optimizing its own
metric while no one owns the citation outcome — the coordination the
[Adaptability pillar](/pillars/adaptability) depends on.

## What skills should the owner have?

Coordination plus AEO literacy. They need to understand the [citation
cascade](/learn/aeo-canon), [answer-first content](/learn/answer-first-sentence),
[authority building](/learn/build-ai-authority-from-scratch), and [per-engine
measurement](/learn/per-engine-vs-blended-measurement), and be able to align teams
toward citation share. They don't have to do every part themselves — they own the
*outcome* and direct the contributing functions, which is why an existing SEO or
content lead is often the natural fit. For staffing it out, see [building an AEO
team](/learn/build-an-aeo-team).
