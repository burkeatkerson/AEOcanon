## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each great student experience earns a review and a referral; [readable
programs](/learn/martial-arts-school-service-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews earn citations for 'best
martial arts school near me'; those bring new students; and families who stay through belt progression
produce more reviews and referrals — while the ad spend you save funds better instruction and community.
The paid-ad model is the opposite: a faucet you pay to keep running, that stops the moment you stop. AEO
builds the [compounding asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead — students who found you and stay
for years.

## Why is retention central to dojo growth?

Because a martial arts school's value *is* recurring tuition, and filling the top of the funnel only pays
off if students stay. A child who trains for years toward a black belt is worth many times one who quits
in a month — and long-tenured, happy families are exactly who write the [reviews that mention instructors
and progress](/learn/martial-arts-school-reviews-ai-recommendations) and refer friends, feeding the AI
recommendations that bring the next students. So growth isn't just AEO at the top; it's AEO plus
retention together, each compounding the other. Win students with citations, keep them with great
instruction and belt progression, and the flywheel accelerates.

## How does this lower my cost per student?

By replacing recurring ad spend with assets that keep paying. A paid ad costs every time and stops when
you stop; an earned citation, a loyal long-term family, a referral, or a [filled kids
program](/learn/kids-martial-arts-classes-aeo) costs mostly upfront and pays for years. As your cited,
referred, and retained base grows, your cost per student falls and your revenue stabilizes — and because
AI names only a few schools, a real, well-reviewed local dojo can win recommendations a faceless
franchise listing [can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
