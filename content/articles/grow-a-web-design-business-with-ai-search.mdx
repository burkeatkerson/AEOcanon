## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each well-served client produces reviews and referrals — and renews a care
plan; reviews and [answer-first content](/learn/web-design-service-pages-for-ai) earn citations and
rankings; those bring direct inquiries; and the money saved on marketplace fees and undercut bids funds
more content and a sharper [niche](/learn/ecommerce-web-design-aeo). The marketplace model is the
opposite: a faucet you pay to keep running, bidding against a crowd, that stops the moment you stop. AEO
builds the [compounding asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead — clients who found you and stay on
a care plan.

## Why are care plans central to growth?

Because a one-time build is a single payment, while a care plan *is* monthly recurring revenue — and
filling the top of the funnel only pays off if clients stay. A retained client on a maintenance plan for
years is worth many times a single project — and happy, long-tenured clients are exactly who write the
[reviews that mention results](/learn/web-design-reviews-ai-recommendations) and refer fellow owners,
feeding the AI recommendations that bring the next clients. So growth isn't just AEO at the top; it's
AEO plus care-plan retention together, each compounding the other.

## How do I lower my cost per client?

By replacing recurring fees with assets that keep paying. A marketplace bid costs every time and is
[one of twenty for the same job](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands); an earned citation, a retained
care-plan client, or a referral costs mostly upfront and pays for years. As your cited, niche-focused,
and retained base grows, your cost per client falls and your revenue stabilizes. And because AI names
only a few studios, a focused, proven, genuinely reviewed studio can win recommendations a generic
marketplace [can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
