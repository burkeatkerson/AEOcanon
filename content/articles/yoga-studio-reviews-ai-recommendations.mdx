## Why do reviews carry so much weight for studios?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge which studios students actually love — and
choosing a yoga or Pilates studio is a trust-and-community decision. An assistant deciding what to
recommend [synthesizes sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and
review-platform reviews to gauge whether you're a great place to practice. For a studio, that
corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible,
and the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than
backlinks do.

## Do reviews that mention teachers and styles matter more?

Yes — specific reviews are gold, because they speak the language of what students want.

A review naming a teacher, a style, the community, or how a student felt gives the engine detail to tie
you to "beginner-friendly yoga near me" or "best reformer Pilates nearby" — the exact [fit and style
queries](/learn/yoga-pilates-questions-customers-ask-ai) students ask. Invite happy students to mention
what helped them, and the reviews start matching you to the searches that fill classes.

## How many reviews — and how fresh?

Fresh beats big. There's no magic count — recency and a steady flow outweigh a stale total. A studio
with current reviews that mention real experiences signals an active, loved community far better than
one coasting on old ratings. Build a simple habit: invite happy students to review after their intro
package or a milestone class, and [respond to them](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations) —
engagement signals an active studio that cares, which is exactly the place an engine wants to recommend.
