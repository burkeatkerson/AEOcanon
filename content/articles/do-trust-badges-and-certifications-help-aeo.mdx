**Genuine certifications and credentials help AEO when they're verifiable and
corroborated off-site, because they reinforce real expertise and trust — but
decorative trust badges on your own page do little, since engines weight independent
verification over self-displayed images.** Earn recognized credentials, then make
them verifiable.

## Why don't badges do much on their own?

Because they're self-displayed and unverifiable. Anyone can drop a trust-badge image
onto a page, so an engine can't treat it as independent proof of anything — it's the
same problem as [claiming expertise](/learn/how-to-show-expertise-to-ai) versus
demonstrating it. What carries weight is corroboration the engine can confirm, not a
graphic you control. So the badge itself is close to inert as a
[credibility](/pillars/credibility) signal.

## How do I make a certification count?

Make it verifiable and corroborated. Ensure recognized credentials are listed on the
*issuing organization's* site, referenced in your [author
bio](/learn/do-author-bios-help-aeo) and [About page](/learn/does-about-page-help-aeo),
and consistent across your profiles. The signal an engine can use is independent
confirmation of a real qualification — the issuing body vouching for you — not the
badge graphic. That turns a credential into genuine trust.

## Do awards and affiliations help?

Yes, on the same terms. Real awards, memberships, and affiliations that are
[corroborated by the granting bodies](/learn/build-ai-authority-from-scratch) add to
your authority and trust because they're independent recognition. As with
certifications, the value lives in verifiable third-party confirmation, not a logo on
your homepage — genuine recognition the web can confirm, contributing to both
[authority](/pillars/authority) and credibility.
