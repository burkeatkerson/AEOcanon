## What does an AI engine actually do to find you?

It retrieves, ranks, and names. When someone asks where to get coffee, the engine pulls the places it
can read, judges which best fit the request and look well-reviewed, and [names just a
few](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Each step is a filter: if the crawler can't read your
menu, you're out before ranking; if your hours are wrong, you're dropped from 'open now'; if your
reviews are thin, a competitor gets named. Being found means clearing every filter — and for cafés, the
readable menu is the one most often missed.

## Why can't customers find my coffee shop?

Almost always one specific broken gate — and for cafés it's usually the menu. If your menu is a
[PDF or an image the crawler can't read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), the engine doesn't
know what you serve, so it can't recommend you for "best oat milk latte near me" or "cafe with wifi
nearby." Other common breaks: inconsistent hours that make 'open now' wrong, or thin reviews. The fix
is diagnostic — find the *highest* broken gate and repair it first. For most coffee shops, putting the
menu in plain text is the single highest-leverage move.

## Does ranking in Google mean AI can find me?

Not on its own. Ranking and a Google listing help, but AI citation also needs your [menu and answers
in readable text](/learn/coffee-shop-menu-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews. A café can show up on a
map yet be absent from "best coffee near me" answers if the engine can't read the menu or the reviews
are thin. Clear all three gates and you get [found in both](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).
