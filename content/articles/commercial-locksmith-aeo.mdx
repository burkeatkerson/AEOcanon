## How is commercial locksmith AEO different?

The intent is research and security, not a panicked lockout. Where someone locked out acts on the
first answer, a facility or property manager evaluates carefully — and asks the assistant detailed
questions long before contacting anyone. They're researching master key design, access control, and
whether you can handle their building. Because [AEO works for B2B](/learn/does-aeo-work-for-b2b)
exactly here, the locksmith whose pages answer those questions expertly gets [cited during the
research](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations) — and lands on the shortlist before a single call.
And commercial accounts are recurring relationships (rekeys, maintenance, expansions), so each one won
is worth far more than a single lockout.

## What questions do commercial buyers ask?

Security-focused, evaluation-driven, and substantive — each worth a thorough page.

## What content wins commercial citations?

Depth backed by proof. Commercial decisions carry security stakes and longer research cycles, so your
pages should demonstrate genuine expertise — system design knowledge, lock standards and grades,
certifications, and clear contract terms — in thorough, [answer-first](/learn/answer-first-sentence)
pages. The fundamentals are the same as residential: be readable, answer the real question, be
trusted. Commercial just rewards [depth and credibility](/pillars/credibility) more heavily, because
the buyer is betting their building's security on getting the choice right. Be the page that proves
you're the safe expert, and you win the recurring account.
