## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each great order earns a review and a repeat customer — occasions recur every
year; [readable arrangements](/learn/florist-product-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews earn citations
for "best florist near me"; those bring direct orders; and the commission you save on wire-service
orders funds better flowers and marketing. The wire-service model is the opposite: a middleman that
skims every order and owns the customer. AEO builds the [compounding
asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead — loyal buyers who order direct, at full margin.

## What's the fastest place to start?

Fix the [readable arrangements and delivery info](/learn/florist-product-pages-for-ai) first — it's the
highest-leverage gap — then make hours and your same-day cutoff accurate so you win [near-me and
same-day](/learn/same-day-flower-delivery-ai-search), and build a steady flow of [genuine
reviews](/learn/florist-reviews-ai-recommendations). Those win citations within weeks. Then compound it:
turn every happy buyer into a repeat customer (occasions recur), and add [wedding and event
pages](/learn/wedding-and-events-florist-aeo) for high-margin, plannable bookings.

## Why are wire-service orders such a bad deal?

Because they skim the margin and own the customer. Wire services and order-gatherers take a large cut of
every order — often a third or more — and the buyer belongs to them, not you, so you do the work for a
fraction of the margin and build no direct relationship. An earned AI citation sends a full-margin order
that's yours, and that buyer can become a repeat customer who orders every anniversary and holiday. As
your cited, direct, repeat base grows, your margin per order improves — and because AI names only a few
shops, a real, well-reviewed local florist can win recommendations a faceless order-gatherer
[can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
