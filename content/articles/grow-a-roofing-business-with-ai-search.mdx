## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each completed job produces reviews and referrals; reviews and
[answer-first content](/learn/roofing-service-pages-that-get-cited) earn citations and
rankings; those bring direct inquiries; and the money saved on [purchased
leads](/learn/how-roofers-get-leads-without-buying-them) funds more content and more
crews — which produce more jobs. The aggregator model is the opposite: a faucet you pay to
keep running, that stops the moment you stop. AEO builds the [compounding
asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead.

## What's the fastest way to start?

Fix the highest-leverage gaps, then compound. Get a [crawlable, fast
site](/learn/roofing-website-rebuild-for-aeo), [answer-first
pages](/learn/roofing-service-pages-that-get-cited) for your top services and
storm/insurance, and consistent [reviews and listings](/learn/local-aeo-for-roofers) —
those produce direct inquiries within weeks to months. Then make every job feed the
flywheel: a photo, a [review ask](/learn/roofer-reviews-and-reputation), a referral
request, and a published answer to a question homeowners ask. Small habits, compounding.

## Can I really beat the aggregators?

Yes — because AI names only a few sources and [citations spread
thin](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands), a focused local roofer who answers specific
questions and shows real local proof wins recommendations a generic national platform
can't. Being specific, local, certified, and genuinely reviewed is an advantage a
faceless aggregator [can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore). Lower
cost per job, better margins, and demand you own — that's how AI search grows a roofing
business.
