## Why does seasonality matter for groomers?

Because demand — and bookings — surge in predictable waves, and the cited groomer catches them. Spring
and summer bring the heavy shed and de-shedding rush, the biggest grooming window of the year; the
winter holidays bring 'get the dog cleaned up before guests' bookings; cold months bring coat-care
questions. The groomer whose answer is already published, crawled, and trusted [gets cited the moment
the surge hits](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo) — while competitors are still updating their
site. It's the [Freshness pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned into a grooming calendar, and the
difference between a packed shed season and a quiet one.

## How do I prepare for each surge?

Build the content while it's calm, so it's ready when demand spikes.

## Should I make a new page each year for shed season?

No — update one durable evergreen page. A persistent 'de-shedding and shed-season grooming' or 'summer
coat care' page [accumulates authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh it each year,
which beats spinning up a throwaway 'Spring 2026' page that starts from zero and splits your signals.
Keep one strong page that answers the shedding and coat-care questions, sharpen it before each surge,
and link it to your [service and pricing pages](/learn/pet-grooming-service-pages-for-ai). One durable
page, refreshed and ready, beats scrambling when the shed wave hits.
