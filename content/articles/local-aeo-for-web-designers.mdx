## What makes a web studio 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about who you are, where you're based, and who you serve. An answer engine recommends a
studio for "near me" only when it can [place you with certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend)
— right area, right kind of client. That certainty comes from signals that all agree: matching
name-address-phone, a complete profile, a stated service area (local and remote), and reviews. When the
signals agree, you're an easy recommendation; when they conflict, the engine favors a clearer
competitor.

## How do I win 'near me' even if I work remotely?

Build location and service certainty deliberately.

This is the [near-me ranking](/learn/how-to-rank-in-ai-for-near-me-searches) discipline applied to a
studio — and it's also where [service-area pages](/learn/local-service-area-pages-for-ai) earn their
keep.

## Why does NAP consistency matter so much?

Because a mismatch is a doubt, and engines route around doubt. If your phone number differs between your
site and your Google profile, or an old address still shows in a directory, the engine can't be sure
which studio you are or where you operate — so it favors a competitor it can [place with
confidence](/learn/nap-consistency). For studios that have moved, gone fully remote, or rebranded, this
is a common and quiet leak — and an embarrassing one, since clean structured signals are exactly what
you sell. Fix it and you remove the single most fixable reason an engine skips you on a near-me query.
