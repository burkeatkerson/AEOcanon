**Freshness is the seventh pillar of the AEO Canon: answer engines prefer the
recent, and undated content reads as expired.** A page that was true two years
ago and hasn't been touched since signals neglect — and engines route around it.

## Why recency is a form of honesty

A page is a claim about the world. If the world has moved and the page hasn't,
the claim is quietly false. Engines treat an undated or stale page as a risk —
the information might be wrong — and prefer a source that demonstrably keeps up.

<PullQuote cite="Pillar 7 · Freshness">
  An undated page is a claim without a timestamp.
</PullQuote>

<Callout variant="insight" title="The metaphor">
  <p>
    Freshness is the expiration date on a carton of milk. The product might
    still be on the shelf, but without a current date, nobody's willing to serve
    it. A visible, honest date is what makes your content safe to use.
  </p>
</Callout>

The signal is consistent across studies: crawlers spend their budget on recent
content, and the content that gets cited skews newer than what merely ranks. In
a field that changes monthly, recency is also a proxy for accuracy.
