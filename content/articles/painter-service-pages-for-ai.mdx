answer to the cost, timeline, and prep questions, in plain language a homeowner and an engine can lift.**
One self-contained, crawlable page per service beats a single bloated services page every time.

## Why one page per service?

Because a citation is awarded to the page that best answers one specific question — and a catch-all
services page or photo gallery answers none of them well. When you give interior, exterior, cabinets, and
commercial each [their own page](/learn/should-content-sections-stand-alone), each can go deep on its own
cost, timeline, and prep — and each becomes citable for its own query. A single page trying to cover
everything is shallow on all of them, so the engine cites a competitor with a dedicated, focused page.

## What should each page lead with?

The answer the homeowner came for, before anything about you.

This is [answer-first writing](/learn/answer-first-sentence) applied to the trade: the quotable answer up
top, the prep and process that prove you're a pro, the proof at the bottom — described, not just shown.

## What makes a service page extractable?

Plain language and a clean structure. Write the way a homeowner asks — "painting a 12x12 bedroom
typically runs …" — not in jargon, and use [question-shaped headings](/learn/question-shaped-headings)
the engine can match to a query. Keep each answer in a self-contained passage so it can be [lifted
without the surrounding page](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage), reinforce it with [HousePainter
schema](/learn/painter-schema-markup), and make sure the page is fast and crawlable — not a slow gallery.
Answer-first, focused, and proven — that's the page an engine cites and a homeowner calls.
