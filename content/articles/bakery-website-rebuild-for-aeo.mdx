**A bakery needs a website rebuild for AEO when its products live in a PDF or Instagram-only feed, the
site is slow, or hours and custom-order details sit in widgets AI crawlers can't read — because the
engine can only recommend what it can parse.** The rebuild puts your products, hours, and order answers
in readable text everything else depends on.

## Why is the product list the binding constraint?

Because [access is the first gate](/pillars/access), and for a bakery the product list is where you
most often fail it. Your cakes and breads are what customers search for — "custom birthday cake near
me," "gluten-free bakery nearby" — but if they're a [PDF, an image, or an Instagram feed the crawler
can't read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), the engine doesn't know what you make, so it can't
recommend you. Add a slow, browser-only build or hours trapped in a third-party widget, and even your
basics are invisible. That's not a content problem you can write around; it's a foundation problem.

## How do I tell if my site is hurting me?

Run two quick tests, and look for the structural gaps.

If your products are a PDF or Instagram-only, your hours are buried, or the page is slow, the site is
working against you. A fast site with a [readable product list](/learn/bakery-menu-pages-for-ai) and
[clean schema](/learn/bakery-schema-markup) is what makes everything else possible.

## Can't I just keep my Instagram feed and PDF?

No — the Instagram-only or PDF product list is usually the core problem, not a detail to leave alone.
Because your products and custom-order details are exactly what customers search for, an unreadable
product list makes you invisible for cake-level and dietary queries no matter what else you publish.
Converting it to real HTML text — and adding a [custom-order page](/learn/custom-cakes-and-orders-aeo) —
is the single highest-leverage move most bakeries can make. Get the access layer right —
[server-rendered](/learn/server-side-vs-client-side-rendering), fast, with a readable product list and
hours — and the rest of your bakery AEO finally has something to build on.
