over technical details like schema, markup, and speed, so you compete on content
while accepting tighter limits on fine-grained optimization.

## Are hosted builders capable of AEO?

Yes — they clear the hardest technical gate for you. Squarespace and Wix return
[server-rendered HTML](/learn/server-side-vs-client-side-rendering), so an AI crawler
that [doesn't run JavaScript](/learn/javascript-breaks-ai-citation) still receives
your full content. They also handle hosting, HTTPS, and basic SEO, which covers most
of the [Access pillar](/pillars/access) without configuration. Being cited from a
builder site is routine.

## What are the real limits?

Control, not capability. Because you work inside the platform's templates, deep
customization — granular [structured data](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo),
stripping markup bloat, squeezing [page speed](/learn/page-speed-ai-citations) — can
be restricted to whatever the builder exposes. For most small and mid-size sites
that ceiling is high enough. Sites that need fine technical control eventually feel
the walls.

## Should I switch platforms for AEO?

Rarely, and not first. Almost all AEO gains come from [answer-first
content](/learn/answer-first-sentence), real evidence, and originality — which you
can deliver on any capable platform. Before considering a migration, maximize the
schema and speed options your current builder *does* offer and tighten your content.
Switching platforms is a last resort, not an opening move.
