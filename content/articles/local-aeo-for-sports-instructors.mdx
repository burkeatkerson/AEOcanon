## What makes a coach 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about where you teach and what you offer. An answer engine recommends a coach for "near me"
only when it can [place you with certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — right
area, right sport, right level. That certainty comes from signals that all agree: matching
name-address-phone, accurate hours, a complete profile, readable lessons and pricing, and reviews. When
the signals agree, you're an easy recommendation; when they conflict or your lesson menu is hidden in a
widget, the engine favors a clearer competitor.

This is the [near-me ranking](/learn/how-to-rank-in-ai-for-near-me-searches) discipline applied to
coaching — and it's also where [service-area pages](/learn/local-service-area-pages-for-ai) earn their
keep for instructors who teach across multiple courses, clubs, or pools.

## Why does NAP consistency matter so much?

Because a mismatch is a doubt, and engines route around doubt. If your phone number differs between your
site and your Google profile, or an old facility address still shows in a listing, the engine can't be
sure which coach you are or where you teach — so it favors a competitor it can [place with
confidence](/learn/nap-consistency). For instructors who teach at several locations, have rebranded, or
moved facilities, this is a common and quiet leak. Clean it up and you remove the single most fixable
reason an engine skips you on a near-me query.
