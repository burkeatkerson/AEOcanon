## How is custom and repair AEO different?

The intent is trust and logistics, not an off-the-shelf buy. A buyer with a custom idea or a precious
piece to repair researches carefully — and asks the assistant detailed questions before they hand
anything over. They're asking how design works, what it costs, how long a repair takes, and whether you can
be trusted with an heirloom. Because [AEO works for these higher-consideration
decisions](/learn/does-aeo-work-for-b2b), the jeweler whose pages answer those questions clearly gets
[cited during the research](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations) — and wins high-margin work most
stores never even surface for.

## How do I win custom and repair citations?

Give custom design and repair their own answer-first pages — not a buried services menu. Publish your
process, turnaround, price guidance, and what you work on in readable text, with described examples of past
work and a clear inquiry path. Most stores hide all of this in a list, so a clear, dedicated page is an
easy way to get cited for valuable, high-margin work — the kind that compounds your
[growth](/learn/grow-a-jewelry-store-with-ai-search) far beyond a single off-the-shelf sale, and turns a
repair customer into a lifelong one.
