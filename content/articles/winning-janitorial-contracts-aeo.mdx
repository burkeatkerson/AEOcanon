## Why are contracts won before the RFP?

Because the manager builds the shortlist while researching, not when the bids come in. Before a formal
RFP exists, a facility or procurement manager asks how to vet a janitorial company and who the credible
options are — and the [AI answer names only a few companies](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations).
Pew Research [found people click a link just 8% of the time when an AI summary
appears](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer shapes who gets invited to bid at all. The cited company enters the process already
trusted; uncited competitors are still fighting to be noticed — and on a multi-year recurring contract,
trust beats the lowest number.

## What contract-stage questions should I answer?

The vetting questions a manager weighs while deciding who is low-risk enough to let into the building.

## How do I become the cited expert?

Build answer-first pages for the contract-stage questions, the same way you would for [high-intent
searches](/learn/high-intent-commercial-cleaning-ai-search) — clear, honest, self-contained answers a
manager and an engine can lift. Reinforce them with a complete [Google Business
Profile](/learn/commercial-cleaning-google-business-profile-ai), [genuine
reviews](/learn/commercial-cleaning-reviews-ai-recommendations) that name reliability and
responsiveness, and a [specialty](/learn/industry-niche-commercial-cleaning-aeo) that sets you apart on
high-value buildings. Do that, and you stop chasing purchased leads and start being the name the manager
already trusts when the RFP goes out — winning recurring contracts that [compound for
years](/learn/grow-a-commercial-cleaning-business-with-ai-search).
