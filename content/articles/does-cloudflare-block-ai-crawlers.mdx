## How would Cloudflare be blocking my pages?

At the network layer, before a request ever reaches your origin. Cloudflare sits in
front of your server, so its bot-management rules decide which crawlers get through.
It offers an explicit "block AI bots" toggle, and for new domains it began applying
AI-bot blocking by default — meaning you can be blocking GPTBot without ever
touching your own [robots.txt](/learn/allow-ai-crawlers-robots-txt). This is why a
site with perfect content can still be unreadable to engines.

## How do I check and fix it?

Start in the Cloudflare dashboard under Bots and the AI-crawler controls, and review
any WAF or firewall rules that match AI user-agents. Then confirm against your
[server logs](/learn/read-server-logs-ai-crawlers): if AI bots stopped appearing
after a Cloudflare change, that's the cause. To re-open access, disable the
AI-bot-blocking toggle or add explicit allow rules for the engines you want, then
watch the logs for those bots to resume.

## Why this matters more than people think

Because it's an invisible failure. A network-layer block produces no error you'd
notice — your site loads fine for humans while [AI crawlers see
nothing](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site). Teams often spend weeks rewriting
content to chase lost AI citations when the real cause was a CDN setting. Rule out
the [Access](/pillars/access) layer — robots.txt, rendering, *and* your CDN —
first.
