## What makes a groomer 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about where you are and what you offer. An answer engine recommends a groomer for "near me"
only when it can [place you with certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — right
area, right kind of grooming. That certainty comes from signals that all agree: matching
name-address-phone, accurate hours, a complete profile, readable services and pricing, and reviews.
When the signals agree, you're an easy recommendation; when they conflict or your services are hidden
in a widget, the engine favors a clearer competitor.

This is the [near-me ranking](/learn/how-to-rank-in-ai-for-near-me-searches) discipline applied to
grooming — and it's also where [service-area pages](/learn/local-service-area-pages-for-ai) earn their
keep for mobile groomers and salons covering several neighborhoods.

## Why does NAP consistency matter so much?

Because a mismatch is a doubt, and engines route around doubt. If your phone number differs between
your site and your Google profile, or an old address still shows in a listing, the engine can't be sure
which business you are or where you operate — so it favors a competitor it can [place with
confidence](/learn/nap-consistency). For groomers who have moved, rebranded, or switched from mobile to
a salon (or back), this is a common and quiet leak. Clean it up and you remove the single most fixable
reason an engine skips you on a near-me query.
