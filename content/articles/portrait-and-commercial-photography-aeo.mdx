## How is portrait and commercial AEO different?

The intent is broader, more practical, and often recurring — not a once-in-a-lifetime celebration. A parent
booking annual family photos, a professional needing a LinkedIn headshot, a founder building a personal brand,
or a company ordering product, real-estate, or team photography researches carefully and asks the assistant
detailed questions before they call. They're checking pricing, turnaround, deliverables, and usage rights.
Because [AEO works for these higher-consideration and B2B decisions](/learn/does-aeo-work-for-b2b), the
photographer whose pages answer those questions clearly gets [cited during the
research](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations) — and wins bookings, including recurring commercial accounts,
that most photographers never even surface for.

Specific and segment-by-segment — each worth a clear answer on a dedicated page.

## How do I win portrait and commercial citations?

Give each segment its own answer-first page — not a buried line under your wedding work. Publish session
pricing, turnaround, deliverables, and usage rights in readable text, reinforced by your
[reviews](/learn/photographer-reviews-ai-recommendations) and a clear inquiry path. Most photographers either
omit this entirely or bury it behind a gallery, so a clear, dedicated page for headshots, branding, product,
real-estate, and team photography is an easy way to get cited for recurring, predictable work — the steadiest
revenue that compounds your [growth](/learn/grow-a-photography-business-with-ai-search) and keeps the calendar
full between weddings.
