## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a shop the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine can
read your products and place you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as where to shop —
and that leans harder on whether you carry the right styles and sizes and whether shoppers love you.
The engine recommends the shop it can [confidently confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real,
fitting, and well-reviewed, because when it recommends you it's putting its credibility behind the
experience. Your job is to make that confirmation easy — readable products, accurate details, and
genuine reviews.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). Wrong hours, an unreadable site, or a thin review profile introduces
doubt, and doubt sends the shopper elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one boutique over another?

Because it picks the one it's most confident will fit. Faced with several options, the engine names the
shop whose signals best match the request — right area, carries the right styles and sizes — and whose
reviews most clearly say shoppers love it and the fit is great. The competitor with a lookbook-only
site, wrong hours, or thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it loses. Winning is about being the
[easiest to verify as the real, loved local choice](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend)
— which is exactly what this library builds, piece by piece.
