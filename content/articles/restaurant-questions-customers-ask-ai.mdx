('are you open now', 'do you take reservations'), dietary ('vegan options nearby'), and occasion
('romantic dinner near me').** Mapping each to readable content that answers it is the core of a
restaurant AEO content plan.

## How do I find the exact questions?

Listen where diners already ask. Note what guests ask your host and servers, read your reviews and the
questions on your Google profile, scan local food forums, and [prompt the assistants
directly](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask) on your cuisine and neighborhood to see the
follow-ups they surface. Capture the *natural wording* — "where can I get good gluten-free pasta near
[neighborhood]" beats "celiac-friendly dining options" — because engines match the diner's phrasing.
Then prioritize by what genuinely fits your restaurant.

## Should I answer logistics and dietary questions?

Yes — they're the easiest citations most restaurants leave on the table. Diners constantly ask about
hours, reservations, parking, delivery, and dietary options, and answering them in plain text — not
buried in a PDF or an image — makes you the place the engine confidently recommends. It also saves your
staff from fielding the same questions all day. These answers are pure [Alignment](/pillars/alignment):
the real question, answered where the engine can read it. Map every bucket to readable content and
you've built the plan that gets a restaurant cited.
