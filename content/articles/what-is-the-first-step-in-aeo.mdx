**The first step is to confirm AI crawlers can actually read your pages — because if
they can't, nothing else matters.** Once access is verified, lead your most important
page with a direct answer to the real question. Access first, answer-first second,
everything else after.

## Why start with crawlability?

Because it's the gate everything depends on. The [AEO Canon](/learn/aeo-canon) is a
cascade — if a crawler [can't read your raw HTML](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site),
your content, authority, and freshness are invisible no matter how good they are. So
the very first move is verifying [access](/pillars/access): that AI crawlers are
allowed and that your pages aren't [hidden behind
JavaScript](/learn/javascript-breaks-ai-citation). Everything else builds on that
foundation being solid.

## What's the highest-impact first content change?

Putting the answer first. Take your most important page and rewrite the opening to
[answer the core question directly](/learn/answer-first-sentence) in the first
sentence, before any setup. It's a small edit with outsized effect, because engines
lift answer-first passages and skim past buried ones — which is why it's one of the
[cheapest, highest-impact moves](/learn/cheapest-high-impact-aeo-moves) you can make
right after confirming access.

## Where do I go after that?

Run the cascade and fix the highest break. After access and answer-first structure,
diagnose [alignment](/pillars/alignment), [extractability](/pillars/extractability),
[authority](/pillars/authority), and [freshness](/pillars/freshness) in order, and
address the first failure you hit — the [Canon
diagnostic](/learn/aeo-canon-diagnostic) walks exactly this path. That sequencing
focuses effort on the constraint actually holding you back, and a [30-day
plan](/learn/30-day-aeo-plan) turns it into a schedule.
