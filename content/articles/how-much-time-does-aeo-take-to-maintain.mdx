## Is AEO a one-time project or ongoing?

Ongoing, but front-loaded. The initial work — fixing [access](/pillars/access),
restructuring content to be [answer-first](/learn/answer-first-sentence), building the
first [authority](/learn/build-ai-authority-from-scratch) — is the heavy lift. After
that it settles into maintenance: keeping content fresh, expanding your question
coverage, and monitoring citations. The pace becomes steady rather than intense, which
is why a [30-day plan](/learn/30-day-aeo-plan) front-loads the effort and a lighter
rhythm follows.

## What takes the most ongoing time?

Content work, mostly. [Refreshing pages as they decay](/learn/does-content-decay-affect-ai-citation)
and producing new answers for [real question gaps](/learn/find-content-gaps-for-aeo) is
usually the bulk of ongoing effort — [content decay](https://ahrefs.com/blog/content-decay/) is why refreshes never fully stop — followed by [authority
building](/pillars/authority) — slower but lighter-touch. [Citation
tracking](/learn/how-to-track-ai-citations) is light once automated. So budget your
steady time mainly for content and authority, not measurement.

## How do I keep it manageable?

Prioritize and systematize. Focus refreshes on
[high-value decaying pages](/learn/does-updating-old-content-help-aeo), batch new
content around real question gaps, [automate citation
tracking](/learn/tools-to-monitor-ai-citations), and let [topic
volatility](/learn/how-often-should-i-update-content-for-aeo) set your cadence so you're
not over-maintaining stable pages. A simple system keeps maintenance from sprawling —
the same discipline a [content program at scale](/learn/aeo-content-program-at-scale)
uses, sized down to a solo operation.
