## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be the local entity the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the
engine can retrieve you; being *recommended* means it actively names you to a stranded driver — and that's
a higher bar of trust. The engine recommends the company it can [confidently
confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, available now, and well-regarded, because when it
names you it puts its own credibility behind the choice. Your job is to make that confirmation effortless —
and the payoff is a direct call that pays full price, not a motor-club fraction.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). One inconsistency (an old phone number, wrong hours) introduces doubt,
and in an emergency doubt routes the call to a competitor — or back to the club app that pays you scraps.

## Why does AI pick one towing company over another?

Because it picks the one it's most sure about. Faced with several options, the engine names the company
whose signals most confidently match the request — right service, right area, available now, genuinely
reviewed. The competitor with conflicting hours or thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it loses even if
the trucks are just as good. Winning is less about being the biggest operator in town and more about being
the [easiest to verify as the right one](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — which is
exactly what this library builds, piece by piece, so the full-price call comes to you.
