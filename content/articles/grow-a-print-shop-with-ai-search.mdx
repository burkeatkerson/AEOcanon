## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each well-served order produces reviews and referrals — and the business
reorders; reviews and [answer-first content](/learn/print-shop-service-pages-for-ai) earn citations and
rankings; those bring direct orders; and the money saved on lost low-margin online jobs funds more
content and a push into the [high-value signage work](/learn/large-format-and-signage-aeo). The online
giant model is the opposite: a faucet of one-off, race-to-the-bottom jobs that never come back. AEO
builds the [compounding asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead — buyers who found you, ordered,
and reorder.

## Why are recurring B2B accounts central to growth?

Because a business that reorders cards, banners, and signage is steady, predictable revenue, and
filling the top of the funnel only pays off if customers come back. An office that reorders every
quarter is worth many times a single walk-in job — and happy business customers are exactly who write
the [reviews that mention results](/learn/print-shop-reviews-ai-recommendations) and refer fellow
owners, feeding the AI recommendations that bring the next customers. So growth isn't just AEO at the
top; it's AEO plus [recurring accounts](/learn/business-and-bulk-printing-aeo) together, each
compounding the other.

## How do I lower my cost per order?

By replacing chased, low-margin jobs with assets that keep paying. A job won on price alone is a
one-time race to the bottom; an earned citation, a recurring B2B account, or a referral costs mostly
upfront and pays for years. As your cited, repeat-ordering base grows, your cost per order falls and
your revenue stabilizes. And because AI names only a few shops, a real local shop with genuine reviews
and the high-value capabilities — signs, wraps, large format — an online printer can't match
[can't be replicated](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
