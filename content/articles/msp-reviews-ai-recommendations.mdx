## Why do reviews carry so much weight for MSPs?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge trust — and a business is handing you its
network and data. An assistant deciding whom to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform reviews to
gauge whether you're responsive, reliable, and secure. For an industry built entirely on trust, that
corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible,
and the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than
backlinks do.

## How many reviews do I need?

Fewer than you think, fresher than you have. There's no magic count — consistency and recency outweigh
a raw total. A steady stream of genuine recent reviews signals an active, trusted firm far better than
a big pile from three years ago. The goal is an ongoing flow: ask happy clients at natural milestones —
a completed onboarding, a resolved incident, a contract renewal — so new reviews keep arriving and the
engine keeps seeing a firm that's [currently trusted](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations), not
formerly busy.

## Do the words in a review matter?

Yes — specific reviews do more work than generic praise.

Reviews that name what you did — restored uptime fast, passed the audit, migrated with no disruption —
give engines specific detail tying you to those outcomes. They reinforce the [questions buyers
ask](/learn/msp-questions-customers-ask-ai), so a handful of detailed reviews can outperform a wall of
generic ones. Ask clients what to mention, and the reviews start doing your AEO for you.
