## What does electrician schema actually do?

It makes your business data unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business),
and the Electrician subtype, labels your name, address, phone, hours, area served,
services, and reviews so engines parse them cleanly rather than guessing from prose —
reinforcing the [consistent identity](/learn/nap-consistency) local recognition depends
on. It's the [structured data for AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern applied to
the trade: clarity for the parser, on top of content that's already clear for the
customer.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your electrical business — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your data machine-readable,
which *supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [consistent
identity](/learn/local-aeo-for-electricians), [genuine
reviews](/learn/electrician-reviews-ai-recommendations), a verifiable license, and [pages
that answer electrical questions](/learn/electrician-service-pages-for-ai). And don't fake
it: marking up reviews or credentials that don't match your visible page is a misuse
engines can detect — [especially damaging](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations) for a
safety-critical, trust-driven trade. Accurate schema on top of real proof is the
combination that works.
