## Why does a niche win in AI search?

Because specificity beats generality, and AI names the firm that best fits the exact question. When an
owner asks "bookkeeper who understands contractors" or "accounting for an e-commerce business," the
engine looks for a firm that clearly handles that industry's particulars — and a generalist page that
says "we serve all small businesses" matches nothing in particular. A focused niche is the
[Originality](/pillars/originality) edge a small firm can own, and exactly the kind of specific,
expert content [citations spread thin](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands) reward.

## What questions do niche clients ask?

Industry-specific ones — each worth a dedicated, expert page.

## How do I win niche citations?

Give each industry you serve its own answer-first page covering its specific bookkeeping, tax, and
software needs — not a buried line on a generalist page. Show you understand their job costing, their
sales tax, their software, reinforced by [reviews from that
industry](/learn/bookkeeper-reviews-ai-recommendations) and a clear inquiry path. A dedicated
'bookkeeping for contractors' page gets cited for exactly that query, signals real expertise, and lets
you [charge more and grow](/learn/grow-a-bookkeeping-business-with-ai-search) — because specialists
command higher fees than generalists.
