## Why is the engagement ring the highest-value search?

Because it's the biggest purchase many buyers ever make, and it's researched for weeks before a decision.
When someone searches "how much should I spend on an engagement ring" or "custom engagement rings near
me," they're starting a high-stakes journey — and the [AI answer names only a few
stores](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Pew Research [found people click a link just 8% of the
time when an AI summary
appears](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer *is* the shortlist. For a jeweler, owning that journey — especially the budget and custom
questions — is the difference between a flagship sale and a buyer lost to a chain.

## What do buyers ask AI before buying a ring?

Researched, high-stakes questions — and you should own the ones you can answer with authority.

## Why publish price ranges instead of hiding them?

Because the budget question is the first one buyers ask, and the engine answers from what it can read. A
nervous first-time buyer asks AI "how much is an engagement ring," and a store that states ranges in
[plain text](/learn/local-aeo-for-jewelers) — "engagement rings from $1,500 to $15,000, custom from
$3,000" — gets recommended while a store hiding the number gets skipped. Stated ranges also build trust:
they signal you're upfront, which a high-stakes buyer rewards. Keep your ranges and custom process
readable, name your certifications, and you win the highest-value visit a jeweler can earn — instead of
losing it to a chain.
