## What can I do without code?

The bulk of the high-impact work. Writing [answer-first
passages](/learn/answer-first-sentence), using [question-shaped
headings](/learn/question-shaped-headings), [answering real
questions](/learn/what-questions-should-my-content-answer), keeping content fresh, and
building authority through [genuine mentions](/learn/get-brand-mentions-without-links)
are all about clear thinking and writing, not engineering. These are exactly the
[cheapest, highest-impact moves](/learn/cheapest-high-impact-aeo-moves), so a
non-technical person captures most of the value unaided.

## What actually needs a developer?

A few bounded tasks. Making sure pages are [server-rendered so crawlers can read
them](/learn/server-side-vs-client-side-rendering), fixing [crawlability or
robots](/learn/allow-ai-crawlers-robots-txt) issues, and adding [structured
data](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) are genuinely technical — but they're *one-time*
fixes, not ongoing work, so they don't block you from doing everything else. Hand them
to a developer once and the [Access pillar](/pillars/access) is cleared.

## How do I handle the technical parts without coding?

Delegate or use no-code tools. Hand rendering and schema fixes to a developer once, or
use a platform like [WordPress](/learn/is-wordpress-good-for-aeo) or a [hosted
builder](/learn/can-squarespace-wix-do-aeo) that handles server-rendering by default
and offers schema plugins. Either way clears the technical gate so you can focus on the
content and authority work that earns citations — which is most of AEO anyway.
