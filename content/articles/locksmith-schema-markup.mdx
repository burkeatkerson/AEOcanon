## What does locksmith schema actually do?

It makes your business data unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business), and the
Locksmith subtype, labels your name, address, phone, hours, area served, services, and reviews so
engines parse them cleanly rather than guessing from prose — reinforcing the [consistent
identity](/learn/nap-consistency) local recognition depends on. It's the [structured data for
AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern applied to the trade: clarity for the parser, on top of
content that's already clear for the customer.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your locksmith business — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your data machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [consistent
identity](/learn/local-aeo-for-locksmiths), [genuine
reviews](/learn/locksmith-reviews-ai-recommendations), a verifiable license, and [pages that answer
locksmith questions](/learn/locksmith-service-pages-for-ai). And don't fake it: marking up reviews or
credentials that don't match your visible page is a misuse engines can detect — [especially
damaging](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations) for a trade already fighting fake-listing scams.
Accurate schema on top of real proof is the combination that works.
