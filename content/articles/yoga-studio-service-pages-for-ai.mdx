## Why are your class pages the most important pages?

Because what you teach is what students search for — and a citation goes to the page the engine can
read. When someone asks "beginner hot yoga near me" or "reformer Pilates studio nearby," the engine
matches the query against class styles it can [actually
parse](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site). If your schedule and pricing live only inside a booking
widget, the engine doesn't know what you offer, so you're invisible for those style-level queries — the
most valuable, highest-intent searches there are. A readable schedule turns your whole studio into
citable answers.

## What makes a class page citable?

Readable text, organized the way students think.

## Why describe styles, not just list them?

Because description is the language of fit. Students ask AI for "a beginner-friendly yoga studio" or "a
gentle class to start with," and a bare schedule gives the engine little to match. A sentence
describing each style — who it's for, what to expect, the vibe — gives the engine the [extractable
detail](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage) that ties you to those specific, high-intent searches,
and mirrors the [reviews](/learn/yoga-studio-reviews-ai-recommendations) students write about your
teachers. A readable, described schedule is the foundation every other studio AEO move builds on.
