scrambling after.

## How do I prepare for each surge?

Build the content while it's quiet, so it's ready when demand turns. Publish or refresh your winter page
(ditch pulls, dead batteries, 'stuck in the snow') in late fall, your storm-season page before your
region's severe-weather window, and your summer breakdown page in late spring — ahead of when drivers
search. Pages need to be [crawled and trusted](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo) before the surge;
reacting after the first storm is too late to be cited in the moment. It's the [Freshness
pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned into a towing calendar — and accurate 24-hour hours on every page so the
engine knows you're available when the surge hits.

## Should I make a new page each year?

No — update one durable page per seasonal topic. A persistent 'stuck in the snow' or 'storm recovery' page
[accumulates authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh the details each year, which beats
spinning up a throwaway page annually that starts from zero and splits your signals. Keep one strong page
per topic, sharpen it before each surge, and link it to your [service
pages](/learn/towing-service-pages-for-ai). One durable page, refreshed and ready, wins the full-price
calls when the surge turns — instead of scrambling after.
