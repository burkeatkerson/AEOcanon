**Yoga and Pilates studios should use the HealthClub or SportsActivityLocation (LocalBusiness subtypes)
schema with accurate name, address, phone, hours, and class styles, plus FAQ schema on answer pages —
it helps engines parse what you offer and confirm where and when you're open.** Schema clarifies
readable content for AI; it never rescues a booking widget or wrong hours.

## What does studio schema actually do?

It makes your studio's details unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business), and the
HealthClub or SportsActivityLocation subtype, labels your name, address, hours, class styles, and
reviews so engines parse them cleanly rather than guessing — reinforcing the [consistent
identity](/learn/nap-consistency) local recommendations depend on. It's the [structured data for
AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern applied to studios: clarity for the parser, on top of a
schedule and pricing that are already clear and readable for the student.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your studio — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your details machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [a readable schedule and
pricing](/learn/yoga-studio-service-pages-for-ai) the crawler can parse, [accurate
hours](/learn/local-aeo-for-yoga-studios), and [genuine
reviews](/learn/yoga-studio-reviews-ai-recommendations). Schema can't rescue a schedule trapped in a
booking widget or hours that are wrong — and faking reviews or details in markup is a misuse engines
can [detect](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations). Accurate schema on top of a readable schedule and
pricing is the combination that works.
