**Write bookkeeping service pages AI will cite by giving each service its own page that leads with the
answer to the cost, scope, and who-it's-for questions, in plain language an owner and an engine can
lift.** One self-contained, crawlable page per service beats a single bloated services page every time.

## Why one page per service?

Because a citation is awarded to the page that best answers one specific question — and a catch-all
services page answers none of them well. When you give monthly bookkeeping, tax prep, payroll, cleanup,
and advisory each [their own page](/learn/should-content-sections-stand-alone), each can go deep on its
own cost, scope, and fit — and each becomes citable for its own query. A single page trying to cover
everything is shallow on all of them, so the engine cites a competitor with a dedicated, focused page.

## What should each page lead with?

The answer the owner came for, before anything about you.

This is [answer-first writing](/learn/answer-first-sentence) applied to the profession: the quotable
answer up top, the depth below, the proof at the bottom.

## What makes a service page extractable?

Plain language and a clean structure. Write the way an owner asks — "monthly bookkeeping typically runs
…" — not in jargon, and use [question-shaped headings](/learn/question-shaped-headings) the engine can
match to a query. Keep each answer in a self-contained passage so it can be [lifted without the
surrounding page](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage), reinforce it with [AccountingService
schema](/learn/bookkeeper-schema-markup), and make sure the page is fast and crawlable. Answer-first,
focused, and proven — that's the page an engine cites and an owner contacts.
