## What makes a plumber win "near me" in AI?

Clear, consistent placement. For a near-me or service-area question, the engine has to
confidently tie *you* to the searcher's location — so the deciding factor is whether
your location signals are unambiguous. Match your [name, address, and phone across every
listing](/learn/nap-consistency), define your service area explicitly, and name the
specific places you serve. The engine recommends the plumber it can place with
confidence and skips the one it can't — and [nearly half of consumers now use AI like ChatGPT to find local businesses](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey/), which makes local AEO largely a
[trust-and-consistency](/pillars/authority) problem.

## Why does NAP consistency matter so much?

Because inconsistency creates doubt, and doubt loses the citation. If your name,
address, or phone differs across your site, [Google Business Profile](https://www.google.com/business/), and directories,
an engine can't be sure which business you are or where you operate — so it favors a
competitor it can place cleanly. [Identical NAP everywhere](/learn/nap-consistency)
removes that doubt and is the foundation everything else in
[local AEO](/learn/local-aeo) builds on. It's unglamorous and decisive.

## Do I need a page for every town I serve?

For your priority areas, yes — but real ones, not spam. A genuine, useful page per key
town or service area, answering area-specific questions, helps engines connect you to
those locations. What to avoid is thin, near-[duplicate](/learn/does-duplicate-content-hurt-aeo)
doorway pages that just swap the town name — those add no value and can hurt. Make each
local page specific enough that it would help a real customer in that town, and it'll
help the engine too.

## How does this connect to the rest?

Local signals are the [Authority](/pillars/authority) and
[Credibility](/pillars/credibility) gates for a trade. Pair them with a [complete
Google Business Profile](/learn/plumber-google-business-profile-ai), [real
reviews](/learn/plumber-reviews-ai-recommendations), and [correct local
schema](/learn/plumber-schema-markup), and you've built the trust layer that gets a
local plumber named over a national directory — the core promise of
[AEO for plumbers](/learn/aeo-for-plumbers).
