and pages that name the areas you serve.** The engine recommends the local business it
can confidently place in the searcher's location, so being easy to place is most of the
battle.

## What does AI actually check for a near-me query?

It checks whether it can place you with confidence. The engine infers the searcher's
location from their device or context, then looks for businesses it can tie to that
area cleanly — drawing on your [Google Business
Profile](https://support.google.com/business/answer/7091), consistent listings, and
pages that name your service area. If your signals agree, you're a candidate; if they
conflict, it favors a competitor it can place without doubt. Near-me AEO is, at its
core, a [recognition and consistency](/learn/local-aeo) problem.

## How do I make my location unmistakable?

Eliminate ambiguity everywhere your business appears. Match your [name, address, and
phone exactly](/learn/nap-consistency) across your site, Google Business Profile, Bing
Places, and directories; define your service area explicitly; and name the specific
places you serve. Small mismatches — an old phone, a missing suite number, "Co." vs
"Company" — fragment the engine's picture of you and cost you the recommendation.

## What if I still don't appear?

Then your location is ambiguous or your presence is thin — fix that before anything
else. A missing or incomplete profile, conflicting details, no [service-area
pages](/learn/local-service-area-pages-for-ai), or few reviews all leave an engine
unsure where you operate. Work the [Access](/pillars/access) and
[Authority](/pillars/authority) gates locally — be readable and be a recognized,
well-reviewed entity in your area — and diagnose specifics in [why isn't my local
business showing up in AI](/learn/why-isnt-my-local-business-showing-up-in-ai).
