or wrong hours.

## What does brewery schema actually do?

It makes your taproom details unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness and Brewery
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business) label your
name, address, hours, amenities, price range, and menu so engines parse them cleanly rather than guessing
— reinforcing the [consistent identity](/learn/nap-consistency) and availability local recommendations
depend on. It's the [structured data for AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern applied to the
taproom: clarity for the parser, on top of a tap list and hours that are already clear and readable for
the drinker.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your brewery — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your details machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on a [readable tap
list](/learn/brewery-menu-pages-for-ai) the crawler can actually parse, [accurate
hours](/learn/local-aeo-for-breweries), and [genuine
reviews](/learn/brewery-reviews-ai-recommendations). Schema can't rescue a chalkboard photo the engine
can't read or hours that are wrong — and faking reviews or details in markup is a misuse engines can
[detect](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations). Accurate schema on top of a real, readable tap list is
the combination that works.
