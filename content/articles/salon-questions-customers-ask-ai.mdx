**Clients ask AI salon questions in four buckets — price ('how much is balayage'), service ('do you do
fades or extensions'), fit ('salon good with curly hair'), and logistics ('do you take walk-ins', 'is
there parking').** Mapping each to readable content that answers it is the core of a salon AEO content
plan.

## How do I find the exact questions?

Listen where clients already ask. Note what people ask at the front desk and in the chair, read your
reviews and the questions on your Google profile, scan local beauty groups and forums, and [prompt the
assistants directly](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask) on your specialty and area to see the
follow-ups they surface. Capture the *natural wording* — "is there a barber near me that does good
fades" beats "tonsorial fade services" — because engines match the client's phrasing. Then prioritize by
what genuinely fits your salon.

## Should I publish pricing if competitors hide it?

Yes — and it's an edge, not a risk. Clients constantly ask AI "how much is balayage near me," and a
clear price or range in readable text lets the engine recommend you for those high-intent cost searches —
while competitors who bury pricing in a widget get skipped. Transparency also builds trust with a
price-conscious client before they ever book. It's pure [Alignment](/pillars/alignment): the real
question, answered where the engine and the client can read it. Map every bucket to readable content and
you've built the plan that gets a salon cited.
