## Why are your offerings the binding constraint?

Because [access is the first gate](/pillars/access), and for a dog trainer the programs and pricing are
where you most often fail it. Owners search for "puppy training near me" or "affordable board-and-train,"
but if your offerings live inside a [booking widget the crawler can't
read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), the engine doesn't know what you offer, so it can't
recommend you. Add a slow build or a homepage that's all images, and even your basics are invisible.
That's not a content problem you can write around; it's a foundation problem.

## How do I tell if my site is hurting me?

Run two quick tests, and look for the structural gaps.

If your programs are widget-trapped, your pricing is hidden, or the page is slow, the site is working
against you. A fast site with [readable programs and pricing](/learn/dog-training-service-pages-for-ai)
and [clean schema](/learn/dog-training-schema-markup) is what makes everything else possible.

## Can't I just keep my booking widget?

Keep the widget for booking — but you also need your programs, methods, and pricing in readable HTML
text on the page. Because the widget is usually invisible to crawlers, relying on it alone hides exactly
the offerings owners search for. Add readable text alongside it, get the access layer right —
[server-rendered](/learn/server-side-vs-client-side-rendering), fast, with readable offerings — and the
rest of your dog training AEO finally has something to build on.
