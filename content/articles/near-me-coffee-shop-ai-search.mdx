## Why is 'near me' the highest-value coffee search?

Because it's the moment of decision, and it converts immediately. When someone asks "best coffee near
me" or "café open now," they're choosing where to get their coffee in the next few minutes — and the
[AI answer names only a few places](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Pew Research [found people
click a link just 8% of the time when an AI summary
appears](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer *is* the customer. For a café, owning that moment is the difference between a full room
of regulars and an empty counter.

In-the-moment, specific questions — and you should own the ones that fit your café.

## Why is 'open now' the make-or-break signal?

Because a huge share of coffee searches are in-the-moment — often before work, when a locked door loses
the customer for good — and the engine answers 'open now' straight from your hours. Accurate,
consistent hours mean you get recommended when a caffeine-seeker is deciding; wrong or inconsistent
hours mean you're dropped from the answer — or you send someone to a closed door and earn a bad review.
Keeping hours current everywhere, including holidays, is the highest-leverage move for winning the
in-the-moment customer, and it's the same [local certainty](/learn/local-aeo-for-coffee-shops) that
wins every near-me query.
