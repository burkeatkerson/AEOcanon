**Yes — small businesses can absolutely compete in AI search, because engines cite
the best passage for a specific question, not the biggest brand.** A focused,
well-evidenced answer plus genuine niche authority can win citations over far
larger competitors on the queries that matter to you.

## Why can small businesses win in AI search?

Small businesses can win because [citation](/glossary/citation) is decided at the passage level, not the
domain level. An answer engine retrieves and quotes the specific passage that best
answers a query, so a single excellent, well-evidenced paragraph on a small site
can be cited over a buried answer on a huge one. You're not trying to beat a giant
across their whole domain — just to be the best answer to one question. That's the
core of [extractability](/pillars/extractability), and it's inherently
more achievable than out-ranking enterprise SEO.

## How do I beat bigger competitors?

Beat bigger competitors by being more specific and more genuinely [authoritative in
a narrow niche](/glossary/topical-authority). Large brands spread their content thin across everything; a focused
business can own high-intent, specific questions with first-hand expertise,
original local data, and real community presence. Crucially, engines weigh brand
**[mentions](/glossary/branded-mention)** over backlinks —
[Ahrefs found a 0.664 correlation for mentions versus 0.218 for backlinks](https://ahrefs.com/blog/ai-overview-brand-correlation/)
— and earning genuine mentions in a niche is far more attainable than matching an
enterprise link profile. That's the [authority pillar](/pillars/authority)
working in your favor.

## Doesn't this require a big budget?

No — the highest-leverage AEO moves cost effort more than money. Answer-first
writing, inline evidence, crawlable pages, genuine reviews, and participation in
your niche communities don't require enterprise budgets. This is a real shift from
link-heavy traditional SEO, where spend mattered more. For local businesses
especially, first-hand knowledge and community trust are advantages money can't
easily buy.

## What should a small business do first?

Start narrow and deep. Identify the specific, high-intent questions your customers
actually ask, write the definitive answer-first page for each, confirm it's
crawlable, and build genuine niche authority — real reviews, local mentions,
community participation. A handful of pages that are genuinely the best answer to
their questions will outperform broad, shallow coverage. Begin with
[what is AEO](/learn/what-is-aeo) and use [The AEO Canon](/learn/aeo-canon) as your
checklist.
