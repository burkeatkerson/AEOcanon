## Why one page per service?

Because a citation is awarded to the page that best answers one specific question — and a catch-all
services page answers none of them well. When you give home lockout, car key replacement, rekey,
commercial, and lock install each [their own
page](/learn/should-content-sections-stand-alone), each can go deep on its own cost, timing, and
details — and each becomes citable for its own query. A single page trying to cover everything is
shallow on all of them, so the engine cites a competitor with a dedicated, focused page instead.

## What should each page lead with?

The answer the customer came for, before anything about you.

This is [answer-first writing](/learn/answer-first-sentence) applied to the trade: the quotable answer
up top, the depth below, the proof and honest pricing front and center.

## What makes a service page extractable?

Plain language and a clean structure. Write the way a customer asks — "a home lockout typically
costs…" — not in jargon, and use [question-shaped headings](/learn/question-shaped-headings) the
engine can match to a query. Keep each answer in a self-contained passage so it can be [lifted without
the surrounding page](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage), reinforce it with [Locksmith
schema](/learn/locksmith-schema-markup), and make sure the page is fast and crawlable. Answer-first,
honest, and proven — that's the page an engine cites and a customer trusts enough to call.
