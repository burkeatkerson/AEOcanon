**E-commerce shoppers ask AI for product recommendations and comparisons, and
engines answer from reviews, roundups, marketplaces, and video — so AEO for
e-commerce means winning those review-and-comparison sources and structuring product
content answer-first.** Being genuinely well-reviewed beats having the slickest
product page.

## What query patterns do e-commerce shoppers use?

Shoppers query AI with buying intent: **"best [product] for [need / budget]"**,
**"[product A] vs [product B]"**, **"is [product] worth it"**, **"what to look for
in [category]"**, and **"[product] alternatives"**. These are decision-stage
questions, and they map to buying guides, comparison content, and answer-first
product detail. Build your content plan from the real questions shoppers ask (mine
reviews, support, and search), then answer each directly — the
[alignment](/pillars/alignment) pillar applied to a shopping funnel.

shoppers trust:

Being talked about and reviewed across these matters more than backlinks —
[mentions correlate with AI visibility far more strongly than
links](/pillars/authority) — and a brand the ecosystem genuinely recommends wins the
"best X" answers. (Note: structured product data aids
[understanding, not citation rate directly](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations).)

## What should you build first?

Prioritize the content and signals that match buying-stage queries:

<Steps
  items={[
    { title: "1. A buying guide for your hero category", body: "'Best [category] for [need]' — an honest, answer-first guide that states who each option suits. This is the query shoppers ask most and the one roundups compete for." },
    { title: "2. Answer-first product pages for bestsellers", body: "Rewrite your top sellers to open with who-it's-for, key specs, and honest pros and cons — not a marketing paragraph." },
    { title: "3. A review engine", body: "Make earning genuine verified-buyer reviews routine across your marketplace and Trustpilot profiles. Reviews are the trust signal that drives recommendations." },
    { title: "4. One credible roundup inclusion", body: "Pitch a relevant independent reviewer or 'best [category]' publisher with a genuinely strong product. Accurate third-party inclusion is high-leverage." },
  ]}
/>

Get these four moving before scaling breadth. A well-reviewed hero category with a
strong buying guide outperforms a thin page on every SKU — and it targets the
decision-stage queries that actually convert.
