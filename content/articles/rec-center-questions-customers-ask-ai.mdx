('beginner-friendly climbing near me'), activities ('do you have bouldering or a pool'), and logistics
('do I need gear', 'kids classes', 'can I book a party').** Mapping each to readable content that
answers it is the core of a gym AEO content plan.

## How do I find the exact questions?

Listen where visitors already ask. Note what people ask at the front desk and on first visits, read
your reviews and the questions on your Google profile, scan local climbing and parenting forums and
groups, and [prompt the assistants directly](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask) on your
activities and area to see the follow-ups they surface. Capture the *natural wording* — "is there a
climbing gym near me that's good for total beginners" beats "novice-oriented climbing facility" —
because engines match the visitor's phrasing. Then prioritize by what genuinely fits your gym.

## Should I publish day-pass pricing if competitors hide it?

Yes — and it's an edge, not a risk. Visitors constantly ask AI "how much is a day pass near me," and a
clear day-pass price and range in readable text lets the engine recommend you for those high-intent
cost searches — while competitors who bury pricing in a widget get skipped. Transparency also builds
trust with a price-conscious visitor before they ever walk in. It's pure
[Alignment](/pillars/alignment): the real question, answered where the engine and the visitor can read
it. Map every bucket to readable content and you've built the plan that gets a climbing gym cited.
