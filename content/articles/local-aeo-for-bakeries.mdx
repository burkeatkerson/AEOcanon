## What makes a bakery 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about where you are, what you make, and whether you're open. An answer engine recommends a
bakery for "near me" only when it can [place you with certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend)
— right area, right specialty, open now. That certainty comes from signals that all agree: matching
address and hours, a complete profile, a readable product list, and reviews. When the signals agree,
you're an easy recommendation; when your hours conflict or your products are unreadable, the engine
sends the customer elsewhere.

This is the [near-me ranking](/learn/how-to-rank-in-ai-for-near-me-searches) discipline applied to
baking — and accurate hours are where most bakeries quietly lose.

## Why do accurate hours matter so much?

Because 'open now' and 'bakery near me' are among the most common queries, and the engine answers them
straight from your hours. If your hours are wrong or inconsistent across listings, the engine either
drops you from 'open now' answers or — worse — sends a customer to a closed door, costing you the order
and a review. Keep hours accurate and identical everywhere, update them for holidays when demand peaks,
and you remove the single most common reason a bakery gets skipped on a near-me query. It's the same
[NAP consistency](/learn/nap-consistency) discipline, with the clock attached.
