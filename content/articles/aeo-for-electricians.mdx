## Why does AEO matter so much for electricians?

Because electrical work is high-trust, often urgent, and decided on the first credible
answer. When a breaker keeps tripping or an outlet sparks, the customer asks an
assistant and acts immediately — and the
[AI answer names only two or three sources](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations),
not a page of links. Pew Research
[found people clicked a link just 8% of the time when an AI summary appeared, versus
15% without](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so for a trade where the customer has to trust whoever they let into their panel, the
answer *is* the front door. Being the cited electrician is the new equivalent of being
the name the neighborhood already trusts.

## How do electricians get recommended by AI?

By clearing the three gates a customer — and an engine — judge you on, in order.

Most electricians have a slow site and a reputation that's inconsistent across listings
— so they fail gates one and three without realizing the contest happened. Fixing those
is the highest-leverage work, and it's the same foundation that
[ranks you in Google](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together) too.

## Isn't this just SEO?

It's mostly the SEO you should already be doing, pointed at a new surface. Roughly
[70 to 80 percent overlaps](/learn/is-aeo-replacing-seo): the crawlable, fast,
locally-trusted site that ranks is the same one that gets cited. AEO adds answering
questions [directly and answer-first](/learn/answer-first-sentence), building
[service pages for extraction](/learn/electrician-service-pages-for-ai), and tightening
the [reviews and listings](/learn/electrician-reviews-ai-recommendations) that answer
engines lean on. You don't choose between them — you run one program that wins both.

## Where do local electricians have the advantage?

In specificity and trust. AI names only a few sources and
[citations spread thin](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands), so a focused local
electrician who answers "how much does a 200-amp panel upgrade cost in [city]" better
than a national directory can take the recommendation. The directory is generic and
faceless; you're specific, local, licensed, and real. That's the
[Originality](/pillars/originality) and [local authority](/learn/local-aeo-for-electricians)
edge a big aggregator can't easily copy — and for a safety-critical trade, a verifiable
license is trust a directory listing simply can't show.
