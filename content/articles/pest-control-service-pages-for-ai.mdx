**Write pest control service pages AI will cite by giving each pest and service its own page
that leads with the answer to the cost, safety, and service-area questions, in plain language
a customer and an engine can lift.** One self-contained, crawlable page per pest beats a
single bloated services page every time.

## Why one page per pest?

Because a citation is awarded to the page that best answers one specific question — and a
catch-all services page answers none of them well. When you give bed bugs, termites, rodents,
mosquitoes, and recurring plans each [their own
page](/learn/should-content-sections-stand-alone), each can go deep on its own cost, safety,
and treatment details — and each becomes citable for its own query. A single page trying to
cover every pest is shallow on all of them, so the engine cites a competitor with a dedicated,
focused page instead.

## What should each page lead with?

The answer the customer came for, before anything about you.

This is [answer-first writing](/learn/answer-first-sentence) applied to the trade: the
quotable answer up top, the depth below, the proof at the bottom.

## What makes a service page extractable?

Plain language and a clean structure. Write the way a customer asks — "bed bug treatment
typically costs…" — not in jargon, and use [question-shaped
headings](/learn/question-shaped-headings) the engine can match to a query. Keep each answer
in a self-contained passage so it can be [lifted without the surrounding
page](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage), reinforce it with [LocalBusiness
schema](/learn/pest-control-schema-markup), and make sure the page is fast and crawlable.
Answer-first, focused, and proven — that's the page an engine cites and a customer calls.
