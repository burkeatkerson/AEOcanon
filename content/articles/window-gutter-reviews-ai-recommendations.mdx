## Why do reviews carry so much weight for this trade?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge reliability — and a homeowner is trusting you
on ladders around their home. An assistant deciding whom to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform reviews to
gauge whether you're careful, thorough, and reliable. For recurring work where careful service matters,
that corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made
visible, and the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more
than backlinks do.

## How many reviews do I need?

Fewer than you think, fresher than you have. There's no magic count — consistency and recency outweigh a
raw total. A steady stream of genuine recent reviews signals an active, trusted company far better than a
big pile from three years ago. The goal is an ongoing flow: ask happy clients after each job, so new
reviews keep arriving and the engine keeps seeing a company that's [currently
trusted](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations), not formerly busy.

## Do the words in a review matter?

Yes — specific reviews do more work than generic praise.

Reviews that name what you did — cleared clogged gutters fast, spotless windows, careful around the
property — give engines specific detail tying you to those services and to careful, reliable work. They
reinforce the [questions homeowners ask](/learn/window-gutter-questions-customers-ask-ai), so a handful
of detailed reviews can outperform a wall of generic ones. Ask clients what to mention, and the reviews
start doing your AEO for you.
