## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a place the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine can
read your styles and place you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as where to get inked —
and that leans harder on whether clients love you. The engine recommends the studio it can [confidently
confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, skilled, and well-reviewed, because when it
recommends you it's putting its credibility behind the artistry. Your job is to make that confirmation
easy — readable styles, accurate hours, and genuine, loving reviews.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). Wrong hours, a hidden portfolio, or a thin review profile introduces
doubt, and doubt sends the client elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one studio over another?

Because it picks the one it's most confident clients will love. Faced with several options, the engine
names the studio whose signals best match the request — right style, right area, clean — and whose
reviews most clearly say people love the work. The competitor with a gallery-hidden portfolio, wrong
hours, or thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it loses. Winning is about being the [easiest to verify
as the loved local choice](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — which is exactly what
this library builds, piece by piece.
