**You already use AI to run the gym — scheduling classes, marketing to members,
winning back lapsed sign-ups — but being recommended by AI when someone new asks
where to work out is a completely different game, and it's the one most studios
are losing.** Prospective members have started asking AI *where to join*, and it
names one or two gyms. If yours isn't one, AI is quietly sending memberships to a
competitor.

## How are gyms & fitness studios using AI today?

Deeply, from the front desk to the inbox. Platforms like Mindbody, Vagaro, and
Glofox use AI to optimize class schedules, flag members at risk of churning, and
fire off automated win-back offers. Marketing tools draft the class descriptions,
challenge promos, and social reels that fill Saturday's HIIT slot, and ChatGPT
handles member emails and FAQ replies. Some studios even use AI to help build
programming and track member progress. All of it keeps your community engaged and
your calendar full — which is exactly why it's easy to assume AI is handling your
new-member marketing too.

## But is AI recommending your gym?

It isn't the same job. The model that flags a churning member isn't the system
deciding where a newcomer joins — and even when it's the same product, it
recommends based on what it can find and trust *about you on the open web*, not on
your private member data. When someone asks for a recommendation, the engine
retrieves and quotes the sources that best answer *their* question: your site (if
it's readable and answer-first), your reviews, and mentions of you across local
listings and fitness roundups. Your retention automation is invisible to that
process. See [is AI recommending your business](/learn/use-ai-but-is-ai-recommending-you)
for the full split.

## How do customers use AI to find a gym?

They ask it like they'd ask a fit friend. Instead of comparing a dozen sites,
they type "best gym near me," "where should I do [class type] in [town]," or
"gym with childcare and a sauna nearby." The assistant answers in place and names
only a couple of gyms. Because it compresses a whole page of options down to one
or two names, this is a **winner-take-most** moment: the gym it cites gets the
tour booking, and the rest are invisible — no matter how strong the community
nobody was shown.

## How do you know if AI is sending your prospects to a competitor?

Ask the engines yourself. Open ChatGPT, [Google AI Mode](/glossary/google-ai-mode),
and [Perplexity](/glossary/perplexity), and run the real questions prospects ask:
"best gym in [your city]," "where should I do [your specialty]," "fitness studio
near me." Note who gets named. If competitors show up and you don't — or the AI
lists the wrong hours, classes, or price — you've found the gap.

## What should a gym do about it?

You optimize to be the answer — that's [Answer Engine Optimization](/learn/what-is-aeo).
Practically: make your most important page lead with a complete, self-contained
answer to your core join question — what you offer, where you are, and what
membership costs to start — on a page an AI crawler can actually read; then earn
the reviews and off-site mentions engines trust. Start with our
[gyms & fitness hub](/industries/gyms) and the
[AEO guide for gyms](/learn/aeo-for-gyms). Keep using AI to run the studio — just
don't mistake it for being found by one.
