**A furniture store needs a website rebuild for AEO when the site is an image-only catalog with no
readable specs, slow, or built only in a third-party product slider — because the engine can only
recommend what it can read.** The rebuild puts your products, materials, dimensions, prices, and
delivery info in readable text everything else depends on.

## Why are your products the binding constraint?

Because [access is the first gate](/pillars/access), and for a furniture store the all-image catalog is
where you most often fail it. Shoppers search for "solid oak dining table near me" or "furniture store
that delivers to [town]," but if your products and specs live only in [photos or a slider the crawler
can't read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), the engine doesn't know what you sell, at what
price, or whether you deliver — so it can't recommend you. Add a slow, image-heavy build, and even your
basics are invisible. That's not a content problem you can write around; it's a foundation problem.

## How do I tell if my site is hurting me?

Run two quick tests, and look for the structural gaps.

If your products are photo-only, your specs are slider-trapped, or pages are slow, the site is working
against you. A fast site with [readable products and delivery
info](/learn/furniture-product-pages-for-ai) and [clean schema](/learn/furniture-store-schema-markup) is
what makes everything else possible.

## Can't I just keep my product slider?

Keep the slider for browsing — but you also need your products, materials, dimensions, prices, and
delivery info in readable text on the page. Because the slider is usually invisible to crawlers, relying
on it alone hides exactly the products shoppers search for. Add readable text alongside it, get the
access layer right — [server-rendered](/learn/server-side-vs-client-side-rendering), fast, with readable
products — and the rest of your furniture-store AEO finally has something to build on.
