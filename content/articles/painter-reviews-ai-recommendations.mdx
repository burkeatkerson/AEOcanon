## Why do reviews carry so much weight for painters?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge trust — and painting is a visible, in-your-home
project where quality and cleanliness matter. An assistant deciding whom to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform reviews to
gauge whether you deliver quality, clean, on-time work. For a project a homeowner will see every day,
that corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made
visible, and the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more
than backlinks do.

## How many reviews do I need?

Fewer than you think, fresher than you have. There's no magic count — consistency and recency outweigh a
raw total. A steady stream of genuine recent reviews signals an active, trusted contractor far better
than a big pile from three years ago. The goal is an ongoing flow: ask happy clients at project
completion, so new reviews keep arriving and the engine keeps seeing a contractor that's [currently
trusted](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations), not formerly busy.

## Do the words in a review matter?

Yes — specific reviews do more work than generic praise.

Reviews that name what you did — flawless interior, clean prep, on-time, transformed the cabinets — give
engines specific detail tying you to quality work. They reinforce the [questions homeowners
ask](/learn/painting-questions-customers-ask-ai), so a handful of detailed reviews can outperform a wall
of generic ones. Ask clients what to mention, and the reviews start doing your AEO for you.
