## How is corporate-wellness AEO different?

The intent is a researched B2B decision, not an individual sign-up. An HR manager or business owner
setting up a wellness benefit researches carefully — and asks the assistant detailed questions long
before they call. They're researching group rates, billing, and whether you can support their team.
Because [AEO works for B2B](/learn/does-aeo-work-for-b2b) exactly here, the gym whose pages answer those
questions clearly gets [cited during the research](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations) — and wins a
recurring contract most gyms never even surface for. And a corporate contract is a block of recurring
members at once, worth far more than a single sign-up.

## What questions do companies ask?

B2B, logistics-focused, and specific — each worth a clear answer on a dedicated page.

## How do I win corporate-wellness citations?

Give corporate memberships and wellness partnerships their own answer-first page — not a buried line on
your homepage. Publish what you offer, how group pricing and billing work, program options, and your
experience in readable text, reinforced by your [reviews](/learn/gym-reviews-ai-recommendations) and a
clear inquiry path. Most gyms either omit this entirely or hide it, so a clear, dedicated page is an
easy way to get cited for valuable, recurring B2B contracts — a block of members at once that compounds
your [growth](/learn/grow-a-gym-with-ai-search).
