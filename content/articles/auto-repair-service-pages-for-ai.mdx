bloated services page every time.

## Why one page per service?

Because a citation is awarded to the page that best answers one specific question — and a
catch-all services page answers none of them well. When you give brakes, diagnostics, AC,
transmission, and oil changes each [their own
page](/learn/should-content-sections-stand-alone), each can go deep on its own cost, timing, and
details — and each becomes citable for its own query. A single page trying to cover everything is
shallow on all of them, so the engine cites a competitor with a dedicated, focused page instead.

## What should each page lead with?

The answer the driver came for, before anything about you.

This is [answer-first writing](/learn/answer-first-sentence) applied to the trade: the quotable
answer up top, the depth below, the proof at the bottom.

## What makes a service page extractable?

Plain language and a clean structure. Write the way a driver asks — "front brake pads typically
cost…" — not in jargon, and use [question-shaped headings](/learn/question-shaped-headings) the
engine can match to a query. Keep each answer in a self-contained passage so it can be [lifted
without the surrounding page](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage), reinforce it with
[AutoRepair schema](/learn/auto-repair-schema-markup), and make sure the page is fast and
crawlable. Answer-first, focused, and proven — that's the page an engine cites and a driver calls.
