**Salons should use the HairSalon or BeautySalon (LocalBusiness subtypes) schema with accurate name,
address, phone, hours, and services, plus FAQ schema on answer pages — it helps engines parse what you
offer and confirm where and when you're open.** Schema clarifies readable content for AI; it never
rescues a booking widget or wrong hours.

## What does salon schema actually do?

It makes your salon details unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business), and the
HairSalon or BeautySalon subtype, labels your name, address, hours, services, and reviews so engines
parse them cleanly rather than guessing — reinforcing the [consistent identity](/learn/nap-consistency)
local recommendations depend on. It's the [structured data for AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo)
pattern applied to hair: clarity for the parser, on top of a menu and pricing that are already clear and
readable for the client.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your salon — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your details machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [a readable service menu and
pricing](/learn/salon-service-pages-for-ai) the crawler can parse, [accurate
hours](/learn/local-aeo-for-salons), and [genuine
reviews](/learn/salon-reviews-ai-recommendations). Schema can't rescue a menu trapped in a booking
widget or hours that are wrong — and faking reviews or details in markup is a misuse engines can
[detect](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations). Accurate schema on top of a readable menu and pricing is
the combination that works.
