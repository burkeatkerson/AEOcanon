## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each repair produces a review, a referral, and ideally a returning
customer on a maintenance schedule; reviews and [answer-first
content](/learn/auto-repair-service-pages-for-ai) earn citations and rankings; those bring direct
inquiries; and the money saved on purchased leads funds more content and more capacity — which
serves more cars. The directory model is the opposite: a faucet you pay to keep running, that stops
the moment you stop. AEO builds the [compounding asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead — and
a loyal base makes it spin even faster.

## How does retention accelerate it?

It turns one-time repairs into a loyal base and recurring revenue. A maintenance reminder, a
follow-up, and honest service convert a single repair into a returning customer — and each return
visit is a new touchpoint for a [fresh review](/learn/auto-repair-reviews-ai-recommendations), a
referral, and the next service. That steady reputation feeds the signals AI uses to recommend you,
while the recurring work keeps bays full between breakdown spikes. Every [maintenance
question](/learn/auto-repair-questions-customers-ask-ai) you answer is a chance to earn the next
visit — and the loyal base it builds.

## Can I really lower my cost per car?

Yes — by replacing recurring fees with assets that keep paying. A purchased lead costs every time
and is [resold to your competitors](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands); an earned citation,
ranking, loyal customer, or referral costs mostly upfront and pays for years. As your owned
pipeline and loyal base grow, your cost per car falls and your margins improve, because you're no
longer renting every customer. And because AI names only a few sources, a trusted, certified,
genuinely reviewed local shop can win recommendations a generic national directory
[can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
