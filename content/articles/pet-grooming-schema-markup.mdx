## What does grooming schema actually do?

It makes your business details unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business) labels
your name, address, hours, services, and reviews so engines parse them cleanly rather than guessing —
reinforcing the [consistent identity](/learn/nap-consistency) local recommendations depend on. It's the
[structured data for AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern applied to grooming: clarity for the
parser, on top of services and pricing that are already clear and readable for the pet parent.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your salon — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your details machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [readable services and
pricing](/learn/pet-grooming-service-pages-for-ai) the crawler can parse, [accurate
hours](/learn/local-aeo-for-pet-groomers), and [genuine
reviews](/learn/pet-grooming-reviews-ai-recommendations). Schema can't rescue a service menu trapped in
a booking widget or hours that are wrong — and faking reviews or details in markup is a misuse engines
can [detect](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations). Accurate LocalBusiness schema on top of readable
services is the combination that works.
