**Staffing agencies get found by AI search when their site is crawlable, answers the real questions
employers and candidates ask, and is backed by stated specialties and genuine reviews.** The AI names
only a few firms, so the agency that clears all three is the one recommended instead of a resold lead.

## What does an AI engine actually do to find you?

It retrieves, ranks, and names. When an employer asks for help filling roles, the engine pulls the
agencies it can read, judges which best answer the question and look trustworthy, and [names just a
few](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Each step is a filter: if a crawler can't fetch your
page, you're out before ranking; if your page doesn't answer the fee or process question, you're out at
ranking; if your trust signals are thin, a competitor gets named. Being found means clearing every
filter — and the same logic decides whether a candidate finds you.

## What are the three gates?

The same three an engine — and an employer — apply, in order.

## Why can't employers find my staffing agency?

Almost always one specific broken gate — not bad luck. Your site is thin or built only to feed job
boards so [crawlers see little](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), your pages dodge the fee and
process questions, or your reviews and listings are inconsistent. The fix is diagnostic: find the
*highest* broken gate and repair it first, because a great reputation can't rescue a site bots can't
read, and a fast site can't rescue a page that never answers the question. This is the same
[why-isn't-my-business-showing-up](/learn/why-isnt-my-local-business-showing-up-in-ai) logic, applied
to the profession.

## Does ranking in Google mean AI can find me?

Not on its own. Ranking proves Google can read and rank you — a good sign for the first gate — but AI
citation also needs an [extractable, answer-first page](/learn/answer-first-sentence) and trusted
signals like specialties and reviews. An agency can rank yet be absent from AI answers if the answer is
buried or trust is thin. The overlap is real but not total: clear all three gates and you get [found in
both](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).
