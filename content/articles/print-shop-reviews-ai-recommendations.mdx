## Why do reviews carry so much weight for print and sign shops?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge whether you'll deliver — and a buyer is
trusting you with a deadline, a brand's look, or a storefront sign. An assistant deciding whom to
recommend [synthesizes sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and
review-platform reviews to gauge whether your quality is sharp, your turnaround is honest, and your
shop is reliable. That corroborated reputation is the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made
visible — and the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility
more than backlinks do. It's also where you beat [Vistaprint and online
printers](/learn/aeo-for-print-and-sign-shops): they're faceless, you have a local, named reputation.

## How many reviews do I need?

Fewer than you think, fresher than you have. There's no magic count — consistency and recency outweigh
a raw total. A steady stream of genuine recent reviews signals an active, dependable shop far better
than a big pile from two years ago. The goal is an ongoing flow: ask happy customers right at pickup or
delivery, and ask your [recurring B2B accounts](/learn/business-and-bulk-printing-aeo) after a smooth
reorder — so new reviews keep arriving and the engine keeps seeing a shop that's
[currently trusted](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations), not formerly busy.

## Do the words in a review matter?

Yes — specific reviews do more work than generic praise.

Reviews that name what you did — nailed the color, hit the deadline, handled the reorders — give
engines specific detail tying you to those outcomes. They reinforce the [questions buyers
ask](/learn/print-shop-questions-customers-ask-ai), so a handful of detailed reviews can outperform a
wall of generic ones. Tell customers what to mention, and the reviews start doing your AEO for you.
