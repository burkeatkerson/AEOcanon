winter no-starts and dead batteries, summer AC repair and road-trip checks — before each surge, on durable
pages you update yearly.** Be the cited answer when the cold snap or heat wave hits, not scrambling after.

## What does the mobile-mechanic year look like?

Demand turns season by season, and the cited mechanic catches each turn. Here's the calendar to publish

The winter battery and no-start rush is your peak: when the temperature drops, weak batteries die in
driveways everywhere and every one of those drivers asks an assistant who can come start their car. Summer's
AC and road-trip season is the second reliable surge.

## How do I prepare for each surge?

Build the content while it's quiet, so it's ready when demand turns. Publish or refresh your winter
no-start and battery page in late fall, your AC-repair and road-trip page in late spring — ahead of when
drivers search. Pages need to be [crawled and trusted](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo) before the
surge; reacting after the first cold snap is too late to be cited in the moment. It's the [Freshness
pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned into a mobile-mechanic calendar.

## Should I make a new page each year?

No — update one durable page per seasonal topic. A persistent 'why won't my car start in the cold' or
'mobile AC repair' page [accumulates authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh the details
each year, which beats spinning up a throwaway page annually that starts from zero and splits your signals.
Keep one strong page per topic, sharpen it before each surge, and link it to your [service
pages](/learn/mobile-mechanic-service-pages-for-ai). One durable page, refreshed and ready, beats scrambling
when the cold snap hits.
