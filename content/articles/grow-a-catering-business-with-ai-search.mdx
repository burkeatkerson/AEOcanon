## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each great event earns a review, a referral, and a repeat client; [readable
packages](/learn/caterer-menu-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews earn citations for 'wedding caterer near
me' and 'corporate catering nearby'; those bring direct inquiries; and the lead fees you save fund better
food and a better team — which create more great events. The lead-platform model is the opposite: a
faucet you pay a fee to keep running, that resells your lead and stops the moment you stop. AEO builds the
[compounding asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead — loyal clients who inquire direct.

## What's the fastest place to start?

Fix the [readable packages and pricing](/learn/caterer-menu-pages-for-ai) first — it's the
highest-leverage gap — then make your service area and listings accurate so you win [near-me and
service-area](/learn/local-aeo-for-caterers) queries, and build a steady flow of [genuine
reviews](/learn/caterer-reviews-ai-recommendations). Those win citations within weeks. Then compound it:
turn every happy client into a review, a referral, and a recurring account, and add
[wedding](/learn/wedding-catering-ai-search) and [corporate catering](/learn/corporate-catering-aeo)
pages for high-value, plannable bookings. Small habits, compounding into an owned pipeline of direct
demand.

## How does this lower my cost to win a booking?

By replacing per-lead fees with assets that keep paying. A lead-platform inquiry costs a fee every single
time and is resold to four competitors; an earned citation, a recurring corporate account, or a
word-of-mouth referral costs mostly upfront and brings events for years. As your owned, direct demand
grows, your cost per booking falls and your margins improve — and because AI names only a few businesses,
a real, well-reviewed local caterer can win recommendations a faceless lead-site listing
[can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
