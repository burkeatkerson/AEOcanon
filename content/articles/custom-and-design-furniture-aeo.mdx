## How is custom and design AEO different?

The intent is weeks of planning, not a stock purchase. A buyer commissioning custom furniture or hiring
a designer researches carefully — and asks the assistant detailed questions long before they commit.
They're researching lead times, cost, materials, and whether you're right for their vision. Because [AEO
works for these higher-consideration decisions](/learn/does-aeo-work-for-b2b), the store whose pages
answer those questions clearly gets [cited during the research](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations)
— and wins a high-value, high-margin service a marketplace can't deliver at all.

## What questions do custom and design buyers ask?

Planning-stage, specific questions — each worth a clear page.

not a bare gallery.

## How do I win custom and design citations?

Give custom orders and design services their own answer-first pages — not a buried sentence or gallery.
Publish lead-time and cost guidance, your materials, your consultation process, and a described
portfolio of past work in readable text, with a clear inquiry path. Most stores hide all of this, so a
clear, dedicated page is an easy way to get cited for the high-margin work that sets you apart from every
marketplace — the kind that compounds your [growth](/learn/grow-a-furniture-store-with-ai-search) far
beyond a single stock sale.
