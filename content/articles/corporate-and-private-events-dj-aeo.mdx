## How is corporate and private event AEO different?

The intent is recurring, logistics-driven booking, not a one-off celebration. An event planner,
office manager, or venue coordinator booking a gala, a holiday party, a school dance, or a house-DJ slot
researches carefully — and asks the assistant detailed logistics questions long before they call. They're
checking sound for the room, appropriate playlists, MC and announcements, insurance, and lead time. Because
[AEO works for these higher-consideration bookings](/learn/does-aeo-work-for-b2b), the DJ whose pages answer
those questions clearly gets [cited during the research](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations) — and wins
a recurring relationship most DJs never even surface for.

## What questions do planners ask?

Logistics-focused and specific — each worth a clear answer on a dedicated page.

## How do I win corporate and private event citations?

Give corporate, gala, school dance, and private event DJing their own answer-first pages — not a buried
line under your wedding work. Publish pricing by event type, sound and setup, playlist and MC handling,
insurance, and lead time in readable text, reinforced by your [reviews](/learn/dj-reviews-ai-recommendations)
and a clear inquiry path. Most DJs either omit this entirely or trap it in a PDF, so a clear, dedicated
page is an easy way to get cited for recurring, predictable bookings and venue relationships — the steadiest
demand that compounds your [growth](/learn/grow-a-dj-business-with-ai-search).
