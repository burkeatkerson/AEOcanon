**Originality is the sixth pillar of the AEO Canon: machines are built to find
the best source, and original data exists in exactly one place — so the engine
cites that place.** Everything else in the Canon can be replicated by a
competitor. Your original contribution cannot.

## Why originality is the only uncopyable advantage

A competitor can match your site speed, your schema, your answer-first
structure, even your authority over time. What they cannot match is something
that only exists because _you_ produced it — a number you measured, a result you
achieved, a pattern only you have seen.

<PullQuote cite="Pillar 6 · Originality">
  Be the study everyone cites — not the article that cites it.
</PullQuote>

<Callout variant="insight" title="The metaphor">
  <p>
    Originality is the lighthouse in a sea of generated content. When every
    generic answer looks the same, the source with something specific and true
    to add is the one that stands out — and gets cited.
  </p>
</Callout>

## The economics of a generated web

Large language models made adequate, generic prose free and instant. That
collapsed the value of "good enough" content to roughly zero and concentrated all
the remaining value in what models _can't_ produce on their own.

Because citation is so distributed, you don't need to dominate the whole web. You
need to own one specific, valuable answer so completely that any engine
addressing that question has to come to you.
