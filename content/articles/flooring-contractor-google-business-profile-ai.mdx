## Why does Google Business Profile matter for AI search?

Because it's a structured, trusted confirmation of who and where you are — exactly what an engine
needs to recommend a local installer. When the assistant decides whom to name for a near-me or
material query, it leans on the profile to verify your identity, service area, hours, and reputation
[before recommending you](/learn/does-google-business-profile-help-ai-search). A complete, accurate
profile makes you an easy, safe recommendation; a thin or stale one leaves the engine unsure and
routes the inquiry elsewhere.

The reviews on your profile do double duty — they're a top input to [whether AI recommends
you](/learn/flooring-contractor-reviews-ai-recommendations) at all.

## Does GBP replace a good website?

No — it's half the picture. The profile is a powerful placement and trust signal, but engines also
retrieve and cite your [actual service pages](/learn/flooring-contractor-service-pages-for-ai) for the
detailed questions a profile can't answer — what hardwood costs per square foot, how long an LVP
install takes, whether you remove old flooring. You need both: a complete profile so the engine can
confidently place and trust you, and answer-first pages for the substance that wins the detailed query.
Treat them as one system, kept [consistent everywhere](/learn/nap-consistency).
