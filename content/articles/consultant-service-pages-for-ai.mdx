## Why one page per offering?

Because a citation is awarded to the page that best answers one specific question — and a catch-all
services page answers none of them well. When you give your advisory retainer, project engagements,
fractional role, assessment, and workshop each [their own
page](/learn/should-content-sections-stand-alone), each can go deep on its own scope, outcomes, and
engagement model — and each becomes citable for its own query. A single page trying to cover everything
is shallow on all of them, so the engine cites a competitor with a dedicated, focused page.

## What should each page lead with?

The answer the owner came for, before anything about you.

This is [answer-first writing](/learn/answer-first-sentence) applied to the profession: the quotable
answer up top, the depth below, the proof at the bottom.

## What makes an offering page extractable?

Plain language and a clean structure. Write the way an owner asks — "a fractional CFO engagement
typically runs …" — not in jargon, and use [question-shaped
headings](/learn/question-shaped-headings) the engine can match to a query. Keep each answer in a
self-contained passage so it can be [lifted without the surrounding
page](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage), reinforce it with [ProfessionalService and FAQ
schema](/learn/consultant-schema-markup), and make sure the page is fast and crawlable. Answer-first,
focused, and proven — that's the page an engine cites and an owner contacts.
