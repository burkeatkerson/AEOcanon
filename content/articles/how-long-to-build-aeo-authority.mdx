**Off-site AEO authority typically builds over months, not weeks, because it depends
on other people mentioning and corroborating you — unlike on-page fixes you control
directly.** Starting narrow in one topic and earning genuine, consistent mentions
shortens the path, but authority is the slowest pillar by design.

## Why is authority the slow pillar?

Because you don't control it outright. [Access](/pillars/access),
[alignment](/pillars/alignment), and [extractability](/pillars/extractability) are
yours to fix — you can ship them in days. [Authority](/pillars/authority) requires
journalists, communities, and reference sources to mention and corroborate you, which
happens on *their* timeline, not yours. That dependency on third parties is exactly
what makes it take months, and why [overall AEO results
compound](/learn/how-long-does-aeo-take) rather than arrive at once.

## How do I make it faster?

Narrow and useful. Concentrate on one topic so [corroboration accumulates around a
single area](/learn/build-ai-authority-from-scratch) instead of scattering, target
the [platforms and publications engines already
cite](/learn/which-platforms-build-ai-authority), and become a [quotable
expert](/learn/does-being-quoted-by-journalists-help-aeo). Depth and relevance compound
faster than broad, thin effort — you're trying to be consistently described as expert
in a specific space, and focus is what gets you there sooner.

## Should I wait for authority before the rest?

No — run it in parallel. Fix the faster pillars first so you can capture citations as
authority grows: a crawlable, [answer-first](/learn/answer-first-sentence) page can
win citations on lower-competition questions even before your authority is fully
built, then benefit more as it compounds. Treat authority as the long-running
background investment while your on-page work earns wins now.
