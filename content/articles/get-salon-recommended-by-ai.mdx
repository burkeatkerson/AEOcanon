## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a place the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine can
read your menu and place you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as where to book — and
that leans harder on whether clients love you. The engine recommends the salon it can [confidently
confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, skilled, and well-reviewed, because when it
recommends you it's putting its credibility behind the experience. Your job is to make that confirmation
easy — a readable menu, accurate hours, and genuine, loving reviews.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). Wrong hours, a hidden menu, or a thin review profile introduces
doubt, and doubt sends the client elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one salon over another?

Because it picks the one it's most confident clients will love. Faced with several options, the engine
names the salon whose signals best match the request — right specialty, right area — and whose reviews
most clearly say people love it. The competitor with a widget-hidden menu, wrong hours, or thin reviews
is the riskier vouch, so it loses. Winning is about being the [easiest to verify as the loved local
choice](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — which is exactly what this library builds,
piece by piece.
