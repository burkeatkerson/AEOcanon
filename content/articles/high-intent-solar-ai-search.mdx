## Why is high-intent the most valuable solar search?

Because it's the moment money is about to move, and the assistant frames the whole decision. A
homeowner ready to go solar researches whether it's worth it, system cost, and payback before they
contact anyone — and the [AI answer names only a few sources](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations).
Pew Research [found people click a link just 8% of the time when an AI summary
appears](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer shapes the shortlist before you ever hear from them. The installer cited at this stage is
the one the skeptical homeowner trusts enough to call first.

The practical, commitment-stage ones — and you should own every one.

## Why does answering cost and payback honestly matter so much?

Because cost and payback are the first things a homeowner researches and the questions most solar sites
dodge or gate behind a form. A clear, honest cost range and payback estimate — with the factors that
move them — earns trust and citations precisely when intent is highest. Dodging it ("every home is
unique, get a free quote") sends the skeptical buyer to a competitor or a lead-gen platform that
actually answered. Being the installer who explains the money and the math honestly is the
[Credibility](/pillars/credibility) and [Originality](/pillars/originality) edge that gets you cited
and called — map every one of these questions to a page in your
[questions library](/learn/solar-questions-customers-ask-ai), and own the
[incentives and financing](/learn/solar-incentives-financing-aeo) angle too.
