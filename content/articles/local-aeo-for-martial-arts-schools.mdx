## What makes a dojo 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about where you are and what you offer. An answer engine recommends a martial arts school for
"near me" only when it can [place you with certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend)
— right area, right kind of dojo. That certainty comes from signals that all agree: matching
name-address-phone, accurate hours, a complete profile, readable programs and pricing, and reviews. When
the signals agree, you're an easy recommendation; when they conflict or your programs are hidden in a
widget, the engine favors a clearer competitor.

## Why does NAP consistency matter so much?

Because a mismatch is a doubt, and engines route around doubt. If your phone number differs between your
site and your Google profile, or an old address still shows in a listing, the engine can't be sure which
business you are or where you operate — so it favors a competitor it can [place with
confidence](/learn/nap-consistency). For dojos that have moved, rebranded, or opened a second location,
this is a common and quiet leak. Clean it up and you remove the single most fixable reason an engine
skips you on a near-me query.
