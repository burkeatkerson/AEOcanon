feeds the local data engines draw on.** It won't get you cited single-handedly, but an
incomplete or inconsistent profile makes you hard to recommend.

## How does the profile influence AI recommendations?

By anchoring who and where you are. A complete [Google Business
Profile](https://support.google.com/business/answer/7091) shapes how Google's own AI
surfaces understand your business, and its data propagates into the wider local web
that other engines draw on. So whether or not a given engine reads it directly, a
strong profile improves the consistent local picture *every* engine builds of you —
which is what [local recognition](/learn/local-aeo) depends on.

## What should I actually complete?

All of it, accurately. Verify the profile, then fill every field — category, hours,
service area, services, description, attributes, and current photos — with your
[name, address, and phone matching your website exactly](/learn/nap-consistency).

## Is the profile enough on its own?

No — it's the foundation, not the whole house. The profile confirms who and where you
are, but citations also depend on [genuine reviews](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations),
[consistent local citations](/learn/local-citations-and-ai-search), local mentions,
and [pages that answer local questions](/learn/local-service-area-pages-for-ai). Get
the profile right first, then build the trust and content layers on top — together they
make you the local business AI [chooses to
recommend](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend).
