## Why do reviews carry so much weight for boutiques?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge which shops people trust — and a clothing
purchase is a personal, fit-and-style decision. An assistant deciding what to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform reviews to
gauge whether you carry great styles and the fit is right. For a boutique, that corroborated reputation
is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the [off-site
mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks do.

## Do reviews that mention fit and style matter more?

Yes — because so much of clothing-buying is about getting the right fit and look.

A review naming a great style and true-to-size fit reassures the engine you deliver what the shopper
wants — exactly what someone searching for a [specific style or
niche](/learn/near-me-boutique-ai-search) needs to hear. Invite happy shoppers to mention what they
bought and how it fit, and the reviews start matching you to the searches that bring sales.

## How many reviews — and how fresh?

Fresh beats big. There's no magic count — recency and a steady flow outweigh a stale total. A shop with
current reviews that mention styles, fit, and service signals an active, trusted business far better
than one coasting on old ratings. Build a simple habit: invite happy shoppers to review, and [respond to
them](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations) — engagement signals an active shop that cares,
which is exactly what an engine wants to recommend.
