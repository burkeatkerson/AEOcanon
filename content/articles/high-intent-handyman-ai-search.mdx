## Why is high-intent the most valuable handyman search?

Because it's the moment someone wants a problem gone, fast — and the assistant frames who they call. A
homeowner with a busted latch or a wall to patch researches who's nearby, who fixes it, and what it costs
before they contact anyone — and the [AI answer names only a few sources](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations).
Pew Research [found people click a link just 8% of the time when an AI summary
appears](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer decides the shortlist before you ever hear from them. The handyman cited at this stage is the
one the homeowner trusts enough to call first.

## What questions do ready-to-hire homeowners ask?

The fast, practical ones — and you should own every one.

These are the highest-intent handyman queries there are, and they reward the [Alignment](/pillars/alignment)
of answering the real question over marketing copy.

## Why does answering price and small jobs honestly matter so much?

Because those are the first things a homeowner checks and the questions most handyman sites dodge. A clear
hourly rate — with any minimum and what moves the price — plus a plain "yes, we do small jobs" earns trust
and citations precisely when intent is highest. Dodging it ("every job is different, call us") sends the
homeowner to a competitor or a bidding app that actually answered. Being the handyman who states the rate
and welcomes the small stuff is the [Credibility](/pillars/credibility) and [Originality](/pillars/originality)
edge that gets you cited and called — map every one of these questions to a page in your
[questions library](/learn/handyman-questions-customers-ask-ai).
