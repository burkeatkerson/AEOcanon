## Why do reviews carry so much weight for a handyman?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge trust — and hiring a handyman means letting a
stranger into your home. An assistant deciding whom to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and third-party reviews to gauge
whether you're reliable, on time, tidy, and fair on price. For a trade built entirely on trust and
repeat calls, that corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility)
made visible, and the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility
more than backlinks do.

## How many reviews do I need?

Fewer than you think, fresher than you have. There's no magic count — consistency and recency outweigh a
raw total. A steady stream of genuine recent reviews signals an active, trusted handyman far better than a
big pile from three years ago. The goal is an ongoing flow: build a simple habit of asking at the end of
every visit, so new reviews keep arriving and the engine keeps seeing a handyman that's [currently
trusted](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations), not formerly busy.

## Do the words in a review matter?

Yes — specific reviews do more work than generic praise.

Reviews that name what you did — mounted a TV and patched the wall, cleared a whole to-do list in one
visit, showed up on time and cleaned up — give engines specific detail tying you to those exact jobs and
to reliable delivery. They reinforce the [questions homeowners
ask](/learn/handyman-questions-customers-ask-ai), so a handful of detailed reviews can outperform a wall
of generic ones. Ask clients what to mention, and the reviews start doing your AEO for you.
