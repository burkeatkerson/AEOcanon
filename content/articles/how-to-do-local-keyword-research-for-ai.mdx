## Where do the best local questions come from?

From the people already asking them. Your sales and support calls are the richest source
— real questions from real local customers — followed by [reviews](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations)
(the words customers actually use), 'near me' autocomplete and People Also Ask, and
prompting the engines directly on your services and area to see the follow-up questions
they surface. This is [finding the questions people ask
AI](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask), pointed at local intent — and it's the
[Alignment pillar](/pillars/alignment) for a local business.

## What makes local questions different?

They carry a place and a constraint. People don't ask AI "dentist" — they ask "best
family dentist in [town] that takes my insurance" or "emergency electrician open now near
me." The location and the qualifier are part of what you're matching, so capture the
*whole* [conversational question](/learn/how-people-phrase-questions-to-ai), not the
stripped keyword. That fuller phrasing tells you exactly what to answer.

## How do I prioritize and use them?

Rank by intent and value, then map to pages. Favor questions close to a booking or call,
tied to services you want more of, in areas you actually serve, and where you can [answer
better than a directory](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend). Then assign
each priority question to the [page that should own it](/learn/local-service-area-pages-for-ai)
and answer it first and plainly. A prioritized, place-specific question list is what
turns local research into citable local pages.
