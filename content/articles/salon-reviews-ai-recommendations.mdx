## Why do reviews carry so much weight for salons?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge which salons clients actually love — and
choosing where to get your hair done is a trust decision. An assistant deciding what to recommend
[synthesizes sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform
reviews to gauge whether you're a great place to book. For a salon, that corroborated reputation is
decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the [off-site
mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks do.

## Do reviews that mention stylists and services matter more?

Yes — specific reviews are gold, because they speak the language of what clients want.

A review naming a stylist, a service, or a result gives the engine detail to tie you to "balayage
specialist near me" or "best barber for fades nearby" — the exact [specialty and result
queries](/learn/salon-questions-customers-ask-ai) clients ask. Invite happy clients to mention who did
their hair and what they got, and the reviews start matching you to the searches that fill the chair.

## How many reviews — and how fresh?

Fresh beats big. There's no magic count — recency and a steady flow outweigh a stale total. A salon with
current reviews that name real services signals an active, loved business far better than one coasting
on old ratings. Build a simple habit: invite happy clients to review at checkout, and [respond to
them](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations) — engagement signals an active salon that cares,
which is exactly the place an engine wants to recommend.
