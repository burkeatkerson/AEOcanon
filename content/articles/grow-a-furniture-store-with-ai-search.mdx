## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each great sale earns a review and a repeat customer — people furnish a room
this year and the next room later; [readable products](/learn/furniture-product-pages-for-ai) and
genuine reviews earn citations for "best furniture store near me"; those bring high-ticket buyers; and
the margin you protect by not feeding a marketplace funds a better showroom and delivery. The
marketplace model is the opposite: a commodity listing that competes on price and owns the customer. AEO
builds the [compounding asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead — loyal buyers and design clients
who buy direct, at full margin.

## What's the fastest place to start?

Fix the [readable products and details](/learn/furniture-product-pages-for-ai) first — it's the
highest-leverage gap — then make hours, delivery, and lead-time info accurate so you win [near-me and
style searches](/learn/near-me-furniture-ai-search), and build a steady flow of [genuine
reviews](/learn/furniture-store-reviews-ai-recommendations). Those win citations within weeks. Then
compound it: turn every happy buyer into a design client or repeat customer (rooms get furnished over
years), and add [custom and design service pages](/learn/custom-and-design-furniture-aeo) for
high-margin, plannable work.

## Why are marketplace sales such a bad deal?

Because they commoditize your product and own the customer. Wayfair, IKEA, and Amazon turn your sofa
into an interchangeable listing, compete it down on price, and keep the buyer relationship — so you do
the work for thin margin and build no loyalty. An earned AI citation sends a full-margin, high-ticket
buyer who's yours, and that buyer can become a design client who furnishes a whole house over years. As
your cited, direct, repeat base grows, your margin per sale improves — and because AI names only a few
stores, a real, well-reviewed local store can win recommendations a faceless marketplace
[can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
