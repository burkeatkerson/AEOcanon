## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each great class experience earns a review and a referral; a [readable
schedule and pricing](/learn/yoga-studio-service-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews earn citations for
'best yoga studio near me'; those bring new students; and the intro-offer students who convert and stay
produce more reviews and referrals — while the ad and aggregator spend you save funds better teaching
and community. The ClassPass-and-ads model is the opposite: a faucet you pay to keep running, that
takes a cut and stops sending the moment you stop. AEO builds the [compounding
asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead — recurring members who found you and stay.

## Why is retention central to studio growth?

Because a studio's value *is* recurring revenue, and filling the top of the funnel only pays off if
intro-offer students convert and stay. A member who practices for years is worth many times a drop-in
who churns — and long-tenured, happy students are exactly who write the [reviews that mention teachers
and how they felt](/learn/yoga-studio-reviews-ai-recommendations) and refer friends, feeding the AI
recommendations that bring the next students. So growth isn't just AEO at the top; it's AEO plus
retention together, each compounding the other. Win members with citations, keep them with a great
experience, and the flywheel accelerates.

## How does this lower my cost per member?

By replacing recurring ad and aggregator spend with assets that keep paying. A ClassPass booking takes
a cut every time and a paid ad stops when you stop; an earned citation, a loyal long-term member, a
referral, or a [corporate contract](/learn/corporate-yoga-wellness-aeo) costs mostly upfront and pays
for years. As your cited, referred, and retained base grows, your cost per member falls and your
revenue stabilizes — and because AI names only a few studios, a real, well-reviewed local studio can
win recommendations a faceless chain or aggregator listing
[can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
