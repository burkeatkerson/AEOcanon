## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be an entity the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine can
retrieve you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as the answer — and that's a higher bar of
trust. The engine recommends the studio it can [confidently confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities)
is real, capable, and well-regarded, because when it recommends you it's putting its own credibility
behind the choice. Your job is to make that confirmation effortless and consistent.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

  items={[

  ]}

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). One inconsistency (an old phone number, a mismatched address)
introduces doubt, and doubt routes the recommendation elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one studio over another?

Because it picks the one it's most sure about. Faced with several options, the engine names the studio
whose signals most confidently match the request — right service, right area or remote, clearly capable,
genuinely reviewed. The competitor with conflicting listings or thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it
loses even if the work is just as good. Winning is less about being the most talented studio in town and
more about being the [easiest to verify as the best](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend)
— which is exactly what this library builds, piece by piece. And there's a bonus: a studio that wins its
own recommendation has proven it can win one for clients.
