## Why does Google Business Profile matter for AI search?

Because it's a structured, trusted confirmation of where you are, what you serve, and whether you're
open — exactly what an engine needs to recommend a restaurant. When the assistant decides whom to name
for a near-me or 'open now' query, it leans on the profile to verify your location, hours, cuisine, and
reputation [before recommending you](/learn/does-google-business-profile-help-ai-search). A complete,
accurate profile makes you an easy recommendation; wrong hours or a thin profile leaves the engine
unsure — or sends a diner to a closed door.

The reviews on your profile do double duty — they're a top input to [whether AI recommends
you](/learn/restaurant-reviews-ai-recommendations) at all.

## Does GBP replace a good website?

No — it's half the picture. The profile is a powerful placement and 'open now' signal, but engines
also retrieve and cite your own [readable menu](/learn/restaurant-menu-pages-for-ai) and pages for
dish-level and detailed questions — "best carbonara near me," "do you have a private room." You need
both: a complete, accurate profile so the engine can place you and confirm you're open, and a readable
menu and pages for the substance that wins the discovery query. Treat them as one system, kept
[consistent everywhere](/learn/nap-consistency).
