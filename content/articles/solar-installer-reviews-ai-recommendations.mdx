## Why do reviews carry so much weight for installers?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge trust — and going solar is a big, anxious,
twenty-year commitment. An assistant deciding whom to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and third-party reviews to
gauge whether you're reliable, honest about cost and payback, and deliver systems that actually
produce. For a trade where a homeowner is handing over five figures and skeptics fear fly-by-night
door-knockers, that corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility
pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks)
that correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks do.

## How many reviews do I need?

Fewer than you think, fresher than you have. There's no magic count — consistency and recency outweigh
a raw total. A steady stream of genuine recent reviews signals an active, trusted installer far better
than a big pile from three years ago. The goal is an ongoing flow: build a simple habit of asking at
install completion and again after the first few production months, so new reviews keep arriving and
the engine keeps seeing an installer that's [currently trusted](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations),
not formerly busy.

## Do the words in a review matter?

Yes — specific reviews do more work than generic praise.

Reviews that name what you did — a system that produces as promised, an honest payback conversation, a
clean install with on-time interconnection — give engines specific detail tying you to real results
and straight dealing. They reinforce the [questions homeowners
ask](/learn/solar-questions-customers-ask-ai), so a handful of detailed reviews can outperform a wall
of generic ones. Ask clients what to mention, and the reviews start doing your AEO for you.
