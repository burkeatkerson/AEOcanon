## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a local entity the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine
can retrieve you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as the answer — and that's a higher bar
of trust, because hiring a mover is a high-value, high-stakes decision. The engine recommends the company
it can [confidently confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, licensed, and well-regarded,
because when it recommends you it's putting its own credibility behind the choice. Your job is to make
that confirmation effortless and consistent.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). One inconsistency (an old phone number, no visible license) introduces
doubt, and doubt routes the recommendation elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one moving company over another?

Because it picks the one it's most sure about. Faced with several options, the engine names the mover
whose signals most confidently match the request — right service, right route, clearly licensed,
genuinely reviewed. The competitor with no visible license or thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it
loses even if the crews are just as good. Winning is less about being the best mover in town and more
about being the [easiest to verify as the best](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) —
which is exactly what this library builds, piece by piece.
