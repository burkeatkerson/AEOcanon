## What makes a jeweler 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about where you are and what you do. An answer engine recommends a jeweler for "near me" or a
service query only when it can [place you with certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend)
— right area, does engagement rings or custom or repair, open when the buyer needs it. That certainty
comes from signals that all agree, matching name-address-phone, a complete profile, named services in
text, and reviews. When the signals agree, you're an easy recommendation; when your services are vague or
buried in an image, the engine favors a store that made them clear.

This is the [near-me ranking](/learn/how-to-rank-in-ai-for-near-me-searches) discipline applied to
jewelry — and clearly named services are where most stores quietly lose.

## Why do my services matter so much?

Because most jewelry searches are really about a specific need — "engagement rings near me," "jewelry
repair near me," "custom jeweler nearby." If your services and specialties aren't clear, accurate, and in
[readable text](/learn/jeweler-product-pages-for-ai), the engine can't confirm you do that work, so it
recommends a store that made it obvious. State exactly what you offer — bridal, custom design, resizing,
repair, GIA-certified diamonds — keep it consistent with your [listings](/learn/nap-consistency), and you
remove the most common reason a jeweler gets skipped on a high-intent near-me query.
