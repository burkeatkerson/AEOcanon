## What does an AI engine actually do to find you?

It retrieves, ranks, and names. When someone asks for a tattoo studio, the engine pulls the places it
can read, judges which best fit the request and look well-reviewed, and [names just a
few](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Each step is a filter: if the crawler can't read your
styles and pricing, you're out before ranking; if your hours are wrong, you're dropped from near-me; if
your reviews are thin, a competitor gets named. Being found means clearing every filter — and for
studios, the readable styles and visible pricing are often what's missing.

## Why can't clients find my studio?

Almost always one specific broken gate — and for studios it's usually the image-only gallery. If your
portfolio and prices are trapped in [pictures or an Instagram embed the crawler can't
read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), the engine doesn't know what styles you do or what they
cost, so it can't recommend you for "cover-up artist near me" or "affordable fine-line tattoo nearby."
Other common breaks: inconsistent hours, or thin reviews. The fix is diagnostic — find the *highest*
broken gate and repair it first. For most studios, getting styles, artists, and pricing into plain text
is the highest-leverage move.

## Does ranking in Google mean AI can find me?

Not on its own. Ranking and a map listing help, but AI citation also needs your [styles and pricing in
readable text](/learn/tattoo-studio-service-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews. A studio can show on a
map yet be absent from "best realism tattoo artist near me" answers if the engine can't read the
portfolio or the reviews are thin. Clear all three gates and you get [found in
both](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).
