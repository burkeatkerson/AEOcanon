## Why does seasonality matter for coaches?

Because motivation — and bookings — surge in predictable waves, and the cited coach catches them. Spring
brings the golf and tennis wave as courses and courts reopen; summer brings camp and junior-clinic
season; late summer brings pre-season tune-ups before school sports. The coach whose answer is already
published, crawled, and trusted [gets cited the moment the wave
hits](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo) — while competitors are still updating their site. It's
the [Freshness pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned into a demand calendar, and the difference between a
packed spring and a quiet one.

## How do I prepare for each wave?

Build the content while it's calm, so it's ready when motivation spikes.

## Should I make a new seasonal page each year?

No — update one durable evergreen page per theme. A persistent 'spring golf lessons' or 'summer junior
camp' page [accumulates authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh it each year, which
beats spinning up a throwaway 'Summer 2026' page that starts from zero and splits your signals. Keep one
strong page per season that answers the beginner, camp, and pre-season questions, sharpen it before each
wave, and link it to your [lesson and pricing pages](/learn/sports-instructor-service-pages-for-ai). One
durable page, refreshed and ready, beats scrambling when the season hits.
