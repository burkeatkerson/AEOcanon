## Why are your arrangements the binding constraint?

Because [access is the first gate](/pillars/access), and for a florist the all-image site is where you
most often fail it. Buyers search for "sympathy arrangements near me" or "florist that delivers to
[town]," but if your arrangements and delivery info live only in [photos or a widget the crawler can't
read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), the engine doesn't know what you offer or where you
deliver, so it can't recommend you. Add a slow build, and even your basics are invisible. That's not a
content problem you can write around; it's a foundation problem.

## How do I tell if my site is hurting me?

Run two quick tests, and look for the structural gaps.

If your arrangements are photo-only, your delivery info is widget-trapped, or the page is slow, the site
is working against you. A fast site with [readable arrangements and delivery
info](/learn/florist-product-pages-for-ai) and [clean schema](/learn/florist-schema-markup) is what
makes everything else possible.

## Can't I just keep my order widget?

Keep the widget for ordering — but you also need your arrangements, occasions, and delivery info in
readable text on the page. Because the widget is usually invisible to crawlers, relying on it alone
hides exactly the offerings buyers search for. Add readable text alongside it, get the access layer
right — [server-rendered](/learn/server-side-vs-client-side-rendering), fast, with readable arrangements
— and the rest of your florist AEO finally has something to build on.
