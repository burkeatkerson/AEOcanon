**Furniture stores get found by AI search when their products, materials, dimensions, and prices are
readable as real text, they answer the questions shoppers ask, and they're backed by accurate listings
and genuine reviews.** The AI names only a few stores, so the one that clears all three is the one
recommended.

## What does an AI engine actually do to find you?

It retrieves, ranks, and names. When someone needs furniture, the engine pulls the stores it can read,
judges which best fit the request and look well-reviewed, and [names just a
few](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Each step is a filter: if the crawler can't read your
products, you're out before ranking; if your delivery and price info are missing, you're dropped from
high-intent queries; if your reviews are thin, a competitor gets named. Being found means clearing
every filter — and for furniture stores, the readable products and details are often what's missing.

## Why can't shoppers find my furniture store?

Almost always one specific broken gate — and for furniture stores it's usually the image-only catalog.
If your products and specs live only in [photos the crawler can't
read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), the engine doesn't know what you sell or at what price,
so it can't recommend you for "mid-century sofa near me" or "solid wood dining table under $1,500."
Other common breaks: inconsistent hours and delivery info, or thin reviews. The fix is diagnostic —
find the *highest* broken gate and repair it first. For most furniture stores, putting products,
materials, dimensions, and prices in real text is the highest-leverage move.

## Does ranking in Google mean AI can find me?

Not on its own. Ranking and a map listing help, but AI citation also needs your [products and details
in readable text](/learn/furniture-product-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews. A store can show on a map
yet be absent from "best place to buy a mattress near me" answers if the engine can't read the site or
the reviews are thin. Clear all three gates and you get [found in
both](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).
