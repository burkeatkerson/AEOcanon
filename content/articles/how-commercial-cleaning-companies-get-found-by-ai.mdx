questions managers ask, and is backed by stated insurance, certifications, and genuine reviews.** The

lead.

## What does an AI engine actually do to find you?

It retrieves, ranks, and names. When a manager asks for a cleaning company, the engine pulls the
companies it can read, judges which best answer the question and look trustworthy, and [names just a
few](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Each step is a filter: if a crawler can't fetch your
page, you're out before ranking; if your page doesn't answer the cost or frequency question, you're out
at ranking; if your trust signals are thin, a competitor gets named. Being found means clearing every
filter.

## What are the three gates?

The same three an engine — and a manager — apply, in order.

## Why can't managers find my cleaning company?

Almost always one specific broken gate — not bad luck. Your site is a thin brochure so [crawlers see
little](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), your pages dodge the cost and frequency questions, or
your reviews and listings are inconsistent. The fix is diagnostic: find the *highest* broken gate and
repair it first, because a great reputation can't rescue a site bots can't read, and a fast site can't
rescue a page that never answers the question. This is the same
[why-isn't-my-business-showing-up](/learn/why-isnt-my-local-business-showing-up-in-ai) logic, applied
to the industry.

## Does ranking in Google mean AI can find me?

Not on its own. Ranking proves Google can read and rank you — a good sign for the first gate — but AI
citation also needs an [extractable, answer-first page](/learn/answer-first-sentence) and trusted
signals like insurance, certifications, and reviews. A company can rank yet be absent from AI answers
if the answer is buried or trust is thin. The overlap is real but not total: clear all three gates and
you get [found in both](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).
