## What does coffee shop schema actually do?

It makes your café details unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness and CafeOrCoffeeShop
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business) label
your name, address, hours, menu, price range, and amenities so engines parse them cleanly rather than
guessing — reinforcing the [consistent identity](/learn/nap-consistency) and availability local
recommendations depend on. It's the [structured data for AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern
applied to coffee: clarity for the parser, on top of a menu and hours that are already clear and
readable for the customer.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your café — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your details machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on a [readable
menu](/learn/coffee-shop-menu-pages-for-ai) the crawler can actually parse, [accurate
hours](/learn/local-aeo-for-coffee-shops), and [genuine
reviews](/learn/coffee-shop-reviews-ai-recommendations). Schema can't rescue a PDF menu the engine
can't read or hours that are wrong — and faking reviews or details in markup is a misuse engines can
[detect](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations). Accurate schema on top of a real, readable menu is the
combination that works.
