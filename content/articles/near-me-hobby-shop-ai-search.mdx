## Why is near-me the highest-value hobby shop search?

Because it's the moment of intent, and it converts on the spot. When someone searches "game store near
me" or "where to buy Warhammer nearby," they're ready to go — and the [AI answer names only a few
stores](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Pew Research [found people click a link just 8% of the
time when an AI summary
appears](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer *is* the visit. For a hobby shop, owning that moment — especially the niche, brand-specific
queries — is the difference between a busy Saturday and a quiet one.

## What do customers ask AI for a hobby shop near them?

Specific, niche questions — and you should own the ones that fit you.

## Why does naming my niche make or break it?

Because "hobby shop" is broad and customers search narrowly. A Magic player wants a game store, a knitter
wants a yarn shop, an RC fan wants a model hobby shop — and the engine answers straight from how clearly
you state what you are. A precise, readable niche with named brands means you get recommended for the
exact query; a generic label means you're skipped for a competitor who was specific. State exactly what
you are and carry in [plain text](/learn/local-aeo-for-hobby-shops), keep it consistent with your
[listings](/learn/nap-consistency), and you win the near-me visit that skips Amazon entirely.
