address, phone, hours, and services, plus FAQ schema on answer pages — it helps engines parse what you
do and confirm where and when you're open.** Schema clarifies readable content for AI; it never rescues
an image-only gallery or wrong hours.

## What does tattoo studio schema actually do?

It makes your studio details unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business), and the
TattooParlor subtype, labels your name, address, hours, services, and reviews so engines parse them
cleanly rather than guessing — reinforcing the [consistent identity](/learn/nap-consistency) local
recommendations depend on. It's the [structured data for AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern
applied to tattooing: clarity for the parser, on top of styles and pricing that are already clear and
readable for the client.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your studio — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your details machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [readable styles and
pricing](/learn/tattoo-studio-service-pages-for-ai) the crawler can parse, [accurate
hours](/learn/local-aeo-for-tattoo-studios), and [genuine
reviews](/learn/tattoo-studio-reviews-ai-recommendations). Schema can't rescue a portfolio trapped in an
image-only gallery or hours that are wrong — and faking reviews or details in markup is a misuse engines
can [detect](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations). Accurate schema on top of readable styles and pricing
is the combination that works.
