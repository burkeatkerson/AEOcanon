## Why do reviews carry so much weight for breweries?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge which taprooms drinkers actually love — and
where-to-drink is driven by reputation and vibe. An assistant deciding what to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform reviews to
gauge whether you're a great choice. For a brewery, that corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the
[Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the [off-site
mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks do.

## Do reviews that mention beers matter more?

Yes — beer- and vibe-level reviews are gold, because they speak the language of discovery.

A review naming a beer, the food trucks, the patio, or the dog-friendly vibe gives the engine detail to
tie you to "best hazy IPA near me" or "dog-friendly brewery nearby" — the exact [discovery and amenity
queries](/learn/brewery-questions-customers-ask-ai) drinkers ask. Invite happy visitors to mention what
they loved, and the reviews start matching you to the searches that fill the taproom.

## How many reviews — and how fresh?

Fresh beats big. There's no magic count — recency and a steady flow outweigh a stale total. A brewery
with current reviews that mention beers and the vibe signals an active, loved taproom far better than one
coasting on old ratings. Build a simple habit: invite happy visitors to review, and [respond to
them](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations) — engagement signals an active taproom that cares,
which is exactly the place an engine wants to recommend.
