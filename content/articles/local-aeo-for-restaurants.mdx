## What makes a restaurant 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about where you are, what you serve, and whether you're open. An answer engine recommends a
restaurant for "near me" only when it can [place you with certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend)
— right area, right cuisine, open now. That certainty comes from signals that all agree: matching
address and hours, a complete profile, a readable menu, and reviews. When the signals agree, you're an
easy recommendation; when your hours conflict or your menu is unreadable, the engine sends the diner
elsewhere.

This is the [near-me ranking](/learn/how-to-rank-in-ai-for-near-me-searches) discipline applied to
dining — and accurate hours are where most restaurants quietly lose.

## Why do accurate hours matter so much?

Because 'open now' is one of the most common dining queries, and the engine answers it straight from
your hours. If your hours are wrong or inconsistent across listings, the engine either drops you from
'open now' answers or — worse — sends a hungry diner to a closed door, costing you the cover and a
review. Keep hours accurate and identical everywhere, update them for holidays, and you remove the
single most common reason a restaurant gets skipped on a near-me query. It's the same [NAP
consistency](/learn/nap-consistency) discipline, with the clock attached.
