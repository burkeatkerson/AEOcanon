## Why does Google Business Profile matter for AI search?

Because it's a structured, trusted confirmation of who and where you are — exactly what an engine needs
to recommend a firm. When the assistant decides whom to name for a near-me or 'bookkeeper for
[industry]' query, it leans on the profile to verify your identity, service area, services, and
reputation [before recommending you](/learn/does-google-business-profile-help-ai-search). A complete,
accurate profile makes you an easy recommendation; a thin or stale one leaves the engine unsure and
routes the inquiry elsewhere.

Everything an engine checks before vouching for you.

The reviews on your profile do double duty — they're a top input to [whether AI recommends
you](/learn/bookkeeper-reviews-ai-recommendations) at all.

## Does GBP replace a good website?

No — it's half the picture. The profile is a powerful placement and trust signal, but engines also
retrieve and cite your [actual service pages](/learn/bookkeeper-service-pages-for-ai) for the detailed
questions a profile can't answer — what bookkeeping costs, whether you serve a contractor or an
e-commerce business, your software. You need both: a complete profile so the engine can confidently
place and trust you, and answer-first pages for the substance that wins the detailed query. Treat them
as one system, kept [consistent everywhere](/learn/nap-consistency).
