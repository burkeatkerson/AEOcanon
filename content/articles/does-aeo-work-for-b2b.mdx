## Why is AEO so valuable for B2B?

Because B2B buying is long, research-heavy, and increasingly AI-mediated. Buyers work
through detailed, technical questions — often well before contacting sales — and if
you're [the source cited](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations) when they research
your category, you shape the consideration set before any conversation. With high deal
values, even small gains in [citation share](/learn/share-of-voice-ai-search) can be
very worthwhile, which makes the [business case](/learn/business-case-for-aeo)
especially strong for B2B.

## What B2B content gets cited?

Specific, evidenced answers to real evaluation questions — comparisons, how-tos,
technical explanations, and [original data](/learn/does-original-research-get-cited-more)
— structured [answer-first](/learn/answer-first-sentence). Generic marketing copy
doesn't get cited; the substantive, credible content that addresses genuine buyer
questions does, which happens to be exactly what B2B audiences already want. The
detailed nature of B2B topics is an advantage for the [Originality](/pillars/originality)
and [Extractability](/pillars/extractability) pillars.

## B2B or B2C — where does AEO fit better?

Both, but B2B's economics make the payoff especially clear. The complex,
research-heavy buying journey and high deal values mean being cited carries outsized
value, while B2C benefits too — particularly for considered purchases and [product
recommendations](/learn/how-ai-recommends-products). The fundamentals are identical
across both; B2B simply tends to have the detailed questions and deal sizes that reward
AEO most, much as [SaaS](/learn/aeo-for-saas) and
[enterprise](/learn/aeo-for-enterprise) do.
