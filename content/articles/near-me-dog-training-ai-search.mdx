## Why is 'near me' the highest-value dog training search?

Because it's the moment someone decides to hire, and it converts into recurring revenue. When an owner
asks "best dog trainer near me" or "board-and-train nearby," they're ready to commit — and the [AI
answer names only a few places](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Pew Research [found people
click a link just 8% of the time when an AI summary
appears](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer *is* the shortlist. For a dog business, owning that moment is the difference between a
full program and an empty one — and a client who enrolls and later boards keeps paying.

## Why does matching your niche matter so much?

Because owners search by exactly what they need, and a generic trainer matches nothing in particular.
The person searching "reactive dog trainer near me" or "board-and-train for a puppy" wants a specialist
that clearly fits — and if your site states your niche and strengths in plain text, the engine can match
you to that specific, high-intent query. A vague "we train all dogs" page is invisible to these
searches. Be specific about who you're great for, back it with [reviews that prove
it](/learn/dog-training-reviews-ai-recommendations), and you win the clients most likely to enroll and
stay — the [Originality](/pillars/originality) and [Alignment](/pillars/alignment) edge that fills
programs.
