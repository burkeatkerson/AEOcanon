## What makes a coffee shop 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about where you are, what you serve, and whether you're open. An answer engine recommends a
café for "near me" only when it can [place you with certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend)
— right area, right offering, open now. That certainty comes from signals that all agree: matching
address and hours, a complete profile, a readable menu, and reviews. When the signals agree, you're an
easy recommendation; when your hours conflict or your menu is unreadable, the engine sends the customer
elsewhere.

This is the [near-me ranking](/learn/how-to-rank-in-ai-for-near-me-searches) discipline applied to
coffee — and accurate hours are where most cafés quietly lose.

## Why do accurate hours matter so much?

Because 'open now' is one of the most common coffee queries — often before work, when a locked door
loses the customer for good — and the engine answers it straight from your hours. If your hours are
wrong or inconsistent across listings, the engine either drops you from 'open now' answers or sends a
caffeine-seeker to a closed door, costing you the sale and a potential daily regular. Keep hours
accurate and identical everywhere, update them for holidays, and you remove the single most common
reason a café gets skipped on a near-me query. It's the same [NAP
consistency](/learn/nap-consistency) discipline, with the clock attached.
