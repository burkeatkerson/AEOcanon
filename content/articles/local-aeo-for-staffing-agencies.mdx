your location and coverage signals clear — consistent name, address, and phone, a complete Google
Business Profile, and pages that state the markets, industries, and roles you staff.** Engines
recommend the agency they can confidently place.

## What makes a staffing agency 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about who you are, where you operate, and who you staff. An answer engine recommends an
agency for "near me" only when it can [place you with certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend)
— right market, right kind of role. That certainty comes from signals that all agree: matching
name-address-phone, a complete profile, stated coverage areas, named industries and roles, and reviews.
When the signals agree, you're an easy recommendation; when they conflict, the engine favors a clearer
competitor.

## How do I win 'near me' across the markets I serve?

Build location and coverage certainty deliberately.

This is the [near-me ranking](/learn/how-to-rank-in-ai-for-near-me-searches) discipline applied to the
profession — and it's also where [service-area pages](/learn/local-service-area-pages-for-ai) earn
their keep for multi-branch firms.

## Why does NAP consistency matter so much?

Because a mismatch is a doubt, and engines route around doubt. If your phone number differs between
your site and your Google profile, or an old branch address still shows in a directory, the engine
can't be sure which agency you are or where you operate — so it favors a competitor it can [place with
confidence](/learn/nap-consistency). For firms that have opened branches, moved, or rebranded, this is
a common and quiet leak. Clean it up and you remove the single most fixable reason an engine skips you
on a near-me query.
