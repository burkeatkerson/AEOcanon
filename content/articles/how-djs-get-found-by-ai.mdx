answer the questions clients ask, and they're backed by accurate listings and genuine reviews.** The AI
names only a few businesses, so the DJ that clears all three is the one recommended.

## What does an AI engine actually do to find you?

It retrieves, ranks, and names. When someone asks who can DJ an event, the engine pulls the
businesses it can read, judges which best fit the request and look well-reviewed, and [names just a
few](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Each step is a filter: if the crawler can't read your
packages, you're out before ranking; if your service area is unclear, you're dropped from 'near me'; if
your reviews are thin, a competitor gets named. Being found means clearing every filter — and for
DJs, the readable packages and pricing are the one most often missed.

## Why can't clients find my DJ business?

Almost always one specific broken gate — and for DJs it's usually the packages and pricing. If your
packages are a [PDF or hidden behind a check-availability form the crawler can't
read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), the engine doesn't know what you offer, so it can't
recommend you for "wedding DJ near me" or "corporate event DJ with uplighting." Other common breaks: an
unclear service area, or thin reviews. The fix is diagnostic — find the *highest* broken gate and repair it
first. For most DJs, putting packages and pricing in plain text is the single highest-leverage move.

## Does ranking in Google mean AI can find me?

Not on its own. Ranking and a Google listing help, but AI citation also needs your [packages and answers
in readable text](/learn/dj-service-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews. A DJ can show up on a map
yet be absent from "best wedding DJ near me" answers if the engine can't read the packages or the
reviews are thin. Clear all three gates and you get [found in both](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).
