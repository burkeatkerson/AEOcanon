— holiday sales, moving season, new-home season, outdoor-furniture spring — before each rush, on durable
pages you update yearly.** Be the cited answer when the buying season hits, not scrambling after it
starts.

## Why is seasonality a big AEO lever for a furniture store?

Because furniture demand clusters into predictable seasons, and the cited store catches the surge. The
big sale events — Black Friday, Presidents' Day, Labor Day, Memorial Day — drive waves of "furniture sale
near me" searches; moving and back-to-school season sends new renters and students looking; new-home and
tax-refund season brings whole-house buyers; and spring sets off the outdoor-furniture rush. The store
whose answer is already published, crawled, and trusted [gets cited the moment the season
hits](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo) — while competitors scramble. It's the [Freshness
pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned into a retail calendar, and the difference between a record season and
a missed one.

## How do I prepare for each season?

Build the content while it's calm, so it's ready when shopping intent surges.

## Should I make a new page each year?

No — update one durable page per season. A persistent 'holiday furniture sale' or 'outdoor furniture'
page [accumulates authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh the dates, offers, and
featured pieces each year, which beats spinning up a throwaway page annually that starts from zero and
splits your signals. Keep one strong page per season, sharpen it before each peak, and link it to your
[product pages](/learn/furniture-product-pages-for-ai). One durable page, refreshed and ready, beats
scrambling when the season hits.
