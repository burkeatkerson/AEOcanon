schema with accurate name, address, phone, service area, hours, and services, plus FAQ schema on
answer pages — it helps engines parse and confirm who you are and what you do.** Schema clarifies
correct content for AI; it never rescues a slow site or a buried answer.

## What does solar schema actually do?

It makes your business data unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business), and a
subtype like HomeAndConstructionBusiness or Electrician, labels your name, address, phone, hours, area
served, services, and reviews so engines parse them cleanly rather than guessing from prose —
reinforcing the [consistent identity](/learn/nap-consistency) local recognition depends on. It's the
[structured data for AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern applied to the trade: clarity for the
parser, on top of content that's already clear for the homeowner.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your solar business — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your data machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [consistent
identity](/learn/local-aeo-for-solar-installers), [genuine
reviews](/learn/solar-installer-reviews-ai-recommendations), verifiable certifications, and [pages that
answer cost and payback questions](/learn/solar-installer-service-pages-for-ai). And don't fake it:
marking up reviews or certifications that don't match your visible page is a misuse engines can detect
— [especially damaging](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations) for a high-trust, high-ticket trade full
of fly-by-night operators. Accurate schema on top of real proof is the combination that works.
