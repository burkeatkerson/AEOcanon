**Get onto the "best of" lists AI pulls from by earning a place on the third-party
roundups and comparison pages engines already trust — pitch relevant publishers and
reviewers, make your product genuinely qualify, and ensure your entity is
recognizable.** Engines often cite these lists, so being listed gets you recommended
indirectly.

## Why do these lists matter so much?

Because engines lean on them to recommend. When someone asks an AI for the best tool
in a category, the engine often [synthesizes from third-party roundups and
reviews](/learn/how-ai-recommends-products) rather than inventing a ranking — so the
"best X" lists it trusts become the source of the recommendation. Appearing on those
lists gets you surfaced even though you don't control them, which makes inclusion a
high-leverage [authority](/pillars/authority) play.

## How do I earn a place?

Find the lists, then deserve inclusion. Identify which "best of" articles actually get
cited for your category — [test the engines](/learn/track-competitor-ai-citations) and
see what they pull from — then pitch those publishers and reviewers with a genuine
case. Make sure your product truly fits the category and criteria, offer reviewers
real access and information, and keep your [entity
recognizable](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) so they describe you accurately.
Authentic outreach plus a product that belongs beats any attempt to game placement.

## Should I just publish my own?

You can, but weight it correctly. A genuinely useful, honest comparison on your own
site can get cited — especially if it's [well-structured for
extraction](/learn/how-to-structure-a-comparison-for-ai) — but self-serving lists that
always crown you are discounted, because engines favor third-party corroboration over
self-promotion. Publish your own as a fair resource if you can stay honest, but invest
the most in getting onto the independent lists.
