## How is group-events AEO different?

The intent is a researched booking decision, not an individual day pass. A parent planning a birthday,
a teacher arranging a field trip, or an office manager booking team-building researches carefully — and
asks the assistant detailed questions long before they call. They're researching capacity, package
prices, and whether you can handle their group and ages. Because [AEO works for
B2B](/learn/does-aeo-work-for-b2b) and group planning exactly here, the gym whose pages answer those
questions clearly gets [cited during the research](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations) — and wins a
high-value booking most gyms never even surface for. And a party or group event is a block of revenue
at once, worth far more than a single day pass.

## What questions do planners ask?

Logistics-focused and specific — each worth a clear answer on a dedicated page.

## How do I win party and group-event citations?

Give parties and group events their own answer-first page — not a buried line on your homepage. Publish
capacity, package prices, age requirements, what's included, and the group types you handle in readable
text, reinforced by your [reviews](/learn/rec-center-reviews-ai-recommendations) and a clear inquiry
path. Most gyms either omit this entirely or hide it in a PDF, so a clear, dedicated page is an easy way
to get cited for valuable, high-margin bookings — a block of revenue at once that compounds your
[growth](/learn/grow-a-rec-center-with-ai-search).
