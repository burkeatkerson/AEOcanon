**Access is the first pillar of the AEO Canon, and it is a binary gate: if an AI
crawler cannot read your page, it cannot quote you.** There is no partial credit
here — no clever copy or schema rescues a page the machine never sees. Before
anything else in [the Canon](/learn/aeo-canon), you have to open
the door.

## Why access is the price of admission

Every other pillar — authority, credibility, originality — assumes the engine
can actually retrieve your words. If it can't, none of them matter. That makes
access the cheapest pillar to get wrong and the most expensive to ignore.

<PullQuote cite="Pillar 1 · Access">
  If a machine can’t read your page, it can’t quote you.
</PullQuote>

The trap is that your page looks fine to _you_. You open it in a browser,
JavaScript runs, content appears. But an AI crawler is not a browser.

## The evidence: crawlers don't run your JavaScript

The single most important fact about AI crawlers is how little they execute.
Vercel and MERJ analyzed more than 500 million requests from GPTBot and found it
executed **zero** JavaScript — it reads only the HTML your server returns. If
your headline, your answer, or your key facts are injected by client-side
scripts, the crawler sees an empty shell.

A robots.txt that welcomes the major answer engines looks like this:

```text
# Allow AI answer-engine crawlers
User-agent: GPTBot
Allow: /

User-agent: ClaudeBot
Allow: /

User-agent: PerplexityBot
Allow: /

User-agent: Google-Extended
Allow: /

Sitemap: https://example.com/sitemap.xml
```

<Callout variant="warning" title="The most common silent failure">
  <p>
    Many businesses block AI crawlers without knowing it — through a default CDN
    bot rule, a security plugin, or a JavaScript-only framework. You can be
    doing everything else right and still be invisible. Check this first.
  </p>
</Callout>

## How to check whether you pass

Two quick tests tell you almost everything:

- **View source, not the rendered page.** Right-click → "View Page Source" (not
  "Inspect"). If your headline and key answers aren't in that raw HTML, a crawler
  can't see them.
- **Read your server logs.** Filter for `GPTBot`, `ClaudeBot`, and
  `PerplexityBot`. Confirm they're fetching your important pages and getting a
  `200` with real content — not a block or an empty shell.
