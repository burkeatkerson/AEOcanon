## Why do reviews carry so much weight for caterers?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge which businesses clients actually trust with a
high-stakes event — and catering is a trust purchase. A host hiring a wedding caterer is betting one of
the biggest days of their life on you, so an assistant deciding whom to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform reviews to
gauge whether you deliver. For a caterer, that corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility
pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that
correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks do.

## Do reviews that name the event matter more?

Yes — event-level reviews are gold, because they speak the language of the catering decision.

A review naming the event, the cuisine, the guest count, or the service gives the engine detail to tie you
to "wedding caterer for a big guest count" or "corporate catering that handles dietary needs" — the exact
[questions clients ask](/learn/catering-questions-customers-ask-ai). Invite happy clients to mention the
event and what they loved, and the reviews start matching you to the searches that book events.

## How many reviews — and how fresh?

Fresh beats big. There's no magic count — recency and a steady flow outweigh a stale total. A caterer with
current reviews that name recent events signals an active, trusted business far better than one coasting on
old ratings. Build a simple habit: invite happy clients to review after each event, and [respond to
them](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations) — engagement signals an active caterer that cares,
which is exactly the business an engine wants to recommend.
