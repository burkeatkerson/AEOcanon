**Customers ask AI HVAC questions in four buckets — emergency ('AC blowing warm air'),
cost ('how much is a new AC'), repair-vs-replace ('is my furnace worth fixing'), and how-to
('why is my AC freezing up').** Mapping each question to the page that should own it is the
core of an HVAC AEO content plan.

## How do I find the exact questions?

Listen where customers already ask. Mine your phone log and service calls for the questions
people actually voice, check Reddit threads, community forums, and the People Also Ask box,
and [prompt the assistants directly](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask) on your
services to see the follow-ups they surface. Capture the *natural wording* — "why is my AC
blowing warm air" beats "refrigerant charge diagnostics" — because engines match the
customer's phrasing, not your jargon. Then prioritize by intent and job value.

## Should I answer how-to questions that don't book a job?

Yes — they're how you earn the trust that books the next one. Answering "why is my AC
freezing up" helpfully makes you the source a customer remembers when the DIY check fails
or the fix is plainly a pro's job. How-to content builds the credibility and visibility
that win the booking later, and it's exactly the [helpful, original
content](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore) engines reward — the opposite of a thin
directory page. Map every bucket to a page and you've built the content plan that gets an
HVAC company cited.
