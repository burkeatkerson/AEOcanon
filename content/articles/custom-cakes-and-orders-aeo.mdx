## How is custom-cake and special-order AEO different?

The intent is planning, not a walk-in. Someone booking a wedding or a milestone birthday researches
carefully — and asks the assistant detailed questions long before they call. They're researching lead
time, pricing, design options, and whether you can handle their theme and dietary needs. Because [AEO
works for these higher-consideration decisions](/learn/does-aeo-work-for-b2b), the bakery whose pages
answer those questions clearly gets [cited during the research](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations)
— and wins a high-margin booking most bakeries never even surface for.

## What questions do custom-cake customers ask?

Logistics-focused and specific — each worth a clear answer on a dedicated page.

## How do I win custom-cake citations?

Give custom cakes, wedding cakes, and special orders their own answer-first pages — not a buried line on
your homepage or a photo gallery with no text. Publish lead time, pricing structure, design and flavor
options, dietary accommodations, and how to book in readable text, reinforced by your
[reviews](/learn/bakery-reviews-ai-recommendations) that name the cakes you've made and a clear inquiry
path. Most bakeries either omit this entirely or trap it in a contact form, so a clear, dedicated page is
an easy way to get cited for valuable wedding, birthday, and corporate cake orders — the high-margin
revenue that compounds your [growth](/learn/grow-a-bakery-with-ai-search).
