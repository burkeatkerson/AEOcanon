## What does local schema actually do?

It makes your location data unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business)
labels your name, address, phone, hours, area served, and services so engines parse them
cleanly rather than guessing from prose — reinforcing the [consistent
identity](/learn/nap-consistency) that local recognition depends on. It's the local
application of [structured data for AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo): clarity for the
parser, on top of content that's already clear for the reader.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your business — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your data machine-readable
and unambiguous, which *supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on
[consistent identity](/learn/local-citations-and-ai-search), [genuine
reviews](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations), corroboration, and [content that
answers the question](/learn/local-service-area-pages-for-ai). And don't stuff or fake
it: marking up information that doesn't match your visible page is a misuse engines can
detect — the same [schema-honesty](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations) rule that
applies everywhere, pointed at local data.
