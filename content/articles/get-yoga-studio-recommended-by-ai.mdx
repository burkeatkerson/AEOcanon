## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a place the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine can
read your schedule and place you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as where to practice
— and that leans harder on whether students love you. The engine recommends the studio it can
[confidently confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, well-run, and well-reviewed, because
when it recommends you it's putting its credibility behind the experience. Your job is to make that
confirmation easy — a readable schedule, accurate hours, and genuine, loving reviews.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). Wrong hours, hidden class styles, or a thin review profile
introduces doubt, and doubt sends the student elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one studio over another?

Because it picks the one it's most confident students will love. Faced with several options, the engine
names the studio whose signals best match the request — right kind of studio, right area — and whose
reviews most clearly say people love it. The competitor with a widget-hidden schedule, wrong hours, or
thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it loses. Winning is about being the [easiest to verify as the
loved local choice](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — which is exactly what this
library builds, piece by piece.
