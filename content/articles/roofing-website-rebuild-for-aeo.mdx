## Why does the site itself decide your AEO?

Because [access is the gate everything else depends on](/learn/aeo-for-roofers). An answer
engine can only cite a roofer whose site it can [fetch and
read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site) — and roofing sites are often the worst
offenders: gorgeous, image-heavy, slow, sometimes built so a crawler sees an empty shell.
When that happens, your reviews, certifications, and content are invisible, no matter how
good they are. The rebuild isn't vanity; it's clearing the [Access pillar](/pillars/access)
so the rest of your work can count.

## How do I know if my site is the problem?

Check the three things that decide whether engines can use it.

If a crawler can't read your real content or the site drags, it's working against you —
the most common reason a [roofer is invisible in AI](/learn/how-roofers-get-found-by-ai).

## Is a rebuild worth it?

If the current site blocks the access gate, yes — it's the constraint capping everything
else, so fixing it has the highest leverage of any move you can make. A fast, crawlable,
[answer-first site](/learn/roofing-service-pages-that-get-cited) earns citations and
rankings *and* converts homeowners better, and a single extra job a month typically dwarfs
the cost. If your site is already sound, don't rebuild for its own sake — invest in
[content and reputation](/learn/grow-a-roofing-business-with-ai-search) instead.
