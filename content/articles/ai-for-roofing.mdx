**You already lean on AI to run your roofing business — but the game that
actually fills next month's calendar is being the roofer AI *recommends*, and
that's a completely different skill.** You use AI to measure roofs and build
proposals; meanwhile homeowners with a leak or storm damage are now asking AI
*who to call* — and it names one or two companies. If yours isn't one, AI is
handing that job to a competitor.

## How are roofing businesses using AI today?

More than most owners realize. The common ones:

- **Aerial measurement and takeoffs** — AI-assisted roof measurements from
  satellite and drone imagery, so you quote without climbing every roof.
- **Estimating and proposals** — tools that turn measurements into priced
  proposals fast and keep material math consistent.
- **CRM and lead follow-up** — automated texts and emails that chase storm leads
  before they go cold.
- **Review replies and bookkeeping** — ChatGPT drafting responses to reviews, and
  automated invoicing and expense sorting.

All of it makes your operation faster and cheaper to run. None of it makes an AI
assistant put your name forward when a homeowner is deciding who to call.

## But is AI recommending your roofing company?

Probably not — and using AI internally won't change that. The model that drafts
your review replies isn't the system deciding who to recommend, and even when
it's the same product, it recommends based on what it can find and trust *about
you on the open web*. When a homeowner asks for the best roofer near them, the
engine pulls the sources that best answer that question: your website (if it's
readable and answer-first), your reviews, and mentions of you across other sites.
Your private measurement software is invisible to that process. That's why a
company can automate its whole office with AI and still never surface when a
prospect asks AI who to hire.

## How do customers use AI to find a roofer?

They ask it like they'd ask a trusted neighbor. Instead of scrolling a page of
links, more homeowners now type "best roofer in [town]," "who handles storm
damage and insurance claims near me," or "how much for a new roof in [town]" —
and act on the short list the assistant gives back. Because the AI answers in
place and names only a couple of roofers, this is a **winner-take-most** moment:
the companies it cites get considered, and everyone else is invisible. After a
hailstorm, that first named name often gets the inspection before you'd know you
were in the running.

## How do you know if AI is sending your customers to a competitor?

Ask the engines yourself. Open ChatGPT, [Google AI Mode](/glossary/google-ai-mode),
[Perplexity](/glossary/perplexity), and Gemini and run the real questions your
customers ask:

<Checklist
  title="Questions to ask the AI about your service area"
  items={[
    "Best roofer in [your city]?",
    "Who does storm damage repair and insurance claims near me?",
    "Who can fix a roof leak in [town] quickly?",
    "How much for a new roof in [your area], and who installs them?",
  ]}
/>

Note who gets named. If competitors show up and you don't — or the AI describes
you with the wrong service area or stale details — you've found the gap. That gap
is what [Answer Engine Optimization](/learn/what-is-aeo) closes.
