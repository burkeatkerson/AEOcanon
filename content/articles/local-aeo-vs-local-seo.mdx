## What do they share?

Almost the entire foundation. Consistent [name, address, and
phone](/learn/nap-consistency), a complete [Google Business
Profile](/learn/does-google-business-profile-help-ai-search), genuine
[reviews](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations), and consistent
[citations](/learn/local-citations-and-ai-search) feed *both* the map pack and AI
recommendations. The work that makes you a recognized, trusted local entity helps you
rank and helps you get recommended — the same [one-foundation, two-surfaces
relationship](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together) that AEO and SEO have everywhere,
pointed at your area.

Read it across and the pattern is the familiar one: AEO doesn't erase a single Local SEO
requirement — it refines the content for the [questions people ask
AI](/learn/how-to-do-local-keyword-research-for-ai) and adds a new way to measure
success.

## Should I pick one?

No — do them as one program. The shared foundation serves both surfaces, so strong Local
SEO already advances Local AEO; layer on [answer-first local
pages](/learn/local-service-area-pages-for-ai) and [track whether AI recommends
you](/learn/how-to-measure-local-aeo), and you cover the map pack and the AI answer from
a single effort. Local SEO isn't dead — the AI recommendation draws on the same Google
Business Profile and local signals, so the foundation matters more than ever.
