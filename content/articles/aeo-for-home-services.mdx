## What query patterns do local customers use?

Local service customers query AI with immediate hiring intent: **"best [trade] in
[city]"**, **"[service] near me"**, **"how much does [job] cost"**, **"who can fix
[problem]"**, **"emergency [trade] [city]"**, and **"is [company] reliable"**. These
are bottom-of-funnel, location-specific, and often urgent. The content that answers
them is service pages (one per service, [answer-first](/glossary/inverted-pyramid)), pricing guidance, and a
recognizable local presence. Map the exact questions customers call and search with,
and answer each plainly — [alignment](/pillars/alignment) pointed at local hiring.

## What should you build first?

For local services, the order is recognition, then content, then reach:

<Steps
  items={[
    { title: "1. Google Business Profile, fully complete", body: "Claim, verify, and fill every field. It's the single biggest local-recognition lever and the fastest to do." },
    { title: "2. Consistent listings everywhere", body: "Fix your name, address, and phone to one canonical version across every directory and profile, so AI merges them into one business." },
    { title: "3. An answer-first page per service", body: "One page per service with scope, area, and an honest price range — the content that answers 'how much does X cost' and 'who does Y near me'." },

  ]}

These four cover most of the local recommendation outcome, and they're nearly all
free. Do them before anything fancier.
