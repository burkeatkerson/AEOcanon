## Why does seasonality matter for salons?

Because styling demand — and high-value bookings — surge in predictable waves, and the cited salon
catches them. Spring brings prom hair and makeup; summer brings wedding season; December brings
holiday-party styling and color refreshes. The salon whose answer is already published, crawled, and
trusted [gets cited the moment the surge hits](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo) — while
competitors are still updating their site. It's the [Freshness pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned into a
styling calendar, and the difference between a fully-booked season and a quiet one.

## How do I prepare for each surge?

Build the content while it's calm, so it's ready when demand spikes.

## Should I make a new prom or holiday page each year?

No — update one durable evergreen page per theme. A persistent 'prom hair and makeup' or 'holiday party
styling' page [accumulates authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh it each year, which
beats spinning up a throwaway 'Prom 2026' page that starts from zero and splits your signals. Keep one
strong page that answers the occasion's questions, sharpen it before each surge, and link it to your
[service and pricing pages](/learn/salon-service-pages-for-ai) and your
[bridal page](/learn/bridal-and-event-hair-aeo). One durable page, refreshed and ready, beats scrambling
when the season hits.
