## Why are the menu and schedule the most important pages?

Because what you serve and where you are is exactly what customers search for — and a citation goes to the
page the engine can read. When someone asks "best gluten-free tacos near me" or "where's the birria truck
today," the engine matches the query against menus and locations it can [actually
parse](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site). If your menu is an image and your location is buried in a
feed, the engine doesn't know what you serve or where you are, so you're invisible for the most valuable
discovery and find-a-truck-now searches there are. Readable menu and schedule pages turn your whole
offering — and today's spot — into citable answers.

## What makes these pages citable?

Readable text, organized the way customers think — and kept current.

location, reinforced by [FoodEstablishment schema](/learn/food-truck-schema-markup).

## Why describe dishes and keep the schedule live?

Because description and freshness are the language of discovery for a moving business. Customers ask AI for
"the best birria near me" or "where's that taco truck today," and a bare list of names or a stale post
gives the engine little to match. A sentence describing each signature dish gives the engine the
[extractable detail](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage) that ties you to those searches, and a live
schedule gives it the location to answer 'near me today'. Together they mirror the [dish-level
reviews](/learn/food-truck-reviews-ai-recommendations) customers write — and they're the foundation every
other food-truck AEO move builds on.
