## Why does AEO matter so much for bakeries?

Because buying decisions are fast, local, and made on the first good answer. When someone asks "where
can I get a custom cake," they act on what the assistant names — and the [AI answer names only two or
three places](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations), not a page of listings. Pew Research [found
people clicked a link just 8% of the time when an AI summary appeared, versus 15%
without](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer *is* the decision. Being the cited bakery is the new equivalent of being the shop
everyone recommends — and it sends a customer who orders directly, including the wedding and custom
cakes where the margin lives.

## How do bakeries get recommended by AI?

By clearing the three gates a customer — and an engine — judge you on, in order.

    { title: "Answer the real question (Alignment + Extractability)", body: "Plain-text answers to 'do you make custom and wedding cakes', 'how far ahead to order', 'do you have gluten-free or vegan options', 'do you deliver', and 'where are you'." },
    { title: "Be trusted (Authority + Credibility)", body: "Consistent name, address, and phone everywhere; a complete Google Business Profile; and genuine, recent reviews that mention specific cakes and pastries. This is what decides who gets named." },

Most bakeries fail gate one without knowing it — the products live in a PDF or an Instagram feed,
invisible to the engine. Fixing that, plus accurate listings and reviews, is the highest-leverage
work, and it's the same foundation that [ranks you in Google](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together)
too.

## Isn't this just SEO?

It's mostly the local SEO you should already be doing, pointed at a new surface. The fast, crawlable,
well-reviewed bakery with a readable product list that ranks is the same one that gets
[cited](/learn/is-aeo-replacing-seo). AEO adds putting your products and answers in [plain
text](/learn/answer-first-sentence), [structured data](/learn/bakery-schema-markup) for your bakery and
products, and the [review consistency](/learn/bakery-reviews-ai-recommendations) answer engines lean
on. You don't choose between them — you run one program that wins both.

## Where do independent bakeries have the advantage?

In specificity and authenticity. AI names only a few places and [citations spread
thin](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands), so an independent with a readable product list, clear
custom-cake pages, and genuine reviews that name cakes can win the recommendation over a grocery-store
bakery or a faceless aggregator listing. That's the [Originality](/pillars/originality) and [local
authority](/learn/local-aeo-for-bakeries) edge a chain can't copy — and every directly-booked custom
cake is the high-margin work.

## What should a bakery do first?

Start at the binding constraint. [Make your products readable](/learn/bakery-menu-pages-for-ai) as
real text, then fix your [hours and listings](/learn/local-aeo-for-bakeries) so 'open now' is accurate,
then build [genuine reviews](/learn/bakery-reviews-ai-recommendations). Work down the rest of this
library from there — [winning 'near me'](/learn/near-me-bakery-ai-search),
[Google Business Profile](/learn/bakery-google-business-profile-ai),
[schema](/learn/bakery-schema-markup), and the
[questions customers actually ask](/learn/bakery-questions-customers-ask-ai).
