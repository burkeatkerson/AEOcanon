## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each treatment produces a review, a referral, and ideally a
recurring-plan signup; reviews and [answer-first content](/learn/pest-control-service-pages-for-ai)
earn citations and rankings; those bring direct inquiries; and the money saved on purchased
leads funds more content and more routes — which serve more customers. The directory model is
the opposite: a faucet you pay to keep running, that stops the moment you stop. AEO builds the
[compounding asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead — and the recurring base makes it
spin even faster.

## How do recurring plans accelerate it?

They turn one-time jobs into predictable revenue and a stream of fresh signals. A quarterly or
monthly plan converts a single treatment into a recurring relationship — and each visit is a
new touchpoint for a [fresh review](/learn/pest-control-reviews-ai-recommendations), a
referral, and an upsell. That steady reputation feeds the signals AI uses to recommend you,
while the recurring revenue stabilizes your cash flow and funds more content. Every seasonal
and [prevention question](/learn/pest-control-questions-customers-ask-ai) you answer is a
chance to introduce the plan that prevents next season's problem — and the recurring base it
builds.

## Can I really lower my cost per customer?

Yes — by replacing recurring fees with assets that keep paying. A purchased lead costs every
time and is [resold to your competitors](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands); an earned
citation, ranking, plan member, or referral costs mostly upfront and pays for years. As your
owned pipeline and recurring base grow, your cost per customer falls and your margins improve,
because you're no longer renting every job. And because AI names only a few sources, a focused,
licensed, genuinely reviewed local company can win recommendations a generic national directory
[can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
