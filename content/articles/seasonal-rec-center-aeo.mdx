**Seasonal AEO for climbing gyms and rec centers means publishing and refreshing answers to seasonal
questions — summer camps, winter indoor-activity surges, school breaks — before each wave, on durable
pages you update yearly.** Be the cited answer when demand spikes, not scrambling after the rush starts.

## Why does seasonality matter for climbing gyms and rec centers?

Because demand — and bookings — surge in predictable waves, and the cited gym catches them. Spring
brings summer-camp registration, the biggest planning window of the year for parents; fall and winter
bring a surge in "indoor activities near me" as the weather turns; and every school break sends parents
searching for something to do. The gym whose answer is already published, crawled, and trusted [gets
cited the moment the wave hits](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo) — while competitors are still
updating their site. It's the [Freshness pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned into a recreation calendar,
and the difference between a full camp roster and a quiet one.

## How do I prepare for each wave?

Build the content while it's calm, so it's ready when demand spikes.

## Should I make a new page each year for summer camp?

No — update one durable evergreen page. A persistent 'kids climbing camps' or 'school-break programs'
page [accumulates authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh it each year, which beats
spinning up a throwaway 'Summer 2026' page that starts from zero and splits your signals. Keep one
strong page that answers the camp, ages, dates, and pricing questions, sharpen it before each wave, and
link it to your [pass and program pages](/learn/rec-center-service-pages-for-ai). One durable page,
refreshed and ready, beats scrambling when registration opens.
