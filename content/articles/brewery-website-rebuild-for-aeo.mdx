slow, or hours and content live in beer-app embeds AI crawlers can't read — because the engine can only
recommend what it can parse.** The rebuild puts your beers, hours, and answers in readable text
everything else depends on.

## Why is the tap list the binding constraint?

Because [access is the first gate](/pillars/access), and for a brewery the tap list is where you most
often fail it. The tap list is what drinkers search for — "best hazy IPA near me," "sour beer nearby" —
but if it's a [chalkboard photo or an embed the crawler can't read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site),
the engine doesn't know what you pour, so it can't recommend you. Add a slow, browser-only build or hours
trapped in a third-party beer-app widget, and even your basics are invisible. That's not a content
problem you can write around; it's a foundation problem.

## How do I tell if my site is hurting me?

Run two quick tests, and look for the structural gaps.

If your tap list is a photo, your hours are buried, or the page is slow, the site is working against you.
A fast site with a [readable tap list](/learn/brewery-menu-pages-for-ai) and [clean
schema](/learn/brewery-schema-markup) is what makes everything else possible.

## Can't I just keep my chalkboard-photo tap list?

No — the photo or embed tap list is usually the core problem, not a detail to leave alone. Because what
you pour is exactly what drinkers search for, an unreadable tap list makes you invisible for style-level
and food queries no matter what else you publish. Converting it to real HTML text is the single
highest-leverage move most breweries can make. Get the access layer right —
[server-rendered](/learn/server-side-vs-client-side-rendering), fast, with a readable tap list and hours
— and the rest of your brewery AEO finally has something to build on.
