train on it and engines treat it as a trusted reference, but you can't and shouldn't
fabricate one.** Notability is earned through genuine third-party coverage, so build
the real-world authority first and a legitimate entry can follow.

## Why does Wikipedia carry weight with AI?

Because it's both trusted and trained-on. Models ingest Wikipedia heavily, and engines
treat it as a reliable, structured reference, so an entry consolidates verified facts
about you and connects your [entity](/learn/what-is-entity-aeo) to the
[knowledge graph](/glossary/knowledge-graph) that engines lean on — Google's own [Knowledge Graph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Knowledge_Graph) draws on exactly this kind of source. That reinforces who
you are and what you're known for across the very systems that decide citations — a
high-value [authority](/pillars/authority) signal.

## Can I just create my own page?

No — and faking notability doesn't work. Wikipedia requires significant coverage in
independent, reliable sources, and self-created or promotional pages are routinely
flagged and deleted, which wastes effort and can damage credibility. You also
shouldn't write your own entry. The legitimate path is to [earn genuine third-party
coverage](/learn/get-brand-mentions-without-links) first; if that makes you notable, a
neutral entry can be created properly by the community.

## What if I'll never qualify?

Build the same underlying authority elsewhere — most of it helps AEO directly anyway.
[Get into Wikidata](/learn/how-to-get-into-wikidata) ([the structured, open knowledge base](https://www.wikidata.org/) which has a lower bar and feeds
the knowledge graph), earn credible [mentions and
corroboration](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks), and strengthen your entity with
[consistent identity signals](/learn/sameas-strategy). Those move citation whether or
not a Wikipedia article ever exists, so a missing entry is a gap, not a wall.
