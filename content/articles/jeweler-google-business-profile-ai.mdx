## Why does Google Business Profile matter for AI search?

Because it's a structured, trusted confirmation of where you are, when you're open, and what you do —
exactly what an engine needs to recommend a jeweler. When the assistant decides whom to name for a near-me
or engagement-ring query, it leans on the profile to verify your location, hours, services, and reputation
[before recommending you](/learn/does-google-business-profile-help-ai-search). A complete, accurate
profile makes you an easy recommendation; wrong hours or a thin profile leaves the engine unsure — or
sends a buyer to a store that's closed.

The reviews on your profile do double duty — they're a top input to [whether AI recommends
you](/learn/jeweler-reviews-ai-recommendations) at all.

## Does GBP replace a good website?

No — it's half the picture. The profile is a powerful placement signal, but engines also retrieve and cite
your own [readable collection and service pages](/learn/jeweler-product-pages-for-ai) for detailed
questions — "custom engagement rings near me," "jewelry repair turnaround." You need both: a complete,
accurate profile so the engine can place you and confirm services, and readable collections and pricing
for the substance that wins the high-value query. Treat them as one system, kept [consistent
everywhere](/learn/nap-consistency).
