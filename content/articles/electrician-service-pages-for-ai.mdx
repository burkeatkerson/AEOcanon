**Write electrician service pages AI will cite by giving each service its own page that
leads with the answer to the cost, timing, and service-area questions, in plain language
a customer and an engine can lift.** One self-contained, crawlable page per service beats
a single bloated services page every time.

## Why one page per service?

Because a citation is awarded to the page that best answers one specific question — and a
catch-all services page answers none of them well. When you give panel upgrades,
rewiring, EV chargers, generators, and emergency service each [their own
page](/learn/should-content-sections-stand-alone), each can go deep on its own cost,
timing, and details — and each becomes citable for its own query. A single page trying to
cover everything is shallow on all of them, so the engine cites a competitor with a
dedicated, focused page instead.

## What should each page lead with?

The answer the customer came for, before anything about you.

This is [answer-first writing](/learn/answer-first-sentence) applied to the trade: the
quotable answer up top, the depth below, the proof at the bottom.

## What makes a service page extractable?

Plain language and a clean structure. Write the way a customer asks — "a 200-amp panel
upgrade typically costs…" — not in jargon, and use [question-shaped
headings](/learn/question-shaped-headings) the engine can match to a query. Keep each
answer in a self-contained passage so it can be [lifted without the surrounding
page](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage), reinforce it with [Electrician
schema](/learn/electrician-schema-markup), and make sure the page is fast and crawlable.
Answer-first, focused, and proven — that's the page an engine cites and a customer calls.
