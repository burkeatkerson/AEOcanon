**Customers ask AI electrical questions in four buckets — emergency ('breaker won't
reset, is it safe'), cost ('how much to upgrade to a 200-amp panel'), local ('licensed
electrician near me'), and how-to ('why do my lights flicker').** Mapping each question
to the page that should own it is the core of an electrical AEO content plan.

## How do I find the exact questions?

Listen where customers already ask. Mine your phone log and service calls for the
questions people actually voice, check Reddit threads, community forums, and the People
Also Ask box, and [prompt the assistants directly](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask)
on your services to see the follow-ups they surface. Capture the *natural wording* — "why
does my breaker trip when the AC kicks on" beats "circuit overload causes" — because
engines match the customer's phrasing, not your jargon. Then prioritize by intent and job
value.

## Should I answer how-to questions that don't book a job?

Yes — they're how you earn the trust that books the next one. Answering "why do my lights
flicker" helpfully makes you the source a customer remembers when the DIY check fails or
the fix is plainly a pro's job. How-to content builds the credibility and visibility that
win the booking later, and it's exactly the [helpful, original
content](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore) engines reward — the opposite of a
thin directory page. Map every bucket to a page and you've built the content plan that
gets an electrician cited.
