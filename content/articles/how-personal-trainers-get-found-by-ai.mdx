**Personal trainers get found by AI search when their services, specialties, and pricing are readable
as real text, they answer the questions clients ask, and they're backed by accurate listings and
genuine reviews.** The AI names only a few people, so the trainer who clears all three is the one
recommended.

## What does an AI engine actually do to find you?

It retrieves, ranks, and names. When someone asks for a trainer, the engine pulls the people it can
read, judges which best fit the request and look well-reviewed, and [names just a
few](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Each step is a filter: if the crawler can't read your
services and pricing, you're out before ranking; if your hours are wrong, you're dropped from near-me;
if your reviews are thin, a competitor gets named. Being found means clearing every filter — and for
trainers, the readable specialties and visible pricing are often what's missing.

The same three an engine — and a client — apply, in order.

## Why can't clients find my training business?

Almost always one specific broken gate — and for trainers it's often the services and pricing. If
they're trapped in a [booking widget or bio the crawler can't
read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), the engine doesn't know what you offer or what it costs,
so it can't recommend you for "weight-loss trainer near me" or "affordable personal trainer nearby."
Other common breaks: inconsistent hours, or thin reviews. The fix is diagnostic — find the *highest*
broken gate and repair it first. For many trainers, getting services and pricing into plain text is the
highest-leverage move.

## Does ranking in Google mean AI can find me?

Not on its own. Ranking and a map listing help, but AI citation also needs your [services and pricing in
readable text](/learn/personal-trainer-service-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews. A trainer can show on
a map yet be absent from "best personal trainer near me" answers if the engine can't read the
specialties or the reviews are thin. Clear all three gates and you get [found in
both](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).
