## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a café the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine can
read your menu and place you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as where to get coffee —
and that leans harder on whether customers love you. The engine recommends the café it can [confidently
confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, open, and well-reviewed, because when it
recommends you it's putting its credibility behind the experience. Your job is to make that
confirmation easy — a readable menu, accurate hours, and genuine, loving reviews.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). Wrong hours or a thin review profile introduces doubt, and doubt
sends the customer elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one coffee shop over another?

Because it picks the one it's most confident customers will love. Faced with several options, the engine
names the café whose signals best match the request — best coffee, work-friendly, right area, open now
— and whose reviews most clearly say people love it. The competitor with an unreadable menu, wrong
hours, or thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it loses. Winning is about being the [easiest to verify
as the loved local choice](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — which is exactly what
this library builds, piece by piece, into a base of daily regulars.
