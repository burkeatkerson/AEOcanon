## What makes a climbing gym 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about where you are and what you offer. An answer engine recommends a gym for "near me" only
when it can [place you with certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — right area,
right kind of place. That certainty comes from signals that all agree: matching
name-address-phone, accurate hours, a complete profile, readable passes and pricing, and reviews. When
the signals agree, you're an easy recommendation; when they conflict or your offerings are hidden in a
widget, the engine favors a clearer competitor.

## Why does NAP consistency matter so much?

Because a mismatch is a doubt, and engines route around doubt. If your phone number differs between
your site and your Google profile, or an old address still shows in a listing, the engine can't be sure
which business you are or where you operate — so it favors a competitor it can [place with
confidence](/learn/nap-consistency). For gyms that have moved, rebranded, or opened a second location,
this is a common and quiet leak. Clean it up and you remove the single most fixable reason an engine
skips you on a near-me query.
