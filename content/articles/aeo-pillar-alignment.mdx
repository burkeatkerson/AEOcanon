**Alignment is the second pillar of the AEO Canon: you have to answer the
question your customers actually ask AI — not the keyword you wish they
searched.** A page can be fast, readable, and authoritative and still earn
nothing, because it answers a question no one is asking. Alignment comes before
optimization for a reason.

## Why alignment comes before optimization

The instinct, when you sit down to write, is to optimize: pick a keyword, hit
the word count, add the schema. But optimization is acceleration — it only helps
if you're pointed in the right direction. Alignment is the steering.

## The shift: from keywords to conversations

Search engines trained us to compress. We typed `best crm small business`
because that's what a search box rewarded. Answer engines invert this — people
type, or speak, the whole thought.

When the exact question is used as the query, the right pages surface: Seer
found **87%+** of ChatGPT citations matched Bing's top results for that question.
The lesson is direct — find the question, then _be_ its answer.

## A quick alignment test

Before you publish, ask: _would a real person type or say this heading to an
assistant?_ If the heading is a keyword fragment (`hvac services austin`), it
fails. If it's a sentence a human would actually ask (`who repairs AC the same
day in Austin?`), it passes.
