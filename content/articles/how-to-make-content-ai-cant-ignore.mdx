**Make content AI can't ignore by giving it something no other source has — original
data, first-hand experience, a distinct point of view, or simply the most complete,
best-evidenced answer to the question.** When you're the only source for something
useful, the engine has no choice but to cite you.

## What makes content irreplaceable?

Being the only place to get something valuable. [Original
research](/learn/does-original-research-get-cited-more) only you have, [lived
experience competitors can't fake](/learn/does-first-hand-experience-help-aeo), or a
uniquely thorough and clear answer all make you irreplaceable — and irreplaceable is
the opposite of [generic](/learn/will-ai-cite-generic-content). If the engine can get
the same thing elsewhere you're replaceable; if it can't, you become a required
citation. That's the [Originality pillar](/pillars/originality) in one idea.

## Is being more thorough enough?

Often, if "thorough" means genuinely more complete and better-evidenced — not just
longer. Being the most comprehensive, accurate, [answer-first
treatment](/learn/answer-first-sentence) of a question can make you the default
citation even without proprietary data, because the engine prefers the source that
covers the question best. But [padding length without
substance](/learn/long-form-vs-short-form-content) doesn't count; depth has to add
something real.

## How do I test for it?

Ask whether the engine could answer just as well without you. If a competitor's page
would serve it equally, you're optional and easy to drop. If your data, experience, or
completeness is something it can't get elsewhere, you're hard to leave out. Aim to be
the source the answer would be measurably *worse* without — that's the bar for
unignorable, and the goal every originality lever serves.
