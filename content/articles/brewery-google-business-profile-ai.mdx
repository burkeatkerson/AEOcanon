## Why does Google Business Profile matter for AI search?

Because it's a structured, trusted confirmation of where you are, what you offer, and whether you're open
— exactly what an engine needs to recommend a brewery. When the assistant decides whom to name for a
near-me or 'open now' query, it leans on the profile to verify your location, hours, amenities, and
reputation [before recommending you](/learn/does-google-business-profile-help-ai-search). A complete,
accurate profile makes you an easy recommendation; wrong hours or a thin profile leaves the engine unsure
— or sends a visitor to a closed door.

The reviews on your profile do double duty — they're a top input to [whether AI recommends
you](/learn/brewery-reviews-ai-recommendations) at all.

## Does GBP replace a good website?

No — it's half the picture. The profile is a powerful placement and 'open now' signal, but engines also
retrieve and cite your own [readable tap list](/learn/brewery-menu-pages-for-ai) and pages for beer-level
and detailed questions — "best hazy IPA near me," "do you have a private event space." You need both: a
complete, accurate profile so the engine can place you and confirm you're open, and a readable tap list
and pages for the substance that wins the discovery query. Treat them as one system, kept [consistent
everywhere](/learn/nap-consistency).
