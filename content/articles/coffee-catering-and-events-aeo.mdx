## How is catering and wholesale AEO different?

The intent is planning, not a walk-in. An office manager ordering weekly coffee, an event planner
booking an espresso cart, or a buyer setting up a wholesale-bean account researches carefully — and
asks the assistant detailed logistics questions long before they call. They're researching capacity,
pricing, and whether you can supply their volume and schedule reliably. Because [AEO works for these
higher-consideration B2B decisions](/learn/does-aeo-work-for-b2b), the café whose pages answer those
questions clearly gets [cited during the research](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations) — and wins
a recurring, high-value account most cafés never even surface for.

## What questions do planners and buyers ask?

Logistics-focused and specific — each worth a clear answer on a dedicated page.

## How do I win catering and wholesale citations?

Give office catering, event carts, and wholesale beans their own answer-first pages — not a buried line
on your homepage. Publish capacity, pricing structure, sample packages, dietary options, and lead time
in readable text, reinforced by your [reviews](/learn/coffee-shop-reviews-ai-recommendations) and a
clear inquiry path. Most cafés either omit this entirely or trap it in a PDF, so a clear, dedicated
page is an easy way to get cited for valuable corporate, event, and wholesale orders — recurring,
high-margin B2B revenue that compounds your [growth](/learn/grow-a-coffee-shop-with-ai-search).
