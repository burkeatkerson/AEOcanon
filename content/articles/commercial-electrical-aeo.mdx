## How is commercial electrical AEO different?

The intent is research, not rescue. Where a homeowner with a tripping breaker acts on the
first answer, a facility manager or general contractor evaluates carefully — and asks the
assistant detailed questions long before contacting anyone. They're researching code
requirements, service capacity, and whether you've done their kind of building. Because
[AEO works for B2B](/learn/does-aeo-work-for-b2b) exactly here, the electrician whose pages
answer those questions expertly gets [cited during the
research](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations) — and lands on the shortlist before a
single call.

## What questions do commercial buyers ask?

Technical, evaluation-focused, and substantive — each worth a thorough page.

content](/learn/how-to-show-expertise-to-ai), not marketing fluff.

## What content wins commercial citations?

Depth backed by proof. Commercial decisions carry higher stakes and longer research
cycles, so your pages should demonstrate genuine expertise — code knowledge, project
examples, licensing and certifications, and clearly scoped services — in thorough,
[answer-first](/learn/answer-first-sentence) pages. The fundamentals are the same as
residential: be readable, answer the real question, be trusted. Commercial just rewards
[depth and credibility](/pillars/credibility) more heavily, because the buyer is
betting their building's uptime on getting the choice right. Be the page that proves you're
the safe expert, and you shape the shortlist.
