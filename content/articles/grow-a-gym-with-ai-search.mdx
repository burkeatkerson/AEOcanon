## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each great member experience earns a review and a referral; [readable
offerings](/learn/gym-service-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews earn citations for 'best gym near me';
those bring new members; and members who stay produce more reviews and referrals — while the ad spend
you save funds better coaching and community. The paid-ad model is the opposite: a faucet you pay to
keep running, that stops the moment you stop. AEO builds the [compounding
asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead — recurring members who found you and stay.

## Why is retention central to gym growth?

Because a gym's value *is* recurring revenue, and filling the top of the funnel only pays off if members
stay. A member who trains for years is worth many times one who churns in two months — and long-tenured,
happy members are exactly who write the [reviews that mention coaches and
results](/learn/gym-reviews-ai-recommendations) and refer friends, feeding the AI recommendations that
bring the next members. So growth isn't just AEO at the top; it's AEO plus retention together, each
compounding the other. Win members with citations, keep them with a great experience, and the flywheel
accelerates.

## How does this lower my cost per member?

By replacing recurring ad spend with assets that keep paying. A paid ad costs every time and stops when
you stop; an earned citation, a loyal long-term member, a referral, or a [corporate
contract](/learn/corporate-wellness-gym-aeo) costs mostly upfront and pays for years. As your cited,
referred, and retained base grows, your cost per member falls and your revenue stabilizes — and because
AI names only a few places, a real, well-reviewed local gym can win recommendations a faceless chain
listing [can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
