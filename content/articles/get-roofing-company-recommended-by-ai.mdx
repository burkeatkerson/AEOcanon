## What makes AI pick one roofer over another?

Recognition and trust, layered on relevance. Between two roofers answering the same
question, the engine favors the one it can [place
confidently](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — consistent name,
address, and phone, a [complete profile](/learn/roofing-company-google-business-profile) —
and trust more: more and better [reviews](/learn/roofer-reviews-and-reputation), real
certifications, a consistent web presence, and a [crawlable page that answers the
question](/learn/roofing-service-pages-that-get-cited). The good news for a real local
roofer: proof a homeowner would value is exactly the proof an engine weighs.

## Do certifications really help?

Yes — for a high-ticket trade they're among the strongest trust signals, when they're
real and verifiable. Manufacturer certifications, licensing, and warranties are precisely
what an anxious homeowner (and an engine) look for before trusting a roofer with a
five-figure job.

That verifiable, corroborated proof is the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility)
made concrete for roofing — and a [certification listing on a manufacturer's
site](/learn/get-brand-mentions-without-links) is exactly the kind of trusted off-site
mention engines reward.

## How long does it take?

On-page work surfaces in weeks; the trust layer compounds over months — and roofing
rewards the patience, because the same trust that earns the AI recommendation also closes
the homeowner. Build the [recognized entity](/learn/local-aeo-for-roofers), keep earning
reviews and certifications, and the recommendations follow as your reputation
consolidates. It's the [Authority pillar](/pillars/authority), and it's the slowest but
most durable part of the work.
