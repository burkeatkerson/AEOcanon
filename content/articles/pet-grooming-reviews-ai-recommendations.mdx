## Why do reviews carry so much weight for groomers?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge which groomers pet parents actually trust —
and handing over a beloved dog is a high-trust decision. An assistant deciding what to recommend
[synthesizes sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and
review-platform reviews to gauge whether you're a safe, skilled, gentle place. For a groomer, that
corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible,
and the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than
backlinks do.

## Do reviews that mention breeds and handling matter more?

Yes — specific reviews are gold, because they speak the language of what pet parents want.

A review naming a breed, gentle handling, an anxious or senior pet, or a clean de-shed gives the engine
detail to tie you to "groomer for anxious dogs near me" or "doodle groomer nearby" — the exact [breed
and handling queries](/learn/pet-grooming-questions-customers-ask-ai) pet parents ask. Invite happy
clients to mention their dog and what went well, and the reviews start matching you to the searches that
fill your calendar.

## How many reviews — and how fresh?

Fresh beats big. There's no magic count — recency and a steady flow outweigh a stale total. A groomer
with current reviews that mention real experiences signals an active, trusted salon far better than one
coasting on old ratings. Build a simple habit: invite happy pet parents to review at pickup, and
[respond to them](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations) — engagement signals a salon that cares,
which is exactly the place an engine wants to recommend.
