## Why one page per service?

Because a citation is awarded to the page that best answers one specific question — and a catch-all
services page answers none of them well. When you give local moving, long-distance, packing, storage,
labor-only, and specialty moves each [their own page](/learn/should-content-sections-stand-alone), each
can go deep on its own cost, scope, and details — and each becomes citable for its own query. A single
page trying to cover everything is shallow on all of them, so the engine cites a competitor with a
dedicated, focused page.

## What should each page lead with?

The answer the customer came for, before anything about you. Compare these two openings:

<VersusSplit
  left={{ title: "Weak (buries the answer)", items: ["'At Summit Moving we treat every move like family...'", "Decades of combined experience", "A commitment to honest service", "Nothing the engine can quote"] }}
  right={{ title: "Strong (answer-first, citable)", items: ["'A local 2-bedroom move typically runs $600–$1,400'", "Driven by crew size, distance, and stairs/access", "Includes loading, transport, and basic protection", "Usually 4–7 hours; licensed and insured"] }}
  caption="The engine can lift the second opening directly into 'how much to move a 2-bedroom near me' — the first gives it nothing to quote."
/>

Then add the detail, the licensing, and the proof below. This is [answer-first
writing](/learn/answer-first-sentence) applied to the trade.

## What makes a service page extractable?

Plain language and a clean structure. Write the way a customer asks — "a local 2-bedroom move typically
runs …" — not in jargon, and use [question-shaped headings](/learn/question-shaped-headings) the engine
can match to a query. Keep each answer in a self-contained passage so it can be [lifted without the
surrounding page](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage), reinforce it with [MovingCompany
schema](/learn/moving-company-schema-markup), and make sure the page is fast and crawlable. Answer-first,
focused, and proven — that's the page an engine cites and a customer requests a quote from.
