## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each great cup earns a review and a regular; a [readable
menu](/learn/coffee-shop-menu-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews earn citations for 'best coffee near
me'; those bring direct customers; and the commission you save on app orders funds better beans and a
better team — which create more great cups. The delivery-app model is the opposite: a faucet you pay a
commission to keep running, that stops the moment you stop. AEO builds the [compounding
asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead — daily regulars who order direct.

## What's the fastest place to start?

Fix the [readable menu](/learn/coffee-shop-menu-pages-for-ai) first — it's the highest-leverage gap —
then make hours and listings accurate so you win [near-me and 'open
now'](/learn/near-me-coffee-shop-ai-search), and build a steady flow of [genuine
reviews](/learn/coffee-shop-reviews-ai-recommendations). Those win citations within weeks. Then compound
it: turn every happy visitor into a review and a daily regular, and add [catering and wholesale
pages](/learn/coffee-catering-and-events-aeo) for high-value, recurring B2B orders. Small habits,
compounding into a base of loyal, direct demand.

## How does this lower my cost per customer?

By replacing per-order commissions with assets that keep paying. A delivery-app or aggregator order
costs a commission every single time; an earned citation, a daily regular, or a word-of-mouth referral
costs mostly upfront and brings customers for years — and a coffee habit means that regular comes back
five mornings a week. As your owned, direct demand grows, your cost per customer falls and your margins
improve — and because AI names only a few places, a real, well-reviewed local café can win
recommendations a faceless aggregator listing
[can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
