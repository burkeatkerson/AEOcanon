## What makes a photographer 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about where you serve, what you shoot, and that you cover the client's venue. An answer engine
recommends a photographer for "near me" only when it can [place you with
certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — right area, right session type, travels to
that venue. That certainty comes from signals that all agree: a matching address, a stated service area, a
complete profile, readable packages, and reviews. When the signals agree, you're an easy recommendation; when
your service area is unclear or your packages are unreadable, the engine sends the client elsewhere.

This is the [near-me ranking](/learn/how-to-rank-in-ai-for-near-me-searches) discipline applied to
photography — and an unclear service area or travel policy is where most photographers quietly lose.

## Why does a clear service area matter so much?

Because photography is often delivered at the client's venue or location, not at your studio, so 'do you
travel to my area' is one of the first things a client — and the engine — checks. If your service area or
travel radius is missing or inconsistent across listings, the engine can't confirm you cover the client's
venue, so it drops you from the answer or sends an inquiry you can't fulfill. State your cities, venues, and
travel policy in plain text and schema, keep them identical everywhere, and you remove the most common reason
a photographer gets skipped on a near-me query. It's the same [NAP consistency](/learn/nap-consistency)
discipline, with the map attached.
