## Why is long-distance the highest-value moving search?

Because it's the largest booking and the most carefully researched. A cross-country move costs thousands
and is planned weeks ahead, so the customer asks an assistant repeatedly — cost, timeline, estimates,
licensing — and builds a shortlist long before they call. Pew Research [found people click a link just 8%
of the time when an AI summary
appears](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer shapes the shortlist. For a mover, owning these questions wins the biggest moves on the
calendar — and the referrals a smooth interstate move generates.

Several research-heavy questions at once — and you should own all of them.

## Why does licensing decide the long-distance booking?

Because interstate moving is federally regulated, and the engine treats a valid USDOT number and proper
licensing as a core trust signal. If your licensing isn't stated plainly, the engine can't confidently
vouch for you on a high-stakes cross-country move — so it names a competitor that spelled it out alongside
binding estimates and insurance. State your USDOT and state licensing in readable text, explain your
binding estimate and valuation options, and you remove the biggest reason an interstate customer skips
you. This is the same [trust certainty](/learn/get-moving-company-recommended-by-ai) that wins every
high-value move, sharpened by federal regulation.
