## What's the core difference between AEO and social media?

The core difference is audience versus citation. Social media marketing builds a following
and distributes your message on platforms you don't own; AEO earns a position as the source
[AI answers cite](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/ai-features). One optimizes for human attention on a feed; the other for being lifted
into an answer. They feel separate — until you notice that some of the platforms where you
build social presence are exactly the platforms AI engines cite from.

## Where do they overlap?

On the platforms engines actually cite. [Reddit is the single most-cited domain across AI
answers, and YouTube is a major source](/learn/reddit-ai-citations), so authentic
participation there builds audience *and* the off-site [authority](/pillars/authority) AEO
depends on — one effort, two outcomes. The caveat is authenticity: engines (and
communities) reward genuine, helpful contribution, not promotion, and the citation-rich
platforms vary by topic and market.

## So how should you split effort?

Concentrate where the two overlap. Put social effort into the platforms that *also* earn
AEO authority — the communities and channels engines cite in your niche — so a single
investment builds audience and citations at once. Spreading thin across every platform
serves neither well. For the off-site playbook, see [Reddit and AI
citations](/learn/reddit-ai-citations) and the [Authority pillar](/pillars/authority).
