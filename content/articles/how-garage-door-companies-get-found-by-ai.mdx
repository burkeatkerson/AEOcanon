**Garage door companies get found by AI search when their site is crawlable, answers the real
questions homeowners ask, and is backed by consistent local trust signals like reviews and
warranties.** The AI retrieves and ranks the few sources that clear all three — so the company
that does is the one named in the answer.

## What does an AI engine actually do to find you?

It retrieves, ranks, and names. When someone asks for garage door help, the engine pulls the
sources it can read, judges which best answer the question and look trustworthy, and [names just a
few](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations) in the answer. Each step is a filter: if a crawler
can't fetch your page, you're out before ranking; if your page doesn't answer the question, you're
out at ranking; if your trust signals are thin, a competitor gets named instead. Being found means
clearing every filter.

## What are the three gates?

The same three an engine — and a homeowner — apply, in order.

## Why can't customers find my garage door business?

Almost always one specific broken gate — not bad luck. Your site renders content only in the
browser so [crawlers see nothing](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), or it's slow; your pages
bury the answer under company history; or your reviews and listings are thin and inconsistent. The
fix is diagnostic: find the *highest* broken gate and repair it first, because a great reputation
can't rescue a site bots can't read, and a fast site can't rescue a page that never answers the
question. This is the same
[why-isn't-my-business-showing-up](/learn/why-isnt-my-local-business-showing-up-in-ai) logic,
applied to the trade.

## Does ranking in Google mean AI can find me?

Not on its own. Ranking proves Google can read and rank you — a good sign for the first gate — but
AI citation also needs an [extractable, answer-first page](/learn/answer-first-sentence) and trusted
local signals. A company can rank on page one yet be absent from AI answers if the answer is buried
three paragraphs down or off-site trust is thin. The overlap is real but not total: clear all three
gates and you get [found in both](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).
