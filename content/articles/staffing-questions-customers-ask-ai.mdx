direct-hire), speed (time-to-fill), risk (guarantees), and fit (do you specialize in my role or
industry).** Mapping each to a clear page is the core of a staffing AEO content plan.

## How do I find the exact questions?

Listen where employers already ask. Note what prospects ask on intake calls, read your reviews and FAQs,
scan HR and industry forums, and [prompt the assistants directly](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask)
on staffing and your specialty to see the follow-ups they surface. Capture the *natural wording* — "fill
my warehouse shift fast" beats "light-industrial workforce solutions" — because engines match the
employer's phrasing. Then prioritize by intent and value, and don't forget the candidate side of the
market.

## Why answer fee and markup questions publicly?

Because cost is the first thing an employer researches and the question most agency sites dodge. A clear
explanation of your direct-hire fee percentage or temp markup model — with what drives it — earns trust
and citations precisely when intent is highest. Dodging it ("every engagement is custom, contact us")
sends the employer to a competitor that actually answered. Map every bucket to an [answer-first service
page](/learn/staffing-agency-service-pages-for-ai), and you've built the content plan that gets a firm
[cited](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
