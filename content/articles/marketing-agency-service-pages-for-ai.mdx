**Write agency service pages AI will cite by giving each service — SEO, PPC, social, branding, web —
its own page that leads with the answer to the scope, who-it's-for, and results questions, in plain
language a buyer and an engine can lift.** One self-contained, crawlable page per service beats a single
bloated services page every time.

## Why one page per service?

Because a citation is awarded to the page that best answers one specific question — and a catch-all
services page answers none of them well. When you give SEO, PPC, paid social, branding, and web design
each [their own page](/learn/should-content-sections-stand-alone), each can go deep on its own scope,
fit, and results — and each becomes citable for its own query like "agency for paid social" or "SEO
agency for B2B." A single page trying to cover everything is shallow on all of them, so the engine cites
a competitor with a dedicated, focused page.

## What should each page lead with?

The answer the buyer came for, before anything about you.

This is [answer-first writing](/learn/answer-first-sentence) applied to the agency business: the
quotable answer up top, the depth below, the proof at the bottom — and the proof is where your
[case studies](/learn/agency-case-studies-and-results-aeo) earn their keep.

## What makes a service page extractable?

Plain language and a clean structure. Write the way a buyer asks — "a typical SEO retainer covers …" —
not in jargon or manifesto, and use [question-shaped headings](/learn/question-shaped-headings) the
engine can match to a query. Keep each answer in a self-contained passage so it can be [lifted without
the surrounding page](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage), reinforce it with [ProfessionalService
schema](/learn/marketing-agency-schema-markup), and make sure the page is fast and crawlable.
Answer-first, focused, and proven — that's the page an engine cites and a buyer contacts.
