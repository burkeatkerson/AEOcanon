**Mobile mechanics get found by AI search when their site is crawlable, answers the real questions drivers
ask, and is backed by consistent listings and genuine reviews.** The AI names only a few mechanics, so the
one that clears all three is recommended and called directly instead of a resold lead.

## What does an AI engine actually do to find you?

It retrieves, ranks, and names. When a driver asks for a mechanic to come fix their car, the engine pulls
the mobile mechanics it can read, judges which best answer the question and look trustworthy, and [names
just a few](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Each step is a filter: if a crawler can't fetch your
page, you're out before ranking; if your page doesn't answer the cost or come-to-you question, you're out at
ranking; if your reviews are thin, a competitor gets named. Being found means clearing every filter — and
the reward is a direct call, not a resold lead.

## What are the three gates?

The same three an engine — and a driver — apply, in order.

## Why can't customers find my mobile mechanic business?

Almost always one specific broken gate — not bad luck. Your site is thin or just an app profile so [crawlers
see little](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), your pages dodge the cost and come-to-you questions, or
your reviews and service-area listings are inconsistent. The fix is diagnostic: find the *highest* broken
gate and repair it first, because a great reputation can't rescue a site bots can't read, and a fast site
can't rescue a page that never answers the question. This is the same
[why-isn't-my-business-showing-up](/learn/why-isnt-my-local-business-showing-up-in-ai) logic, applied to the
trade.

## Does ranking in Google mean AI can find me?

Not on its own. Ranking proves Google can read and rank you — a good sign for the first gate — but AI
citation also needs an [extractable, answer-first page](/learn/answer-first-sentence) and genuine reviews. A
mobile mechanic can rank yet be absent from AI answers if the answer is buried or reviews are thin. The
overlap is real but not total: clear all three gates and you get [found in
both](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).
