## Why does seasonality matter for bakeries?

Because bakery demand spikes hard around occasions, and customers plan them ahead. Thanksgiving and
Christmas pre-orders, Valentine's, Easter, and wedding season all bring searches weeks — or for weddings,
months — before the date: "Thanksgiving pie pre-order near me," "Christmas Yule log," "wedding cake
baker in [city]." The bakery whose answer is already published, crawled, and trusted [gets cited the
moment planning starts](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo) — while competitors are still updating
their PDF or posting to Instagram. It's the [Freshness pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned into a
pre-order calendar.

## How do I prepare for each occasion?

Build the content while it's calm, so it's ready when customers start ordering.

## Should I make a new page each year?

No — update one durable page per recurring occasion. A persistent 'Thanksgiving pre-orders' or 'wedding
cakes' page [accumulates authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh the date, menu, and
pricing each year. That beats spinning up a throwaway page annually that starts from zero and splits your
signals. Keep one page per occasion, refresh it ahead of the pre-order wave, and link it to your
[custom-cakes and special-orders page](/learn/custom-cakes-and-orders-aeo) — one durable page, refreshed
and ready, beats scrambling when the rush arrives.
