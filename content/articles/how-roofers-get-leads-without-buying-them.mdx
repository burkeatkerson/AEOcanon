## Why are bought roofing leads such a bad deal?

Because you're renting demand the platform owns. A lead-gen service sells the same lead
to several roofers at once, so you pay to bid against competitors for a job — then
compete on price and often lose. The cost recurs with every lead, the homeowner feels no
loyalty to you, and you build no lasting asset. It's a treadmill: stop paying and the
leads stop instantly. The [business case for AEO](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) is
exactly this contrast — rented demand versus an asset you own.

## What replaces bought leads?

An owned pipeline, built from three habits. First, [earn AI citations and
rankings](/learn/aeo-for-roofers) with a crawlable, answer-first site so homeowners find
you directly. Second, build [local trust](/learn/local-aeo-for-roofers) — reviews,
certifications, consistent listings — so you're the one recommended. Third, turn every
completed job into a [review and a referral](/learn/roofer-reviews-and-reputation): a
photo, a quick review ask, a follow-up. Each job then feeds the next, the opposite of a
lead you consume and discard.

## How long until it replaces the spend?

It builds over weeks to months, then compounds — so shift the mix, don't flip a switch.
On-page and reputation work starts producing direct inquiries within weeks to a couple
of months and grows from there. Most roofers keep buying leads *while* they build the
owned pipeline, then taper the spend as direct calls and referrals climb. The endpoint
is the goal of [growing a roofing business with AI
search](/learn/grow-a-roofing-business-with-ai-search): demand you own, not demand you
rent.
