## Why offer AEO and SEO at all?

Because owners don't want a website — they want to be found and win customers, and a build that's ready
to [rank in Google and be cited by AI](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together) sells the outcome they
actually care about. A bare site is a commodity owners shop on price; a site engineered to be the answer
an assistant gives is a result worth paying a premium for. That reframing is the
[Alignment](/pillars/alignment) of selling what the client wants instead of what's easy to quote — and
it justifies higher fees on every project.

## How does this become a higher-margin retainer?

Because citations aren't a one-time setup — they're earned and defended with ongoing content and
maintenance. So the natural package is an AEO/SEO-ready build, then a monthly care-and-content retainer
that keeps the site fast, the schema clean, and the answer-first content compounding.

## How do I package and sell it?

Build your own site to be cited first — fast, crawlable, [answer-first](/learn/answer-first-sentence),
with clean [schema](/learn/web-design-schema-markup) — so you have proof. Then offer clients an
AEO/SEO-ready build with [service pages that lead with the answer](/learn/web-design-service-pages-for-ai),
followed by a monthly retainer for content and care. Use your own citations as the demo, point
ready-to-hire prospects at your [high-intent pages](/learn/high-intent-web-design-ai-search), and lean
into a [high-value niche](/learn/ecommerce-web-design-aeo) where premium retainers are easiest to win.
You're no longer selling a website — you're selling the outcome, proven by your own results.
