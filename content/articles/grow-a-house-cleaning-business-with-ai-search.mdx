## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each clean produces a review and a referral — and ideally a recurring plan;
reviews and [answer-first content](/learn/house-cleaning-service-pages-for-ai) earn citations and
rankings; those bring direct bookings; and the money saved on purchased leads funds more crews. The
lead-gen model is the opposite: a faucet you pay to keep running, selling the same lead to three
services, that stops the moment you stop. AEO builds the [compounding
asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead — a book of recurring clients you own.

## Why is recurring revenue central to growth?

Because a cleaning client *is* recurring revenue every week or two, and filling the top of the funnel
only pays off if clients stay. A client who stays for years is worth many times a one-time clean — and
long-tenured, happy clients are exactly who write the [reviews that mention
trust](/learn/house-cleaning-reviews-ai-recommendations) and refer neighbors, feeding the AI
recommendations that bring the next clients. So growth isn't just AEO at the top; it's AEO plus retention
together, each compounding the other.

## How do I lower my cost per client?

By replacing recurring fees with assets that keep paying. A purchased lead costs every time and is
[resold to competitors](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands); an earned citation, a recurring client, or a
referral costs mostly upfront and pays for years. As your cited, recurring, retained book grows, your
cost per client falls and your revenue stabilizes. And because AI names only a few services, a real,
well-reviewed, insured local company can win recommendations a generic app
[can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
