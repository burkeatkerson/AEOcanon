## Why do reviews carry so much weight for mobile mechanics?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge trust — and a customer is letting a stranger work
on their car in their own driveway. An assistant deciding whom to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform reviews to gauge
whether you're fair, reliable, and honest. For a trade built on inviting someone to your home, that
corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and
the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks
do.

## How many reviews do I need?

Fewer than you think, fresher than you have. There's no magic count — consistency and recency outweigh a raw
total. A steady stream of genuine recent reviews signals an active, trusted mechanic far better than a big
pile from three years ago. The goal is an ongoing flow: ask happy customers right after the job, while the
convenience of you coming to them is fresh, so new reviews keep arriving and the engine keeps seeing a
mechanic who's [currently trusted](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations), not formerly busy.

## Do the words in a review matter?

Yes — specific reviews do more work than generic praise.

Reviews that name what you did — came to the house, fixed it on-site, fair price, showed up on time — give
engines specific detail tying you to convenient, honest service. They reinforce the [questions drivers
ask](/learn/mobile-mechanic-questions-customers-ask-ai), so a handful of detailed reviews can outperform a
wall of generic ones. Ask customers what to mention, and the reviews start doing your AEO for you.
