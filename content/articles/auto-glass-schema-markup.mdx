address, phone, hours, and services, plus FAQ schema on answer pages — it helps engines parse who you are,
where you work, and what you do.** Schema clarifies clear content for AI; it never rescues a thin site or
wrong hours.

## What does auto-glass schema actually do?

It makes your business details unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business), and the
AutomotiveBusiness subtype, labels your name, address, phone, hours, services, and reviews so engines
parse them cleanly rather than guessing — reinforcing the [consistent identity](/learn/nap-consistency)
and availability local recognition depends on. It's the [structured data for
AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern applied to the trade: clarity for the parser, on top of
content and hours that are already clear for the driver.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your shop — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your details machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [accurate hours and
listings](/learn/local-aeo-for-auto-glass-shops), [genuine
reviews](/learn/auto-glass-shop-reviews-ai-recommendations), and [pages that answer driver
questions](/learn/auto-glass-shop-service-pages-for-ai). Schema can't rescue wrong hours or a thin site —
and faking reviews in markup is a misuse engines can [detect](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations).
Accurate schema on top of a readable, answer-first site is the combination that works.
