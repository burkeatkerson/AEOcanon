## Why do citations matter to an engine?

Because they're independent corroboration. An engine builds its picture of you by
merging mentions from across the web into one business — and consistent citations on
reputable directories confirm your [name, address, and phone](/learn/nap-consistency)
agree everywhere. That agreement is what lets the engine recognize you as a single,
trustworthy local [entity](/learn/what-is-entity-aeo). It's the local face of the same
[authority and corroboration](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that drives AI visibility
generally.

## Which citations should I prioritize?

The major platforms and the ones that matter in your niche. Cover the core directories
first — Google Business Profile, Bing Places, Apple Business Connect, Yelp — then the
industry and local directories relevant to your trade and area, plus organizations like
your chamber of commerce.

## Is more always better?

No — consistency beats count, and bad citations can hurt. Conflicting details across
directories make engines unsure which business you are or where you operate, favoring a
competitor they can place cleanly. So a handful of accurate, reputable citations that
all agree outperforms a pile of inconsistent or spammy ones — and chasing low-quality
directory listings for volume is the local version of the
[link-count mistake](/learn/do-i-need-backlinks-for-aeo). Clean and consistent wins.
