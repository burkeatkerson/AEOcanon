## What does it take to get found?

Clearing the three gates a homeowner and an engine both judge you on. First
[readable](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site): a fast, crawlable, server-rendered
site — not the slow, image-heavy build most roofers have, which bots see as empty.
Second [relevant](/learn/roofing-service-pages-that-get-cited): pages that lead with the
answer to the repair-vs-replace, cost, and insurance questions homeowners ask. Third
[trusted](/learn/local-aeo-for-roofers): consistent listings, real reviews, and
manufacturer certifications. It's the [AEO Canon](/learn/aeo-canon) applied to roofing,
and the same foundation that [ranks you in Google](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).

## Why isn't my roofing company showing up?

Almost always one specific, fixable gap — diagnose it top-down.

The first gap you hit is your fix — the local version of the [Canon
cascade](/learn/why-isnt-my-site-cited-by-ai), where a higher broken gate can't be
compensated for by a lower one.

## Do I need to be on the lead-gen sites?

No — and that's the opportunity. Aggregators get cited because they're large and
crawlable, but a [crawlable, answer-first, locally-trusted roofing
site](/learn/aeo-for-roofers) can be named alongside or instead of them. The difference:
when the AI names *your* site, the homeowner comes straight to you with no lead fee —
the whole case for [getting leads without buying them](/learn/how-roofers-get-leads-without-buying-them).
You're not trying to beat the aggregator at being generic; you're beating it at being
the specific, trusted local roofer.
