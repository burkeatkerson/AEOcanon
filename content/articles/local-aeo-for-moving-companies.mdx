## What makes a moving company 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about where you are and which routes you run. An answer engine recommends a mover for "near
me" or a specific move only when it can [place you with
certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — right area, serves the route. That
certainty comes from signals that all agree: matching name-address-phone, named service areas, a complete
profile, visible licensing, and reviews. When the signals agree, you're an easy recommendation; when your
service area is vague, the engine names a competitor that spelled it out.

This is the [near-me ranking](/learn/how-to-rank-in-ai-for-near-me-searches) discipline applied to the
trade — and vague or missing service areas are where most movers quietly lose.

## Why do named service areas matter so much?

Because moving searches carry an origin and usually a destination, and the engine answers them from the
areas you state you serve. Clear, specific service areas mean you get recommended when someone in your
market needs a mover or that exact route; a vague "we serve the region" leaves the engine unsure, so it
names a competitor it can confidently place. Name your cities, neighborhoods, and routes in readable text,
keep them consistent everywhere, and you remove a common reason a mover gets skipped. It's the same [NAP
consistency](/learn/nap-consistency) discipline, with the map attached.
