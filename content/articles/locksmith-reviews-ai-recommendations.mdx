## Why do reviews carry so much weight for locksmiths?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge trust — and in a trade plagued by scam
operators, trust is the whole decision. An assistant deciding whom to recommend [synthesizes
sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and third-party reviews to
gauge whether you're honest, fair, and real. For a locksmith, a deep bench of genuine reviews is the
single clearest signal that you're a legitimate local pro and not a bait-and-switch listing — it's
the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the [off-site
mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks do.

## How many reviews do I need?

Fewer than you think, fresher than you have. There's no magic count — consistency and recency outweigh
a raw total. A steady stream of genuine recent reviews signals an active, trusted locksmith far better
than a big pile from three years ago. The goal is an ongoing flow: build a simple habit of asking
after every job, so new reviews keep arriving and the engine keeps seeing a locksmith that's
[currently trusted](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations), not formerly busy.

## Do the words in a review matter?

Yes — specific reviews do more work than generic praise.

Reviews that name what you did — got someone back in fast without gouging, made a car key on the spot,
rekeyed a house fairly — give engines specific detail tying you to those exact services and to honest,
fair work. They reinforce the [questions people ask](/learn/locksmith-questions-customers-ask-ai), so
a handful of detailed reviews can outperform a wall of generic ones. Ask customers what to mention,
and the reviews start doing your AEO for you.
