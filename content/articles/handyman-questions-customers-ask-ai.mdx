scope ('do you do small jobs', 'who fixes X'), trust ('are you insured'), and decision ('handyman or
specialist').** Mapping each question to the page that should own it is the core of a handyman AEO
content plan.

## How do I find the exact questions?

Listen where customers already ask. Mine your calls and texts for the questions people actually voice,
check Reddit threads, neighborhood groups, home-improvement forums, and the People Also Ask box, and
[prompt the assistants directly](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask) on common repairs to see the
follow-ups they surface. Capture the *natural wording* — "do handymen do small jobs" beats "small-scope
service inquiry" — because engines match the homeowner's phrasing, not your jargon. Then prioritize by
intent and value.

## Should I answer trust and scope questions?

Yes — they're how you earn the trust that wins the job. Answering "are you insured", "do you do small
jobs", or "is this a handyman job or a plumber job" honestly makes you the source a homeowner remembers
when they're ready to call. Trust content captures the wary homeowner who's been burned before; decision
content wins the relationship before they even decide who to call. Both build the credibility and
visibility engines [reward](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore) — the opposite of a thin one-page
site. Map every bucket to a page and you've built the content plan that gets a handyman cited.
