## How is corporate catering AEO different?

The intent is recurring, logistics-driven procurement, not a one-off celebration. An office manager,
executive assistant, or event coordinator booking weekly lunches or a quarterly all-hands researches
carefully — and asks the assistant detailed logistics questions long before they call. They're checking
delivery, dietary handling for a whole office, invoicing, and lead time. Because [AEO works for these
higher-consideration B2B decisions](/learn/does-aeo-work-for-b2b), the caterer whose pages answer those
questions clearly gets [cited during the research](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations) — and wins a
recurring account most caterers never even surface for.

## What questions do office managers ask?

Logistics-focused and specific — each worth a clear answer on a dedicated page.

## How do I win corporate catering citations?

Give corporate and office catering their own answer-first pages — not a buried line under your wedding
work. Publish per-head pricing, delivery and setup, dietary handling at scale, invoicing terms, and lead
time in readable text, reinforced by your [reviews](/learn/caterer-reviews-ai-recommendations) and a clear
inquiry path. Most caterers either omit this entirely or trap it in a PDF, so a clear, dedicated page is an
easy way to get cited for recurring, predictable corporate accounts — the steadiest, highest-margin
revenue that compounds your [growth](/learn/grow-a-catering-business-with-ai-search).
