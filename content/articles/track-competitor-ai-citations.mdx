**To know if AI is citing your competitors, run a fixed set of your priority
questions across the major engines on a regular schedule and log which brands get
named.** Because AI citations are volatile and differ by engine, a single check
tells you almost nothing — the signal is in repeated, consistent measurement.

## How do I actually check who gets cited?

You check by turning your customers' real questions into a fixed [prompt set](/glossary/prompt-set) and
running it across the major engines on a repeating schedule. For each prompt,
record which brands and sources each engine names. A spreadsheet and a recurring
calendar block is enough for a handful of priority questions; dedicated
AI-visibility tools automate the same process across many prompts and engines. The
discipline that matters is consistency — same questions, same cadence — so results
are comparable over time. This is the [Adaptability pillar](/pillars/adaptability)
made operational.

## Why do the results keep changing?

Results keep changing because AI citations are genuinely [volatile](/glossary/citation-volatility).
[Semrush found that 40–60% of the sources LLMs cite change from month to
month](https://www.semrush.com/blog/url-volatility-ai-overviews/), and that Google
keeps a given URL in an AI Overview only about **3.87 days** on average. So any
single check is a snapshot that can mislead. Only repeated measurement reveals who
*consistently* gets cited versus who appeared once by chance — which is exactly why
a one-time competitor audit is close to useless.

## What should I measure, beyond who's cited?

Measure your [citation share](/glossary/citation-share) relative to competitors, broken down by engine and by
question. Four things to log: how often you're named versus each competitor; which
engines cite whom (they differ sharply); what *kinds* of sources get cited (your
pages, Reddit, review sites, publications); and the specific questions where
competitors appear and you don't. That last one is gold — those gaps are your
prioritized roadmap.

## Why measure each engine separately?

Measure each engine separately because they live in different citation universes —
they overlap on only a fraction of the sources they cite. A competitor who
dominates Perplexity may be absent from Google AI Overviews, and vice versa.
Averaging across engines hides where you're actually winning or losing. Track them
individually, then prioritize the engines your audience uses most.

## What should I do with the findings?

Turn the gaps into work: for every high-value question where a competitor is cited
and you aren't, make your page the better, more extractable, better-evidenced
answer, and build the authority that earns trust. Then re-measure to confirm
movement. Start from [what is AEO](/learn/what-is-aeo) and use
[The AEO Canon](/learn/aeo-canon) to diagnose each gap.
