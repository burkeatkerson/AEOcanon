## Why do reviews carry so much weight for food trucks?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge which trucks people actually love and seek out —
and mobile food is driven by reputation and word of mouth. An assistant deciding what to recommend
[synthesizes sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform
reviews to gauge whether you're a great choice. For a truck that moves, that corroborated reputation is
decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the [off-site
mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks do.

## Do reviews that mention dishes matter more?

Yes — dish-level reviews are gold, because they speak the language of discovery.

A review naming a dish, the wait worth it, or where they found you gives the engine detail to tie you to
"best birria near me" or "food truck at [brewery]" — the exact [discovery and find-a-truck
queries](/learn/food-truck-questions-customers-ask-ai) customers ask. Invite happy customers to mention
what they loved, and the reviews start matching you to the searches that draw a crowd.

## How many reviews — and how fresh?

Fresh beats big. There's no magic count — recency and a steady flow outweigh a stale total. A truck with
current reviews that mention dishes signals an active, loved truck people seek out far better than one
coasting on old ratings. Build a simple habit at the window: a sign or a card inviting happy customers to
review, and [respond to them](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations) — engagement signals an active
truck that cares, which is exactly the one an engine wants to recommend.
