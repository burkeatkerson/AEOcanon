## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a business the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine can read
your packages and place you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as who to hire — and that leans
harder on whether clients love you. The engine recommends the photographer it can [confidently
confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, skilled, and well-reviewed, because when it recommends
you it's putting its credibility behind how the wedding or session will go. Your job is to make that
confirmation easy — readable packages, a clear service area, and genuine, loving reviews.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). An unclear service area or a thin review profile introduces doubt, and
doubt sends the inquiry elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one photographer over another?

Because it picks the one it's most confident clients will love. Faced with several options, the engine names
the photographer whose signals best match the request — right session type, right area, right style — and
whose reviews most clearly say people love the experience. The competitor with a wordless gallery, an unclear
service area, or thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it loses. Winning is about being the [easiest to verify
as the loved local choice](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — which is exactly what this
library builds, piece by piece.
