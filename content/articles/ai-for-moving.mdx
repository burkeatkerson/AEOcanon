**Using AI to run your moving company and being recommended by AI to customers
are two different games — and you've probably won the first while quietly losing
the second.** You send virtual video estimates in minutes and your dispatch board
sorts crews and trucks; meanwhile customers have started asking AI *who to hire for
a move* — and it names one or two companies. If yours isn't one of them, AI is
booking that move with a competitor.

## But is AI recommending your moving company?

Probably not — and that's the part that costs you booked jobs. The model that
builds your estimate isn't the system deciding who to recommend, and even when it's
the same product, it recommends based on what it can find and trust *about you on
the open web*. When a customer asks for a mover, the engine retrieves and quotes
the sources that best answer that question: your website (if it's readable and
answer-first), your reviews, and mentions of you across other sites. Your slick
estimating tool is invisible to that process. So a company can automate its whole
sales flow and still never surface when a customer asks AI who to book.

## How do customers use AI to find a moving company?

They ask it like they'd ask a friend who just moved. Instead of scrolling a page
of links and dialing five companies, someone now types "movers near me," "best
moving company in [town]," "who can move me across state lines next month," or "who
does last-minute local moves" — and acts on the short list the assistant gives
back. Trust matters more here than in most trades, so a clean review record carries
weight. Because the AI answers in place and names only a couple of options, this is
a **winner-take-most** moment: the companies it cites get the estimate, and everyone
else is invisible. That compresses a whole page of choices down to one or two names.

## How do you know if AI is sending your customers to a competitor?

Ask the engines yourself. Open ChatGPT, [Google AI Mode](/glossary/google-ai-mode),
and [Perplexity](/glossary/perplexity), and run the real questions your customers
ask: "best moving company in [your city]," "local movers near me," "long distance
movers from [town]." Note who gets named. If competitors show up and you don't — or
the AI misses that you do long-distance or is unsure you're licensed and insured —
you've found the gap. For the bigger picture on why this happens, read
[you use AI, but is AI recommending you](/learn/use-ai-but-is-ai-recommending-you).
