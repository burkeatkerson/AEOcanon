## What does web-design schema actually do?

It makes your studio's details unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business), and the
ProfessionalService subtype, labels your name, address, phone, hours, area served, services, and reviews
so engines parse them cleanly rather than guessing — reinforcing the [consistent
identity](/learn/nap-consistency) local recognition depends on. It's the [structured data for
AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern applied to a studio: clarity for the parser, on top of
content that's already clear for the owner. As a developer, shipping it cleanly is also part of proving
you can.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your studio — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your details machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [consistent
identity](/learn/local-aeo-for-web-designers), [genuine
reviews](/learn/web-design-reviews-ai-recommendations), a real portfolio, and [pages that answer owner
questions](/learn/web-design-service-pages-for-ai). And don't fake it: marking up reviews or services
that don't match your visible page is a misuse engines can detect — [especially
damaging](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations) when clients hire you precisely to get this right.
Accurate schema on top of real proof is the combination that works — and the standard you should sell.
