questions — gutters and weatherproofing in fall, winterizing before the freeze, spring fix-up lists —
before each wave, on durable pages you update yearly.** Be the cited answer when homeowners start their
list, not scrambling after.

## Why does seasonality matter for a handyman?

Because demand is uneven and the research happens just before the task. Homeowners think about gutters and
weatherproofing as the leaves fall, winterizing before the first freeze, fix-up and deck repairs as spring
arrives, and to-do lists all summer. The handyman whose answer is already published, crawled, and trusted
[gets cited the moment the task comes to mind](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo) — before the
homeowner has called anyone. It's the [Freshness pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned into a maintenance
calendar, and a way to fill the pipeline ahead of each wave.

## How do I prepare for each seasonal wave?

Build the content while it's quiet, so it's ready when homeowners start their list.

## Should I make a new page each year?

No — update one durable page per seasonal topic. A persistent URL [accumulates
authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh it each year with current rates and details.
That beats spinning up a throwaway page annually that starts from zero and splits your signals. Keep one
'fall gutter cleaning' page, one 'winterize your home' page, one 'spring fix-up checklist' page — and
refresh each ahead of its wave. One durable page, refreshed and ready, beats scrambling when the calls
come all at once.
