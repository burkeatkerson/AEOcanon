**Using AI to run your garage door company and being recommended by AI to
customers are two different games — and you've probably won the first while
quietly losing the second.** You use software to dispatch techs and price spring
jobs; meanwhile homeowners have started asking AI *who to call* when a door won't
open — and it names one or two companies. If yours isn't one of them, AI is
handing those same-day calls to a competitor.

## How are garage door companies using AI today?

More than most owners admit. Field service platforms like ServiceTitan,
Housecall Pro, and Jobber schedule techs, route trucks, and auto-text customers
an arrival window. ChatGPT drafts the follow-up email, cleans up the estimate
language, and writes the "spring replacement vs. full door" explainer you send
homeowners. AI answering services and receptionists catch the after-hours
broken-spring calls you used to lose to voicemail. Some shops even use AI to
summarize the day's job notes into invoices. All of it makes you faster and
leaner — and none of it makes you the company AI recommends.

## But is AI recommending your garage door company?

That's the question that actually grows the business, and it has nothing to do
with your software stack. When a homeowner opens an assistant and asks "who
should I call to fix my garage door," the engine doesn't look at how well you run
dispatch. It retrieves and quotes the sources it can *read and trust about you on
the open web* — your service pages, your Google reviews, your directory listings.
You can run a paperless, AI-automated shop and still never surface in that answer,
because your internal tools are invisible to the system doing the recommending.

## How do customers use AI to find a garage door pro?

They ask it like they'd ask a neighbor. Instead of scrolling a page of listings,
more people now type "best garage door repair near me," "who fixes a broken
spring same day," or "garage door won't go up who do I call" — and act on the
short list the assistant hands back. Because the AI answers in place and names
only a couple of companies, this is a **winner-take-most** moment: the shops it
cites get the call, and everyone else is invisible. For an urgent, high-trust job
like a snapped spring or a door stuck shut, the first credible name usually wins.

## How do you know if AI is sending your customers to a competitor?

Ask the engines yourself. Open ChatGPT, [Google AI Mode](/glossary/google-ai-mode),
and [Perplexity](/glossary/perplexity) and run the real questions your customers
ask: "garage door repair in [your city]," "best garage door installer near me,"
"same-day broken spring repair [your area]." Note who gets named. If competitors
show up and you don't — or the AI describes you with stale hours, the wrong
service area, or a number you dropped years ago — you've found the gap. That
mismatch is exactly what [AEO for garage door companies](/learn/aeo-for-garage-door-companies)
is built to fix.
