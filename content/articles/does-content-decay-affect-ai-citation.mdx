## What is content decay?

It's a page going stale while sitting still. Prices change, tools update, best
practices shift — and a page that was accurate slowly becomes outdated even though its
text never changed. Its *correctness* decayed, and engines respond by favoring sources
that kept up. That slow erosion is what the [Freshness pillar](/pillars/freshness)
exists to counter: staying current relative to a moving world.

## How do I spot it?

Watch the trend, then audit. A page that stops getting cited or loses referral
traffic, or that you know contains outdated facts, is decaying — so your [citation and
traffic monitoring](/learn/track-competitor-ai-citations) is the early-warning system.
Periodic audits against the current state of the topic catch decay before it becomes
total invisibility. The danger of decay is precisely that it's quiet; you have to look
for it.

## How do I reverse it?

Refresh substantively. [Update stale facts and numbers, add new developments, remove
the obsolete](/learn/does-updating-old-content-help-aeo), improve the answer, and
update the date. Reversing decay restores accuracy and freshness on a page that still
holds its accumulated [authority](/pillars/authority) — faster than starting over and
exactly why a [refresh system](/learn/content-refresh-system) pays off. Catch the decay
early and a refresh recovers the citation; ignore it and you rebuild from zero.
