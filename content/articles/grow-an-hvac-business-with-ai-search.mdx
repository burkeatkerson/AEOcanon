## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each completed job produces reviews, referrals, and often a
maintenance-plan signup; reviews and [answer-first
content](/learn/hvac-service-pages-for-ai) earn citations and rankings; those bring direct
inquiries; and the money saved on purchased leads funds more content and more crews — which
produce more jobs. The directory model is the opposite: a faucet you pay to keep running,
that stops the moment you stop. AEO builds the [compounding
asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead.

## How do maintenance plans and heat pumps accelerate it?

They add recurring revenue and a fresh growth wave. Maintenance plans turn one-time service
into a recurring base — each seasonal visit is a new touchpoint for a [fresh
review](/learn/hvac-reviews-ai-recommendations), a referral, and an upsell, and that steady
reputation feeds the signals AI uses to recommend you. Meanwhile the shift to heat pumps and
high-efficiency systems is new, high-value demand: homeowners are [asking the assistant
whether it's worth it](/learn/hvac-questions-customers-ask-ai), and the contractor whose
answer is cited captures the install. Own both and the flywheel spins faster.

## Can I really lower my cost per job?

Yes — by replacing recurring fees with assets that keep paying. A purchased lead costs every
time and is [resold to your competitors](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands); an earned
citation, ranking, maintenance-plan member, or referral costs mostly upfront and sends jobs
for years. As your owned pipeline grows, your cost per job falls and your margins improve,
because you're no longer renting every customer. And because AI names only a few sources, a
focused, certified, genuinely reviewed local contractor can win recommendations a generic
national directory [can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
