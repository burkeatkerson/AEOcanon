## Why does Google Business Profile matter for AI search?

Because it's a structured, trusted confirmation of where you are, when you're open, and what kind of
store you are — exactly what an engine needs to recommend a hobby shop. When the assistant decides whom
to name for a near-me or niche query, it leans on the profile to verify your location, hours, category,
and reputation [before recommending you](/learn/does-google-business-profile-help-ai-search). A complete,
accurate profile makes you an easy recommendation; wrong hours or a thin profile leaves the engine unsure
— or sends a customer to a store that's closed when they show up for game night.

The reviews on your profile do double duty — they're a top input to [whether AI recommends
you](/learn/hobby-shop-reviews-ai-recommendations) at all.

## Does GBP replace a good website?

No — it's half the picture. The profile is a powerful placement signal, but engines also retrieve and
cite your own [readable brand, category, and event pages](/learn/hobby-shop-product-pages-for-ai) for
detailed questions — "store that carries Warhammer near me," "game store with a Friday league." You need
both: a complete, accurate profile so the engine can place you, and readable brands and events for the
substance that wins the niche query. Treat them as one system, kept [consistent
everywhere](/learn/nap-consistency).
