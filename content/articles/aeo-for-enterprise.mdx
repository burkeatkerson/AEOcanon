the moves are known, but executing them across thousands of pages, many teams, and a
big brand requires an operating model, measurement at scale, and a content program
with quality gates.** The brand authority is an advantage; the scale is the
challenge.

## What's different about enterprise?

What's different is scale and coordination. An enterprise already has the thing
smaller players struggle for — broad [brand authority](/glossary/brand-authority), the kind of [authority](/pillars/authority) that is the
strongest correlate of AI visibility — but it also has thousands of pages, many
teams, and approval layers, so the bottleneck isn't knowing what to do; it's doing it
consistently and well at scale — holding to [Google's people-first quality guidance](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/creating-helpful-content)
across the whole estate, not just on flagship pages. That makes enterprise AEO largely an operating-model
problem: governance, standardized systems, and measurement, so quality holds across
the whole estate. The two failure modes are squandering brand authority on
unreadable or generic content, and scaling output without quality.
