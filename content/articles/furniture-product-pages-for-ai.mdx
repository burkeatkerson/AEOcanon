## Why are readable products the most important pages?

Because what you sell is what shoppers search for — and a citation goes to the page the engine can read.
When someone asks "mid-century sofa near me" or "solid wood dining table under $1,500," the engine
matches the query against products and specs it can [actually
parse](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site). If your site is all catalog photos with no descriptions,
the engine doesn't know what you sell or at what price, so you're invisible for those style, item, and
price queries — the most valuable, highest-intent searches. Readable product details turn your whole
showroom into citable answers.

## What makes a furniture product page citable?

Readable text, organized the way shoppers think.

## Why describe products, not just show them?

Because description is the language of discovery. Shoppers ask AI for "a comfortable sectional for a
small living room" or "a solid wood bed frame that lasts," and a wall of catalog photos gives the engine
nothing to match. A sentence describing each piece — its material, dimensions, the style and room it
suits — gives the engine the [extractable detail](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage) that ties you
to those searches, and mirrors the [reviews](/learn/furniture-store-reviews-ai-recommendations) buyers
write about your quality. Readable, described products and delivery info are the foundation every other
furniture-store AEO move builds on.
