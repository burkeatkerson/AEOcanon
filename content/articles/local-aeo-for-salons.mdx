## What makes a salon 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about where you are and what you offer. An answer engine recommends a salon for "near me"
only when it can [place you with certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) — right
area, right kind of salon. That certainty comes from signals that all agree: matching
name-address-phone, accurate hours, a complete profile, a readable menu and pricing, and reviews. When
the signals agree, you're an easy recommendation; when they conflict or your menu is hidden in a widget,
the engine favors a clearer competitor.

## Why does NAP consistency matter so much?

Because a mismatch is a doubt, and engines route around doubt. If your phone number differs between your
site and your Google profile, or an old address still shows in a listing, the engine can't be sure which
business you are or where you operate — so it favors a competitor it can [place with
confidence](/learn/nap-consistency). For salons that have moved, rebranded, or where a stylist has gone
independent, this is a common and quiet leak. Clean it up and you remove the single most fixable reason
an engine skips you on a near-me query.
