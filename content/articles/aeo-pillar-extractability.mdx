**Extractability is the third pillar of the AEO Canon: answer engines cite
passages, not pages, so you have to write the sentence you want quoted and put
it first.** Even a page that passes [Access](/learn/aeo-pillar-access) and nails
[Alignment](/learn/aeo-pillar-alignment) will go uncited if its best answer is
buried in paragraph nine.

## Why the passage is the unit of citation

Modern answer engines use retrieval-augmented generation (RAG): they break the
web into passages, retrieve the ones most relevant to a query, and a reranker
scores those passages for relevance, authority, and freshness before the model
writes its answer. Your page is just the container. The **passage** is what
competes.

<PullQuote cite="Pillar 3 · Extractability">
  AI doesn’t read your book. It photocopies a paragraph.
</PullQuote>

That changes how you write. You are not composing an essay that builds to a
conclusion — you are placing self-contained answers the engine can lift cleanly,
out of context, and still have them make sense.

## The anatomy of a citable passage

Here is the pattern, made concrete:

<Callout variant="definition" title="Question-shaped heading">
  <p>
    <strong>How much does a ductless mini-split cost to install?</strong> A
    ductless mini-split typically costs $3,000–$5,000 per indoor zone installed,
    including the outdoor condenser, with most single-zone homes landing near
    $4,000. Price moves with the number of zones, the unit's efficiency rating
    (SEER2), and line-set length. (This opening sentence is the answer; the rest
    supports it.)
  </p>
</Callout>

Notice the structure: the heading is the question, the first sentence is a
complete answer with a number, and the support comes after. An engine can lift
that opening line and it stands on its own.

<Callout variant="warning" title="The most common extractability failure">
  <p>
    Burying the answer. "There are many factors to consider when thinking about
    the cost of a mini-split…" tells the engine — and the reader — nothing. If
    your first sentence could open any article, rewrite it until it answers the
    question.
  </p>
</Callout>
