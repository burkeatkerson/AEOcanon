## Why do reviews carry so much weight for bakeries?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge which shops customers actually love — and a
cake, especially a wedding or custom cake, is a high-trust purchase driven by reputation. An assistant
deciding what to recommend [synthesizes sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from
your Google and review-platform reviews to gauge whether you're a great choice. For a bakery, that
corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the [Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible,
and the [off-site mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than
backlinks do.

## Do reviews that mention specific cakes matter more?

Yes — product-level reviews are gold, because they speak the language of discovery.

A review naming a cake, the occasion, or a dietary win gives the engine detail to tie you to "gluten-free
wedding cake near me" or "custom birthday cake nearby" — the exact [discovery and occasion
queries](/learn/bakery-questions-customers-ask-ai) customers ask. Invite happy customers — especially
custom-cake clients after their event — to mention what they loved, and the reviews start matching you
to the searches that fill the order book.

## How many reviews — and how fresh?

Fresh beats big. There's no magic count — recency and a steady flow outweigh a stale total. A bakery
with current reviews that name cakes signals an active, loved shop far better than one coasting on old
ratings. Build a simple habit: invite happy customers to review after a great order, and [respond to
them](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations) — engagement signals an active bakery that cares,
which is exactly the shop an engine wants to recommend.
