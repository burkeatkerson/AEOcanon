## Why one page per service?

Because a citation is awarded to the page that best answers one specific question — and a catch-all
services page or photo gallery answers none of them well. When you give weekly mowing, cleanups,
design, hardscape, and irrigation each [their own page](/learn/should-content-sections-stand-alone),
each can go deep on its own cost, scope, and details — and each becomes citable for its own query. A
single page trying to cover everything is shallow on all of them, so the engine cites a competitor with
a dedicated, focused page.

## What should each page lead with?

The answer the homeowner came for, before anything about you.

This is [answer-first writing](/learn/answer-first-sentence) applied to the trade: the quotable answer
up top, the depth below, the proof at the bottom — described, not just shown.

## What makes a service page extractable?

Plain language and a clean structure. Write the way a homeowner asks — "weekly mowing typically runs …"
— not in jargon, and use [question-shaped headings](/learn/question-shaped-headings) the engine can
match to a query. Keep each answer in a self-contained passage so it can be [lifted without the
surrounding page](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage), reinforce it with [LocalBusiness
schema](/learn/landscaper-schema-markup), and make sure the page is fast and crawlable — not a slow
gallery. Answer-first, focused, and proven — that's the page an engine cites and a homeowner calls.
