## Why is 'near me' the highest-value cleaning search?

Because it's the moment a homeowner decides to book, and it often becomes recurring revenue. When
someone asks "house cleaning near me" or "recurring cleaning service nearby," they're ready to act — and
the [AI answer names only a few services](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Pew Research [found
people click a link just 8% of the time when an AI summary
appears](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer *is* the shortlist. For a cleaning company, owning that moment is the difference between a
full schedule and an empty one — and a recurring client keeps booking every week or two.

## What do homeowners ask AI when choosing a cleaner?

Ready-to-book, specific questions — and you should own the ones that fit your services.

## Why does publishing pricing and trust help so much?

Because homeowners ask AI "how much is a house cleaning near me" and "are you insured," and clear answers
in readable text let the engine recommend you for those high-intent searches — while competitors who
hide pricing or trust signals get skipped. For a service where someone lets you into their home,
stating your pricing, insurance, and background checks plainly is pure [Alignment](/pillars/alignment)
and trust. Be specific and transparent, back it with [reviews](/learn/house-cleaning-reviews-ai-recommendations),
and you win the near-me search that turns into a recurring client.
