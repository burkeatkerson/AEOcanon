**Yes — reviews and ratings strongly influence AI product recommendations, because
engines synthesize sentiment from review sites, forums, and third-party sources to
decide what to recommend.** Genuine, plentiful, recent reviews across trusted
platforms make you a safer pick; thin or fake ones do little or backfire.

## Why do reviews sway recommendations?

Because they're independent evidence of quality. When an engine [recommends a product
or service](/learn/how-ai-recommends-products), it draws on sentiment from review
sites, marketplaces, and forums rather than taking your word for it — so the volume,
recency, and quality of your reviews directly shape whether you make the cut. Strong
genuine reviews are [credibility](/pillars/credibility) the engine can lean on, making
you a safer recommendation.

## Which reviews count most?

The ones on platforms engines actually read. Reviews concentrated on trusted
third-party sites — major review platforms, marketplaces, and the
[communities relevant to your category](/learn/which-platforms-build-ai-authority) —
carry more weight than testimonials on your own site, because independent sentiment is
more credible than self-published praise. Spread genuine reviews across the platforms
that matter for your space rather than collecting them all in one place.

## Do fake reviews work?

No — they hurt. Fake or incentivized reviews risk detection, violate platform rules,
and create inconsistent sentiment that engines and platforms increasingly catch; they
add no genuine corroboration and can damage trust if exposed. The durable approach is
to earn real reviews — ask satisfied customers at the right moment, make it easy,
respond to show engagement, and keep a steady recent flow. Slower, but it's what
actually moves recommendations.
