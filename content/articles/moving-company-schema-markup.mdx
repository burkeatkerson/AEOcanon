address, phone, service area, and services, plus FAQ schema on answer pages — it helps engines parse who
you are, where you move, and what you do.** Schema clarifies clear content for AI; it never rescues a
thin site or a vague service area.

## What does moving-company schema actually do?

It makes your business details unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business), and the
MovingCompany subtype, labels your name, address, phone, service area, services, and reviews so engines
parse them cleanly rather than guessing — reinforcing the [consistent identity](/learn/nap-consistency)
and service-area clarity local recognition depends on. It's the [structured data for
AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern applied to the trade: clarity for the parser, on top of
content and licensing that are already clear for the customer.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your company — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your details machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [clear service areas and
listings](/learn/local-aeo-for-moving-companies), [genuine
reviews](/learn/moving-company-reviews-ai-recommendations), and [pages that answer customer
questions](/learn/moving-company-service-pages-for-ai). Schema can't rescue a vague service area or a thin
site — and faking reviews in markup is a misuse engines can [detect](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations).
Accurate schema on top of a readable, answer-first site is the combination that works.
