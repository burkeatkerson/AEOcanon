## Why is events and community AEO the defensible advantage?

Because it's the one thing a marketplace can't replicate. Amazon can ship a box cheaper and faster, but
it can't run a Friday Night Magic, teach a model-painting class, or build a scene where everyone knows
your staff. When a hobbyist asks the assistant "where can I play Warhammer near me" or "game store with a
beginner D&D group," the store whose events are [readable and
cited](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations) wins a regular — not a one-off sale. This is the
[Originality pillar](/pillars/originality) in its purest form: a real local community no algorithm can
copy, surfaced where the engine and the customer can read it.

## How do I win event and community citations?

Give your events their own answer-first pages — not a Facebook-only calendar. Publish your game nights,
leagues, tournaments, and classes in readable text on your own site, with days, times, formats, skill
levels, and how to join. Most stores hide all of this on social, so a clear, dedicated events page is an
easy way to get cited for the community searches that build regulars — the loyal base that compounds your
[growth](/learn/grow-a-hobby-shop-with-ai-search) far beyond any single sale and gives Amazon nothing to
compete with.
