**There is no magic review number.** AI recommendations turn on whether you have
*enough recent reviews to look credible next to local competitors*, a solid rating,
steady velocity, and visible responses — not a fixed count you unlock at 50 or 100.

## Is there a threshold I need to hit?

No. Anyone quoting an exact number is guessing — engines do not publish a review count,
and it varies by market and category. What an engine can actually assess is whether your
reviews make you a *safe, current recommendation* compared with the other businesses it
could name. In a quiet rural category, a handful of recent reviews may be plenty; in a
crowded metro, the bar is higher because your competitors set it.

## So what should I aim for instead?

Aim to look *actively and genuinely well-reviewed for your market*. Earn a steady flow
of real recent reviews on the platforms engines read, keep your rating honest and solid,
and respond to show you are engaged. That combination is what makes you a safer pick than
the competitor with a bigger but staler pile — which is the comparison the engine is
really making.
