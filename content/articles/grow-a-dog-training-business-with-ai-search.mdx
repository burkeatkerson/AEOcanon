## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each great client experience earns a review and a referral; [readable
programs](/learn/dog-training-service-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews earn citations for 'best dog
trainer near me'; those bring new clients; and clients who keep boarding and refer friends produce more
reviews — while the commissions you save fund better training and care. The Rover model is the
opposite: a faucet you pay to keep running, where the platform owns the client. AEO builds the
[compounding asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead — clients who found you directly and keep
coming back.

## Why is recurring revenue central to growth?

Because a one-time training graduate is worth far less than a client who boards every trip or brings
their dog to daycare weekly, and filling the top of the funnel only pays off if clients come back. A
puppy-class graduate who later boards and refers friends is worth many times one who never returns —
and those happy regulars are exactly who write the [reviews that mention trainers and
results](/learn/dog-training-reviews-ai-recommendations) and refer friends, feeding the AI
recommendations that bring the next clients. So growth isn't just AEO at the top; it's AEO plus repeat
revenue together, each compounding the other. Win clients with citations, keep them with a great
experience, and the flywheel accelerates.

## How does this lower my cost per client?

By replacing recurring commissions and ad spend with assets that keep paying. A Rover booking costs a
cut every time and the platform owns the relationship; an earned citation, a loyal boarding regular, a
referral, or a [board-and-train graduate](/learn/dog-boarding-and-daycare-aeo) costs mostly upfront and
pays for years. As your cited, referred, and repeat base grows, your cost per client falls and your
revenue stabilizes — and because AI names only a few places, a real, well-reviewed local business can
win recommendations a faceless aggregator listing [can't
replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
