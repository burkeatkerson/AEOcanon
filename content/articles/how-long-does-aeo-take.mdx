**AEO works on two timelines: extractability and access fixes can change how
engines parse and cite you within days to weeks, while the authority and freshness
that win competitive queries compound over months.** There's no overnight switch —
but the fastest levers are also the ones you control directly.

## What can I change quickly?

You can change extractability and access quickly — within a crawl cycle. Rewriting
a passage to lead with the answer, adding a specific statistic or source, and
ensuring AI crawlers can read your server-rendered HTML — basic [crawlability](/glossary/crawlability) — are all on-page changes you
control directly. Engines pick them up the next time they crawl, so these are the
days-to-weeks wins. They're also the [extractability](/pillars/extractability)
and access fundamentals every AEO program starts with.

## What takes longer?

Authority takes longer because it's earned off your own site, over time. The
strongest correlate of AI visibility is genuine brand mentions across the web, and
you can't toggle those on — they accumulate through digital PR, genuine
participation, and being worth mentioning. Winning competitive queries against
established sources is therefore a multi-month effort, the same horizon as building
[authority](/pillars/authority) in traditional SEO.

## Does freshness speed things up?

Yes — keeping content current can accelerate re-evaluation. Engines weight recency,
and [Seer found 65% of AI crawler visits target content under a year
old](https://www.seerinteractive.com/insights/study-ai-brand-visibility-and-content-recency).
Substantively updating an important page and showing a visible last-updated date
signals [content freshness](/glossary/content-freshness), which can help you get re-crawled and re-considered sooner,
especially on time-sensitive queries. That's the
[freshness pillar](/pillars/freshness) at work.

## Why isn't AEO instant?

AEO isn't instant because engines must re-crawl your changes, the dominant signal
(authority) is earned gradually, and citations themselves are volatile — see [citation volatility](/glossary/citation-volatility), where sources
shift month to month. So visibility tends to build and stabilize over time rather
than switch on. Set expectations accordingly: quick on-page wins first, then a
steady authority climb. The full framework is [The AEO Canon](/learn/aeo-canon).

## What's a realistic plan?

A realistic plan is: in the first few weeks, fix access and rewrite your top pages
answer-first with inline evidence; over the following months, build off-site
mentions and a freshness cadence. Measure citation share and AI referrals, not
just week-one movement. Start from [what is AEO](/learn/what-is-aeo).
