  - q: What is an AEO editorial workflow?
    a: A repeatable, staged process for producing content answer engines cite — brief, research and originality, draft, evidence and links, quality control, publish, then measure and refresh. Each stage has an owner and a gate, so a page can't advance until it meets the standard. The workflow exists to make citable quality repeatable instead of accidental, especially as volume grows.
  - q: What's the most important stage in the workflow?
    a: Quality control, because it's where the two decisive gates are enforced — originality (something only you can say) and verified evidence (every claim cited and checked). QC is run by an editor who didn't draft the page, who sends weak drafts back rather than polishing them through. Skipping or softening QC is how generic, unverified content slips out at scale.
  - q: Where does AI fit in the editorial workflow?
    a: In the research and draft stages as an accelerant — synthesizing sources, outlining, and producing a first draft to improve. **An AEO editorial workflow moves a real question through seven stages — brief,
research, draft, evidence, QC, publish, refresh — with a gate at each so quality
holds as you scale.** The two gates that matter most are originality and verified
evidence, enforced by an editor who isn't the drafter.

## What are the seven stages?

The workflow takes a real question and turns it into a citable, current page. Each
stage hands off only when its gate is met:

<Steps
  items={[
    { title: "1. Question & brief", body: "Source the real question; fill a brief that forces answer-first structure, required evidence, and an original angle. The brief is the contract for 'done'." },
    { title: "2. Research & originality", body: "Gather primary sources and decide the angle only you can provide — proprietary data, first-hand experience, a defended POV." },
    { title: "3. Draft (AI-assisted optional)", body: "Write answer-first under question-shaped headings, in ~120–180-word passages. AI may help; the draft is raw material, not a finished page." },
    { title: "4. Evidence & links", body: "Add inline statistics, quotations, and named sources, plus internal links and external primary sources." },
    { title: "5. Quality control", body: "An editor (not the drafter) runs the QC checklist — originality, verified facts, extractability — and rejects weak drafts." },
    { title: "6. Publish", body: "Named author with credentials, publish and last-updated dates, Article + FAQ schema, clean server-rendered HTML." },
    { title: "7. Measure & refresh", body: "Track citation share, schedule the next review by clock-speed, and feed gaps back to stage 1." },
  ]}
/>

## Why does QC sit with a separate editor?

QC sits with a separate editor because the person who drafted a page is the worst
judge of whether it's generic or its facts are checked — they're too close, and the
temptation is to polish a weak draft through rather than send it back. A separate
editor applies the two gates coldly: *is there an original angle, and is every claim
cited against a [primary source](/glossary/primary-source) and verified?* This is the single structural decision that keeps quality from
sliding as volume grows, and it's why QC enforces the [originality](/pillars/originality)
and [credibility](/pillars/credibility) pillars rather than leaving them to chance.
The full QC method is in [quality control for AI
content](/learn/quality-control-ai-content).

## How does the brief make QC faster?

The brief makes QC faster by defining "done" before anyone drafts. When the brief
specifies the target question, the required evidence, the original angle, and the
answer-first ([inverted pyramid](/glossary/inverted-pyramid)) structure up front, QC becomes verification against a known standard,
not a rescue mission. Writers know exactly what they're accountable for, drafts
arrive closer to citable, and the editor checks boxes instead of rewriting. The
briefing system and a downloadable template are in [how to brief writers for
AEO](/learn/brief-writers-for-aeo).
