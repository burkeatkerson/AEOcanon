## Why do comparison searches matter so much?

Because they're the decision, and the decision happens before the call. A homeowner rarely starts
ready to buy hardwood — they start asking *"is LVP or tile better for a kitchen?"* and *"what's the
most durable floor for dogs?"* The assistant answers by [naming a few
sources](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations), and the installer cited in that comparison is the
one who framed the choice and earned the trust. Own the comparison and you're not bidding on a
decided job — you helped make the decision, which is the [Originality](/pillars/originality) and
[Alignment](/pillars/alignment) edge a generic platform can't match.

## How do I structure a comparison page AI will cite?

Lead with the verdict, then the side-by-side. Open with the direct answer — *"For a kitchen with pets,
LVP usually wins on cost and waterproofing; choose hardwood for resale and tile for the hardest
wear"* — then break out cost, durability, moisture, pets, maintenance, and resale in
[question-shaped sections](/learn/question-shaped-headings) an engine can match and lift. Keep it
honest and even-handed; you supply and install all of them, so the trustworthy comparison wins the
homeowner whichever material they pick. Map each comparison to its own page and link them to your
[material service pages](/learn/flooring-contractor-service-pages-for-ai) and
[high-intent cost pages](/learn/high-intent-flooring-ai-search), and you own the whole decision
journey from "which floor" to "who installs it."
