## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each great visit earns a review and a regular; a [readable tap
list](/learn/brewery-menu-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews earn citations for 'best [style] near me';
those bring direct visitors; and the loyal following you build funds new releases and a better taproom —
which create more great visits. The app model is the opposite: rented attention that resets the moment
you stop feeding it. AEO builds the [compounding asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead — loyal
drinkers who come back and bring friends.

## What's the fastest place to start?

Fix the [readable tap list](/learn/brewery-menu-pages-for-ai) first — it's the highest-leverage gap —
then make hours and listings accurate so you win [near-me and 'open
now'](/learn/near-me-brewery-ai-search), and build a steady flow of [genuine
reviews](/learn/brewery-reviews-ai-recommendations). Those win citations within weeks. Then compound it:
turn every happy visitor into a review and a regular, and add [event and tour
pages](/learn/brewery-events-and-private-bookings-aeo) for high-value, plannable bookings. Small habits,
compounding into a base of loyal, direct demand.

## How does this lower my cost per visit?

By replacing rented platform attention with assets that keep paying. App and aggregator visibility
resets the moment you stop feeding it; an earned citation, a loyal regular, or a word-of-mouth referral
costs mostly upfront and brings drinkers for years. As your owned, direct demand grows, your cost per
visit falls and your margins improve — and because AI names only a few places, a real, well-reviewed
local taproom can win recommendations a faceless aggregator listing
[can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
