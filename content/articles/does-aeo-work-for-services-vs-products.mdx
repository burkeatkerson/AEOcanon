## What does product AEO emphasize?

Structured, comparable information. Clear specs, pricing,
[comparisons](/learn/how-to-structure-a-comparison-for-ai), genuine
[reviews](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations), and product schema let engines
include you when [synthesizing recommendations](/learn/how-ai-recommends-products).
Because AI shopping answers draw heavily on third-party reviews and [roundup
lists](/learn/how-to-get-cited-on-listicles), off-site product presence matters as much
as your own pages — getting [recommended by AI](/learn/get-products-recommended-by-ai)
is as much about corroboration as catalog.

## What does service AEO emphasize?

Expertise and trust. Service buyers ask how-and-why questions, so [demonstrating real
expertise](/learn/how-to-show-expertise-to-ai), answering those questions thoroughly,
showing [credentials and reviews](/learn/do-author-bios-help-aeo), and getting [local
and entity signals](/learn/local-aeo) right matter most. There's no spec sheet to lean
on, so [credibility](/pillars/credibility) and answer quality carry more of the weight
— which is why service AEO looks more like proving expertise than listing features.

## Are the fundamentals the same?

Yes — the difference is weighting, not rules. Crawlable pages,
[answer-first](/learn/answer-first-sentence) content, [authority](/pillars/authority),
credibility, and [freshness](/pillars/freshness) apply to both; products just lean
toward structured data and reviews while services lean toward expertise and local
signals. It's one [Canon](/learn/aeo-canon) foundation, emphasized differently for what
buyers in each case actually ask.
