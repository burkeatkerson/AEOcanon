## How is commercial roofing AEO different?

It's a B2B research problem, not an emergency one. Where residential is urgent and
emotional, commercial roofing involves facility managers, property managers, and building
owners who [research thoroughly before contacting
anyone](/learn/does-aeo-work-for-b2b) — comparing membrane systems, warranties, and
maintenance programs, often via AI. So the goal shifts from winning the storm-season call
to being the cited expert that shapes the consideration set well before a conversation.
The [Alignment pillar](/pillars/alignment) here means answering evaluation questions, not
emergency ones.

## What do commercial buyers ask AI?

Detailed, technical, evaluation-focused questions. Which membrane system (TPO, EPDM,
modified bitumen) fits their building, expected lifespan and warranty terms, repair vs
re-roof vs restoration, maintenance program scope, energy and code requirements, and your
experience with their building type. These are substantive questions that reward
[evidenced, expert content](/learn/how-to-show-expertise-to-ai) — exactly the depth a
generic directory can't provide and a real commercial roofer can. Map them like
[residential questions](/learn/roofing-questions-customers-ask-ai), tuned to the buyer.

## Does it need different content?

Yes — more depth and proof. Commercial decisions carry higher stakes and longer research,
so content should [demonstrate expertise](/learn/how-to-show-expertise-to-ai): system
knowledge, project examples, certifications, warranty detail, and clear scope, in
thorough [answer-first pages](/learn/roofing-service-pages-that-get-cited). The
fundamentals are identical to residential — crawlable, answer-first, trusted — but
commercial rewards [credibility](/pillars/credibility) and depth more heavily, because the
buyer is evaluating, not reacting. Building and maintaining that depth alongside your
residential pages is a sustained effort, and one the [done-for-you
program](/pricing) can run for you.
