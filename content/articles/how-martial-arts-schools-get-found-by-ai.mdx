real text, they answer the questions parents and students ask, and they're backed by accurate listings
and genuine reviews.** The AI names only a few schools, so the dojo that clears all three is recommended.

## What does an AI engine actually do to find you?

It retrieves, ranks, and names. When someone asks for a martial arts school, the engine pulls the dojos
it can read, judges which best fit the request and look well-reviewed, and [names just a
few](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Each step is a filter: if the crawler can't read your
programs and pricing, you're out before ranking; if your hours are wrong, you're dropped from near-me;
if your reviews are thin, a competitor gets named. Being found means clearing every filter — and for
dojos, the readable class schedule and visible tuition are often what's missing.

The same three an engine — and a parent — apply, in order.

## Why can't parents find my dojo?

Almost always one specific broken gate — and for dojos it's often the schedule and pricing. If they're
trapped in a [booking widget or image the crawler can't read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site),
the engine doesn't know what you offer or what it costs, so it can't recommend you for "affordable kids
karate near me" or "BJJ gym nearby." Other common breaks: inconsistent hours, or thin reviews. The fix
is diagnostic — find the *highest* broken gate and repair it first. For many dojos, getting programs and
pricing into plain text is the highest-leverage move.

## Does ranking in Google mean AI can find me?

Not on its own. Ranking and a map listing help, but AI citation also needs your [programs and pricing in
readable text](/learn/martial-arts-school-service-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews. A dojo can show on a
map yet be absent from "best martial arts school for my child" answers if the engine can't read the
schedule or the reviews are thin. Clear all three gates and you get [found in
both](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).
