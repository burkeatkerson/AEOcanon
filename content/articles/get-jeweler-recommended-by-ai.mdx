## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a store the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine can
read your collections and place you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as where to buy — and
that leans harder on whether you're a trusted expert and buyers love you. The engine recommends the store
it can [confidently confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, expert, and well-reviewed,
because when it recommends you for an engagement ring it's putting its credibility behind a high-stakes
purchase. Your job is to make that confirmation easy — readable collections, named services, and genuine
reviews.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). Wrong hours, an unreadable catalog, or a thin review profile introduces
doubt, and doubt sends a high-value buyer elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one jeweler over another?

Because it picks the one it's most confident will deliver. Faced with several options, the engine names
the store whose signals best match the request — does custom rings, in the right area — and whose reviews
most clearly say buyers love the work and trust the store with high-value purchases. The competitor with
an image-only catalog, wrong hours, or thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it loses. Winning is about
being the [easiest to verify as the real, expert, loved local choice](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend)
— which is exactly what this library builds, piece by piece.
