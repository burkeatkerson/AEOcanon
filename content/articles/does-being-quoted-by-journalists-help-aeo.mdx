**Yes — being quoted as an expert in trusted publications is one of the strongest
authority signals for AEO, because it's independent corroboration that you know your
topic, in exactly the sources engines lean on.** The quote builds your entity's
credibility whether or not it carries a link back to you.

## Why do expert quotes carry weight?

Because they're someone else vouching for you. When a respected publication chooses to
quote you on a subject, it's independent validation of your expertise — precisely the
[corroboration engines use](/learn/how-many-mentions-to-get-cited) to decide who's
authoritative. That's worth far more than self-description, and it lands in the
trusted sources engines lean on, making it a high-value
[authority](/pillars/authority) signal.

## Do I need the link?

No — the quote is enough. Because [brand mentions correlate with AI visibility more
than backlinks](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks), being named as an expert in a credible
outlet builds your standing whether or not the article links back. A link is a bonus,
not the point; the credible attribution does the work. This is the clearest case of
why [unlinked mentions matter](/learn/get-brand-mentions-without-links).

## How do I get quoted?

Be the source reporters reach for. Respond quickly to journalist queries with
specific, useful insight; develop a clear, [original point of
view](/learn/does-original-research-get-cited-more) on your topic; bring data others
lack; and build relationships with reporters who cover your space. Repeat quotes go to
people who are helpful, responsive, and make a story stronger — so become that person
rather than pitching for coverage, the same logic behind earning any genuine mention.
