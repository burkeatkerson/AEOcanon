## What does it take to be recommended, not just found?

To be a business the engine recognizes and trusts enough to vouch for. Being *found* means the engine can
read your packages and place you; being *recommended* means it actively names you as who to hire — and
that leans harder on whether clients love you. The engine recommends the DJ it can [confidently
confirm](/learn/how-ai-recognizes-entities) is real, capable, and well-reviewed, because when it
recommends you it's putting its credibility behind how the event will go. Your job is to make that
confirmation easy — readable packages, a clear service area, and genuine, loving reviews.

## How does the engine build that confidence?

From signals that all corroborate each other.

Each signal reinforces the others — and together they make you the [entity the engine
trusts](/learn/build-your-entity). An unclear service area or a thin review profile introduces doubt, and
doubt sends the inquiry elsewhere.

## Why does AI pick one DJ over another?

Because it picks the one it's most confident clients will love. Faced with several options, the engine
names the DJ whose signals best match the request — right event type, right area, capable at the
guest count and venue — and whose reviews most clearly say people love it. The competitor with hidden
pricing, an unclear service area, or thin reviews is the riskier vouch, so it loses. Winning is about being
the [easiest to verify as the loved local choice](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend) —
which is exactly what this library builds, piece by piece.
