**Guest posts can help AEO when they put genuine, on-topic mentions of you on credible
sites your audience and the engines already trust — but low-quality guest-post
networks do little and can signal manipulation.** The value is the corroboration and
exposure, not a link, so write where being seen actually builds authority.

## When does a guest post actually help?

When it's a real contribution on a site that matters. A genuine, useful guest piece on
a reputable, on-topic site builds exposure and [corroborates your
expertise](/learn/build-ai-authority-from-scratch) — the off-site
[authority](/pillars/authority) AEO rewards. The signal is being credibly associated
with your topic where engines and your audience already look, which is why placement
quality, not volume, decides the value.

## Is it about the link or the mention?

The mention and its context, far more than the link. Because [brand mentions correlate
with AI visibility more strongly than backlinks](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) — an [Ahrefs analysis of 75,000 brands](https://ahrefs.com/blog/ai-overview-brand-correlation/) put the correlations at ~0.664 versus ~0.218 — a
guest post's worth is the credible association it creates on a trusted site — whether
or not the link passes any value. Write for the audience and the corroboration, not to
plant a link, and you'll be optimizing for what engines actually weigh.

## Can it backfire?

Low-quality guest posting can. Spammy networks, irrelevant sites, and obvious link
schemes add no trust, may read as [manipulation](/learn/do-press-releases-help-aeo),
and waste effort better spent on genuine coverage. The simple test: would you be proud
of the placement even if it carried no SEO benefit? If yes, it's building authority;
if no, skip it.
