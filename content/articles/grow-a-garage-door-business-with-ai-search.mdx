## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each repair produces a review, a referral, and often an upsell to a new
door or opener; reviews and [answer-first content](/learn/garage-door-service-pages-for-ai) earn
citations and rankings; those bring direct inquiries; and the money saved on purchased leads funds
more content and more crews — which complete more jobs. The directory model is the opposite: a
faucet you pay to keep running, that stops the moment you stop. AEO builds the [compounding
asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead.

## How do upsells and referrals accelerate it?

They turn small jobs into bigger ones and one customer into many. A spring-repair visit is the
perfect moment to recommend a new quiet opener, a matched second spring, or a full door replacement
— turning a $200 job into a much larger one. And every visit is a chance for a [fresh
review](/learn/garage-door-reviews-ai-recommendations) and a referral to a neighbor. That steady
reputation feeds the signals AI uses to recommend you, while the upsells lift your average ticket.
Every [repair-vs-replace question](/learn/garage-door-questions-customers-ask-ai) you answer is a
chance to earn the bigger job honestly.

## Can I really lower my cost per job?

Yes — by replacing recurring fees with assets that keep paying. A purchased lead costs every time
and is [resold to your competitors](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands); an earned citation, ranking,
referral, or upsell costs mostly upfront and pays for years. As your owned pipeline grows, your cost
per job falls and your margins improve, because you're no longer renting every customer. And because
AI names only a few sources, a trusted, genuinely reviewed local company can win recommendations a
generic national directory [can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
