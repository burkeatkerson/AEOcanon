rescues a booking widget or wrong hours.

## What does dojo schema actually do?

It makes your martial arts details unambiguous to a machine. [LocalBusiness
schema](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/local-business), and the
SportsActivityLocation subtype, labels your name, address, hours, programs, and reviews so engines parse
them cleanly rather than guessing — reinforcing the [consistent identity](/learn/nap-consistency) local
recommendations depend on. It's the [structured data for AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) pattern
applied to martial arts: clarity for the parser, on top of programs and pricing that are already clear
and readable for the parent.

## What should I include?

The full, accurate picture of your dojo — matched to what's visible.

## Will schema get me cited on its own?

No — it's a clarity layer, not a citation lever. Schema makes your details machine-readable, which
*supports* recognition, but the citation still depends on [readable programs and
pricing](/learn/martial-arts-school-service-pages-for-ai) the crawler can parse, [accurate
hours](/learn/local-aeo-for-martial-arts-schools), and [genuine
reviews](/learn/martial-arts-school-reviews-ai-recommendations). Schema can't rescue a schedule trapped
in a booking widget or hours that are wrong — and faking reviews or details in markup is a misuse engines
can [detect](/learn/does-schema-help-ai-citations). Accurate schema on top of readable programs and
pricing is the combination that works.
