readable as real text, they answer the questions students ask, and they're backed by accurate listings
and genuine reviews.** The AI names only a few studios, so the one that clears all three is recommended.

## What does an AI engine actually do to find you?

It retrieves, ranks, and names. When someone asks for a yoga or Pilates studio, the engine pulls the
places it can read, judges which best fit the request and look well-reviewed, and [names just a
few](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Each step is a filter: if the crawler can't read your
schedule and pricing, you're out before ranking; if your hours are wrong, you're dropped from near-me;
if your reviews are thin, a competitor gets named. Being found means clearing every filter — and for
studios, the readable schedule and visible pricing are often what's missing.

The same three an engine — and a student — apply, in order.

## Why can't students find my studio?

Almost always one specific broken gate — and for studios it's often the schedule and pricing. If
they're trapped in a [booking widget or image the crawler can't
read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), the engine doesn't know what styles you teach or what it
costs, so it can't recommend you for "beginner yoga near me" or "affordable reformer Pilates nearby."
Other common breaks: inconsistent hours, or thin reviews. The fix is diagnostic — find the *highest*
broken gate and repair it first. For many studios, getting the schedule and pricing into plain text is
the highest-leverage move.

## Does ranking in Google mean AI can find me?

Not on its own. Ranking and a map listing help, but AI citation also needs your [class styles and
pricing in readable text](/learn/yoga-studio-service-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews. A studio can
show on a map yet be absent from "best yoga studio near me for beginners" answers if the engine can't
read the schedule or the reviews are thin. Clear all three gates and you get [found in
both](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).
