**A moving company needs a website rebuild for AEO when the site is thin or slow, hard for AI crawlers to
read, or without per-service answer-first pages and schema — because no amount of content fixes a
foundation engines can't parse.** The rebuild is the access layer every booking depends on.

<Callout variant="key" title="Quick answer">
  <p>
    You need a rebuild when the site is <strong>thin, slow, uncrawlable, or structureless</strong> — a
    one-pager, rendered only in the browser, no per-service pages, no pricing guidance or licensing,
    missing schema. Engines can't cite what they can't <strong>read and parse</strong>, so content
    layered on a broken foundation is wasted. <strong>Fix the access layer first</strong>.
  </p>
</Callout>

## Why is the site the binding constraint?

Because [access is the first gate](/pillars/access), and a gate you fail ends the contest before content
matters. If an AI crawler fetches your page and sees a thin one-pager or an empty shell — because the
content renders only in the browser — or the page is too slow, you're invisible no matter how good your
crews or your reviews are. Many moving-company sites are brochures that [give a bot almost
nothing](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site). That's not a content problem you can write your way out
of; it's a foundation problem.

## How do I tell if my site is hurting me?

Run two quick tests, and look for the structural gaps.

<Steps
  items={[
    { title: "The JavaScript-off test", body: "Load a key page with JavaScript disabled. If the content vanishes, AI crawlers likely see the same empty page — a fatal access problem." },
    { title: "The speed test", body: "Check your load time. Slow pages get crawled less and trusted less; speed is part of whether you're readable at all." },
    { title: "The structure test", body: "Do you have a dedicated page per service (local, long-distance, packing, storage) with scope and service areas, or one thin page? No per-service pages means nothing focused to cite." },
    { title: "The schema test", body: "Is there accurate MovingCompany/LocalBusiness structured data, or none? Missing schema leaves the engine guessing." },
  ]}
/>

If a page is empty without scripts, thin, slow, has no [dedicated service
pages](/learn/moving-company-service-pages-for-ai), or lacks [clean schema](/learn/moving-company-schema-markup),
the site is working against you. A fast, server-rendered foundation with real content is what makes
everything else possible.

## Can't I just add content instead?

Only if the foundation is already sound. Adding [answer-first
pages](/learn/answer-first-sentence) to a fast, crawlable site works beautifully — that's the whole
program. But adding content to a thin, slow, or client-rendered site is building on sand: the engine still
can't read or trust it, so the new pages never get cited. The honest sequence is foundation first, content
second. Get the access layer right — [server-rendered](/learn/server-side-vs-client-side-rendering),
fast, structured — and the content you publish on top finally has a chance to be found.
