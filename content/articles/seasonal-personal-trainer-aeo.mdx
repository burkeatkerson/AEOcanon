## Why does seasonality matter for personal trainers?

Because motivation — and hiring — surge in predictable waves, and the cited trainer catches them.
January brings the resolution wave, the biggest hiring window of the year; spring brings pre-summer
goals; the post-holiday reset brings people determined to change. The trainer whose answer is already
published, crawled, and trusted [gets cited the moment the wave
hits](/learn/does-seasonal-content-work-for-aeo) — while competitors are still updating their site. It's
the [Freshness pillar](/pillars/freshness) turned into a motivation calendar, and the difference between
a fully booked January and a quiet one.

## How do I prepare for each wave?

Build the content while it's calm, so it's ready when motivation spikes.

## Should I make a new 'New Year' page each year?

No — update one durable evergreen page. A persistent 'weight-loss training' or 'start your fitness
journey' page [accumulates authority](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) while you refresh it each year, which
beats spinning up a throwaway 'New Year 2026' page that starts from zero and splits your signals. Keep
one strong page that answers the weight-loss and beginner questions, sharpen it before each wave, and
link it to your [services and pricing pages](/learn/personal-trainer-service-pages-for-ai). One durable
page, refreshed and ready, beats scrambling when the resolution wave hits.
