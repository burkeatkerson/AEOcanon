## What does an AI engine actually do to find you?

It retrieves, ranks, and names. When a homeowner asks for a painter or about a painting project, the
engine pulls the contractors it can read, judges which best answer the question and look trustworthy,
and [names just a few](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations). Each step is a filter: if a crawler can't
fetch your page, you're out before ranking; if your page doesn't answer the cost or timeline question,
you're out at ranking; if your reviews are thin, a competitor gets named. Being found means clearing
every filter.

## What are the three gates?

The same three an engine — and a homeowner — apply, in order.

## Why can't customers find my painting business?

Almost always one specific broken gate — and for painters it's often the gallery-only site. If your site
is mostly before-and-after photos with little [readable text the crawler can
parse](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), the engine doesn't know what you offer or what it costs,
so it can't recommend you for "interior painters near me" or "cabinet painting in [town]." Other common
breaks: inconsistent listings, or thin reviews. The fix is diagnostic — find the *highest* broken gate
and repair it first. For most painters, putting services and pricing in real text is the highest-leverage
move.

## Does ranking in Google mean AI can find me?

Not on its own. Ranking proves Google can read and rank you — a good sign for the first gate — but AI
citation also needs an [extractable, answer-first page](/learn/answer-first-sentence) and genuine
reviews. A painter can rank yet be absent from AI answers if the answer is buried or reviews are thin.
The overlap is real but not total: clear all three gates and you get [found in
both](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together).
