## Why does a niche win in AI search?

Because specificity beats generality, and AI names the firm that best fits the exact question. When an
employer asks "staffing agency that understands warehouse hiring" or "recruiter for medical billing
roles," the engine looks for a firm that clearly handles that industry's particulars — and a generalist
page that says "we staff all industries" or a faceless job board matches nothing in particular. A focused
niche is the [Originality](/pillars/originality) edge a small agency can own, and exactly the kind of
specific, expert content [citations spread thin](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands) reward — the edge a
generic aggregator can't copy.

## What questions do niche staffing clients ask?

Role- and industry-specific ones — each worth a dedicated, expert page.

These reward [demonstrated expertise](/learn/how-to-show-expertise-to-ai) — the kind a generalist or a
job board can't fake.

## How do I win niche citations?

Give each industry or role you specialize in its own answer-first page covering its specific hiring, pay,
and compliance picture — not a buried line on a generalist page. Show you understand the role, the pay
rates, the certifications, reinforced by [reviews from that
industry](/learn/staffing-agency-reviews-ai-recommendations) and a clear inquiry path for both employers
and candidates. A dedicated 'staffing agency for warehouse roles' page gets cited for exactly that query,
signals real expertise, and [helps you grow](/learn/grow-a-staffing-business-with-ai-search) — because
specialists out-cite generalists and command higher fees.
