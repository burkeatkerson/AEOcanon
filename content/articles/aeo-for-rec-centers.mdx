## Why does AEO matter so much for climbing gyms and rec centers?

Because choosing where to climb or take the family is a local, high-consideration decision, and the
answer is the new front door. When someone wants to climb, swim, or book a party, they ask an
assistant and act on what it names — and the [AI answer names only two or three
places](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations), not a page of listings. Pew Research [found people
clicked a link just 8% of the time when an AI summary appeared, versus 15%
without](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/),
so the answer shapes the shortlist. Being the cited gym is the new equivalent of being the place
everyone recommends — and each member or party is revenue, not a one-time sale.

## How do climbing gyms and rec centers get recommended by AI?

By clearing the three gates a visitor — and an engine — judge you on, in order.

<Steps
  items={[
    { title: "Be readable (Access)", body: "Your passes, programs, schedule, pricing, and location as real text AI crawlers can fetch — not trapped in a booking widget, a PDF, or an image. Many gym sites hide day-pass prices and the class schedule where bots can't read them." },
    { title: "Answer the real question (Alignment + Extractability)", body: "Plain-text answers to 'how much is a day pass', 'do you have bouldering', 'is there a beginner class', 'kids climbing', 'do you have a pool', 'can I book a party', and 'where are you'." },
    { title: "Be trusted (Authority + Credibility)", body: "Consistent name, address, and phone everywhere; a complete Google Business Profile; and genuine, recent reviews that mention the walls, staff, classes, and kids programs. This is what decides who gets named." },
  ]}
/>

Most gyms fail gate one without knowing it — the day-pass price and schedule live in a booking widget
the engine can't read. Fixing that, plus accurate listings and reviews, is the highest-leverage work,
and it's the same foundation that [ranks you in Google](/learn/how-aeo-and-seo-work-together) too.

## Isn't this just SEO?

It's mostly the local SEO you should already be doing, pointed at a new surface. The fast, crawlable,
well-reviewed gym with readable passes and pricing that ranks is the same one that gets
[cited](/learn/is-aeo-replacing-seo). AEO adds putting your schedule, programs, and answers in [plain
text](/learn/answer-first-sentence), [structured data](/learn/rec-center-schema-markup), and the
[review consistency](/learn/rec-center-reviews-ai-recommendations) answer engines lean on. You don't
choose between them — you run one program that wins both.

## Where do independent climbing gyms have the advantage?

In specificity and community. AI names only a few places and [citations spread
thin](/learn/is-aeo-only-for-big-brands), so an independent with readable passes, clear pricing, and
genuine reviews naming the walls, setters, and kids programs can win the recommendation over a faceless
chain listing. That's the [Originality](/pillars/originality) and [local
authority](/learn/local-aeo-for-rec-centers) edge a chain can't copy — your community and your walls
are exactly what a visitor is looking for.
