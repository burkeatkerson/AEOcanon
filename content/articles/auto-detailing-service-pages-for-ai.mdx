every time.

## Why one page per package?

Because a citation is awarded to the page that best answers one specific question — and a catch-all
services page answers none of them well. When you give the full detail, interior, ceramic coating, paint
correction, and mobile service each [their own page](/learn/should-content-sections-stand-alone), each
can go deep on its own cost, scope, and details — and each becomes citable for its own query. A single
page trying to cover everything is shallow on all of them, so the engine cites a competitor with a
dedicated, focused page.

## What should each page lead with?

The answer the owner came for, before anything about you. Compare these two openings for the same page:

<BeforeAfter
  label="A detailing package page's opening"
  before="Welcome to Apex Detailing! We're passionate about cars and have been serving the community for over a decade with a love for perfection..."
  after="A full detail at Apex runs $180–$280 depending on vehicle size and condition, takes 3–5 hours, and includes a deep interior clean, hand wash, clay bar, and wax. We also offer mobile service across [city]."
  beforeLabel="Weak (buries the answer)"
  afterLabel="Strong (answer-first, citable)"
  note="The engine can lift the second opening directly into 'how much is a full detail near me' — the first gives it nothing to quote."
/>

Then add the detail, the process, and the proof below. This is [answer-first
writing](/learn/answer-first-sentence) applied to the trade.

## What makes a package page extractable?

Plain language and a clean structure. Write the way an owner asks — "a full detail typically runs …" —
not in jargon, and use [question-shaped headings](/learn/question-shaped-headings) the engine can match
to a query. Keep each answer in a self-contained passage so it can be [lifted without the surrounding
page](/learn/nine-properties-citable-passage), reinforce it with [AutomotiveBusiness
schema](/learn/auto-detailing-schema-markup), and make sure the page is fast and crawlable. Answer-first,
focused, and proven — that's the page an engine cites and an owner books.
