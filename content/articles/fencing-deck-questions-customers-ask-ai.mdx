## How do I find the exact questions?

Listen where homeowners already ask. Mine your consultations and bids for the questions people actually
voice, check Reddit threads, home-improvement forums, and the People Also Ask box, and
[prompt the assistants directly](/learn/how-to-find-questions-ai-users-ask) on common projects to see
the follow-ups they surface. Capture the *natural wording* — "is composite decking worth the extra
money" beats "engineered decking cost-benefit analysis" — because engines match the homeowner's
phrasing, not your jargon. Then prioritize by intent and value.

## Should I answer material and trust questions?

Yes — they're how you earn the trust that wins the project. Answering "wood vs composite deck" or "how
do I avoid a bad deck builder" honestly makes you the source a homeowner remembers when they're ready
to build. Material content captures the homeowner weighing options; trust content wins the wary one
who's heard horror stories. Both build the credibility and visibility engines
[reward](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore) — the opposite of a thin photo-gallery page. Map
every bucket to a page and you've built the content plan that gets a fence and deck builder cited.
