**A tattoo studio needs a website rebuild for AEO when its portfolio lives in an image-only gallery or
Instagram embed, the site is slow, or content renders only in the browser — because the engine can only
recommend what it can read.** The rebuild puts your styles, artists, pricing, and hours in readable text
everything else depends on.

## Why is your portfolio the binding constraint?

Because [access is the first gate](/pillars/access), and for a tattoo studio the image-only gallery is
exactly where you fail it. Clients search for "fine-line tattoo artist near me" or "cover-up specialist
nearby," but if your work lives in [a wall of photos and an Instagram embed the crawler can't
read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), the engine doesn't know what styles you do, so it can't
recommend you. AI crawlers read text — a gorgeous gallery with no words is, to a bot, a blank page. Add a
slow build on top and even your basics are invisible. That's not a content problem you can write around;
it's a foundation problem.

## How do I tell if my site is hurting me?

Run two quick tests, and look for the structural gaps.

If your portfolio is image-only, your pricing is hidden, or the page is slow, the site is working
against you. A fast site with [readable styles and pricing](/learn/tattoo-studio-service-pages-for-ai)
and [clean schema](/learn/tattoo-studio-schema-markup) is what makes everything else possible.

## Can't I just keep my Instagram feed?

Keep the feed and gallery for fresh work — but you also need your styles, artists, and pricing in
readable HTML text on the page. Because the embed is usually invisible to crawlers, relying on it alone
hides exactly the styles clients search for. Add readable text alongside it, get the access layer right —
[server-rendered](/learn/server-side-vs-client-side-rendering), fast, with described styles — and the
rest of your tattoo studio AEO finally has something to build on.
