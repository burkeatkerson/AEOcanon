**This is a day-by-day, four-week plan to get your small business recognized and
recommended by AI — no jargon, no big budget.** Week 1 makes your site readable,
Week 2 writes answer-first pages, Week 3 builds off-site mentions, and Week 4
covers local details and measurement. Check off each day as you go.

## Week 1 — Make your site readable by AI

The goal this week is simple: make sure AI tools can find and read your site, and
figure out what to write. Nothing here needs a developer for most site builders.

<Checklist
  title="Week 1 — Readable foundations"
  items={[
    "Day 1 — Open yourdomain.com/robots.txt and confirm it isn't blocking AI tools (ask your site builder's support if unsure).",
    "Day 2 — Check your main pages show their text without anything fancy loading (turn off JavaScript in your browser and reload — your words should still be there).",
    "Day 3 — Compress oversized images and remove heavy add-ons so pages load fast.",
    "Day 4 — List the top 15–20 questions customers actually ask you (from calls, emails, and your inbox).",
    "Day 5 — Sort those questions by how close they are to buying; star the ones a ready-to-buy customer asks.",
    "Day 6 — Pick the 5 most valuable questions to answer first.",
    "Day 7 — Skim the AEO Foundations course intro so you know the why behind the what.",
  ]}
/>

The [robots.txt](/glossary/robots-txt) and rendering checks are the [access](/pillars/access) basics —
plain guide in [how to allow AI crawlers in
robots.txt](/learn/allow-ai-crawlers-robots-txt). See Google's own
[robots.txt documentation](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/robots/intro)
if you want the technical reference.

## Week 2 — Write pages that answer clearly

This week you turn those questions into pages AI can quote. The one rule: put the
answer in the first sentence. AI tools favor the top of the page —
[Profound found 44% of citations come from the first third](/learn/answer-first-sentence)
— so don't bury it.

<Checklist
  title="Week 2 — Answer-first pages"
  items={[
    "Day 8 — Write a plain, complete answer to question #1 in the first sentence, then explain below.",
    "Day 9 — Do the same for question #2. Use the customer's real wording as the heading (e.g. 'How much does X cost?').",
    "Day 10 — Answer question #3, and add one real fact or number to back it up.",
    "Day 11 — Answer question #4; keep each answer tight (a short paragraph, not an essay).",
    "Day 12 — Answer question #5.",
    "Day 13 — Re-read each answer alone — does it make sense on its own? Fix any that don't.",
    "Day 14 — Add a short FAQ of 4–5 more questions to your most important page.",
  ]}
/>

That's [writing content AI will quote](/learn/write-content-ai-will-quote), in five
days.

## Week 3 — Get mentioned and reviewed

Now build the off-site trust that makes AI recommend you. Being *mentioned* around
the web — earning a [branded mention](/glossary/branded-mention) — matters even more than links — [Ahrefs found mentions tracked AI visibility
far more strongly than backlinks](/pillars/authority). None of this costs money.

<Checklist
  title="Week 3 — Off-site trust"
  items={[
    "Day 15 — Claim and fully fill out your Google Business Profile.",
    "Day 16 — Claim your other key profiles (industry directories, Bing Places, relevant platforms).",
    "Day 17 — Ask 5 happy customers for an honest review, with a direct link to make it easy.",
    "Day 18 — Find the Reddit threads and forums where your topic comes up.",
    "Day 19 — Answer one or two of those threads genuinely and helpfully (no spam).",
    "Day 20 — Reach out to one local publication, blog, or partner for a mention or feature.",
    "Day 21 — Make sure your business is listed in 3–5 reputable directories for your area or industry.",
  ]}
/>

This is the [Authority](/pillars/authority) work; the [Off-Site
Playbooks](/authority) go deeper, and [Reddit
citations](/learn/reddit-ai-citations) explains why communities matter.

## Week 4 — Local details and measurement

Finish by tightening the details that help AI know exactly who and where you are,
then set up a simple way to check your progress.

<Checklist
  title="Week 4 — Local details + measure"
  items={[
    "Day 22 — Make your business name, address, and phone identical everywhere they appear online.",
    "Day 23 — Add your service area and hours clearly on your site and profiles.",
    "Day 24 — Write a clear 'about' description and use the same one across profiles.",
    "Day 25 — Ask 5 more customers for reviews; aim for a steady trickle, not a one-time burst.",
    "Day 26 — Pick 10 of your real questions to track, and ask ChatGPT and Google each one.",
    "Day 27 — Note whether you (or competitors) get mentioned, and where you're missing.",
    "Day 28 — Fix the easiest gap you found (usually a missing or buried answer).",
    "Day 29 — Set a monthly reminder to re-check those 10 questions.",
    "Day 30 — Review what moved, and plan next month's answers from the gaps.",
  ]}
/>

Keeping your details consistent is [NAP consistency](/glossary/nap-consistency); the
question-tracking habit is the simple version of [measuring your AI
visibility](/tools/measure-aeo).
