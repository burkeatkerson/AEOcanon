## Why are your services the binding constraint?

Because [access is the first gate](/pillars/access), and for a groomer the service menu and pricing are
where you most often fail it. Pet parents search for "de-shedding groomer near me" or "affordable dog
groomer," but if your services live inside a [booking widget the crawler can't
read](/learn/check-ai-crawlers-can-read-site), the engine doesn't know what you offer, so it can't
recommend you. Add a slow build or a homepage that's all gallery images, and even your basics are
invisible. That's not a content problem you can write around; it's a foundation problem.

## How do I tell if my site is hurting me?

Run two quick tests, and look for the structural gaps.

If your services are widget-trapped, your pricing is hidden, or the page is slow, the site is working
against you. A fast site with [readable services and pricing](/learn/pet-grooming-service-pages-for-ai)
and [clean schema](/learn/pet-grooming-schema-markup) is what makes everything else possible.

## Can't I just keep my booking widget?

Keep the widget for booking — but you also need your services, breed/size pricing, and hours in readable
HTML text on the page. Because the widget is usually invisible to crawlers, relying on it alone hides
exactly the services pet parents search for. Add readable text alongside it, get the access layer right
— [server-rendered](/learn/server-side-vs-client-side-rendering), fast, with readable services — and
the rest of your grooming AEO finally has something to build on.
