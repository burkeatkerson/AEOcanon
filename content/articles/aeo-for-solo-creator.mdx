## What's different about being a solo creator?

What's different is that you have one scarce resource — your time — and one
outsized asset: genuine, first-hand originality. You can't match a publisher's volume
or a brand's budget, but you also don't have to, because engines cite the best,
most distinctive answer to a question, not the biggest name. Citations are spread
thin enough ([Evertune found no domain over ~5%](/pillars/originality)) that a focused
creator can own specific questions a big publisher answers generically. So the whole
strategy is leverage: put your limited time into the original, answer-first work only
you can produce, and amplify it through free channels.
