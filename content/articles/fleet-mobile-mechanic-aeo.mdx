## How is fleet mobile-mechanic AEO different?

The intent is a researched B2B decision, not a one-off retail call. A fleet manager — running delivery vans,
a contractor's trucks, or a rideshare fleet — evaluates carefully and asks the assistant detailed questions
long before they call. They're researching on-site maintenance scope, which vehicle types you service, and
how you keep their vehicles running. Because [AEO works for B2B](/learn/does-aeo-work-for-b2b) exactly here,
the mechanic whose pages answer those questions clearly gets [cited during the
research](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations) — and wins a recurring account worth far more than a single
retail repair.

Operations-focused and substantive — each worth a clear page.

your come-to-them model is the whole selling point: their vehicles never leave the yard.

## How do I win fleet citations?

Give on-site fleet maintenance its own answer-first page — not a buried line on your homepage. Publish your
program scope, the vehicle types you cover, billing terms, and your downtime approach in readable text, with
a clear inquiry path. Most mobile mechanics omit this entirely, so a clear, dedicated page is an easy way to
get cited for valuable, recurring fleet accounts — the kind that anchor steady revenue and compound your
[growth](/learn/grow-a-mobile-mechanic-business-with-ai-search).
