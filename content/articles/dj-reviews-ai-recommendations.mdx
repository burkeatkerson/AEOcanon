## Why do reviews carry so much weight for DJs?

Because they're the off-site proof an engine uses to judge which businesses clients actually trust with a
high-stakes event — and hiring a DJ is a trust purchase. A couple hiring a wedding DJ is betting the
energy of one of the biggest days of their life on you, so an assistant deciding whom to recommend
[synthesizes sentiment](/learn/do-reviews-affect-ai-recommendations) from your Google and review-platform
reviews to gauge whether you deliver. For a DJ, that corroborated reputation is decisive — it's the
[Credibility pillar](/pillars/credibility) made visible, and the [off-site
mentions](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) that correlate with AI visibility more than backlinks do.

## Do reviews that name the event matter more?

Yes — event-level reviews are gold, because they speak the language of the booking decision.

A review naming the event, the venue, the music, or the MC work gives the engine detail to tie you
to "wedding DJ for a large reception" or "corporate event DJ who can MC" — the exact
[questions clients ask](/learn/dj-questions-customers-ask-ai). Invite happy clients to mention the
event and what they loved, and the reviews start matching you to the searches that book events.

## How many reviews — and how fresh?

Fresh beats big. There's no magic count — recency and a steady flow outweigh a stale total. A DJ with
current reviews that name recent events signals an active, trusted business far better than one coasting on
old ratings. Build a simple habit: invite happy clients to review after each event, and [respond to
them](/learn/local-reviews-and-ai-recommendations) — engagement signals an active DJ that cares,
which is exactly the business an engine wants to recommend.
