## Have backlinks lost their importance?

They've been demoted, not deleted. A link from a trusted, relevant site still signals
authority and aids discovery, but [Ahrefs found brand mentions correlate with AI
visibility far more strongly than backlinks (~0.664 vs
~0.218)](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) ([Ahrefs study of 75,000 brands](https://ahrefs.com/blog/ai-overview-brand-correlation/)). So the thing AEO actually rewards is the web
discussing you credibly — and a link is just one way that discussion shows up. Links
remain a useful signal inside a bigger [authority](/pillars/authority) picture.

## What matters more?

Mentions and corroboration. AI engines weigh how often and how consistently reputable
sources describe you — across articles, communities, and reference sources — whether
or not those mentions carry a link. Being a [recognized
entity](/learn/what-is-entity-aeo) that the web vouches for is a stronger driver of
citation than any link total, because it's what an engine uses to decide who to trust.

## So should I stop link building?

No — rebalance it. Keep the links that arrive with genuine coverage, but stop treating
link count as the objective. Redirect effort toward [earning real
mentions](/learn/get-brand-mentions-without-links) and being talked about credibly,
which builds the off-site authority AEO rewards and tends to produce links as a
byproduct. Pursue the coverage; let the links follow.
