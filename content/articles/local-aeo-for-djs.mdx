## What makes a DJ 'local' to an engine?

Confidence about where you serve, what you do, and that you cover the client's event. An answer engine
recommends a DJ for "near me" only when it can [place you with certainty](/learn/how-ai-chooses-local-business-to-recommend)
— right area, right event type, serves that venue. That certainty comes from signals that all agree: a
matching address, a stated service area, a complete profile, readable packages, and reviews. When the
signals agree, you're an easy recommendation; when your service area is unclear or your packages are
unreadable, the engine sends the client elsewhere.

This is the [near-me ranking](/learn/how-to-rank-in-ai-for-near-me-searches) discipline applied to
DJing — and an unclear service area is where most DJs quietly lose.

## Why does a clear service area matter so much?

Because a DJ travels to the client's venue, so 'do you cover my area' and 'is there a travel charge' are
among the first things a client — and the engine — checks. If your service area is missing or
inconsistent across listings, the engine can't confirm you cover the client's location, so it drops you
from the answer or sends an inquiry you can't fulfill. State your cities, venues, and travel radius in
plain text and schema, keep them identical everywhere, and you remove the most common reason a DJ
gets skipped on a near-me query. It's the same [NAP consistency](/learn/nap-consistency) discipline,
with the map attached.
