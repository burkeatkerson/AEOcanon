## What's the growth model?

A flywheel, not a faucet. Each great visit earns a review and a regular — your game nights and leagues
bring people back weekly; [readable brands](/learn/hobby-shop-product-pages-for-ai) and genuine reviews
earn citations for "best game store near me"; those bring new visits; and every new face is a chance to
join the community. Amazon's model is the opposite: a transaction that owns the customer and serves no
scene. AEO builds the [compounding asset](/learn/business-case-for-aeo) instead — loyal regulars who buy
from you because you're their store.

## What's the fastest place to start?

Fix the [readable brands and events](/learn/hobby-shop-product-pages-for-ai) first — it's the
highest-leverage gap — then make hours and listings accurate so you win [near-me and niche
searches](/learn/near-me-hobby-shop-ai-search), and build a steady flow of [genuine
reviews](/learn/hobby-shop-reviews-ai-recommendations). Those win citations within weeks. Then compound
it: turn every customer into a regular through your [events and
community](/learn/events-and-community-hobby-shop-aeo), and own the [seasonal gift
surges](/learn/seasonal-hobby-shop-aeo) when new players and hobbyists come looking.

## Why is losing sales to Amazon such a problem?

Because Amazon takes the transaction and owns the customer, and you can't out-price or out-ship it. But
here's the thing it can't touch: Amazon can't run a Friday Night Magic, teach a model-painting class, or
build a scene where everyone knows your staff. An earned AI citation brings a real person through your
door who can join that community and become a regular — recurring revenue and loyalty a marketplace can
never replicate. As your cited, local, regular base grows, your store gets stronger in exactly the place
Amazon is weakest, and because AI names only a few stores, a real, well-reviewed shop can win
recommendations a faceless marketplace [can't replicate](/learn/how-to-make-content-ai-cant-ignore).
