**Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) is the practice of structuring content so AI
answer engines can extract, cite, and surface it as a direct answer.** Where
classic SEO competes for a ranked position in a list of links, AEO competes to
_be the answer_ an engine synthesizes for the user.

## Why AEO matters now

Search is shifting from "ten blue links" to synthesized answers. When a user
asks Google, ChatGPT, or Perplexity a question, the engine increasingly returns
a single composed response — often without the user clicking through. To earn
visibility in that world, your content has to be easy for a model to read,
trust, and quote.

### What changed

Large language models read pages differently from classic crawlers. They reward
content that:

- States the answer **first**, then supports it.
- Uses clean, semantic HTML with descriptive headings.
- Carries explicit structured data describing what the page is.
- Demonstrates first-hand experience and credible authorship (E-E-A-T).

## The core principles of AEO

### Answer-first writing

Lead each section with a direct, self-contained answer. The first paragraph
under a heading should stand alone as a quotable response to the question that
heading implies.

### Structured data

Mark up articles, FAQs, and how-tos with schema.org types so engines can parse
the role of each passage. See our guide on
[structured data for AEO](/learn/structured-data-for-aeo) for the implementation
details.

### Authority and trust

Answer engines weigh credibility heavily. Clear authorship, citations, and
demonstrable expertise make your content safer for a model to surface.

## How AEO and SEO work together

AEO does not replace SEO — it builds on it. The same clean semantics and
authority signals that help a model quote you also help a search engine rank
you. The practical move is to write once, optimize for both, and let structured
data do the translation.
