"Who's a good wedding DJ near me?" used to mean scrolling a gig platform and waiting for five DJs to message back.
Now it means asking an assistant — *"best wedding DJ near me"*, *"DJ and photo booth package for 150 guests"*,
*"event DJ who can MC and do uplighting"* — and the answer names two or three businesses. The couple
picks one and inquires. For most DJs, that answer leans on lead platforms like The Knot, GigSalon, and
Thumbtack that charge for a lead and resell the same one to your competitors. **This library is about
flipping that: becoming the DJ AI recommends directly, so you book events you own instead of
renting inquiries from a platform.**

## Why AEO is the highest-leverage move for a DJ or event service

Because event bookings are high-value, planned, and made on the first good answer — and the answer
is the new front door. When someone asks who can DJ their event, they act on what the assistant
names; the [AI answer names only two or three businesses](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations), not
a page of listings. Pew Research
[found people clicked a link just 8% of the time when an AI summary appeared, versus 15%
without](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/).
For a DJ, being the *cited* business is the modern version of being the name every venue coordinator passes
along — and it sends an inquiry that books directly, not one a platform resold three times over.

The lead platforms won this spot by accident — they're big, crawlable, and mentioned everywhere. The
good news: the signals they win on are *earnable* by a real DJ that treats its own site, packages,
and reviews as the answer. That's the whole point of the [Authority](/pillars/authority) and
[Extractability](/pillars/extractability) pillars — and unlike a resold lead, a citation you earn keeps
sending direct inquiries.

## What actually decides who AI recommends?

Three things, in order — and they map onto exactly how a couple (and an answer engine) decides who to
hire.

That third gate is where most DJs quietly lose. Ahrefs'
[study of 75,000 brands found off-site brand mentions correlate with AI visibility far more than
backlinks (0.664 vs 0.218)](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) — and for a DJ, those mentions are
reviews, venue and planner recommendations, and clients naming the event you nailed. Earn them and you
become the cited business; skip them and the lead platform keeps charging you for the inquiry.
