## Why AEO is the highest-leverage move for a tire shop

Because tire needs are urgent, local, and decided on the first credible answer — and the answer is the
new front door. When a tire blows or wears out, the driver asks an assistant and acts immediately; the
[AI answer names only two or three shops](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations), not a page of
listings. Pew Research
[found people clicked a link just 8% of the time when an AI summary appeared, versus 15%
without](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/).
Being the *cited* shop is the modern version of being the tire place the neighborhood trusts — and a new
set of tires is the start of years of rotations, alignments, and repeat business.

The chains won this spot by accident — they're big, crawlable, and mentioned everywhere. The good news:
the signals they win on are *earnable* by a real local shop that treats its own site and reviews as the
answer. That's the whole point of the [Authority](/pillars/authority) and
[Extractability](/pillars/extractability) pillars — and unlike a purchased lead, a citation you earn
keeps sending customers.

## What actually decides who AI recommends?

Three things, in order — and they map onto exactly how a driver (and an answer engine) decides where to
buy tires.

That third gate is where most shops quietly lose. Ahrefs'
[study of 75,000 brands found off-site brand mentions correlate with AI visibility far more than
backlinks (0.664 vs 0.218)](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) — and for a tire shop, those mentions are
reviews, local press, and drivers naming your shop. Earn them and you become the cited shop; skip them
and the chain keeps the spot.
