"Where should we grab a beer tonight?" used to mean scrolling a check-in app. Now it means asking an
assistant — *"best brewery near me"*, *"taproom with food open now"*, *"dog-friendly brewery with a
patio"* — and the answer names two or three places. The drinker picks one and goes. For most
breweries, that answer leans on aggregators and untappd-style apps that own your audience and bury your
own taproom. **This library is about flipping that: becoming the brewery AI recommends directly, so you
fill the taproom, build a following of regulars, and book high-value events you own — instead of
renting attention from a platform.**

## Why AEO is the highest-leverage move for a brewery

Because where-to-drink decisions are fast, local, and made on the first good answer — and the answer is
the new front door. When someone asks where to grab a beer, they act on what the assistant names; the
[AI answer names only two or three places](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations), not a page of
listings. Pew Research
[found people clicked a link just 8% of the time when an AI summary appeared, versus 15%
without](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/).
For a brewery, being the *cited* place is the modern version of being the taproom everyone tells their
friends about — and it sends a visitor who walks in tonight, not one a platform keeps for itself.

The aggregators and beer apps won this spot by accident — they're big, crawlable, and mentioned
everywhere. The good news: the signals they win on are *earnable* by a real brewery that treats its own
site, tap list, and reviews as the answer. That's the whole point of the [Authority](/pillars/authority)
and [Extractability](/pillars/extractability) pillars — and unlike a rented lead, a citation you earn
keeps filling the taproom.

## What actually decides who AI recommends?

Three things, in order — and they map onto exactly how a visitor (and an answer engine) decides where to
drink.

That third gate is where most breweries quietly lose. Ahrefs'
[study of 75,000 brands found off-site brand mentions correlate with AI visibility far more than
backlinks (0.664 vs 0.218)](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) — and for a brewery, those mentions are
reviews, local beer press and blogs, and drinkers naming your IPA. Earn them and you become the cited
taproom; skip them and the app keeps the audience it built on your beer.
