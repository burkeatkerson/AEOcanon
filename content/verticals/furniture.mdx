Someone furnishing a room — a new sofa, a mattress, a dining set for a first home — doesn't page
through a directory anymore. They ask an assistant *"furniture store near me"*, *"where to buy a
mid-century sofa in [city]"*, *"best place for a quality mattress nearby"* — and the answer names two
or three stores. They visit the first one they trust. For most independent furniture retailers, that
answer leans on Wayfair, IKEA, and Amazon — the giants that win on scale, not on the design help,
custom work, and local delivery you actually offer. **This library is about flipping that: becoming
the furniture store AI recommends directly, so you keep the high-ticket sale instead of watching it
ship from a warehouse two states away.**

## Why AEO is the highest-leverage move for a furniture store

Because furniture-buying is high-consideration, high-ticket, and often local — decided by the first
store the shopper trusts enough to visit or order from. When someone needs a sofa or a bedroom set,
they ask an assistant and shortlist what it names; the [AI answer names only two or three
stores](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations), not a page of listings. Pew Research [found people
clicked a link just 8% of the time when an AI summary appeared, versus 15%
without](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/).
Being the *cited* store is the modern version of being the showroom the neighborhood recommends — and
a direct, full-margin sale beats a thin sale you lose to a marketplace.

The big boxes won this spot by accident — they're huge, crawlable, and mentioned everywhere. The good
news: the signals they win on are *earnable* by a real local store that treats its own site and reviews
as the answer. That's the whole point of the [Authority](/pillars/authority) and
[Extractability](/pillars/extractability) pillars — and unlike a thin marketplace order, a citation you
earn keeps sending full-margin, high-ticket sales and the design and delivery work that comes with them.

## What actually decides who AI recommends?

Three things, in order — and they map onto exactly how someone (and an answer engine) chooses where to
buy a sofa.

That third gate is where most stores quietly lose. Ahrefs'
[study of 75,000 brands found off-site brand mentions correlate with AI visibility far more than
backlinks (0.664 vs 0.218)](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) — and for a furniture store, those mentions
are reviews, local design press, home-tour features, and customers naming your showroom. Earn them and
you become the cited store; skip them and the marketplace keeps the sale it's commoditizing.
