## Why AEO is the highest-leverage move for a towing company

Because roadside trouble is the most urgent, act-on-the-first-answer search there is — and the answer is
the new front door. When a driver is stranded, they ask an assistant and call immediately; the [AI answer
names only one or two companies](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations), not a page of listings. Pew
Research
[found people clicked a link just 8% of the time when an AI summary appeared, versus 15%
without](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/).
Being the *cited* company is the modern version of being the tow truck everyone knows to call — and a
direct call pays full price, unlike a motor-club dispatch.

The directories and club apps won this spot by accident — they're big, crawlable, and mentioned

## What actually decides who AI recommends?

Three things, in order — and they map onto exactly how a stranded driver (and an answer engine) decides
who to call.

That third gate is where most companies quietly lose. Ahrefs'
[study of 75,000 brands found off-site brand mentions correlate with AI visibility far more than
backlinks (0.664 vs 0.218)](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) — and for a tow company, those mentions are
reviews, local press, and drivers naming you after a rescue. Earn them and you become the cited company;
skip them and the club app keeps the call it pays you scraps for.
