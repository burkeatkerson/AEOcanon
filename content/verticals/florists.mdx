Someone who needs flowers — for an anniversary, a sympathy arrangement, a last-minute apology —
doesn't scroll a directory anymore. They ask an assistant *"florist near me for same-day
delivery"*, *"where can I get funeral flowers nearby"*, *"best florist for a wedding in [city]"* —
and the answer names two or three shops. They order from the first one. For most florists, that
answer leans on the big wire services and order-gatherers that take a huge cut of every order and
often aren't even local. **This library is about flipping that: becoming the florist AI recommends
directly, so you keep the whole order instead of renting it from a middleman.**

## Why AEO is the highest-leverage move for a florist

Because flower-buying is local, occasion-driven, and often urgent — decided on the first good answer.
When someone needs an arrangement, they ask an assistant and order from what it names; the [AI answer
names only two or three shops](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations), not a page of listings. Pew
Research [found people clicked a link just 8% of the time when an AI summary appeared, versus 15%
without](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/).
Being the *cited* florist is the modern version of being the shop the neighborhood recommends — and a
direct order keeps the full margin a wire service would have taken.

The wire services won this spot by accident — they're big, crawlable, and mentioned everywhere. The
good news: the signals they win on are *earnable* by a real local shop that treats its own site and
reviews as the answer. That's the whole point of the [Authority](/pillars/authority) and
[Extractability](/pillars/extractability) pillars — and unlike a commissioned order, a citation you
earn keeps sending full-margin sales.

## What actually decides who AI recommends?

Three things, in order — and they map onto exactly how someone (and an answer engine) chooses where to
buy flowers.

That third gate is where most florists quietly lose. Ahrefs'
[study of 75,000 brands found off-site brand mentions correlate with AI visibility far more than
backlinks (0.664 vs 0.218)](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) — and for a florist, those mentions are
reviews, local press, wedding features, and customers naming your shop. Earn them and you become the
cited shop; skip them and the wire service keeps the order it's skimming.
