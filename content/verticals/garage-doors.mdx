## Why AEO is the highest-leverage move for a garage door company

Because garage door problems are urgent, safety-sensitive, and decided on the first credible
answer — and the answer is the new front door. When a spring snaps or a car's trapped, the
homeowner asks an assistant and acts immediately; the
[AI answer names only two or three sources](/learn/how-ai-engines-choose-citations), not a page
of links. Pew Research
[found people clicked a link just 8% of the time when an AI summary appeared, versus 15%
without](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/).
For a trade where a broken spring is dangerous and a new door is a major purchase, being the
*cited* company is the modern version of being the name the neighborhood trusts.

The directories won this spot by accident — they're big, crawlable, and mentioned everywhere. The

## What actually decides who AI recommends?

Three things, in order — and they map onto exactly how a homeowner (and an answer engine) sizes up
a garage door company before trusting them with an urgent, sometimes dangerous repair.

That third gate is where most companies quietly lose. Ahrefs'
[study of 75,000 brands found off-site brand mentions correlate with AI visibility far more than
backlinks (0.664 vs 0.218)](/learn/mentions-vs-backlinks) — and for a garage door company, those
mentions are reviews, manufacturer dealer listings, local press, and community references. Earn
them and you become the trusted name; skip them and the directory keeps the spot it's reselling to
you.
