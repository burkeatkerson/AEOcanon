marketing teams and agencies, and Profound is the enterprise platform — and
because all three sample daily across overlapping engines, the decision is scale
and budget rather than accuracy.** These three are the most-compared
[AI visibility](/glossary/share-of-voice) trackers in the category, and they line
up almost perfectly as a price-and-scale ladder.

[Profound vs Peec AI](/tools/profound-vs-peec-ai) and
[Otterly vs Peec AI](/tools/otterly-vs-peec-ai) — with Peec sitting in the middle
of both. This page puts all three side by side using only what those pages and the
individual reviews already establish, and says plainly where they are silent.

## Which engines does each one cover?

Otterly and Peec cover the same six engines; Profound is the only one of the three
that reaches beyond them. Our reviews record Otterly tracking Google AI Overviews,
ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Mode, Gemini, and Copilot, and Peec tracking
ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Copilot, Google AI Mode, and AI Overviews — the same
set, differently ordered. Profound covers 10+ engines: ChatGPT, Claude,
Perplexity, Gemini, Copilot, DeepSeek, Grok, Meta AI, and Google AI Overviews.

The practical consequence is narrow but sharp. If your audience uses
[Claude](/glossary/claude) or Grok and you need those tracked, Profound is the
only option among these three — our Peec review lists "no Claude/Grok" explicitly
as a limitation. If your audience lives on the mainstream engines, the coverage
question is settled and you should decide on price and team features instead.

## What is identical across all three?

More than the marketing suggests: all three run a fixed set of your prompts across
AI engines on a daily schedule, record whether your brand appears and which
sources the answer cites, and report the trend against competitors. That shared
core is the whole category. It is why our own roundup opens by saying the choice
is "scale, engines covered, and price" rather than capability — and why comparing
feature lists between these three is mostly a waste of an afternoon.

Three consequences follow from that sameness. First, none of them will make you
more visible; they tell you whether you are, which is a different and much smaller
claim than the category's marketing implies. Second, switching costs are lower
than they look — the asset you build is the prompt set, not the account, so a
carefully constructed list of 15–30 real customer questions migrates between tools
in an afternoon. Third, and least intuitively, the volatility of AI citations
means any of the three will show you noisy readings; what separates a useful
program from a dashboard habit is holding the prompt set still long enough for a
trend to emerge. Choose the tool that you will still be paying for in six months,
because six months is roughly when the data starts being worth anything.

## What does each tool do that the others do not?

Each has exactly one genuine differentiator, and none of the three overlaps with
another.

**Otterly's is the GEO audit.** Our review calls it the feature that most
distinguishes it from a plain tracker: a SWOT, a competitor set, and tactic gaps
that point you at what to fix rather than only what is wrong. It also ships AI
keyword research that turns your keywords, brand, or URL into the prompts people
actually use — useful precisely when you are starting from scratch and have no
prompt set. Full [Otterly review](/tools/otterly-review).

**Peec's is unlimited users.** Every plan includes them, which our review calls
unusual and genuinely useful, alongside multi-brand and sub-brand tracking,
centralized billing, and flexible prompt allocation across client projects. Its
mentions-versus-citations split is also described as clearer than some rivals' —
the distinction between the answer naming you and the answer merely using your
page as a source. Full [Peec AI review](/tools/peec-ai-review).

**Profound's is breadth plus execution.** Beyond the 10+ engines, its Answer
Engine Insights are captured directly from the browser, so what you see is close
to what a real user sees, and its **Agents** workflow generates and refreshes AEO
content — Content Refresh, FAQ generation, competitive research — and publishes to
a CMS. It is also the best-funded player, with a $96M Series C in early 2026 at a
~$1B valuation and enterprise customers including Ramp, DocuSign, and Figma. Full
[Profound review](/tools/profound-review).

## Where do our pages not have an answer?

Being honest about gaps matters more than filling them, so: several comparisons
people ask for are not settled by anything we have published.

We have no measured accuracy comparison between the three. All three sample
daily, and our reviews describe none of them as more reliable than the others — a
claim that one tool detects citations better than another would require testing we
have not run. We also do not state Peec's top-tier pricing, Profound's full
platform price (it is custom by design), or seat counts for Otterly's and
Profound's plans beyond "plan-dependent." And we have not published an
Otterly-versus-Profound head-to-head, because the two rarely land on the same
shortlist — they sit at opposite ends of the ladder. Where you need a number we do
not state, get it from the vendor.

## How should you trial them?

Trial them simultaneously on the same prompt set, for the same two weeks, and
judge them on whether the readings agree — not on which dashboard you find
prettier. This is the only comparison that produces information our reviews
cannot give you, because it is specific to your brand, your questions, and the
engines your buyers actually use.

Build the prompt set first, using
[the prompt-set guide](/tools/build-an-ai-prompt-set), and hold it fixed across
all three tools. Then check four things at the end of the trial. Do the tools
broadly agree on who gets cited for your top ten questions? Does each one let you
see the *sources* behind an answer, not just a mention count? Does the trend line
survive two weeks of noise, or does it swing wildly enough to be unusable? And
what does each plan actually cost at the prompt-and-engine volume you just proved
you need — which is almost never the entry tier. Our Otterly review's warning
about per-prompt scaling and our Peec review's note about prompts-times-models
pricing both land hardest at exactly this moment.

Pick the rung that matches your team today, and expect to move up it rather than
sideways.
