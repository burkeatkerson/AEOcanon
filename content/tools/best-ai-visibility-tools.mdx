## What does an AI visibility tool actually do?

An AI visibility tool measures how AI answer engines represent your brand. You
give it a set of prompts (the questions your customers ask), and it runs them on a
schedule across engines — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Gemini,
Copilot, and others — recording when your brand is **mentioned**, when your site
is **cited** as a source, how **competitors** appear, and which **sources** feed
each answer. In short, it's the measurement layer for
[answer engine optimization](/learn/what-is-aeo): the same role rank trackers play
for SEO. For the manual approach, see [how to measure your AI visibility](/measure-aeo).

## How to evaluate any AI visibility tool

Evaluate trackers on a fixed prompt set, because that's how you'll actually use
them — and because AI citations are volatile.
[Semrush found 40–60% of LLM-cited sources change month to month](https://www.semrush.com/blog/url-volatility-ai-overviews/),
so a one-time reading means little. During a trial, check: which engines it covers
(do they include the ones your audience uses?), how often it samples (daily beats
weekly for trends), whether it separates mentions from citations, how it handles
competitors and source analysis, and the real cost once you add the prompts and
engines you need. Per-prompt caps are where "cheap" plans get expensive.
