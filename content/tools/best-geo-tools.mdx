**The best GEO tools are not a single ranked list — they are five distinct
categories doing five different jobs, and the right answer depends on which job
you have not done yet.** Buying a visibility tracker before you have a prompt set,
or a content platform before you have confirmed AI crawlers can read your site, is
the most common and most expensive mistake in this category.

This page is a map, not a leaderboard. It breaks
[generative engine optimization](/glossary/generative-engine-optimization) software
into the functional categories that actually exist, explains what each one is for,
and places the tools we have reviewed into them. For a narrower, rank-ordered look
at just the measurement layer, see
[the best AI visibility tools](/tools/best-ai-visibility-tools).

## What is a GEO tool, and what is it not?

A GEO tool is any software that helps you get cited by AI answer engines — but no
single product covers the whole discipline, which is why the category is so
confusing to shop for. "GEO software," "AEO software," and "LLM SEO tools" are all
marketing labels for products that do one of five jobs, and vendors rarely say
which one. [GEO and AEO mean the same thing](/learn/what-is-geo); the acronym on
the pricing page tells you nothing about what the tool actually does.

What none of them do is win the citation for you. Every tool in every category
here either measures something, diagnoses something, or speeds up work you would
otherwise do by hand. The work itself — answer-first passages, real evidence,
earned authority — is [The AEO Canon](/learn/aeo-canon), and no purchase
substitutes for it.

## What are the five categories of GEO software?

The five categories are prompt-set monitoring, crawler and log analysis, AI
visibility tracking, content optimization, and SEO suites with AI modules — and
they run roughly in that order, because each depends on the one before it.

**Crawler and log analysis** answers whether AI bots can reach you at all. It is
the cheapest category and the one people skip. **Prompt-set monitoring** defines
the questions you will measure; without it, a tracker measures the wrong thing
precisely. **AI visibility tracking** is the measurement layer — the tools that
run your prompts across engines and log who gets cited. **Content optimization**
is the fixing layer. And **SEO suites with AI modules** are not a sixth job so
much as a delivery mechanism: the same tracking, folded into a platform you may
already pay for.

## What do the AI visibility trackers actually give you?

Every AI visibility tracker does the same core job — run your prompts on a
schedule, record which brands and sources each engine cites, and report the trend
— so the real differences are engine coverage, sampling cadence, team features,
and price.

Our reviews put the trade-offs like this.
[Otterly.AI](/tools/otterly-review) is the accessible on-ramp: six engines, daily
checks, and a GEO audit that turns findings into next steps, with the caveat that
its per-prompt pricing climbs steeply at volume.
[Peec AI](/tools/peec-ai-review) covers the same six engines daily but adds
unlimited users on every plan and multi-brand reporting, which is why it suits
teams and agencies. [Profound](/tools/profound-review) has the widest coverage we
have recorded — 10+ engines including Claude, Grok, DeepSeek, and Meta AI — plus
its Agents workflow that generates and refreshes content and publishes to a CMS.
[Scrunch AI](/tools/scrunch-ai-review) sits at mid-market-to-enterprise and
differentiates with an Agent Experience Platform that serves structured,
LLM-optimized content to AI crawlers.

For the pairwise decisions, see
[Profound vs Peec AI](/tools/profound-vs-peec-ai) and
[Otterly vs Peec AI](/tools/otterly-vs-peec-ai), or the full field in
[the best AI visibility tools](/tools/best-ai-visibility-tools). If you are
specifically shopping down from the enterprise tier,
[Profound alternatives](/tools/profound-alternatives) covers the trade you are
making.

## Where do the SEO suites fit?

If you already pay for a major SEO suite, its AI module is almost always the
cheapest sensible starting point — because the marginal cost is an add-on rather
than a new vendor, and your AI data sits beside the keyword and backlink data you
already trust.

[SE Ranking](/tools/se-ranking-ai-review) is the clearest example: existing
customers can add AI Search tracking for around $89/month, covering six platforms,
with the standalone SE Visible product from ~$189/month — though our review flags
that SE Visible refreshes weekly rather than daily.
[Semrush's AI Visibility Toolkit](/tools/semrush-ai-visibility-review) folds
share-of-voice, sentiment, prompt and competitor research, and an AI site audit
into Semrush One, with Enterprise AIO custom-quoted; reports there also update
weekly. [Ahrefs Brand Radar](/tools/ahrefs-brand-radar-review) is backed by a very
large prompt database and cross-references AI mentions with branded search, but
its AI indexes are sold per platform at ~$199/month each (~$699/month bundled),
and our review notes it does not track Claude or Grok.

The pattern across all three: convenience and consolidation in exchange for
slower refresh or per-module pricing. That is often the right trade — just make it
knowingly.

## What about content optimization tools?

Content optimization tools are the fixing layer — they help you write answer-first,
well-evidenced, extractable passages — but they are the category where software
helps least, because the substance is what earns the citation.

Two of the trackers above reach into this category rather than stopping at
measurement. Profound's **Agents** generates and refreshes AEO content and
publishes to a CMS; Scrunch's **Agent Experience Platform** serves structured
content to AI crawlers without changing the human-facing page. Otterly's **GEO
audit** — SWOT, competitor set, tactic gaps — is a lighter version of the same
idea: pointing you at what to fix rather than only what is wrong. Beyond that, see
[the best content optimization tools for AEO](/tools/best-content-optimization-tools)
and the free [AEO content brief template](/tools/aeo-content-brief-template).

## How should you evaluate any GEO tool?

Evaluate on your own fixed prompt set, during a trial, against the specific job
you identified above — never on a feature list, because feature lists across this
category are nearly identical.

Four questions settle most purchases. Does it cover the engines your audience
actually uses? How often does it sample — daily or weekly? Does it separate
**mentions** (the answer names you) from **citations** (your page is used as a
source), which our Peec review notes is clearer in some tools than others? And
what is the real cost once you add the prompts and engines you need, given that
almost every vendor prices by prompts × engines? That last one is where cheap
plans stop being cheap: 50 questions across five engines is 250 daily checks.

## The bottom line

Buy the category, not the brand. Finish the free access and prompt-set work first;
then, if you already pay for Ahrefs, Semrush, or SE Ranking, try their AI module
before adding a vendor; then choose a tracker sized to your team —
[Otterly](/tools/otterly-review) solo, [Peec AI](/tools/peec-ai-review) for teams
and agencies, [Profound](/tools/profound-review) or
[Scrunch](/tools/scrunch-ai-review) at enterprise scale. Every one of them only
measures. Winning the citation is still [The AEO Canon](/learn/aeo-canon).
