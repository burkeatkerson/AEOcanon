**Build a content brief that produces citable pages, then export it as clean
Markdown to hand to any writer.** A normal brief targets a keyword; an AEO brief
targets a *question* and engineers the page to be the [passage an engine
lifts](/glossary/passage-retrieval) — so the brief itself has to specify the
answer-first summary, the headings, and the evidence.

## Why each field matters

The fields aren't arbitrary — each maps to a pillar. The target question and
[question-shaped headings](/glossary/question-shaped-heading) are
[Alignment](/pillars/alignment): aim at what people actually ask. The
answer-first summary and self-contained sections are
[Extractability](/pillars/extractability): write the line you want quoted, and put
it first. The required-evidence field is [Credibility](/pillars/credibility): every
claim carries a stat, quote, or named [source](/glossary/primary-source). The author field is
[Authority](/pillars/authority). A brief that specifies all of these is the
difference between a generic draft and one an engine will cite.
