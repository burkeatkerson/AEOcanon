**Authority is the fourth pillar of the AEO Canon: AI trusts what the web already
trusts, and it weighs brand mentions more heavily than backlinks.** Once your
content is readable, aimed right, and liftable, Authority is what makes an engine
choose *you* over an equally-extractable competitor — and it's earned off your own
site.

## Why does AI trust what the web already trusts?

AI trusts what the web already trusts because it can't independently verify
expertise — so it relies on proxies, and the strongest proxy is how often credible
sources talk about you. An engine deciding whom to cite is, in effect, asking "who
does the web treat as authoritative here?" That makes Authority a reputation
problem, not a content problem: it's built in the places engines already read, not
on your own pages.

This is why two pages with equally extractable answers don't get cited equally.
The reranker weighs source authority, and the source the web vouches for wins. The
machine is listening to the room — your job is to give the room something true to
say about you.

## Do mentions really beat backlinks?

Yes — for AI visibility, brand mentions outweigh backlinks by more than three to
one. This is the most important and most counterintuitive finding in off-site AEO.

[Ahrefs' analysis of 75,000 brands](https://ahrefs.com/blog/ai-overview-brand-correlation/)
found **brand web mentions correlated with AI visibility at 0.664, versus 0.218
for backlinks** — and a follow-up put **YouTube mentions strongest at ~0.737**.
Crucially, **unlinked mentions count**: engines build their understanding of you
from how the web refers to you, not just from links pointing at you. That reframes
authority work from link-building to being genuinely talked about.

## Which platforms build AI authority?

The platforms that build AI authority are the ones engines cite most — and
**Reddit is the single most-cited domain** across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google
AI Overviews, with YouTube and LinkedIn close behind. Reputable publications,
review platforms, podcasts, and Wikipedia all contribute. The work is earning
genuine presence and mentions on those surfaces, which is detailed in the
[Off-Site & Authority Playbooks](/authority) — Reddit, YouTube, digital PR,
reviews, and more, each done honestly.

## How do you apply the Authority pillar?

Apply Authority by earning genuine brand mentions across the surfaces engines
trust — a digital-PR and participation discipline, not a link scheme.

## What are the most common Authority mistakes?

The most common Authority mistake is treating it like link-building — and the
most damaging is trying to fake it.
