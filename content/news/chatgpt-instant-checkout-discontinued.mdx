**OpenAI discontinued Instant Checkout in March 2026, months after its broader
February launch, because almost no one used it — fewer than 15 of Shopify's
millions of merchants ever went live.** In its place is a discovery-first model:
[ChatGPT](/glossary/chatgpt) surfaces product recommendations and routes shoppers
to merchant apps and storefronts. The durable 2026 rule is now clear —
discover in AI, buy on your own site.

## What exactly did OpenAI change?

OpenAI is retiring in-chat buying and leaning into recommendations. Instant
Checkout — first seen as single-item checkout in September 2025 and launched
more broadly on February 16, 2026 — was powered by the
[Agentic Commerce Protocol](/news/agentic-commerce-protocols-acp-ucp) (ACP),
co-developed by Stripe and OpenAI. Rather than complete the sale itself, ChatGPT
now behaves like a shopping guide: it names products and sends the shopper to
the merchant to finish the transaction.

## Why did buying inside ChatGPT struggle?

Because the checkout worked against how people actually buy. Walmart's data
showed that completing a purchase inside ChatGPT converted roughly **3x worse**
than a click-through to walmart.com — even though ChatGPT drove about **2x** the
new-customer rate of traditional search. In other words, AI was excellent at
introducing shoppers to products and poor at closing the sale in-thread. With
around 800–900M weekly active users and about 50M shopping-related queries a day,
the discovery value was real; the in-chat checkout was not.

## What should ecommerce brands do now?

Stop waiting for AI to become your checkout and start earning the recommendation.
The engine decides which products to name based on how clearly it can read your
catalog, so the work is structured, accurate product data and answer-first pages
that resolve buyer questions. Then make your own site the easiest place to
convert once the shopper arrives.
