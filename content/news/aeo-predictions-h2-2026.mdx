**My bet for the back half of 2026: answer engines get faster and pickier at
once. Cloudflare's move to block mixed-use AI crawlers by default reshapes who
gets cited, a wave of new models raises the bar on answer quality, and
"[discover in AI, buy on site](/glossary/agentic-search)" hardens into the
default commerce path.** None of this is reported news — it's where I think the
real 2026 trends are heading, and what I'd do about each now.

<Callout variant="key" title="The prediction">
  <p>
    By the end of 2026, being cited by an answer engine will depend less on
    ranking and more on three things: whether crawlers can (and are allowed to)
    reach you, whether your content is structured enough for a model to lift,
    and whether engines trust your <a href="/glossary/entity">entity</a>. The
    businesses that treat those as first-class work will pull ahead.

## What's driving this?

Four real trends converging. Cloudflare began pushing AI companies to pay for
publisher content and is moving toward blocking mixed-use AI crawlers by default
around September 15 — which quietly decides who models can even read. Model
releases keep accelerating (GPT-5.6 hit general availability July 9), so answer
quality and the cost of a weak answer both climb. Google pushed past a billion
AI Mode users and keeps steering people from AI Overviews into
[AI Mode](/glossary/google-ai-mode). And commerce is settling on a pattern where
people research in AI but check out on the retailer's own site.

## How should you prepare?

Pick the one that maps to your biggest risk and start there. If most of your
demand is transactional, fix product data and reviews before the holidays. If
you sell trust — services, B2B, local — invest in your
[entity](/learn/build-your-entity) and [authority](/pillars/authority). And
whatever you sell, don't let a crawler config quietly erase you from the answers.

For the fundamentals behind these bets, see [what is AEO](/learn/what-is-aeo) and
[how to track AI citations](/learn/how-to-track-ai-citations). For the trend that
started this, read [Cloudflare's pay-per-crawl move](/news/cloudflare-pay-per-crawl).
