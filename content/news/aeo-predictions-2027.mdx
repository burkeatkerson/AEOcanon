**By 2027, my bet is that AEO stops being a niche tactic and becomes how
marketing is measured. [Citation share](/glossary/share-of-voice) — how often an
answer engine names you — becomes a board-level number, your brand
[entity](/glossary/entity) becomes the durable moat, and agents, not just people,
become an audience you optimize for.** These are predictions grounded in the
2026 trends I'm watching: relentless zero-click growth, personalization inside AI
answers, and measurement tooling finally maturing.

## What's driving this?

Three forces already visible in 2026. First, the click keeps disappearing —
SparkToro found fewer than a third of Google searches still send a click, so
"traffic" is a weaker proxy for demand every quarter. Second, answers are getting
personal: Google's Personal Intelligence tailors responses to each user, which
means there's no single "ranking" to win — only patterns of being recommended.
Third, agentic commerce protocols like ACP and UCP are maturing, so software will
increasingly research and transact on a person's behalf.

## How does the practice change?

The center of gravity moves from pages to entities. When answers are
personalized and resolve on-platform, you can't optimize your way to a fixed
position — you earn a reputation the model carries into every session. That makes
[brand authority](/glossary/brand-authority) and a clean
[knowledge graph](/glossary/knowledge-graph) presence the highest-leverage work,
with answer-first content as the delivery mechanism.
