**At I/O 2026 Google expanded "Personal Intelligence" in
[AI Mode](/glossary/google-ai-mode) to nearly 200 countries and territories across
98 languages, with no subscription required — letting users securely connect apps
like Gmail and Google Photos (Calendar is coming) so answers draw on their own
context.** AI Mode runs on [Gemini](/glossary/gemini) 3.5 Flash by default. The
shift means the "right answer" is increasingly personal, not universal.

## What did Google announce?

Google widened Personal Intelligence, the part of AI Mode that pulls in a user's
own data to shape answers, from a limited rollout to nearly 200 countries and
territories in 98 languages — free, no subscription. Users opt in by securely
connecting apps such as Gmail and Google Photos, with Google Calendar on the way,
so AI Mode can reason over personal context alongside the open web.

## Why does this matter for AEO?

Because personalization dissolves the idea of a fixed #1 result. Two people asking
the same question can get different answers built partly from their own inbox and
photos, so optimizing for one ranking snapshot is a losing game. What persists is
whether Google trusts you enough to surface you as part of the answer — which comes
down to a strong, unambiguous [entity](/glossary/entity) and the
[authority](/pillars/authority) behind it.

## What should you do about it?

Build the entity, then measure like a statistician. Make sure Google can identify
who you are, what you do, and why you're credible — consistent naming, structured
data, and off-site corroboration. Because personalized answers vary, track your
[citation share](/glossary/share-of-voice) across a broad set of queries over time
rather than obsessing over any single result.
