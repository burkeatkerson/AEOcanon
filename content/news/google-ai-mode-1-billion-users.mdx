**Google used its I/O 2026 keynote to confirm what marketers have felt for a year:
AI Mode passed 1 billion monthly users, and Google is now routing searchers from
the shorter [AI Overview](/glossary/google-ai-overviews) into a fuller,
conversational [AI Mode](/glossary/google-ai-mode).** Overviews still reach ~2.5
billion, but the direction is clear — the answer, not the ten blue links, is
becoming the page.

## What did Google actually announce?

Google announced that AI Mode — its conversational, follow-up-friendly search —
crossed **1 billion monthly users** about a year after debut, and that it is
upgrading the experience with Gemini 3.5 Flash as the default model, a redesigned
AI-powered search box, and "Search agents" that can carry out multi-step tasks.
It also began nudging people from a static AI Overview into a back-and-forth AI
Mode session, keeping context as they go deeper.

## Why does this matter for AEO?

Because it accelerates the shift from *ranking* to *being cited*. A conversational
answer engine resolves more questions on the page and sends fewer clicks — so the
win is being the source the model pulls from and mentions by name. That rewards
the fundamentals the [AEO Canon](/learn/aeo-canon) is built on:
[answer-first passages](/pillars/extractability) a model can lift,
a [recognizable entity](/learn/build-your-entity), and the off-site
[authority](/pillars/authority) engines trust.

## What should you do about it?

Stop optimizing only for the blue-link position and start optimizing to be the
quoted answer. Lead each page with a complete, self-contained answer under the
real question; back claims with a specific stat or source; and make sure an AI
crawler can actually read the page. If you only do one thing this quarter, make
your most important page the cleanest answer to its core question on the web.
