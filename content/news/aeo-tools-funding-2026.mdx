**AEO and [generative engine optimization](/glossary/generative-engine-optimization)
tooling has crossed $200M in disclosed funding as of early 2026, and the money is
flowing toward one thing: measuring how often AI engines cite you.** Citation
tracking, once a manual chore, is now a venture-backed category — a sign that
AI visibility has become a metric leadership actually watches.

## Who is raising the money?

Several specialist vendors. Profound has raised roughly $55M across two rounds,
backed by Kleiner Perkins and Sequoia — sometimes reported as a ~$58.5M Series B
led by Sequoia — and was named a G2 AEO Leader for Winter 2026, positioning
itself around enterprise-grade AI-visibility monitoring. Bluefish has raised
about $68M total, while Evertune and Scrunch have each raised about $19M.

## What does the funding wave mean for AEO?

That measurement is maturing fast. You can now track
[citation](/glossary/citation) share and
[share of voice](/glossary/share-of-voice) across AI engines with real rigor,
which makes AI visibility defensible as a line item and easier to improve
deliberately. The catch is that a dashboard reports the score; it does not raise
it. Being cited still comes from readable, authoritative, answer-first content —
the tools just tell you whether it is working.

## What should you do about it?

Instrument before you invest. Establish how often the AI engines cite and
recommend you today, then treat that as a baseline to move — not a goal a tool
achieves for you.

Start with [how to track AI citations](/learn/how-to-track-ai-citations), and
pair it with the fundamentals in [what is AEO](/learn/what-is-aeo).
