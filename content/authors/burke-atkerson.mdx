Burke Atkerson's path into AEO started at eight years old, with a secondhand book
on QBasic. He used it to write a chat program that answered questions in plain
language — a crude ancestor of the assistants people now talk to every day, built
decades before they existed. That first taste of making a machine respond never
left him.

In the years since, Burke has built businesses across a wide range of
industries — from oilfield services and retail to real estate investing and
construction, and a digital marketing company whose work spanned four continents.
Through all of it, the part that made him come alive was the same: the marketing
side, and staying at the front edge of the technology that powers it.

When generative AI arrived, Burke went deep immediately — studying how the models
worked and, more importantly, how they were changing the way people search for and
discover everything. He came away convinced that the move from links to answers is
a shift on the scale of the internet's own arrival, and he found his specialty in
the middle of it: AI search and Answer Engine Optimization.

He founded AEO Canon to turn that conviction into a practical, evidence-grounded
body of work — the framework, the research, and the day-to-day playbooks that help
businesses become the answer AI recommends. He holds an MBA and is completing a
DBA with an emphasis on marketing and AI.
