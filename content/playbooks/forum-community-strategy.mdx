lower-competition surface for becoming the helpful expert engines cite.

## Platform overview

Forums and communities span public industry forums, Stack Exchange sites,
archived Discord and Slack groups, and other Q&A-style venues where practitioners
help each other. They hold deep, specific, experience-based expertise — and the
public, indexable ones are read and cited by engines. Competition for the best
answer is often far lower than on Reddit, making them efficient places to build
authority. This is the [Reddit playbook](/authority/reddit-aeo-playbook) extended
to the rest of the community web.

## Why answer engines trust communities

Answer engines trust specialized communities because their answers are written by
genuine practitioners solving real problems — high-signal, hard-to-fake expertise.

The same dynamic that makes [Reddit](/authority/reddit-aeo-playbook) the most-cited
domain applies to other communities: authentic answers to specific questions are
exactly what engines want. It's the [Authority pillar](/learn/aeo-pillar-authority)
earned through genuine participation.

## What NOT to do

<Callout variant="warning" title="No spam, sockpuppets, or drive-by link drops">
  <p>
    Don't create fake accounts, drop promotional links into unrelated threads, or
    spin up low-effort answers at scale. Communities ban this fast, and it
    associates your brand with spam rather than expertise. The Canon rejects it
    because the only thing engines are rewarding here is authentic helpfulness —
    which manipulation actively destroys.
  </p>
</Callout>

## How to measure it

Measure community participation by the citations and recognition your genuine help
earns. Track: whether your answers appear in AI responses to target questions;
referral traffic from the communities you're active in; mentions of your brand or
experts within them; and your standing (reputation, accepted answers) on sites
that track it. Treat it as ongoing presence, not a campaign.
