## Why off-site authority decides AI trust

Off-site authority decides AI trust because engines judge you largely by what
others say about you, not what you say about yourself. The evidence is unambiguous:
[Ahrefs' study of 75,000 brands](https://ahrefs.com/blog/ai-overview-brand-correlation/)
found brand web mentions correlate with AI visibility at **0.664**, more than three
times the **0.218** for backlinks, with YouTube mentions stronger still. Reddit,
YouTube, Wikipedia, and LinkedIn rank among the most-cited domains in AI answers.
This is the [Authority pillar](/pillars/authority) made measurable: AI
trusts what the web already trusts.

## How to use your score

Use your score to prioritize, not to grade yourself. Each unchecked signal is a
surface where competitors may be getting vouched for and you aren't — and each
links to the playbook that closes it. Work the highest-leverage gaps first: for
most teams that's [Reddit](/authority/reddit-aeo-playbook),
[YouTube](/authority/youtube-aeo-playbook), and
[branded mentions](/authority/build-branded-mentions), because those carry the
strongest signal. Then fill in the surfaces most relevant to your category.

## How to measure off-site authority over time

Measure off-site authority by tracking the breadth, quality, and durability of how
the web references you — then re-checking on a schedule. Watch: total branded
mentions across sources (linked and unlinked); presence and accuracy on the
highest-signal platforms; whether engines describe and cite you accurately; and
branded search lift as a proxy for the web talking about you more.

Because AI citations are volatile — [Semrush found](https://www.semrush.com/blog/url-volatility-ai-overviews/)
that 40–60% of cited sources change monthly and Google keeps a given URL in an AI
Overview only about 3.87 days on average — durability comes from breadth, not any
single placement. Re-run this audit quarterly, which is the
[Adaptability pillar](/pillars/adaptability) applied to your off-site
footprint.
