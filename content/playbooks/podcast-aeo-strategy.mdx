## Platform overview

Podcasts are long-form conversations that increasingly ship with transcripts, show
notes, YouTube versions, and companion articles — turning a spoken appearance into
durable, indexable text across multiple trusted platforms. For your experts, a
guest spot is both a reputation signal and a content multiplier, overlapping
directly with the [YouTube](/authority/youtube-aeo-playbook) and
[branded mentions](/authority/build-branded-mentions) surfaces.

## Why answer engines trust podcasts

Answer engines trust podcast appearances because being invited onto a credible
show is a third-party endorsement of expertise — and the resulting transcripts put
your name and ideas on trusted platforms.

The signal is [corroboration](/glossary/corroboration): respected hosts vouching for your experts, captured
in text engines can read. That's the [Authority pillar](/pillars/authority)
delivered through audio.

## What NOT to do

<Callout variant="warning" title="No pay-to-play passed off as earned, no thin spam shows">
  <p>
    Don't buy guest spots on low-quality "podcast networks" built only for links,
    or spin up a fake show to interview yourself. Manufactured appearances on
    junk platforms carry no real endorsement and can associate your brand with
    spam. The Canon rejects this because the value of a podcast mention is the
    genuine credibility of the host and audience — which can't be bought cheaply.
  </p>
</Callout>

## How to measure it

Measure podcasts by the credible mentions and recognition they build. Track:
number and quality of appearances on relevant shows; whether the resulting
transcripts and pages get cited in AI answers; branded search lift after notable
appearances; and growth in your experts' recognition as named authorities. It's a
relationship-driven, compounding signal — measure the body of work over quarters.
