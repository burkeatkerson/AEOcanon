**Quora and Q&A sites pair real questions with expert answers in exactly the shape
AI engines want to [cite](/glossary/citation), so writing genuinely useful, well-evidenced answers to
questions in your field is a direct off-site [authority](/glossary/brand-authority) move.** It's a
complementary surface to Reddit — smaller, but unusually well-aligned with how
people query AI.

## Platform overview

Quora is a large Q&A platform where people ask questions and individuals answer
from expertise. Because the content is explicitly structured as questions and
answers — and indexed publicly — it maps neatly onto the conversational queries
answer engines serve. It's most valuable for questions where one of your experts
can give the definitive answer, and it complements the broader
[forum and community strategy](/authority/forum-community-strategy).

## Why answer engines value Q&A content

Answer engines value Q&A content because its structure already matches their job:
a clear question paired with a direct, experience-based answer.

## What NOT to do

<Callout variant="warning" title="No answer spam or link-bait">
  <p>
    Don't post thin answers that exist only to drop links, reuse the same canned
    response across questions, or use fake expert profiles. Quora downranks and
    removes this, and it builds no real authority. The Canon rejects it because the
    only thing worth doing here — and the only thing that lasts — is being
    genuinely the best answer to a real question.
  </p>
</Callout>

## How to measure it

Measure Quora by whether your genuine answers get read, cited, and build expert
recognition. Track: views and upvotes on your definitive answers; whether they
surface in AI responses to those questions; [referral traffic](/glossary/ai-referral-traffic) from quora.com; and
growth in your experts' recognition. As a secondary surface, weigh the effort
against higher-priority platforms.
