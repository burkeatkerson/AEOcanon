**Digital PR for AI means creating genuinely newsworthy assets that earn brand
mentions across the web — because for answer engines, mentions matter far more
than backlinks.** It's the engine room of off-site authority: the work that makes
the wider web say your name, which is what engines listen for.

## Platform overview

Digital PR is the practice of earning coverage and mentions in publications,
niche sites, newsletters, and communities by giving them something genuinely worth
writing about. Unlike link building, the deliverable isn't a link — it's your
brand being referenced, quoted, and corroborated across the sources engines read.
It's also what makes other authority surfaces possible: the reliable sources a
[Wikipedia](/authority/wikipedia-strategy) article needs come from PR.

## Why answer engines trust mentions

Answer engines trust brand mentions because they're the web's distributed verdict
on who matters — harder to fake than a link and richer in context.

[Ahrefs' study of 75,000 brands](https://ahrefs.com/blog/ai-overview-brand-correlation/)
found brand web mentions were the strongest on-page-independent correlate of AI
Overview visibility, more than three times stronger than backlinks. That single
finding reframes PR as the center of off-site [authority](/pillars/authority),
not a link-acquisition tactic.

## What NOT to do

<Callout variant="warning" title="No fake news, paid placements posing as editorial, or spam pitches">
  <p>
    Don't fabricate "studies," buy undisclosed sponsored coverage dressed as
    editorial, or blast spam pitches. Manufactured or deceptive coverage erodes
    the trust that makes mentions valuable, and engines (and journalists) are
    increasingly good at spotting thin, AI-spun "data." The Canon rejects this
    because the entire value of a mention is that it was genuinely earned.
  </p>
</Callout>

## How to measure it

Measure digital PR by mentions earned and the authority they build, not just
links. Track: volume and quality of brand mentions across reputable sources;
branded search lift over time; whether engines describe you more accurately and
cite you more often; and coverage in the specific outlets your category's AI
answers draw from. This is a compounding, multi-month signal — measure the trend,
not the week.
