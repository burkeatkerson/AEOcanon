## Platform overview

Review platforms — G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Google, Yelp, and category-specific
sites — aggregate third-party sentiment about your product or service. They're
authoritative, structured, and explicitly about quality, which makes them a
natural input when an engine weighs recommendations. They also feed the broader
[branded mentions](/authority/build-branded-mentions) signal.

## Why answer engines trust review platforms

Answer engines trust review platforms because they aggregate many independent
voices into a structured, hard-to-fake signal of real-world quality.

When an engine recommends options, it leans on sources that corroborate quality
from outside your own site — exactly what review platforms provide. That's the
[Authority pillar](/pillars/authority) at work: trust earned off your own
domain.

## What NOT to do

<Callout variant="warning" title="No fake, bought, or gated reviews">
  <p>
    Never buy reviews, write fake ones, gate incentives on positive ratings, or
    suppress negatives. These violate platform policies and consumer-protection
    law, and review platforms are increasingly good at detecting and penalizing
    manipulation — which can wipe out your profile and your credibility. The Canon
    rejects this because a faked reputation is both wrong and fragile. Earn the
    reviews.
  </p>
</Callout>

## How to measure it

Measure review platforms by the authenticity and trajectory of your reputation,
not a single score. Track: review volume and average rating over time on your
priority platforms; response rate and speed; and whether engines describe your
reputation accurately and recommend you for "best/top" queries in your category.
Watch sentiment trends as a product signal, not just a marketing one.
