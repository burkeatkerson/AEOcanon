# AEO Editorial Workflow Template

A repeatable, seven-stage workflow for producing content answer engines cite.
AI can assist at several stages, but the original angle, the evidence, and the
final judgment are human. Adapt the owners and tools to your team.

---

## Stage 1 — Question & brief
- Source the real question (sales/support, Reddit, "People also ask", prompt set).
- Fill the AEO Content Brief: target question, intent, evidence required, original angle.
- Owner: strategist / editor.

## Stage 2 — Research & originality
- Gather primary sources and the specific stats/quotes you'll cite.
- Decide the original angle only you can provide (proprietary data, first-hand
  experience, defended POV). AI research speeds this up; it can't supply your data.
- Owner: writer / SME.

## Stage 3 — Draft (AI-assisted optional)
- Write answer-first: complete answer in sentence one, question-shaped H2s,
  self-contained 120–180-word passages.
- AI may help with outline or a first draft — treat it as raw material, never a
  finished page. Add the original angle and real examples by hand.
- Owner: writer.

## Stage 4 — Evidence & links
- Add inline statistics, quotations, and named sources (in the sentence).
- Add 3–5 internal links and link external primary sources.
- Owner: writer.

## Stage 5 — Quality control
- Run the AEO QC Checklist: originality, credibility (verified facts), extractability.
- Reject generic, unevidenced, or unverifiable drafts back to Stage 2/3.
- Owner: editor (not the drafter).

## Stage 6 — Publish
- Named author with credentials; publish date and last-updated date.
- Article + FAQPage schema; clean, server-rendered HTML.
- Owner: editor / publisher.

## Stage 7 — Measure & refresh
- Track citation share per engine for the target question.
- Schedule the next review by clock-speed (fast: monthly; slow: 6–12 months).
- Feed gaps back into Stage 1.
- Owner: analyst / editor.

---

### Definition of done
A page is done when it leads with the answer, proves every claim with cited
evidence, says something only you could say, carries a named author and dates, and
has passed editor QC — not when a draft exists.
