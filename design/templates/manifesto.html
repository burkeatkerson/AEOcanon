The AEO Manifesto

The world changed. The game changed with it.

For thirty years the web asked one question: can people find you? A new one has arrived.

Read the creed

For thirty years, the web had a single question: can people find you? The answer was a list. Ten blue links. A race to the top. The machine was a directory, and the goal was to be filed correctly.

That world still exists. But it is no longer the only world that matters.

A new question has arrived: when the machine speaks, does it say your name?

When an answer engine responds, it does not hand the user a menu. It gives an answer — one response, synthesized from the sources it trusts most. The cited gain visibility, credibility, and traffic. Everyone else gets nothing.

The old game was to rank.
The new game is to be cited.
They are not the same game.
01 — The mistake

Most people are playing the wrong game.

The instinct, when a new system appears, is to map old tactics onto it. What is the AEO version of a backlink? Is there a schema to add, a file to publish, a trick that cracks it open? This instinct is understandable. It is also how you lose.

The shortcuts do not work here — and they are not neutral. They are actively counterproductive.

Keyword stuffing — the oldest reflex in optimization
lowers visibility 10%
Schema markup — tested across ~2,000 pages
no citation lift
The llms.txt file — across all AI crawler requests
fetched <0.1%

But the deeper failure is not the tactics. It is the posture. Gaming signals is not a strategy when the machine is built to detect and correct for gaming.

Everything that can be manipulated will eventually be corrected. The only strategy that survives correction is to deserve it.

02 — The conviction

What we believe.

“Becoming the best available source is the entire discipline.”

AEO is not a set of tricks for manipulating machine behavior. It is the practice of being genuinely excellent at communicating — and building the conditions under which any well-designed engine recognizes that excellence. That is not a loophole. It is the design.

The eight beliefs

Every principle is also just honest, useful communication. That is the point.

1

Access is the price of admission.

If a machine cannot read your page, it cannot quote you. Open your doors to the crawlers, render in plain HTML, load fast. Fix this first — before everything else.

2

Alignment before optimization.

You can be the best answer to a question nobody is asking AI. People don’t ask “best CRM small business” — they ask “we’ve outgrown spreadsheets, what should we actually use and why?” Meet them at their real question.

3

The sentence matters more than the page.

Engines retrieve passages, not pages — they photocopy a paragraph, they don’t read the book. Write the answer you want quoted and put it first. The inverted pyramid is the structure of a source worth citing.

44% of citations come from the first third of a page
4

Authority lives off your own site.

You are what the web says you are. The machine is listening to the room — Reddit, YouTube, Wikipedia, the publications that say your name unprompted. Give the room something true to say about you.

mentions 0.664 vs backlinks 0.218
5

Show your work.

Numbers, not adjectives. Quotations, not assertions. Named sources and a real author with real credentials. It is the evidence of a mind that actually engaged with the world — and machines recognize the difference.

statistics +30% · quotations +41%
6

Say what only you can say.

Generic content has always been weak; now it is also infinite. The scarcity has moved to what was always valuable — the specifically true, genuinely known, impossible to replicate. Your data cannot be scraped. Be the primary source.

7

Recency is a form of honesty.

An undated page is a claim without a timestamp. Updating content is not maintenance — it is integrity, the ongoing commitment that what you published still reflects reality. Match your cadence to your topic’s clock-speed.

8

Build systems that bend.

The engines will change; the signals will shift. Don’t optimize harder for today’s configuration — build the measurement habits and adaptive doctrine to move with the river. The principles endure. The specifics are written in pencil.

03 — The long game

A doctrine for a source that compounds.

Original research becomes the citation all derivative content must reference. Authority earned through genuine presence becomes structural recognition in the engines’ models. Answer-first content earns citation that page-level rankings never could. Measured, updated, maintained — none of this erodes. It accumulates.

Shortcuts produce short-term visibility.
The Canon produces enduring citation.

The engines are not finished. They have barely begun. The practitioners who win will not be the ones who cleverly optimized for this quarter’s configuration — they will be the ones who built genuine sources, and had the discipline to update their doctrine as fast as the engines updated theirs.

What we stand for

We stand for content that deserves to be cited. That answers the real question, backs every claim with evidence, says something nobody else has said — and is structured to be lifted and quoted in the first sentence.

We stand against hollow optimization. Against keyword stuffing that degrades the visibility it seeks. Against gaming a signal rather than earning a reputation.

Not because it is clever.
Because it is right.
