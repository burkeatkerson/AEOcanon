Content architecture basics

You audited your visibility last module. Most of what dragged your score down wasn’t missing content — it was content a machine couldn’t cleanly lift. Here’s how to structure a page so the answer is easy to take.

  • Convert one marketing section into a Q&A block

An answer engine doesn’t read your page the way a person does. It scans for self-contained units of meaning it can extract and attribute with confidence. A beautifully written paragraph that buries the answer in its third sentence is, to a model, nearly invisible. The fix isn’t more words — it’s better structure.

The three structures engines reward

Across thousands of cited pages, three patterns recur. None requires a redesign; each is a way of arranging what you already know.

  • The direct answer. Lead with the answer, then explain. “A heat pump should be serviced twice a year” beats four sentences of preamble.
  • The Q&A block. Pose the customer’s actual question as a heading, answer it in 1–3 sentences underneath.
  • The structured fact. Hours, areas served, pricing, credentials — stated as data, not prose, and mirrored in schema.
You’re not writing for a reader who’ll be patient. You’re writing for a machine that will quote you only if you make it easy.

A worked example

Take a line most service sites have: “With years of trusted experience, we proudly deliver premium comfort solutions for your home.” A model can extract nothing from it — no service, no place, no fact. Rewritten as a structured answer:

Before → afterWe install and repair ductless mini-split systems across Travis County, TX. Same-day service available; most repairs completed in one visit.

Same length. Now it names the service, the area, and two facts an engine can lift verbatim into a recommendation.
